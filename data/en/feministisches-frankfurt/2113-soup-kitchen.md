# Soup Kitchen

*Bertha Pappenheim – Jewish Care Work*

Bertha Pappenheim – Jüdische Care Arbeit

![Platzhalter](../../images/ee1966f7f56103ae1baeb63686d7c114_original.jpg)
*Author: Hannah Rödel | License: HMF/Hannah Rödel | Description: Platzhalter*

The Israelitische Suppenanstalt (Israelite Soup Kitchen) at Theobald-Christ-Str. 5 was founded in 1880 to provide support for those in need. Bertha Pappenheim was actively engaged in social work. She contributed to the Israelitische Suppenanstalt (Israelite Soup Kitchen) in Frankfurt’s Ostend district, where around 200 people were provided with kosher meals every day. Nearby was the Israelitisches Mädchenwaisenhaus (Israelite Girls’ Orphanage), directed by Bertha Pappenheim, as well as the Mädchenclub (Girls’ Club) she founded – one of the first institutions of open girls’ work in Frankfurt.
