# Kleine Pfingstweide

*Henriette Zobel – Escalating Protest 1848*

Henriette Zobel – Eskalierender Protest 1848

![Die Pfingstweide](../../images/961c0657b8f235e7d924e22d5e363664_original.jpg)
*Author: Johann Andreas Benjamin Reges | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Die Pfingstweide*

In 1848, the former Kleine Pfingstweide was the scene of a mass rally in which over 10,000 people protested against the Waffenstillstand von Malmö (Malmö Armistice). This moment reflected the disappointment at the lack of democratic renewal after the March Revolution of 1848 and the first free elections to parliament. Henriette Zobel (1813 - 1865) was also among the protesters. She was later accused of being involved in the killing of two members of parliament—an incident that occurred during the heated atmosphere surrounding the protests on the Pfingstweide. Whether she was actually guilty remained controversial. The verdict against her was very harsh and can be seen as a deterrent to further political engagement by women.
