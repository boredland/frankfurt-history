# Damenstift am Roßmarkt

*Karoline von Günderrode – Stifts­le­ben*

![Der Roßmarkt von Südwesten](../../images/a1f42cdcd9401b33b1d67da2148e553b_original.jpg)
*1827 — Author: J. Müller | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Der Roßmarkt von Südwesten*

Karoline von Günderrode (1780–1806) left her parents' home at the age of 17 and moved into the Cronstetten- Stift (Convent) in Kranichhof on Roßmarkt, founded by Justina Catharina Steffan von Cronstetten (1677–1766). At first, she found life there very restrictive, as the daily routine followed strict rules. At the same time, she took advantage of the freedom she was given. The silence and seclusion of the convent gave her space for her literary work. She published her work under a male pseudonym and dealt with issues of education, freedom, and female self-determination—topics that she also reflected on in close exchange with other women.

*Author: Sofia Schnitzler*
