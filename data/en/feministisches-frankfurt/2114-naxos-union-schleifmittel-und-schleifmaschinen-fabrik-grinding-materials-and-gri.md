# Naxos-Union Schleifmittel- und Schleifmaschinen-Fabrik (Grinding Materials and Grinding Machine Factory)

*Marie Eleonore Pfungst – Company Director’s Seat*

Marie E. Pfungst – Unternehmenssitz der Direktorin

The Naxos-Union Schleifmittel- und Schleifmaschinen-Fabrik (Grinding Materials and Grinding Machine Factory), including the Naxoshalle (Naxos Hall), was located on Wittelsbacher Allee since its founding in 1871. Naxos-Union held the exclusive distribution rights for natural emery from the island of Naxos, which was processed into grinding wheels in Frankfurt. The founders, Julius Pfungst (1834–1899) and his wife Rosette Pfungst (1839–1922), were socially committed to the welfare of their workers.

Marie E. Pfungst was born into this successful entrepreneurial family. From 1912 to 1933, she served as CEO of the company. Marie E. Pfungst was an early advocate for women’s rights and education. She was active in the Allgemeiner Deutscher Frauenverein (General German Women’s Association), organized Realgymnasium courses (secondary school courses) for girls, and supported women in need. Through her involvement in numerous associations, she worked to promote fairer educational opportunities and social participation.
