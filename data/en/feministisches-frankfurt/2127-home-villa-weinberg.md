# Home – Villa Weinberg

*Ethel M. von Weinberg – Art venue and military hospital*

Ethel M. von Weinberg - Kunstort und Lazarett

![Album Villa Walfried, Frankfurt am Main, Niederrad, Portierhaus, Fotografie](../../images/270308ed38597a84eea7ae319cbb9d27_original.jpg)
*Author: Friedrich Lauffer | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Album Villa Walfried, Frankfurt am Main, Niederrad, Portierhaus, Fotografie*

In 1898, Carl von Weinberg (1861–1943) and his wife Ethel May von Weinberg (1866-1937) had an English country-style villa built in Niederrad. The property, with its own park, was located on the site of what is now Carl von Weinberg Park. May von Weinberg designed the interior of the approximately 100 rooms and turned the house into a meeting place for family, friends, and business partners. Her extensive art collection was also housed here. During the First World War, the villa was used as a military hospital. May von Weinberg was socially engaged, founding nurseries, orphanages, and a war kitchen. She focused particularly on supporting families and children in difficult life situations.
