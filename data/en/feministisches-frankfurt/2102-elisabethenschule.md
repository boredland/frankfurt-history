# Elisabethenschule

*Henriette Fürth – Professional training for women*

Henriette Fürth – Berufsausbildungen für Frauen

![Elisabethenschule am Börsenplatz, Fotografie](../../images/990813a4eaf446923a5aa5f309e740e6_original.jpg)
*Author: Carl Friedrich Mylius | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Elisabethenschule am Börsenplatz, Fotografie*

The Elisabethenschule was founded in 1803 as the girls' section of the Musterschule. From 1876 onwards, it developed into an independent secondary school for girls and also served as a training center for future teachers. At the age of 15, Henriette Fürth attended the Elisabethenschule teacher training college with the aim of becoming a teacher. However, after only six months, she dropped out of the program on her father's advice.

As a Jewish woman, her chances of finding employment at a public school were slim. In addition, she would have had to give up her career if she married. Instead, she earned a living for her family by writing political articles.
Later, Henriette Fürth became an SPD city councilor and an important voice between the bourgeois, proletarian, and Jewish women's movements.
