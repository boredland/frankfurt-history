# Wanda Pratschke: Betty (1983)

*Women's bodies in opposition to the “male gaze”*

![Wanda Pratschke's "Betty" ist eine von einer Vielzahl an Skulpturen im öffentlichen Raum, mit denen die Künstlerin normative Körperideale herausfordert und Frauen mehr Sichtbarkeit verleiht.](../../images/14b58d2433f69496b9a0ae4ca4f25ccf_original.jpg)
*Author: Hartmut Schroth | License: Hartmut Schroth | Description: Wanda Pratschke's "Betty" ist eine von einer Vielzahl an Skulpturen im öffentlichen Raum, mit denen die Künstlerin normative Körperideale herausfordert und Frauen mehr Sichtbarkeit verleiht.*

Wanda Pratschke's decades of work as a sculptor in a traditionally male-dominated field has emancipatory power, even without loud proclamation. Her voluminous female figures embody the concept of “taking up space” in an impressive way. Whether smooth or rough, powerful or gaunt, Wanda Pratschke's bronzes show the diversity of female bodies and thus contribute to a body-positive visual language that turns away from unrealistic ideals of beauty. Several of her works can be discovered in Frankfurt. “Betty,” a larger-than-life sculpture, stands in the Wallanlagen. The ""Unbesiegbare"" (Invincible)” 2021 lies confidently on the campus of Goethe University, her head stretched toward the sun. At the airport, you will find The ""Schoene"" (Beautiful) 2001 in Terminal I and “Vier Frauen "" (Four Women) 2001 in Terminal II.

*Author: Naomi Rado, Kulturamt Frankfurt, 2025*
