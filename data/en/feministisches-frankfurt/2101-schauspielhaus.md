# Schauspielhaus

*Mathilde Einzig – An exceptional career at the Schauspiel (Theater)*

Mathilde Einzig – Ausnahmekarriere am Schauspiel

![](../../images/297c2933638c231832a81ab419e6abac_original.jpg)
*Author: Nini und Carry Hess | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte/Verweist*

Where the Schauspiel and Opera are located today, there was already a large theater in 1902, but it was severely damaged during World War II in 1944. Mathilde Einzig, aged 16, began her acting career there in 1902. From 1908 onwards, she mainly appeared in so-called “Charakterfach"" (character roles) and became famous for her portrayals of “Komische Alte"" (comic characters). In 1932, she performed at the Römerbergfestspielen (Römerberg Festival). As a Jewish artist, she was replaced by so-called “arische"" (Aryan) actors at the Schauspiel in 1934 after the National Socialists came to power, and shortly thereafter emigrated with her family to Palestine. In 1947, she returned to Europe, continued her career as an actress, and also performed again in Frankfurt.
