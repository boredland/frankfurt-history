# Home - Bleichstraße 60

*Elisabeth H. Winterhalter – Self-determined love*

Elisabeth H. Winterhalter – Selbstbestimmt Lieben

![Bleichstraße-Hinterhäuser: altes Senkenbergisches Institut, Eschenheimer Turm, Aquarell](../../images/096d233d2aec0c67272efde361f19da9_original.jpg)
*Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Bleichstraße-Hinterhäuser: altes Senkenbergisches Institut, Eschenheimer Turm, Aquarell*

Bleichstraße 60 was the shared home of the doctor Elisabeth H. Winterhalter (1856 -1952) and her partner, the painter Ottilie W. Roederstein (1859–1937). On the advice of friends, the two moved to liberal Frankfurt in 1891 to lead a self-determined life as a couple. Elisabeth H. Winterhalter was one of the first gynecologists in Germany. She made her mark in a male-dominated profession and was also committed to promoting access to education for women and girls. The watercolor by Carl Theodor von Reifenstein (1820-1893) shows the backyards of Bleichstraße, the Eschenheimer Tower, and parts of the Senckenbergischen Instituts (Senckenberg Institute). The photograph by Carl F. Mylius (1827-1916) shows the view from Bleichstraße looking south.
