Fembib (Feminist Library Frankfurt)
Come by and discover feminist literature!

The Fembib stands on the shoulders of Frankfurt feminists who fought decades before us!

At the Studierendenhaus Bockenheim (Student House Bockenheim), the Autonomes FrauenLesben-Referat (today: AFLintaR, Autonomous WomenLesbian/FLINTA Council) had maintained a book collection since the 1990s. In the early 2000s, there was briefly a lending library, but for many years the books were difficult to access. We wanted to change that with the Fembib. Thanks to book donations, funding, and voluntary work, the Fembib opened in the winter of 2022.

We wanted to create a space where authors who receive little recognition can become visible. A space where feminists can freely browse, read, and debate. That is why the Fembib presents diverse perspectives on feminist debates, both past and present: it offers scholarly literature alongside feminist novels and poetry, graphic novels, and children’s and young adult books.

The Feministische Bibliothek Frankfurt (Fembib, Feminist Library Frankfurt) is run by a small volunteer collective. It is open every Tuesday from 4:30 p.m. to 7 p.m. Readings and discussion events are held regularly. The Fembib is located in the rooms of the Autonomes FLINTA-Referat (Autonomous FLINTA Council) in the Studierendenhaus Bockenheim, Mertonstraße 26–28.
