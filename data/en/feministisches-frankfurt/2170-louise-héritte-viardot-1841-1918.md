# Louise Héritte-Viardot (1841-1918)

*Composer and fighter for equality*

Dr. Hoch’s Konservatorium

![Portät Heritte Viardot](../../images/a759e04c8f540aa7e20a31ee42b8eebc_original.jpg)
*Author: Unbekannt | License: Aus den "Mémoires de Louise Héritte-Viardot" | Description: Portät Heritte Viardot*

Louise Héritte-Viardot was born in Paris in 1841 and grew up in an artistic environment. She learned to play the piano at an early age and received singing lessons from her mother, Pauline Viardot-Garcia. In 1871, she made her debut as an opera singer in St. Petersburg, but had to give up her singing career due to health problems. Her opera “Lindoro” celebrated a successful premiere. A cantata she submitted to a competition under a male pseudonym won first prize. However, she was stripped of this prize when it was discovered that the work had been written by a woman. In the 1880s, Clara Schumann recommended her to the Dr. Hoch Conservatory in Frankfurt. There she taught the singing and opera class as well as the choir. Louise Héritte-Viardot died in Heidelberg in 1918.
