# Home – Zum Weißen Hirsch

*Maria Belli-Gontard – Starting point for expeditions*

Maria Belli-Gontard – Ausgangspunkt für Expeditionen

![Haus Weißer Hirsch, Vogelschauansicht, Weißfrauenkloster, Zeichnung](../../images/37e1c7bd41e4728d93c353a5bf4a82c7_original.jpg)
*Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Haus Weißer Hirsch, Vogelschauansicht, Weißfrauenkloster, Zeichnung*

The “Wheiße Hirsch” was one of the largest estates with extensive gardens in Frankfurt's old town. It got its name from the historical use of the site as a deer enclosure in the Middle Ages. From 1753 onwards, the estate belonged to the Gontard family. Today, the Commerzbank Tower stands on the site. In 1845, Maria Belli-Gontard (1788-1883) undertook a solo journey to the so-called “Orient” – a remarkable step for a woman of her time. Her travelogues reveal both cultural interest and a Eurocentric perspective. Her privileged position as a wealthy European woman gave her the freedom to transcend social boundaries and go her own way.
