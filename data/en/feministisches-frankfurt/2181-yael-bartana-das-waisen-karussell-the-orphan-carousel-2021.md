# Yael Bartana: "Das Waisen-Karussell" (The Orphan Carousel) 2021

*Memorial for the "Kindertransporte"  1938/1939*

*Author: Alexander Paul Englert | License: Kulturamt Frankfurt | Description: Yael Bartanas Karussell im Frakfurter Bahnhofsviertel erinnert an die Kindertransporte der Jahre 1938/1939, deren organisatorisches Zentrum Frankfurt am Main war.*

Yael Bartana's work encompasses a variety of art forms, including films, light installations, performances, and sculptures. With neon signs such as “I Am A Feminist” or “Patriarchy Is History,” she sends a clear message about her artistic agenda. Her slogan “What If Women Ruled The World,” for example, was displayed on the wall of the National Museum of Contemporary Art in Athens (ΕΜΣΤ) in 2024. This question also forms the starting point for a performance in which Yael Bartana depicts a fictional country with a government made up exclusively of women who must face a global crisis. For the city of Frankfurt, she designed “Das Waisen-Karussell” (The Orphan Carousel), a memorial dedicated to the “ Kindertransporte” of 1938/1939. Further information on the memorial can be found in the thematic section “Frankfurt und der NS"
