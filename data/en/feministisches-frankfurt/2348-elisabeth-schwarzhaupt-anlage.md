# Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt Anlage

*Streets named after women*

![Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt Anlage](../../images/adf4173f78dddffd6ba9c9cf9bbb2bb1_original.jpg)
*2025 — Author: Sofia Schnitzler | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt Anlage*

This park area commemorates Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt, who asserted herself as a woman in a male-dominated political system and became Germany’s first federal minister.

There are around 3,380 official street names in Frankfurt, including streets, squares, bridges, parks, and green spaces. Of these, 1,332 are named after people—1,065 after men and only 156 after women. Another 12 names commemorate one woman and one man each, 98 commemorate families or noble families, and one commemorates a mixed group with no family connection. These figures show how unevenly memory is distributed in urban space. Street names tell us who is visible – and whose history is hardly represented in public space.
