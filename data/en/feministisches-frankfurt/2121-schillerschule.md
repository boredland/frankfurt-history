# Schillerschule

*Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt – Education for girls*

Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt – Bildung für Mädchen

![Platzhalter](../../images/cea2d5f03442cd244040815fa3304311_original.jpg)
*Author: Hannah Rödel | License: HMF/Hannah Rödel | Description: Platzhalter*

Founded in 1908, the Schillerschule was the first high school for girls in Frankfurt and enabled young women to obtain their high school diploma. Elisabeth Schwarzhaupt 1901-1986) took her high school exams here and was already certain at that time: “Was Männer können, können wir Frauen schon lange"" (What men can do, we women have been able to do for a long time).

This guiding principle shaped her life: as a CDU politician, she became the first female federal minister in the history of the Bundesrepublik (Federal Republic) in 1961 and was committed to health reforms and equality for women.
The photograph shows the Rotkäppchen-Brunnen (Little Red Riding Hood fountain) in the schoolyard. An information sheet promotes the work of the then Minister of Health with the title: “Wissen Sie, was ein Minister täglich tut?“ (Do you know what a minister does every day?).
