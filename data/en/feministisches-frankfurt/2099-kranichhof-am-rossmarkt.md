# Kranichhof am Roßmarkt

*Justina von Cronstetten – Founder of the Ladies' Convent*

Justina von Cronstetten – Gründerin Damenstift

![Das Cronstettensche Stift auf dem Roßmarkt, Frank'sches Geschäftshaus, Fotografie](../../images/225c163b7467c760109287efd86ad228_original.jpg)
*Author: Friedrich Weisbrod | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Das Cronstettensche Stift auf dem Roßmarkt, Frank'sches Geschäftshaus, Fotografie*

The Kranichhof am Roßmarkt was the home of Justina Catharina Steffan von Cronstetten, where she lived until her death. In order to secure her social status and preserve the family fortune in the long term, she founded the Cronstetten-Damenstift (Cronstetten Ladies' Convent) in the parental Kranichhof. This was intended to provide financial security and a life befitting their status for twelve unmarried women from the families of the Alten-Limpurg joint inheritance. Deeply influenced by her grandmother's pietistic piety, Justina Cronstetten laid down strict, monastery-like rules for the residents of the convent: daily prayers, simple clothing, and a secluded life without male visitors.
