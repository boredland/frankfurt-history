# Zoo

*Erna Pinner - Tierischer Inspirationsort*

![Studie von 3 Wildrinderköpfen](../../images/4b1709b7eb7b47534a8f68b944c3ab9a_original.jpg)
*Author: Erna Pinner | License: HMF | Description: Studie von 3 Wildrinderköpfen*

After the first ten years of the Zoologischer Garten (Zoological Garden) in Frankfurt’s Westend district, it was relocated and reopened in 1874 as the Frankfurter Zoo (Frankfurt Zoo) at the Pfingstweide in the Ostend district.

Erna Wilhelmine Pinner used the zoo and its animals as motifs for her drawings. Animals from different climate zones fascinated the artist. Her fleeting yet precise style of painting became characteristic of her artistic development and career as an illustrator and draughtswoman. The artist, natural observer, author, and illustrator had studied painting at the Städelsches Kunstinstitut (Städel School of Art). As an established Jewish artist, she fled the Nazi regime at an early stage and lived permanently abroad. In exile in England, she built a new career as an illustrator of scientific books and remained artistically and journalistically active well into old age.
