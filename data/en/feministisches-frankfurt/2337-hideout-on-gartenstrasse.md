# Hideout on Gartenstraße

*Margarate Herberg und Antonie Sandels*

![Aerial view of Sachsenhausen: Gartenstraße 51 and children's home](../../images/4b4ebb8b1aff7a0fa16b71f83aa0644a_original.jpg)
*around 1915 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Homepage "Platz der vergessenen Kinder" | Description: Aerial view of Sachsenhausen: Gartenstraße 51 and children's home*

In Gartenstraße 51, the Jewish pediatrician Dr. Antonie Sandels (1899-1977), her friend Margarete Herberg (1898-1975), and Herberg’s son hid from persecution under the Nazi regime. The photograph shows the house (No. 51), which served as their place of refuge; in the foreground (No. 24) is the children’s home founded by Bertha Pappenheim (1859-1936) and Henriette Fürth (1861-1938).

Dr. Antonie Sandels earned her doctorate in 1928 and worked as a pediatrician in Frankfurt before losing her medical insurance license in 1933 due to anti-Jewish measures. As pressure increased, she and Margarete Herberg searched for ways to survive. Shortly before her own deportation, she was able to flee to Herberg and hide there; later, the two women found another hiding place and survived the final years of the war together.

After 1945, Sandels reopened a pediatric practice in Heidelberg, supported by Margarete Herberg. Their story shows how closely friendship, courage, and mutual support were intertwined in times of persecution.

*Author: Sofia Schnitzler*
