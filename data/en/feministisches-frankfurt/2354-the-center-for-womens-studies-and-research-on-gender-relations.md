# The Center for Women's Studies and Research on Gender Relations

*Robert-Mayer-Str. 5*

![The founding directors of the center at the opening ceremony: From left to right are Ute Gerhard, Heide Schlüpmann, Susanne Opfermann, and Brita Rang.](../../images/66152830bdc391555d92f165068d782b_original.jpg)
*1997 — Author: N.N. | License: gemeinfrei | Description: The founding directors of the center at the opening ceremony: From left to right are Ute Gerhard, Heide Schlüpmann, Susanne Opfermann, and Brita Rang.*

In 1997, the Center for Women's Studies and Gender Research was founded on the Bockenheim campus of Goethe University Frankfurt. The initiative came from Ute Gerhard, who was appointed to the professorship for sociology with a special focus on women's and gender studies at the University of Frankfurt in 1987. This was the first professorship for women's and gender studies in the Federal Republic of Germany. Other founding members were Professors Susanne Opfermann (American Studies), Brita Rang (Educational Sciences), and Heide Schlüpmann (Film Studies). On June 11, 1997, the center began its work with two employees, Ulla Wischermann (Sociology) and Christine Huth-Hildebrand (Educational Sciences).

On December 7, 2000, on the occasion of the 250th birthday of Cornelia Schlosser, née Goethe, the younger sister of Johann Wolfgang Goethe, the center was renamed the Cornelia Goethe Center for Women's Studies and Gender Research. Since 2023, it has been called the Cornelia Goethe Center for Gender Studies (CGC).
