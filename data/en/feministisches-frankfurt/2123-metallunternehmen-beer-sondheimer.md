# Metallunternehmen Beer & Sondheimer

*Tony Sender – Place of Professional Self-Employment*

Tony Sender – Ort der beruflichen Selbstständigkeit

![Platzhalter](../../images/ee1966f7f56103ae1baeb63686d7c114_original.jpg)
*Author: Hannah Rödel | License: HMF/Hannah Rödel | Description: Platzhalter*

The Metallhandels- und Montanunternehmen Beer & Sondheimer built an administrative building with laboratories and apartments at Bockenheimer Landstraße 25. The original building still stands today and is a place of cultural exchange.

In 1910, Tony Sender began working as a foreign language correspondent at the Paris branch of Beer & Sondheimer. Back in Frankfurt, she rose to the position of department head. 
Tony Sender was a trade unionist, editor, SPD member of the Reichstag, and socialist women's rights activist. She fought for education, social justice, and equality for women – in Frankfurt, in the Reichstag, and from 1933 onwards as a democrat and Jew in exile at the United Nations.
