# Rosy Geiger-Kullmann (1886-1964)

*Dedicated Frankfurter, Composer, and Pianist*

Mainzer Landstraße

![Studio-Portrait von Rosy Geiger-Kullmann](../../images/dc986b2db9320229fcd18d4576647dc6_original.jpg)
*Author: Lilian Photo Studio New York | License: Leo Baeck Institute. | Description: Studio-Portrait von Rosy Geiger-Kullmann*

Born in Frankfurt in 1886, Rosy Kullmann displayed great musical talent already as a child. Her first work, Abendlied (Evening Song) for voice and piano, was performed at the Frankfurter Saalbau (Frankfurt Saalbau concert hall) in 1902 and published in 1907. Even after her marriage to Rudolf Geiger in 1906 and the birth of her children, she remained musically active.

Her greatest success came with the fairy-tale opera Tischlein deck dich (“The Wishing Table”), which premiered in Basel in 1929. Twenty-nine opera houses expressed interest in the work, but in 1933 they withdrew their requests due to anti-Semitic performance bans. In response, Rosy Geiger-Kullmann increasingly dedicated her music to Jewish themes and became active on an institutional level, for example in the founding of the Jüdischer Tonkünstlerbund (Jewish Musicians’ Association).

In 1938, she fled into exile in the United States with parts of her family. Rosy Geiger-Kullmann died in Monterey, USA, in 1964.
