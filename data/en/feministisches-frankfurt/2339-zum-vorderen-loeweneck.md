# Zum vorderen Löweneck

*Christina Barbara Metzler - Head of the banking house*

![Excerpt from the Merian plan](../../images/0c82c3eaf0b38194cee52bb4242da270_original.jpg)
*around 1620 — Author: Matthäus Merian | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Excerpt from the Merian plan*

Bankhaus Metzler seel. Sohn & Co. was founded in 1674 and is the oldest family-owned bank in Germany. Originally a cloth trading company, it developed into an international bank.

Christina Barbara Metzler (1703–1793) managed the company from 1757 as managing director with comprehensive responsibility. A signed purchase agreement proves her official representation of the company—a rare example of female leadership in the 18th century.
She looked after customers, developed the company strategically, and expanded it internationally. As an unmarried woman in this role, she was a historical exception.

*Author: Sofia Schnitzler*
