# Stiftungs Universität (Foundation University)

*Erna Auerbach – First place of study for women*

![Die Goethe Universität](../../images/d7335d03ae14aa8b2c8856bdcd03efad_original.jpg)
*Author: J.B. Ciolina | License: HMF | Description: Die Goethe Universität*

In 1914, citizens of Frankfurt founded the Königliche Universität zu Frankfurt am Main (Royal University of Frankfurt am Main), the first foundation university in Germany. Today it is known as Goethe Universität with its main campus in the Westend district.
Erna Auerbach was part of the first generation of female students at the young university in 1917 – at that time, women made up only about ten percent of the students. She received her doctorate in 1923/24 with a thesis on 16th-century German portrait painting, but the Nazi regime prevented the publication of her work.
As a Jewish artist and art historian, Erna Auerbach fled from the Nazis into exile in London in 1933. There she built a new life for herself and worked not only as an artist but also as a recognized specialist in English portrait painting of the Tudor period.
