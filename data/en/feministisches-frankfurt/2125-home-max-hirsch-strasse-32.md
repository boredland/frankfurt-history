# Home– Max-Hirsch-Straße 32

*Johanna Tesch – Political sphere of influence*

Johanna Tesch – Politischer Wirkungsort

![Gedenktafel für Johanna Tesch](../../images/7cfe08ec959c64af1d4f26e67a632a3d_original.jpg)
*Author: Unbekannt | License: Stadt Frankfurt/Günter Maniewski | Description: Gedenktafel für Johanna Tesch*

In 1910, a new workers' housing estate was established in Riederwald. From 1912 onwards, Johanna Tesch (1875-1945) lived with her family at Max-Hirsch-Straße 32, one of the streets in this estate. Her home soon became a meeting place for working-class families, SPD members, and trade unionists. Today, a Gedenktafel (commemorative plaque) commemorates her political commitment.

Johanna Tesch was active in the SPD, founded Vereine für Arbeiterinnen und Hausangestellte (associations for female workers and domestic servants), and fought for better working conditions and women's suffrage. In 1919, she became one of the first female members of the Reichstag and was particularly committed to the rights of working women and mothers. Under the Nazi regime, the National Socialists deported her to the Ravensbrück concentration camp because of her work as a member of parliament, where she died in 1945.
