# Cornelia Schlosser, née Goethe

*Großer Hirschgraben 21*

![Cornelia Schlosser, née Goethe, portrait drawing; red chalk and black chalk over pencil](../../images/80231ceeb0dff0d6e1bf61db6f2207bf_original.jpg)
*circa 1770 — Author: Johann Ludwig Ernst Morgenstern | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Cornelia Schlosser, née Goethe, portrait drawing; red chalk and black chalk over pencil*

Cornelia Friederica Christiana Goethe was born in Frankfurt in 1750, one year after her brother Johann Wolfgang. She initially received the same middle-class education as her brother. She was not allowed to study. Her secret “diary in letters,” in which she vividly and at times critically describes her life as a woman in Frankfurt society, is one of her few surviving writings—only her brother's letters from their extensive correspondence have been preserved. Johann Wolfgang Goethe burned hers.

At the age of 23, she married the lawyer Johann Georg Schlosser. Her husband, who did not appreciate her as a writer or conversation partner, showed little understanding for her lack of interest in housework and her sensitivity. Shortly after the birth of her second daughter on June 8, 1777, she died in Emmerdingen at the age of 26. 
On the occasion of her 250th birthday, the Center for Women's Studies and Gender Research at Goethe University Frankfurt renamed itself the Cornelia Goethe Center for Women's Studies and Gender Research. Since 2023, it has been called the Cornelia Goethe Center for Gender Studies (CGC). The Cornelia Goethe Center's support group organizes the Cornelia Goethe Salon annually on Cornelia Goethe's birthday on December 7.
