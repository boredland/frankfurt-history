# The Cornelia Goethe Center for Gender Studies

*Theodor-W.-Adorno-Platz 6*

![The managing director of the Cornelia Goethe Center, Helma Lutz, and Angela Davis at the press conference for the inauguration of the Angela Davis visiting professorship.](../../images/23fb411c3e7652286ef6910220fa4fe3_original.jpg)
*2013 — Author: Anja Feix | License: Anja Feix | Description: The managing director of the Cornelia Goethe Center, Helma Lutz, and Angela Davis at the press conference for the inauguration of the Angela Davis visiting professorship.*

The Cornelia Goethe Center for Gender Studies (CGC) was founded in 1997 and offers students, doctoral candidates, research assistants, and professors a space for academic exchange. It organizes interdisciplinary and international events such as workshops, lectures, and conferences, coordinates two study programs in gender studies, and supports doctoral candidates and postdocs.

In the Cornelia Goethe Colloquiums, the center's lecture series, current topics in women's and gender studies are discussed each semester. The CGColloquiums are open to all interested persons.
To promote international cooperation, the Center regularly fills the Angela Davis Visiting Professorship for International Gender and Diversity Studies with an internationally renowned personality who deals with the topics of Angela Davis, i.e., racism, classism, and sexism, among others. The first holder of the visiting professorship was Angela Davis herself in 2013. The list of holders of the visiting professorship and recordings of their lectures can be found on the CGC website.
