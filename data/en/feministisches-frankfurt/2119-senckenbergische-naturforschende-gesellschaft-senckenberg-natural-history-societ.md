# Senckenbergische Naturforschende Gesellschaft  (Senckenberg Natural History Society)

*Elisabeth Johanna Friederike Schultz – At the heart of natural science*

Elisabeth Schultz – Am Puls der Naturwissenschaft

![Das Senckenbergische Krankenhospital vom Botanischen Garten aus](../../images/87fabf7f9a60fa19700a1b6b60391614_original.jpg)
*Author: Carl Freidrich Mylius | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Das Senckenbergische Krankenhospital vom Botanischen Garten aus*

The Senckenbergische Naturforschende Gesellschaft (Senckenberg Natural History Society) was founded in 1817 and was originally located near the Eschenheimer Tower. Natural history collections were exhibited and scientifically studied here. Elisabeth Johanna Friederike Schultz made a significant contribution to documenting the biodiversity of her time with her accurate botanical drawings. Her precise gouaches, supplemented by natural history labels, combine artistic accuracy with scientific rigour.

As a woman, she was long denied access to university research and scientific societies. It was not until she was 80 that she received recognition for her work: in 1897, the Senckenbergische Naturforschende Gesellschaft (Senckenberg Natural History Society) appointed her as its first female honorary member. Schultz bequeathed her drawings to the society with the request that they be displayed publicly after her death – a legacy that continues to this day.
