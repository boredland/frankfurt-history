# Gedenktafel Paulskirche (St. Paul’s Church Memorial Plaque)

*Johanna Kirchner – Memorial Site of Resistance*

Johanna Kirchner – Gedenkort des Widerstands

![Johanna Kirchner-Medaille von Peter Gingold](../../images/9a25dba0ac594a332355138e9106603a_original.jpg)
*Author: Wilkens | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Johanna Kirchner-Medaille von Peter Gingold*

The Paulskirche was built in the 18th century as the main Protestant church, was the location of the Frankfurter Nationalversammlung (Frankfurt National Assembly) in 1848, and became a memorial and democracy site after 1945. Today, a Gedenktafel (memorial plaque) commemorates Johanna Kirchner and her resistance against the Nazi regime. As an SPD politician and co-founder of the Frankfurter Arbeiterwohlfahrt - AWO (Frankfurt Workers' Welfare Association), she was particularly committed to women's rights and the welfare of children and young people. In 1944, she was executed by the Nazis in Berlin-Plötzensee for her resistance activities. The Johanna Kirchner Medal was an award presented by the city of Frankfurt from 1991 to 1995 to people who were still alive and had resisted National Socialism.
