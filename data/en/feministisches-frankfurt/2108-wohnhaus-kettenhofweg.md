Clothilde Koch-Gontard's last home was on Kettenhofweg, where she spent her final years with her unmarried daughter. The exact location is unknown. The drawing by Carl Theodor Reiffenstein (1820โ€“1893) shows Kettenhofweg around 1834.

Widowed in her old age, she lost her influence and financial security and had to give up her city palace in the center of town.
Clotilde Koch-Gontard took an early interest in the political developments surrounding the 1848 revolution. Since women officially had no political say, she opened her salon in the city palace on Hirschgraben to leading liberals so that she could participate in the discussions and contribute her perspective.
