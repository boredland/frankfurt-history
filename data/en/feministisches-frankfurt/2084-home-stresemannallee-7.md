# Home - Stresemannallee 7

*Ruth Cahn - Art studio and place of living*

![Blick in die Stresemannalle, Öl auf Leinwand](../../images/6b44e73489167142c2911cd20a5fb95c_original.jpg)
*Author: Emil Dielmann | License: HMF | Description: Blick in die Stresemannalle, Öl auf Leinwand*

The painter Ruth Cahn-Schwarzadler lived with her brother at Stresemannallee 7 before fleeing into exile in Chile in 1935 under threat from the Nazi regime. The painting by Emil Dielmann (1897–1954) shows Stresemannallee in 1934, shortly before Ruth Cahn-Schwarzadler's exile. Ruth Cahn-Schwarzadler was an expressionist artist born in Frankfurt into a Jewish banking family.

At a young age, she decided to pursue an independent life as an artist. She worked in Paris, among other places, where she was part of the avant-garde. Her works were exhibited internationally. Her exile in 1934 marked the end of her artistic career. She returned to Frankfurt in the 1950s, but her paintings had largely been forgotten.
