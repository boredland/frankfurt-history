# Aleida Montijn (1908-1989)

*Ballet Music and Jim Knopf*

Städtische Bühnen

![Porträt Aleida Montijn](../../images/0509ea05879e8ba056e2f5a2cd726ecc_original.jpg)
*Author: Unbekannt | License: Archiv Frauen und Musik | Description: Porträt Aleida Montijn*

Aleida Montijn was born in Mannheim in 1908. From 1927 to 1930, she studied at the Musikhochschule (University of Music) there and worked as a sought-after ballet répétiteur. She collaborated particularly closely with the dancer Mary Wigman. In 1936, Aleida Montijn joined the Reichsmusikkammer (Reich Music Chamber) and took part in the opening ceremony of the Olympic Games in Berlin. For her studies in conducting, which she completed in 1943, she received a special permit, since as a woman in the Nazi era she would not otherwise have been allowed to pursue this training.

In 1945, she fled from Dresden to Frankfurt and worked for the postwar cabaret Struwwelpeter. In 1948, she joined the Städtische Bühnen Frankfurt (Municipal Theatres of Frankfurt), where she later worked as in-house composer for stage music and as conductor. She also composed music for radio plays, including Jim Knopf und Lukas der Lokomotivführer (Jim Button and Luke the Engine Driver). She died in 1989 in Bad Homburg.

*Author: Jelena Rothermel und Valeria Flores (Archiv Frau und Musik)*
