# LUQS

*Lesbenarchiv und queere Sammlung (Lesbian Archive and Queer Collection) Frankfurt - LUQS*

![Event poster for the symposium organised by the lesbian forum ‘Hessisch Lesbisch’. Topic: exclusion among lesbians. The event provided a platform for critical discussion within the lesbian community.](../../images/62bf069a8ea604de63dc83ab8492314f_original.jpg)
*2001 — Author: Hessisch Lesbisch | License: LUQS | Description: Event poster for the symposium organised by the lesbian forum ‘Hessisch Lesbisch’. Topic: exclusion among lesbians. The event provided a platform for critical discussion within the lesbian community.*

Queer history is diverse—it encompasses different identities, lifestyles, and experiences that often overlap. However, this diversity is not always visible. Stories of white, cisgender people who identify as gay or lesbian often dominate. Other voices, such as those of racialized people, trans* or non-binary people, people with disabilities, or queer people who do not want to be categorized as lesbian or gay, receive less attention.

It is therefore important to us that our feminism is intersectional. Only in this way can we acknowledge these realities of life, their interconnections, and the struggles that arise from them. An intersectional practice means actively including voices that are otherwise overlooked and critically examining our own structures.
 
This is also reflected in our archive holdings, such as JAMIL, a Frankfurt-based group of Jewish Afro-German/Black migrant lesbians.

*Author: LUQS Archivcrew*
