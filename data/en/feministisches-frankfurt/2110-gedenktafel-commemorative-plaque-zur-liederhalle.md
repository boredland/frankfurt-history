# Gedenktafel (Commemorative plaque) ‘Zur Liederhalle’

*Rosa Luxemburg – Speech for peace*

Rosa Luxemburg - Rede für den Frieden

![Gedektafel für Rosa Luxemburg Bronze](../../images/c89afb2a666a862a353e4b5cb56e2458_original.png)
*Author: Unbekannt | License: Stadt Frankfurt/Udo Fedderies | Description: Gedektafel für Rosa Luxemburg Bronze*

Around 1900, a building complex was built at Basaltstraße 23. Today, it's home to TITANIA, a theater and cultural venue. Around 1900, the front building housed the restaurant “Zur Liederhalle,” and the adjoining back building had a big ballroom. It became one of Frankfurt's most important venues for political gatherings. On September 29, 1913, Rosa Luxemburg gave a speech there, in which she called for resistance to military service in view of the colonial and military policies of the Deutschen Kaiserreichs (German Empire). A year later, she was charged in Frankfurt and sentenced to one year in prison.
