# Goethehaus- Hirschgraben

*Katharina Elisabeth Goethe – Cultural and political meeting place*

![Großer Hirschgraben, das Goethehaus (Elternhaus), nach dem Umbau 1754, Aquarell auf Papier](../../images/1003f6d18084c20cb9ceb4a8c6e4f0d4_original.jpg)
*1858 — Author: Carl Theodor Reifenstein | License: HMF | Description: Großer Hirschgraben, das Goethehaus (Elternhaus), nach dem Umbau 1754, Aquarell auf Papier*

The house at Großer Hirschgraben 23–25 was the residence of the Goethe family. Johann Wolfgang Goethe was born here in 1749 and spent much of his childhood in this building. Today it is known as the Goethehaus and is open to visitors.

Katharina Elisabeth Goethe, his mother, lived here for many decades. She turned the large bourgeois house into a lively place of cultural exchange. She invited artists, writers, and intellectuals to so-called tea parties. As a self-confident woman of the 18th century, she created a space for herself to participate in the cultural life of the city and helped shape it. She also loved Frankfurt theater and maintained a broad social network throughout her life.
