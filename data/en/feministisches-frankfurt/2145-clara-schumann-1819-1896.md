# Clara Schumann (1819-1896)

*Child prodigy, composer, and piano teacher*

Myliusstraße 32

![Poträt Clara Schumann](../../images/7a4c589d072c9fef52329f6367501dce_original.jpg)
*Author: Franz Hanfsteangl | License: Arhiv Frauen und Musik/Gemeinfrei | Description: Poträt Clara Schumann*

Clara Schumann, née Wieck, was born in Leipzig in 1819. Her father prepared her at an early age for an international career as a pianist, and she undertook many concert tours across Europe. As early as 1831, she published her first composition, later followed by around 70 further works, mainly for piano. In 1840, she married the composer Robert Schumann, with whom she had eight children. The two influenced each other musically, but the expectations placed on her as housewife and mother severely restricted her artistic work.

After Robert Schumann’s death, she began working in 1878 as a professor at the newly founded Dr. Hoch’s Konservatorium (Dr. Hoch’s Conservatory) in Frankfurt. Together with her daughters Marie and Eugenie, she taught in her apartment at Myliusstraße 32, where she died in 1896.
