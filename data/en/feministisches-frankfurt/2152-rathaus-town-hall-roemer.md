# Rathaus (Town Hall) – Römer

*Meta Quarck-Hammerschlag – Municipal Politician*

Meta Quarck-Hammerschlag – Kommunalpolitikerin

![Magistratsmitglieder und Stadtverordnete, Gruppenbild im Römerhöfchen, Fotografie](../../images/df5e0264e903687ffdd637851f9ba235_original.jpg)
*Author: Friedrich Lauffer | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Magistratsmitglieder und Stadtverordnete, Gruppenbild im Römerhöfchen, Fotografie*

The Römer, Frankfurt’s town hall since the 15th century, has long been the center of the city’s politics. In 1919, Meta Quarck-Hammerschlag was elected as the first woman to serve on the Magistrat (municipal executive council) – one of only twelve women among 85 men holding political office in the Römer. With the beginning of the Nazi dictatorship in 1933, she was forced to resign from all political positions.

Throughout her life, Meta Quarck-Hammerschlag was committed to women’s education, women’s suffrage, and social justice. As a co-founder of the Frankfurter Arbeiterwohlfahrt, AWO (Frankfurt Workers’ Welfare Association), and as an active women’s rights advocate, she promoted reforms that particularly benefited disadvantaged women.
The photo shows the members of the Magistrat and city councillors in 1926 in the Römerhöfchen (small inner courtyard of the Römer). In the front row, wearing white – the color of the suffragettes – stands Meta Quarck-Hammerschlag.
