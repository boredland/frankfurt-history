# Städelsche Kunstinstitut  (Städel Art Institute)

*Mathilde Battenberg – Art education at the Städel*

![Das Städel'sche Kunstinstitut in der Neuen Mainzer Landstraße, Druckgrafik](../../images/779b4c064c019bbdae19d06bc06e0602_original.jpg)
*Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF/Gemeinfrei | Description: Das Städel'sche Kunstinstitut in der Neuen Mainzer Landstraße, Druckgrafik*

The Städelsche Kunstinstitut (Städel Art Institute), known today primarily as the Städel Museum with its attached art college, is located directly on the Main River. Johann Friedrich Städel, banker and philanthropist, laid the foundation for a public collection and education in his will in 1816. Mathilde Battenberg was one of the first students of the painter Ottilie W. Roederstein (1859-1937), who taught at the Städel.

From 1908 onwards, Mathilde Battenberg had her own studio at the art institute, where she gave painting lessons. She established herself as a sought-after portrait painter in a male-dominated art world and created an independent artistic existence for herself. Unfortunately, many of her works are now considered lost.
