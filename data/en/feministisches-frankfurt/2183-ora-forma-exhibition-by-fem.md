# ora forma Exhibition by +FEM

*Feminist curating*

*Author: Yoav Kedem | License: © Yoav Kedem | Description: Das Kunstwerk „Cherry Picking“ von Un-Zu Ha-Nul Lee, im Jahr 2022 ausgestellt bei der Ausstellung ora forma im Blumenladen Blumen Ora Ora unweit des Frankfurter Hauptbahnhofs.*

Gender roles are closely linked to our relationship with nature. Historically, nature was understood as the opposite of culture or civilization—something to be either admired or controlled. Similarly, masculinity came to be seen as rational, cultural, and controlling, while femininity and gender fluidity were associated with being natural, physical, and emotional. This classification supports a hierarchical gender relationship based on the assumption that nature—and thus anything marked as “different”—must be subjugated. This reinforces a social power i, but rather as an integral part of cultural meaning . The works on display negotiated the relationship between nature and culture not as a contradiction, but as a dynamic network of mutual influence.
The present text expands the perspective of the exhibition by reflecting theoretically on the connection between images of nature and gender orders.mbalance in which masculinity is considered the norm, while anything that deviates from it is devalued, controlled, or excluded.
The exhibition ora forma (2022) presented feminist artistic positions that understand nature not merely as a source of inspiration

*Author: Kristina Mukhacheva, Naomi Rado*
