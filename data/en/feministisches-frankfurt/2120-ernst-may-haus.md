# Ernst-May-Haus

*Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky – Socially conscious architectur*

Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky – Sozialpolitisches Bauen

![Innenansicht in einem Ernst May Haus, Frankfurter Küche (Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky)](../../images/eb9ddbcc25c1e2c570913bbc510fc3ef_original.jpg)
*Author: Calle Hesslefors | License: HMF | Description: Innenansicht in einem Ernst May Haus, Frankfurter Küche (Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky)*

In the late 1920s, the Römerstadt housing estate was built as part of the Neues Frankfurt (New Frankfurt) housing project led by Ernst May (1886–1970). The project was intended to alleviate the growing housing shortage in Frankfurt and create affordable, functional living space for broad sections of the population. The Ernst-May-Haus in Römerstadt, which is now open to the public, documents this urban development project and displays, among other things, a model of the so-called Frankfurter Küche (Frankfurt kitchen).

Architect Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky (1897-2000) designed this kitchen as a standardized, efficient workspace for the new housing architecture. It is considered the precursor to the modern fitted kitchen. Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky was one of the first female architects in the German-speaking world and was committed throughout her life to social housing and improving the living conditions of working women.
