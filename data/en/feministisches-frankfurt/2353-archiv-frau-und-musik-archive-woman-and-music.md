# Archiv Frau und Musik (Archive Woman and Music)

*Documentation and Research Center on Female Composers and Conductors*

![Reading room of the Archive Woman and Music](../../images/444df1c862bc318eab992a599861cdb5_original.jpg)
*2018 — Author: Andrea Späth | License: © Andrea Späth Fotodesign 2018 | Description: Reading room of the Archive Woman and Music*

Inspired by various feminist groups that were founded in the 1970s, numerous musicologists, musicians, and composers came together in 1979 to establish a working group for the promotion of women in music: Der Internationale Arbeitskreis Frau und Musik e.V. (The International Working Group for Women and Music) was born. In addition to establishing a music archive, the Archiv Frau und Musik (Archive Women and Music), the founders wanted to improve career opportunities for women in the music industry and reduce discrimination.

Today, this globally unique research institution is located in Frankfurt-Niederrad. Its holdings currently comprise around 31,000 media units by over 2,100 female composers, including sheet music, sound recordings, and secondary literature. The archive team advises musicians on program questions, gives lectures and workshops, and offers guided tours of the archive rooms.
