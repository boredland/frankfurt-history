# Paulskirche (St. Paul’s Church)

*Louise Mittermaier – Active at the Site of Revolution*

Louise Mittermaier – Aktiv am Revolutionsort

![Sitzung der Nationalversammlung in der Pauslkirche in Frankfurt](../../images/cb6d454a716dfb9c0c3c997cd321f022_original.jpg)
*Author: Eduard Gustav May (Drucker*in), S. Schember (Verleger*in), Jean Nicolas Ventadour (Zeichner*in) | License: HMF | Description: Sitzung der Nationalversammlung in der Pauslkirche in Frankfurt*

The Paulskirche (St. Paul’s Church), built in the 18th century as Frankfurt’s main Protestant church, was the venue of the Frankfurter Nationalversammlung (Frankfurt National Assembly) in 1848. Louise Mittermaier became familiar with political affairs early on through her father, Karl Joseph Anton Mittermaier (1787–1867), who was a delegate to the Nationalversammlung (National Assembly) for Baden-Baden. As a woman, however, active political participation was denied to her – she could only follow the debates from the spectators’ gallery. Between 1847 and 1857, she kept a friendship album in which many politically engaged visitors to the Mittermaier household left entries. This album is an important personal source from the revolutionary period – offering a female, observant perspective.

*Author: Sofia Schnitzler*
