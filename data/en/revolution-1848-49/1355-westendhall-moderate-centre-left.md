# Westendhall (moderate centre-left)

*Gallusanlage/Neckarstraße*

![Lithograph depicting the Hotel Westendhall](../../images/28752f92c19c4e93112285f490ee8aaa_original.jpg)
*ca. 1848 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Z Nr. 1848-76 | Description: Lithograph depicting the Hotel Westendhall*

In 1848, the Westendhall Hotel stood on the Taunusanlage between the Taunus and the Main-Weser railway stations. The Westendhall faction, which brought together delegates splitting from the Württemberger Hof and Deutschen Hof groups, started meeting in the hotel in August 1848. Their moderate left-leaning stance between the democratic left and the centre left earned them the nickname of the ‘Linke im Frack’ – ‘frock-coated left wingers’.

Heinrich Simon, a Prussian lawyer and civil servant, was regarded as the leader of the Westendhall faction. The group numbered around 40 parliamentarians. The best-known figures were the Frankfurt lawyer Friedrich Siegmund Jucho; Stuttgart banker and politician Gottlob Friedrich Federer; Württemberg lawyer Wilhelm Heinrich Murschel; Cologne journalist Franz Raveaux; Darmstadt civil servant Theodor Reh; Westphalian lawyer and writer Jodocus Temme; Darmstadt officer and radical democratic journalist Friedrich Wilhelm Schulz; and Tübingen literary scholar and philosopher Friedrich Theodor Vischer.

The Westendhall faction adopted the Württemberger Hof’s rather nebulous political programme. However, instead of supporting a republican model of government, they advocated a democratic constitutional monarchy as a pragmatic solution. To create majorities on policy in the National Assembly, they voted with the Casino faction and the supporters of a hereditary emperor. In their own goals, they advocated popular sovereignty with direct, equal and universal suffrage. They supported an imperial constitution with liberal democratic values, but wanted this implemented without having to seek the agreement of each individual state or principality.

The faction disintegrated in late November 1848 when only some of the group joined the Central March Association, even though Westendhall members had supported the idea of this combined left-wing organisation. In 1849, the rump ‘New Westendhall’ faction under Theodor Reh joined the Weidenbusch group and the coalition in favour of a hereditary emperor.

The Westendhall Hotel along with the three railway stations then in the western city were all demolished in the 1880s to make way for the new main station, opened in 1888, and the surrounding quarter along Kaiserstrasse.
