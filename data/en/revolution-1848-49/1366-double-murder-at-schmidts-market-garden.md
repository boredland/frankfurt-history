# Double murder at Schmidt’s market garden

*Merianstraße 23*

![Colored lithograph: Surrounded by insurgents, Lichnowsky faces death in front of the house of the art gardener Schmidt. The lifeless Auerswald is dragged into a ditch.](../../images/907bcb6dfd6951349f757c0591a8f919_original.jpg)
*1848 — Author: Wilhelm Völker | License: HMF C 10164b | Description: Colored lithograph: Surrounded by insurgents, Lichnowsky faces death in front of the house of the art gardener Schmidt. The lifeless Auerswald is dragged into a ditch.*

A terrible scene took place in front of the Friedberg Gate on the afternoon of 18 September 1848. General Major Hans von Auerswald and Prince Felix von Lichnowsky, both liberal right-wing parliamentarians and supporters of the Truce of Malmö, were out together on a reconnaissance ride when they were recognised. An armed mob from Bockenheim and Ginnheim hurried to catch up with them and attacked them.

After being pursued through the gardens outside the city as far as the Bornheim moor, the two men were cornered at the house of the horticulturist Schmidt. The angry mob brutally murdered Auerswald and left Lichnowsky so badly injured that he died of his wounds that same night in the Hospital of the Holy Spirit. At that time, the murder of these two parliamentarians made a profound impression on the public and came to symbolise the terror of the radicals.
