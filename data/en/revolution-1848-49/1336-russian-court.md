# Russian Court

*Zeil 106*

![Lithograph of the hotel "Russischer Hof"](../../images/e00cca682938a02b753bfa07360337a7_original.jpg)
*ca. 1850 — Author: J. H. Martens | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S13 Nr. 283 | Description: Lithograph of the hotel "Russischer Hof"*

In 1848, the site of today’s MyZeil shopping centre was home to the Russischer Hof, one of Frankfurt’s most luxurious hotels at that time. Opened in 1827, the hotel was frequented by kings and princes, and Europe’s powerful and wealthy. In 1848, the imperial regent, Archduke John of Austria also initially stayed in the Russischer Hof with his wife. Afterwards, they resided in the Borgnis town house in Grosse Eschenheimer Gasse 74, later home to the citizens’ association.

In 1848, another guest at the Russischer Hof hotel was the parliamentarian Eduard Martin Simson, who took over as the National Assembly President on 18 December that year. Prince Wilhelm of Prussia, later Kaiser Wilhelm I, also stayed at the hotel. As Supreme Commander of the Prussian army, he had rooms there at the time of the July revolution in Baden. One night when Wilhelm returned from a hunting trip, his fur coat was soaked through. To dry the coat, the Prince’s valet hung it up by the open fire, but left it a little too close. The coat caught fire, and the flames soon spread to the rooms. No doubt world history would have turned out differently if the valet had not saved the future Emperor of Germany from death by asphyxiation. In 1891, this luxury hotel was demolished and so vanished from Frankfurt’s cityscape.
