# Mainlust (‘greater German solution’)

*Untermainkai 29/30*

![Photo of the garden restaurant "Mainlust"](../../images/dd70d2a996fab2d300c3b3a12c5357a4_original.jpg)
*ca. 1860 — Author: Carl Friedrich Mylius | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-507 | Description: Photo of the garden restaurant "Mainlust"*

In 1849, the Mainlust tavern became the meeting point for a cross-faction group united by their support of a greater German solution including Austria. These parliamentarians, from the left to the right of the political spectrum, rejected the lesser German solution with a hereditary Prussian emperor favoured by the Weidenbusch faction. As a counter-proposal, they drafted a constitution strongly based on a federation of states. To accommodate the conservatives, this envisaged a weak centralised power but, to meet demands from the left, included assurances on fundamental civil rights and liberties.
