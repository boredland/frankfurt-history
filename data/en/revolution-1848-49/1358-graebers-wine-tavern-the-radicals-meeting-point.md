# Gräber’s Wine Tavern – The radicals’ meeting point

*Holzgraben 7*

![The central location of the Gräber'sche Weinwirtschaft in the Frankfurt city map](../../images/6b5b93a8d115f0d9a4071e7f59a89537_original.jpg)
*1852 — Author: Eduard Foltz-Eberle | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S8-7 Nr. 8 | Description: The central location of the Gräber'sche Weinwirtschaft in the Frankfurt city map*

On 16 September 1848, after the National Assembly narrowly voted to accept the Truce of Malmö, the non-parliamentary opposition of radical democrats and socialists met in Georg Friedrich Gräber’s wine tavern. Well into the night, they discussed how to proceed. As the regular meeting place of the Workers’ Association and the Democratic-Republican Society, the wine tavern was also the haunt of radical left-wing supporters, including many journeymen, who aspired to realising the sovereignty of the people and felt let down by the National Assembly.

The heated atmosphere in the wine tavern culminated in a call issued to the left-wing delegates to leave the National Assembly and, following the French model during the Revolution, set up a National Convention. To give greater weight to this demand, a large public meeting was announced for Sunday, 17 September 1848 on Frankfurt’s Pfingstweide, a large space. That same night, messengers left Gräber’s wine tavern heading for the surrounding villages and towns to mobilise supporters to take part in the major demonstration planned for the following day.
