# Central March Association (combined left-wing in the Wolfseck)

*An der Hauptwache 11*

![Lithograph depicting the "Haus zum Wolfseck" building](../../images/1c5f01ea601ea71aec10d0a4f3025b59_original.jpg)
*1890 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2000-315 | Description: Lithograph depicting the "Haus zum Wolfseck" building*

Founded in November 1848, the Central March Association (Centralmärzverein) was a pioneering attempt to create a consolidated supra-regional organisation unifying all parliamentary delegates and democratic associations under one umbrella. This union of the left and left-centre Deutscher Hof, Donnersberg and Westendhall factions set out to create a popular non-violent democratic movement outside the National Assembly to secure the March revolution’s achievements and realise, through parliamentary means, its political goals in the individual sovereign states.

In Frankfurt on 6 May 1849, during the Imperial Constitution campaign, it called for defence associations to be founded, members to view themselves as soldiers of the constitution, and to defend it against those ‘rulers in breach of the constitution’. In May 1849, inspired by these republican ideas, there were uprisings in Baden and Saxony, the Rhineland and the Bavarian Palatinate. The capitulation of the Baden revolutionaries in Rastatt on 23 July 1849 ushered in the end of the Imperial Constitution campaign and the Central March Association itself.
