# St. Catherine's Church

*An der Hauptwache 1*

![Illustration of St. Catherine's Church](../../images/1e755addab5476f0cd9566ce52facbaa_original.jpg)
*ca. 1855 — Author: Johann Jakob Tanner | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S13 Nr. 911 | Description: Illustration of St. Catherine's Church*

Political assemblies were not only held in St Paul’s Church, but also in the Church of the Three Kings, the German Reformed Church and St Catherine’s Church. On 24 April 1848, the prayer meeting and divine service at St Catherine’s Church were cancelled. Instead, in an intense atmosphere of campaigning, the candidates competing for a seat in the National Assembly as Frankfurt’s delegate presented their political programmes. The next day at the church, a petition was presented to the Committee of Fifty, which had the task of organising the National Assembly. On 3 May, there followed deliberations on submitting a request to the Senate to allow a constitutional amendment.

Not all the clergy and church representatives were in favour of using religious locations as venues for political discussions. The Protestant parish council took exception to churches being used for political meetings, especially when this had not been formally requested. As a result, the parish council refused to grant permission for some meetings, including the request for a workers’ meeting in St Catherine’s Church – an indication of the reservations among regional church councils towards craft workers and journeymen.
