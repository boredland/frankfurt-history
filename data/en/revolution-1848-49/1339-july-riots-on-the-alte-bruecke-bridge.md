# July riots on the Alte Brücke bridge

*Alte Brücke*

![Photo of the stone Old Bridge](../../images/5b26b6168ee65958f299b21c131f682a_original.jpg)
*ca. 1860 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 738 | Description: Photo of the stone Old Bridge*

In 1848, poor quality bread and undersized rolls could also cause riots – as was the case in July in Sachsenhausen. For the quality of his bread, master baker Jost was treated to a cacophonous mock serenade and had some of his windowpanes broken. When the police then made a series of arbitrary arrests, the situation escalated with Sachsenhausen locals freeing those detained and a gun battle breaking out in Brückenstrasse between 120 line infantry and local residents supported by a division of civilian militia.

When one of the soldiers was killed, the infantry fell back, and the locals set up barricades on the bridge. Thanks to some skilful negotiations, the Senate managed to end the conflict peacefully on 8 July and the barricades were cleared away.
