# Location for committee meetings

*Buchgasse 3*

![Photo of a restaurant in Buchgasse where committee meetings were held](../../images/c227235f39126bbfa4124beeec83135f_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-2609 | Description: Photo of a restaurant in Buchgasse where committee meetings were held*

Numerous parliamentary meetings took place outside St Paul’s Church – including in 1848, for instance, in rooms at Buchgasse 1, opposite the Romanesque-Gothic St Leonhard’s Church. Since St Paul’s Church lacked the space for discussions and consultations, delegates met outside on the square, joined their parliamentary factions in hotels, restaurants and taverns, or took part in committee meetings in rooms rented from these hostelries. The hotels, restaurants and taverns renting rooms to committees were located at Paulsgasse 1 and 5, Neue Kräme 7, Große Sandgasse 2 and Buchgasse 1 and 16. The National Assembly’s chancery was in the Haus zum Korb (Sarasin) at Große Kornmarkt 12.

The National Assembly created 30 committees dedicated to, for example, rules of procedure, elections, the imperial constitution, trade, petitions, the navy, arming the people, army affairs, international issues, ministerial responsibilities, church and school affairs, and finances and receptions.

The delegates had very long days. Except on Sundays and public holidays, they usually met every day for a four-hour session in the morning, attended committee meetings in rented rooms in the afternoon and then moved on to their join their parliamentary group in the evenings.
