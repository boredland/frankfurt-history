# Ceasefire announcement at the Bornheim Gate

*Fahrgasse 111*

![Colored lithograph: Representative Rösler announces a truce on the barricade at the Bornheim Gate.](../../images/2618c24c7a83b811f3dd8b166110dfae_original.jpg)
*1948 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF C 10164c | Description: Colored lithograph: Representative Rösler announces a truce on the barricade at the Bornheim Gate.*

A battalion of the 115th Infantry Regiment (1st Grand Ducal Hessian) advanced from the river through the Fahrgasse and, in a domino effect, took one of the poorly defended roadblocks after another. Since a ceasefire had been announced to put an end to the bloodshed, the battalion came to halt in front of the last barricade between the upper Fahrgasse and the junction of the Bornheim Gate and the Zeil (today: Konstablerwache/corner of Reineckstrasse).

In the afternoon of 18 September 1848, the left-wing delegates in the National Assembly were able to agree with war minister Eduard von Peucker on a ceasefire starting at 5 pm and lasting one hour. Some delegates carrying white flags risked their lives to reach the barricades where the fighting was most intense. Gustav Adolf Rösler, a left-wing parliamentarian, showed great courage in being the first to climb onto the Bornheim Gate barricade in a hail of bullets to announce the ceasefire.

But the peace initiative failed as the soldiers did not retreat from the barricades and the revolutionaries had not been offered an amnesty. Archduke John, the imperial regent, made a final attempt at mediation, but his words were drowned in the thunder of the cannons from the Zeil.
