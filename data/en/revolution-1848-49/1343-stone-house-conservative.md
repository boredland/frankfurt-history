# Stone House (Conservative)

*Markt 44*

![<p>Foto: Carl Friedrich Mylius, 1864, Historisches Museum Frankfurt Ph00021</p>](../../images/2f16efa6caf90762c0098521c5db67c5_original.jpg)
*1864 — Author: Carl Friedrich Mylius | License: HMF | Description: <p>Foto: Carl Friedrich Mylius, 1864, Historisches Museum Frankfurt Ph00021</p>*

Today, this building is home to Frankfurt’s art association, but on 4 June 1848 conservative delegates from the National Assembly founded their parliamentary group here. In May, conservative delegates from Austria had already met in the rooms of the Freemason's Socrates Lodge in Töngesgasse 16.

At the founding meeting of this conservative faction, Prussian Catholic diplomat and politician Joseph von Radowitz presented a draft programme which, at heart, argued for a light-touch approach to reforming the principle of the monarchy governing the state. In late September, the conservatives moved from the Stone House into the Café Milani where they further developed their political ideas.

In the past, the Stone House was a patrician family’s home and accommodation for Frankfurt’s trade fair. Its windows, with their excellent view of the market square, were extremely popular during the ceremonies when kings and emperors were crowned. From the 18th century, the rooms of the Stone House were rented out to tenants. Badly damaged in the bombing raids in 1944, the building was reconstructed from 1959 to 1962, the only patrician house rebuilt at that time apart from Goethe’s House. However, the interior was redesigned and only the façade reconstructed in its original form.
