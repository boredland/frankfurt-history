# Pariser Hof (liberal centre-right)

*An der Hauptwache 7*

![Photo of Schillerplatz with the ‘Pariser Hof’ in the background](../../images/432fb7708bf70c6cc5fe93002d4c75f7_original.jpg)
*1879 — Author: Carl Friedrich Mylius | License: HMF C27048 | Description: Photo of Schillerplatz with the ‘Pariser Hof’ in the background*

On 21 December 1848, the Casino faction split over the question of a lesser or greater Germany with supporters of the latter forming the Pariser Hof group. The Pariser Hof faction followed a robust programme of liberal-conservative policies advocating, for instance, a federal approach without a strong centralised power, and sovereign states allowed to reach individual agreements when implementing the imperial constitution.

This group included, among others, the Hesse legal scholar Carl Theodor Welcker; Koblenz lawyer August Reichensperger, who later helped to promote and finance Cologne Cathedral; Hamburg lawyer Johann Gustav Heckscher, a member of the Wartburg Festival convention in 1817; and Austrian politician Victor Franz von Andrian-Werburg.

From 1709 to 1898, the Pariser Hof Hotel was on the then Schillerplatz square (today An der Hauptwache). Although officially called the Hotel de Paris, it was colloquially known as the Pariser Hof. In 1898, the hotel was replaced by a commercial building with a café. The Allianz Haus, constructed in 1976, now stands where the Pariser Hof faction once met.
