# Landsberg (moderate centre-liberal)

*Ziegelgasse*

![Photo of the old town alleyway Landberggasse](../../images/c7d2462aec894bdcbd091fe00b5c6d85_original.jpg)
*1915 — Author: Carl Andreas Abt | License: HMF C23212 | Description: Photo of the old town alleyway Landberggasse*

The Landsberg group of moderate liberals comprised former members of the Casino and the Württemberger Hof groups. The Landsberg splinter group, formed in September 1848, positioned itself between the left and right centre. Initially, the group met in the Mainlust hostelry. For its critics, the Landsberg group with around 40 members were a ‘party of mediocrities’.

The faction attracted many right-leaning liberals from Prussia and south-west Germany, professors from northern Germany, and middle-class delegates from the Rhineland. These included the Erlangen Protestant theologian Johann Friedrich Christoph Bauer; Hannover lawyer Carl Otto Dammers; East Prussian writer Wilhelm Jordan; Marburg constitutional law expert Sylvester Jordan; Hannover administrative lawyer Heinrich von Quintus-Icilius; and Oldenburg lawyer Maximilian Heinrich Rüder.

In terms of it programme, the Landsberg group saw a robust parliament and a constitutional state as ensuring a balance between a strong centralised power and democratic parity. To achieve this aim, the group advocated a federal state and limiting the rights of the individual states. They supported a constitutional monarchy and called for broad – though not equal – suffrage.
