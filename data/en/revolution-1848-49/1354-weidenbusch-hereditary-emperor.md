# Weidenbusch (hereditary emperor)

*Goetheplatz 6*

![Lithograph depitcting the restaurant and hotel "zum Weidenbusch"](../../images/3cc80d84eb3271f4f1ae027910bade75_original.jpg)
*ca. 1840 — Author: J. H. Martens | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S13 Nr. 271 | Description: Lithograph depitcting the restaurant and hotel "zum Weidenbusch"*

In early 1849, the question of a lesser or greater German solution outweighed any of the political positions – from democratic to liberal and conservative. From then on, the Mainlust hostelry became the meeting point for around 160 proponents of a greater Germany as a confederation including Austria. The Weidenbusch Hotel hosted the meetings of about 220 delegates who favoured the lesser German solution with a hereditary Prussian emperor. A group of parliamentarians with views somewhere in between met in the Braunfels.

Formed in mid-February 1849, the Weidenbusch group comprised parliamentarians from the Casino, Landsberg and Augsburger Hof factions. Later, they were joined by members from the Württemberger Hof and the Westendhall groups. In the hereditary emperor faction, there were also considerable differences over the question of suffrage and the emperor's right of veto. Ultimately in March 1849, Heinrich von Gagern successfully put together a compromise with Heinrich Simon from the Braunfels group to gain a National Assembly majority for the policy of a hereditary imperial sovereign.
