# Fighting at the Töngesgasse barricade

*Töngesgasse 19*

![Colored lithograph: Rush of the Austrian infantry on the fiercely defended barricade in the Töngesgasse.](../../images/46762bf0ff92f1954a25e9d0bd9faf5d_original.jpg)
*1848 — Author: Eduard Gustav May | License: HMF C 8429 | Description: Colored lithograph: Rush of the Austrian infantry on the fiercely defended barricade in the Töngesgasse.*

The barricade at one end of Töngesgasse was nearly two storeys high, and the assault launched on it from the Liebfrauenberg failed dismally. Even while still at some distance, the advancing Austrian troops were met with shouts of ‘Long live Hecker, long live the Republic!’ from a member of the gymnast movement standing on the barricade and waving a red flag.

When the fighters on the barricade set up near the Michel & Kestner leather store were ordered to clear their position, they responded with a volley of gunfire killing one soldier and leaving two others badly injured. In the subsequent exchange of fire, ten other soldiers and two of the revolutionaries were wounded. Under fire from snipers on all sides, the two companies of soldiers had no other choice but to make an orderly retreat.

In the narrow alleyways, the smoke from the guns obscured the view. The Austrian infantry first managed to take the barricade in Töngesgasse by storm during the night of 18 September 1848.
