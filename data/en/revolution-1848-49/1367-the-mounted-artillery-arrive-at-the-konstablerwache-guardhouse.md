# The mounted artillery arrive at the Konstablerwache Guardhouse

*Zeil 65*

![Chalk lithograph: The mounted artillery advances along the Zeil. In the center of the picture the Konstablerwache with clock tower, to the left a section of the barricade in Allerheiligengasse.](../../images/fdedb18b339dc1df2ba6ecb8ac462836_original.jpg)
*1848 — Author: Jean Nicolas Ventadour | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. W 1-9 ZGS Nr. 36-245 | Description: Chalk lithograph: The mounted artillery advances along the Zeil. In the center of the picture the Konstablerwache with clock tower, to the left a section of the barricade in Allerheiligengasse.*

When a mounted artillery unit from Darmstadt arrived in the late afternoon of 18 September 1848, the fate of those fighting on the barricades was sealed. Immediately after the artillery arrived, two canons were set up on the Zeil close to the Konstablerwache guardhouse. After the ceasefire expired, the canons opened fire on the large barricade at the entrance to Allerheiligengasse. Only eight canon shots were needed to make the barricade collapse and put its defenders to flight. The Prussian and Hesse troops stormed the barricade, advancing further into Allerheiligengasse.

The Frankfurter Oberpostamts Zeitung reported in the edition of its newspaper dated 19 September 1848: ‘After 10 o’clock in the evening, the fighting was over; the barricades were all cleared and, in the course of the night, occupied by the soldiers.’

A wave of arrests swept through the city. By the evening of 19 September, the cells in Konstablerwache guardhouse, which served as a police station and prison, were already so overcrowded that 56 arrested revolutionaries had to be taken to Mainz on the Taunus railway. In total, charges were pressed against 216 ‘local’ people and 226 ‘non-residents’.
