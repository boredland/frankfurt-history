# Public meeting on the Pfingstweide

*Pfingstweidstraße 10*

![Colored wood engraving: Workers and gymnasts with their club flags crowd around the speaker's platform on the Pfingstweide.](../../images/121daac38032e015f823c96a645e39e7_original.jpg)
*1848 — Author: P. C. Geissler | License: HMF C 10169 | Description: Colored wood engraving: Workers and gymnasts with their club flags crowd around the speaker's platform on the Pfingstweide.*

On the afternoon of 17 September 1848, over 10,000 demonstrators are said to have streamed onto the spreading Pfingstweide. The speakers included Ludwig Simon von Trier, Friedrich Wilhelm Schlöffel, Hugo Maximilian Wesendonck and Franz Heinrich Zitz, four National Assembly delegates who belonged to the radical left-wing Donnersberg faction. The crowd’s anger was further inflamed by hecklers crying out slogans such as ‘Blow up parliament!’ and ‘Cut the right-wingers’ throats!’ as they swung the cudgels they carried with them.

The gathering charged one delegate with the task of handing in a petition at the next parliamentary session. The petition demanded that the 258 parliamentarians who voted for the Truce of Malmö should be declared ‘traitors to the German people, German freedom and honour’. Since the majority of left-wing delegates refused to secede from parliament, the leaders of the protest called for an armed assembly of the people on Rossmarkt square the following day.

The Pfingstweide demonstration ended by urging those protesters from places outside Frankfurt to stay overnight. As the crowd came back into the city, they sang the ‘Heckerlied’, a revolutionary battle song by Friedrich Hecker. There were also reports of individual cases of pistols fired into the air.
