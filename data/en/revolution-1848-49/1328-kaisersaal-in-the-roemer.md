# Kaisersaal in the Römer

*Römerberg 21*

![Lithograph: published by Heinrich Keller](../../images/193ddac82b5f1c5d9e8dc99f9f85eb68_original.jpg)
*ca. 1880 — Author: verlegt von Heinrich Keller | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 322 | Description: Lithograph: published by Heinrich Keller*

The Imperial Hall saw the opening ceremonies of the preliminary parliament – the Vorparlament – and the subsequent elected National Assembly. On 31 March 1848, the 574 deputies of the preliminary parliament met here at 8:30 in the morning for an address of welcome. Afterwards, they elected Carl Mittermaier, a Heidelberg lawyer and professor of law, as the preliminary parliament’s President – the equivalent of speaker of the house. Frankfurt doctor Georg Varrentrapp was elected as one of eight secretaries and Robert Blum as one of the four vice-presidents. One hour later, the deputies ceremonially walked in procession to St Paul’s Church, accompanied by the cheers of the gathered crowds, church bells ringing and the thunder of canons.

This procedure was repeated for the National Assembly’s opening session on 18 May. On that day, after a welcome in the Römer’s Imperial Hall, Friedrich Lang from Werden was elected as President by Seniority, while the eight elected Secretaries also included Frankfurt-born Friedrich Siegmund Jucho. Afterwards, the 384 deputies present set off in a procession to St Paul’s Church accompanied by cheering crowds, bells ringing, and the sound of canons. Frankfurt’s streets echoed with hurrahs and shouts of acclaim, and black-red-and-gold republican flags hung from the windows of houses. The National Assembly in Frankfurt had begun.
