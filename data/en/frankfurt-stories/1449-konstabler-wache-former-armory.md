# Konstabler Wache, former armory

*Zeil / Fahrgasse*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/d63a976070a3a5aa1b62f4277086e56b_original.png)

From the mid-16th century, the Hauptzeughaus – the main arsenal, which served as the city’s weapons store – stood at the corner of the Zeil and the Fahrgasse. The arsenal had a round tower on its eastern side. Reiffenstein believed this was used to store gunpowder.

In the second half of the 17th century, the Konstablerwache guard house was built to the north of the arsenal. In 1753, the guard house was replaced by a new building. However, since this hampered the flow of traffic, it was demolished less than 70 years later. Afterwards, shops moved into the ground floor of the arsenal, which continued to be known as the Konstablerwache. Until 1886 when it was demolished, the Konstablerwache and arsenal served as a military guard house and police station as well as a jail. Today, only the name of this busy intersection is a reminder of the old guard house that once stood here.
