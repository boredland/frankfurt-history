# Mausgasse

*Domstraße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/f463f3df09cb8c50b9dcd158d1ee6e93_original.png)

Today’s Domstrasse was once the site of the Mausgasse, one of the Old Town’s typical narrow lanes and alleyways. Reiffenstein sketched a romantic picture of the alleyway with a view of the Cathedral in the background. While he paints the evening light reflected in puddles among the cobbles, his written description of the alleyway emphasises its unpleasant smells. He also depicts the adventurous system of drainpipes running along and down the façades.

In 1898, Mausgasse was widened to form a long road axis from the Hasengasse to the Cathedral. Although widening the alleyway involved tearing down the houses to the east, those to the west were initially left standing. They were not even demolished in 1904 when work began on Braubachstrasse, which crosses Domstrasse at right angles. The houses were only finally pulled down in 1914.
