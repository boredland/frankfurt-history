# Philipp Holzmann & Cie.

*Obermainstraße 51*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

Founded in 1856, the construction company Philipp Holzmann & Cie. expanded its activities to other European countries from the 1870s and to other continents from the 1890s. As part of international consortia, Holzmann played a leading role in the construction of the railway between Constantinople and Konya from 1888 to 1896 and, from 1903, in the further construction through areas of present-day Syria and Iraq to Baghdad. German colonial strategists saw the mega infrastructure project known as the ‘Baghdad Railway’ as an important building block for achieving the ‘global standing’ the empire was striving for. Within the German colonial empire, Holzmann was particularly active in German East Africa, where it built the ‘Kaiserhof’ hotel in Tanga, among other things. From 1905 to 1914, the company also built an approximately 1,200-kilometre-long railway line from Ha-fen Dar es Salaam to Lake Tanganyika. Around 20,000 Africans - often re-recruited through kidnapping and false promises - had to work under miserable conditions. On the construction sites, beatings, sometimes with and sometimes without whips, were the order of the day. Some labourers were beaten to death by the supervisors.
