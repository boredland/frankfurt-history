# Jacob S. H. Stern

*Große Gallusstraße 18*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

Founded in 1819, the private bank Jacob S. H. Stern also conducted business in colonial scenarios from the 1880s onwards. Among other things, it took a stake in the Schantung Railway Company, which had railway lines built from the German leased territory of Kiautschou to the Chinese interior. In July 1900, Theodor Stern, one of the bank's partners, supported a fundraising campaign for the German expeditionary force that had been sent to China to put down the anti-colonial Yihetuan rebellion (‘Boxer Rebellion’). The-odor Stern was also one of the founders of the German Colonial Society for South West Africa, which utilised land and mining rights in German South West Africa. From 1908, the bank was involved in the realisation of diamonds from the colony. 
Another partner, Paul Stern, was a member of the supervisory board of the Deutsch-Ostafrikanische Gesellschaft, which operated plantations in German East Africa.
