# Domkirche

*Domplatz 1*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/dc6addc0d33681b4981698288e5bd842_original.png)

As early as 680, this was the site of a church. In 1260, the church was then enlarged into its present form. It became known as the “Kaiserdom” – the Imperial Cathedral. From 1356, the kings and emperors of the Holy Roman Empire were elected in Frankfurt, and from 1562 were also crowned in the Cathedral.

The west tower, 96 metres high, was designed by Madern Gerthener (c. 1365-1430). Although building work started in 1415, the tower was only finished in 1880 – during Reiffenstein’s lifetime. Today, Gerthener’s design is on show in the Historical Museum

The Cathedral was once surrounded by houses, booths and stalls. After a fire in 1867 theses were all torn down, with the last demolished in 1901. With this area cleared, the Cathedral could display the monumental appearance it has today.

The Cathedral was badly damaged in the Second World War. Reconstruction work began in 1948. Today, the Cathedral treasure is kept in the Dommuseum in the cloisters.
