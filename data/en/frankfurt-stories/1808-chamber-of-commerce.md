# Chamber of Commerce

*Börsenplatz 4*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

In 1882, Gustav von Neufville, the long-standing president of the Frankfurt Chamber of Commerce, worked alongside Lord Mayor Miquel to found the German Colonial Association. A year and a half later, the Chamber supported a petition from merchants calling on the government to protect German trade interests in Africa more actively - an indirect appeal for the establishment of German colonies. In addition, the Chamber (like the City of Frankfurt itself) joined the Colonial Economic Committee founded in 1896, which financed expeditions to explore raw material deposits and agricultural potential in the colonies. In 1905, the Chamber took part in the German Colonial Congress in Berlin; in 1907, it organised a lecture with the then Secretary of State for Colonial Affairs Bernhard Dernburg. The colonial policy initiatives of the Chamber of Commerce document the hope for lucrative sources of raw materials and sales markets, which many Frankfurt companies associated with the German colonial empire.
