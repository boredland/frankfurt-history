# South West African Mining Syndicate

*Bockenheimer Anlage 45*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![](../../images/dd92127398a6f6df073a27a4f5428adf_original.jpg)

In 1907, 32 German industrial companies and banks founded the South West African Mining Syndicate to pool capital for the extraction of minerals in German South West Africa. The Metallurgische Gesellschaft, a sub-company of the Frankfurt-based industrial group Metallgesellschaft, took over the management. A total of eight companies from Frankfurt belonged to the syndicate. After sensational diamond discoveries in the Namib sand desert, the Imperial Colonial Office drafted a new production and utilisation architecture in 1909, which included a merger of the syndicate with the German Colonial Company for Southwest Africa. This cartel organised the mining of rough diamonds under the name Deutsche-Diamanten-Gesellschaft. Ovambo migrant labourers performed the hardest physical work in the mining areas, many of whom died under the extreme conditions.
