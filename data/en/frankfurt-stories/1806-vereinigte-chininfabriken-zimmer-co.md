# Vereinigte Chininfabriken Zimmer & Co.

*Darmstädter Landstraße 46*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![View of the factory from Zimmer](../../images/d196f51598c43e49c60567e8e2e6b2c3_original.jpg)
*License: HMF.C41852. | Description: View of the factory from Zimmer*

In 1876, the pharmaceutical company C. Zimmer acquired the ‘Argasarie’ plantation on Java - then part of the colony of the Dutch Indies - in order to obtain cinchona bark for the production of malaria medicines. In 1887, the company merged with Fridr. Jobst from Stuttgart to form Vereinigte Chininfabriken Zimmer & Co. Around 1900, Zimmer employed around 200 people at its two sites in Frankfurt and Stuttgart and exported ‘to all countries of the world’, according to a contemporary exhibition catalogue. From 1908 at the latest, Zimmer also imported cinchona bark from an experimental plantation that had been established in German East Africa around the ‘Amani’ organic agricultural institute.
