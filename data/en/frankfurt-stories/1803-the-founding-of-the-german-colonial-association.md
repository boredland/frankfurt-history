# The founding of the German Colonial Association

*Corner of Junghofstrasse/Neue Schlesinger Gasse*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![The large hall of the Saalbau, 1908. The founding meeting of the German Colonial Association took place in the small hall.](../../images/ed246ebe27de45747801666ffa67e41c_original.jpg)
*1908 — License: HMF.C25741. | Description: The large hall of the Saalbau, 1908. The founding meeting of the German Colonial Association took place in the small hall.*

On 6 December 1882, around 200 interested people from various parts of Germany gathered in the Saalbau in Junghofstraße, the only one in Frankfurt at the time, to found the German Colonial Association. The carefully prepared event was intended to create a powerful organisation to inspire the public, trade and industry as well as the members of the Reichstag for German colonial expansion. As some existing colonial associations joined the organisation, the number of members rose rapidly to 12,500 in 1886. The choice of Frankfurt as the founding location and first headquarters of the association can be explained firstly by the consideration that the financial centre offered favourable conditions for attracting wealthy supporters. Secondly, the Lord Mayor at the time, Johannes von Miquel, took on a leading role in the association. Not least, he hoped that a German colonial empire would have a social integration effect to the advantage of the bourgeois parties, including the National Liberal Party, which he had co-founded.
