# Stalburger Oede

*Glauburgstraße 80*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/42e8fc2b585e7cdc6124fdf63e37792c_original.png)

The Stalburger Oede was a green area in Frankfurt’s immediate surroundings. From the late 1400s, the land was a manor belonging to the Stalburg family. An avenue of tall trees, known as the Seufzerallee (literally, “avenue of sighs”), led from the town to the manor. Today, this is part of Oederweg, one of the city’s main arterial roads. The manor had a residence surrounded by a water-filled moat. The Oede was one of Reiffenstein’s favourite places – even as a child, he drew the well there and sought to capture the lyrical atmosphere.

In 1873, during a period of 19th-century urban expansion, the area was developed as residential housing. Although the main residence was converted, the Stalburg family arms over the door was preserved. In 1879 Reiffenstein reported that an alehouse called “Zur Stalburg” had opened on the premises. Even in 2023, the former residence was home to a theatre and an apple wine tavern.

*Author: Carina Koch*
