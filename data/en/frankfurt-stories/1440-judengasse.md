# Judengasse

*Museum Judengasse*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Judengasse, back houses, 1849, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0271](../../images/55c3b559e80510adb445487fa9423fdb_original.jpg)
*1849 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Judengasse, back houses, 1849, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0271*

The Judengasse – “Jews’ Lane” – was established in 1462. Located outside the town to the east of the city wall, the ghetto was surrounded by a wall with three gates. The densely packed houses were badly damaged in fires three times in the 18th century – 1711, 1721 and 1796. The requirement for Jews to live in the ghetto was finally lifted in 1811.

During the 19th century, many residents moved out of the narrow lane to escape the cramped living conditions. Afterwards, the poor moved in. In Reiffenstein’s eyes, this impoverished neighbourhood perfectly epitomised the Romantic image of the Old Town.

From 1864, the city acquired dilapidated houses with the plan of redeveloping the area. Within ten years, the west side of the lane had been demolished. The east side was torn down by 1885. In 1987, during building work at Börneplatz for the city’s public utility companies, the foundations of several old houses were discovered. After numerous protests, some foundations were preserved and integrated into the Museum Judengasse.

*Author: Carina Koch*
