# Stadium on Bornheimer Hang

*Kettelerallee*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![View of the Bornheimer Hang in not so good weather](../../images/9f18f0415b850c61904581b97bd3ad85_original.png)
*License: Quelle: F. Blendin | Description: View of the Bornheimer Hang in not so good weather*

You are now standing above the stadium on the Bornheimer Hang.

The stadium was opened on 11 October 1931 with a speech by the then president Alfred J. Meyers. It was very modern by the standards of the time and the financing was a huge feat for the club in the context of the inflation at the time. The stadium inauguration was correspondingly impressive.

During the Second World War, an anti-aircraft battery was stationed on the northern side of the pitch, which was the stadium's undoing during an air raid in March 1944: The stands and pitch were bombed and the office and club archives were also destroyed, which was an immeasurable loss. It was no longer possible to play matches.

The stadium was rebuilt after the war. From March 1946, the ball rolled again on the slope, initially still surrounded by rubble. The spectator capacity was gradually increased with temporary facilities. In 1953, a completely new building was finally inaugurated. The opening match against VfB Stuttgart took place under the watchful eye of the former jewish president Alfred J. Meyers, who returned from New York after fleeing to the USA in 1938.

Many memorable things have happened in the stadium. However, FSV Frankfurt's highlight - the 1925 German Championship final - took place in the Waldstadion. But it was here that the first goal in the women's Bundesliga was scored: On 2 September 1990, Katja Bornschein scored the 1:0 for FSV Frankfurt in the 6th minute against TuS Binzen.
