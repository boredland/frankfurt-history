# Slaughterhouse

*Mainkai 15, 16*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/c5cf74081e9ad6b31b54c708ca5aea13_original.png)

As early as the 13th century, the city’s Slaughter House stood near the river and the Metzger Tor – Butchers’ Gate. The Butchers’ Guild, one of Frankfurt’s oldest and richest craft guilds, had its meeting hall for guild masters on the first floor. The artisan crafts in the city were organised in guilds until 1864, when the system was replaced by the freedom of trade.

Reiffenstein sketched the only Frankfurt meeting hall for guild masters still in existence in his day. Its design and furnishings primarily date from a conversion in 1530. In 1819, it was again altered and renovated. The members sat on the benches to discuss guild matters. The walls were hung with historic guild items. Some of the 14th-century stained glass windows with their images of stags, oxen and Frankfurt’s imperial eagles are now in the Historical Museum’s collections. The building was finally demolished in 1893.

*Author: Carina Koch*
