# Gerbermühle

*Gerbermühlstraße 105*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/3cfba2ccf3643fa17f11c8e1c382209b_original.png)

The Gerbermühle was constructed in 1600 as a flour mill. From 1688 to 1723, when animal skins were brought here to be tanned, it became known as the Gerbermühle –Tanners’ Mill.

From 1785, the mill was the summer residence of the Willemer family. The writer Johann Wolfgang Goethe (1749-1832) visited the family in 1814/15. At that time, he and Marianne von Willemer (1784?-1860), in particular, not only exchanged ideas but also some verses of poetry. He included three of her poems in his collection West-östlicher Diwan (“West-Eastern Divan”), though without mentioning her name.

In 1519, a column shrine was set up behind the house. Goethe praised the little shrine as “masterly”. Reiffenstein also regarded it as “one of most beautiful examples” he knew.

The mill building was renovated in 1904. In the post-war years, it was rebuilt in a different form. Ever since the 20th century, the mill has been a popular place for a day out.
