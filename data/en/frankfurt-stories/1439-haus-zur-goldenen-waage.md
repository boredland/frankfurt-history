# Haus Zur Goldenen Waage

*Markt 5 / Domplatz*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Haus Zur Goldenen Waage, 1876, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R1572](../../images/715e597e2bfe81342226144f11ac818c_original.jpg)
*1876 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Haus Zur Goldenen Waage, 1876, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R1572*

Even in Reiffenstein’s day, the Goldene Waage was one of Frankfurt’s most magnificent residences. From 1618/19, Abraham van Hamel, a prosperous confectioner from Tournai in today’s Belgium, lived and worked in the house. The ground floor was built of stone, with richly decorated latticework on the windows and doors. The corner pillars on the two upper storeys were also elaborately carved. The stucco work on the ceilings inside was similarly intricate. Hamel also added a belvedere on the roof, a covered balcony-like structure with a particularly impressive view out across the town.

When Reiffenstein knew the house the half-timbered structure had been plastered over and covered by slates. The corner pillars were rediscovered during renovation work in 1866. The house was left in ruins in the Second World War. It was rebuilt as part of the New Old Town project completed in 2018.

*Author: Carina Koch*
