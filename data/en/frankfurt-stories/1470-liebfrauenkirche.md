# Liebfrauenkirche

*Liebfrauenberg 56*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/547f7f03e76e87cf2b1c63cd005d3408_original.png)

The Liebfrauenkirche – Church of Our Lady – is situated to the north of the former Old Town. In the early 14th century, a chapel stood here directly next to the 12th-century city walls. By 1344, the chapel had been enlarged into a church. The Liebfrauenberg square only became connected to the bustling Zeil when Liebfrauenstrasse was extended in 1855.

The South Portal was most likely designed by Madern Gerthener (c. 1365-1430) around 1420. It shows the Magi presenting gifts to the infant Jesus and his family. In 1824, Friedrich Rumpf (1795-1867) added a new entrance to the portal.

Badly damaged in the Second World War, the church was reconstructed in 1955/56. A statue of Mary, thought to be carved in Paris in 1885, survived in the monastery courtyard. The courtyard is still open to the public today.
