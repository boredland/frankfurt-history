# FSV fan project

*Ratsweg 15*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![Graffiti on Alfred J. Meyers Platz. The building is the fan house of the FSV fan project and the graffiti was created by FSV Frankfurt fans](../../images/a399eb74a8e5189138ee4c4385a671c5_original.jpg)
*Author: Bernd Löser | License: Bernd Löser | Description: Graffiti on Alfred J. Meyers Platz. The building is the fan house of the FSV fan project and the graffiti was created by FSV Frankfurt fans*

The fan project is an educational programme for young FSV fans between the ages of 12 and 27. In the building with the graffiti ‘Father of the Bornheimer Hang’ there is a transformer station and the building behind it, which was originally a public toilet facility, is now the fan centre.

Fans can meet up here and regular events are organised where they can, for example, chat to players from the team in a relaxed atmosphere. The fans also lend a hand when it comes to landscaping the building and the square in front of it.

The graffiti on the transformer building shows Alfred J. Meyers, who was president of FSV Frankfurt from 1928 to 1933 and about whom you have already heard a lot in this city tour. During his time in office, he was instrumental in ensuring that the stadium on the Bornheimer Hang could be built under the most difficult economic conditions.

He did not give up his post as president voluntarily. As he was Jewish, the new rulers began to polemicise against him as early as spring 1933, until finally, on 25 April 1933, a delegation on behalf of the NSDAP and the Lord Mayor Friedrich Krebs appeared at the office and declared him deposed. He was able to flee to the USA in 1938. Despite all the painful memories, he remained associated with the association throughout his life, even after the war.

In memory of him and his achievements, the square here at the Fanhaus was named after him in 2024 in the presence of his grandson.
