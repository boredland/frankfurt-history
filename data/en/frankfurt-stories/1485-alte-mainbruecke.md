# Alte Mainbrücke

*Alte Brücke*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![<p>Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, view of the Old Bridge and Sachsenhausen from the west, 1850/1872, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF RS0004</p>](../../images/97608d3c41341365f1b2348f2b531f80_original.jpg)
*1850/1872 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: <p>Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, view of the Old Bridge and Sachsenhausen from the west, 1850/1872, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF RS0004</p>*

The town’s Alte Brücke – Old Bridge – was first mentioned in written records in 1222. Until 1869, this was the only solid connection between Frankfurt and Sachsenhausen. When merchants transported goods over the bridge, they had to pay customs duty.

As early as 1342, an iron cross topped by a golden cockerel was set in the middle of the bridge to mark the river’s deepest point. Until 1613, this was also where people sentenced to death were executed by drowning. A replica of a Charlemagne statue is set up on the northern section of the bridge. Today, the original by Karl Eduard Wendelstadt (1815-1841) is in the Historical Museum.

The original bridge was regularly damaged by high water and ice from the frozen river. In 1914, it was torn down as it was unable to cope with the growth of river traffic. Today’s bridge dates from 1926. After German troops blew up part of the bridge in 1945, it was provisionally repaired. It took another twenty years before it was properly rebuilt.
