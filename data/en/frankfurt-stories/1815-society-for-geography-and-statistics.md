# Society for Geography and Statistics

*Große Eschenheimer Straße 76*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

The Society for Geography and Statistics, founded in 1836 as the ‘Geographical Society of Frankfurt am Main’ and now operating under the name ‘Frankfurt Geographical Society’, also invited protagonists of the German colonial movement to give lectures from the 1870s at the latest. In 1905, the society took part in the organisation of the German Colonial Congress in Berlin. It awarded its Eduard Rüppell Medal for outstanding achievements in geographical research several times to functionaries of the colonial ruling apparatus, including Hermann von Wissmann (Reichskommissar in German East Africa), Adolf Friedrich zu Mecklenburg (Governor of Togo) and Paul von Lettow-Vorbeck (Commander of the ‘Schutztruppe’ for German East Africa). To mark its 75th anniversary in 1909, the association organised a research expedition to the Malay Archipelago, then part of the colony of the Dutch East Indies.
