# Ulrichstein

*Färberstraße 68, 70, 72, 74*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/e85e910e9edba94968444856412251b8_original.png)

The Ulrichstein was a castle most probably built by Ulrich III, Lord of Hanau (c. 1310-1369/70). As part of the town’s fortifications, it was owned by the city of Frankfurt from the late 1300s. Next to the Ulrichstein, a narrow gateway led into the town. Widened to take wagons in the 16th century, this became known as the Schaumaintor (Schaumain Gate). Although the gate and city fortifications were demolished, the Ulrichstein tower was left standing. Reiffenstein not only painted a scene of the reconstructed castle, but also sketched its remains in his own day. In particular, he liked the old houses in Färberstrasse which had merged with the castle ruins.

Despite some objections, the tower was torn down in 1930 to make way for a flood-free road along the River Main. Today, the Ulrichstein tower’s original location is marked by a circle of cobblestones on the Schaumainkai.
