# Nürnberger Hof

*Hinter dem Lämmchen / Berliner Straße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Nürnberger Hof, entrance from Schnurgasse, 1856, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0400](../../images/8de0ba86c74aec5fc31cc5747a167af2_original.jpg)
*1856 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Nürnberger Hof, entrance from Schnurgasse, 1856, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0400*

The Nürnberger Hof was a courtyard complex between Schnurgasse (today’s Berliner Straße) and the lane known as Hinter dem Lämmchen. This courtyard was especially popular with Nuremberg merchants during the city’s trade fairs. Once it covered two other courtyards as well: Glauburgerhof to the south and the northern Schmidthof, home to the blacksmiths’ guild in the Middle Ages. The Nürnberger Hof complex was long and narrow, and accessed by a gateway to the north and south.

From the 13th century, this was home to the Glauburg family. In the early 15th century, they added a vaulted ceiling decorated with family coats of arms to the southern gate, documented by Reiffenstein in his sketches.

In 1905, the construction of Braubachstrasse, a new access road, divided the complex. After the Second World War, the remnants of Nürnberger Hof to the north were demolished to make way for Berliner Strasse. The arched northern gate and the vaulted southern gateway have survived.

*Author: Carina Koch*
