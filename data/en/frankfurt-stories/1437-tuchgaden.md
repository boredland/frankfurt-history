# Tuchgaden

*Schirn Kunsthalle*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Tuchgaden to the north, 1871, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0579](../../images/76fef848871da63276b38ccda1ee8342_original.jpg)
*1871 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Tuchgaden to the north, 1871, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0579*

The area between the Markt and Bendergasse lanes was densely packed with medieval houses. They bordered the Dom (Cathedral) to the east, and the Römerberg to the west. The houses could only be accessed via narrow alleyways running north-south. The Unter den Tuchgaden alley began directly at the base of the Neues Rotes Haus – the “New Red House” on the Markt lane. In the alleyway, merchants sold textile wares at open booths known as “schirne”, the traditional market stalls in the Old Town. The “schirne” not only lined Tuchgaden alley, but were similarly used by butchers in Bendergasse lane. Reiffenstein liked the picturesque quality of these streets. They also inspired other artists to paint genre scenes of daily life. Today, Frankfurt Old Town’s “schirne” – the open booths of bygone days – are recalled in the name of the Schirn exhibition venue.

*Author: Carina Koch*
