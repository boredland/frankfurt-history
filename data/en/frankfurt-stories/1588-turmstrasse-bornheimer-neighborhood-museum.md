# Turmstraße Bornheimer neighborhood museum

*Turmstraße 11*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![Picture of the sign of the “”Bornheim neighborhood museum - Bornheim, the funny village""](../../images/0bb1a523128d1178a2fd1bb8bf35c5a4_original.jpg)
*Description: Picture of the sign of the “”Bornheim neighborhood museum - Bornheim, the funny village""*

You are now standing at Turmstraße 11 in front of Frankfurt's smallest museum, the ‘Bernemer Museumslädchen’. The museum opened in 1994 and is run by the Bürgerverein und Förderkreis historisches Bornheim e.V. (Citizens' Association and Friends of Historic Bornheim). The ‘Bornheim Archive’, in which archives and exhibits are stored, was set up in the basement of the adjoining residential building. The museum has already hosted a number of exhibitions, perhaps you have been to the ‘125 years of FSV Frankfurt’ exhibition? However, the house, built in 1750, was not always such a beautiful sight. At the beginning of the 1990s, the small, baroque building was a ‘lost place’ and was due to be demolished. Bernhard Ochs dreamed of a house of associations and successfully applied for listed building status. The entire house was demolished, the half-timbering renovated and the building rebuilt true to the original. It was important to the creators of the ‘Bernemer Museumslädchen’ to bring history back to life and make it interesting again as an expression of joie de vivre and a sense of belonging to one's homeland.
