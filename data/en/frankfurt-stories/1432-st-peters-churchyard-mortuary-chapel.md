# St. Peter's churchyard, mortuary chapel

*Schäfergasse / Stephanstraße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, St. Peter's churchyard, entrance gate from the inside with the surrounding houses of the Schäfergasse, 1840/1874, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0854](../../images/6d31b8662ac53354f0ce6fb0f4754504_original.jpg)
*1840/1874 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, St. Peter's churchyard, entrance gate from the inside with the surrounding houses of the Schäfergasse, 1840/1874, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0854*

The Petersfriedhof – St. Peter’s Cemetery – was established after 1450 and expanded a number of times. This was Frankfurt’s central burial ground until the main cemetery was opened in 1828. St. Peter’s Cemetery was also the site of a 16th-century cemetery chapel, sketched several times by Reiffenstein. Until it was demolished in the 19th century, he was fascinated by its increasing decay and how nature reclaimed it.

From 1895, a new building replaced St. Peter’s Church on the northern section. In 1904, the southern section of the cemetery was cleared to facilitate a new road connection from Senckenberg Strasse (today’s Stephanstrasse) to the Schäfergasse. This involved moving the Crucifixion sculpture carved by Hans Backoffen (c. 1470-1519) in 1507/08. Today, the original is in the Historical Museum and the sculpture on site is a copy from 1910.
