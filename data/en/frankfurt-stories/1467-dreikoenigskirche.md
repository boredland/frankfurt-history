# Dreikönigskirche

*Dreikönigsstraße 32*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/c18fa5c0e1304a0ff3fc2cf5289f33d8_original.png)

In 1440, a chapel dedicated to the Three Kings already stood on this site. Twelve years later, the chapel was raised to a church, enlarged in 1498. In the Reformation, the Sachsenhausen congregation became the first in Frankfurt to reject Catholic teachings. In 1531, a Protestant pastor was appointed to the church.

By 1875, the old Church of the Three Kings was in a dilapidated state and was torn down. One year previously, Reiffenstein reported on the auction of the church’s fittings and furnishings. These included oil paintings “acquired by the Sachsenhausen congregation in memory of the old church”. He also sketched the brightly painted keystones on the vaulted ceiling from 1498. The keystones were decorated with various coats of arms that Reiffenstein was able to attribute to Frankfurt families.

The new Church of the Three Kings was consecrated in 1881. It survived the Second World War largely unscathed.
