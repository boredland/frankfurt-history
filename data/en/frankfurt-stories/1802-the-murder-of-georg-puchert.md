# The murder of Georg Puchert

*Guiolettstraße*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![](../../images/3401045fb476770ac2a6662e514a2be0_original.jpg)
*License: ISG*

On the morning of 3 March 1959, arms dealer Georg Puchert was killed by a car bomb in Guiolettstraße. Puchert had supplied explosives to the independence movements that had been fighting against French colonial rule in Algeria and Morocco since the mid-1950s. In addition to business premises in Tangier, he also maintained an office in Frankfurt's Lindenstraße, where he was staying at the time. The Frankfurt public prosecutor's office suspected that the perpetrators were part of the French secret service, which had been carrying out murders and assassinations of supporters of the anti-colonial liberation movements and Algerian politicians in exile in Western Europe since 1956. Witness descriptions of the suspects and their car licence plates confirmed this suspicion. However, because the French authorities refused to co-operate in the investigation, the perpetrators were never caught and convicted.
