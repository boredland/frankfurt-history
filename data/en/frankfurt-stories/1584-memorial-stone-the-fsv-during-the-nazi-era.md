# Memorial stone: The FSV during the Nazi era

*Max-Bromme-Steig*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![The memorial stone today (2024)](../../images/ea5002e336359605d067802a0042e82e_original.jpg)
*Description: The memorial stone today (2024)*

You are now standing in front of the memorial stone, which was erected in 1937 to commemorate the FSV players who died as soldiers in the First World War, while the regime was already working flat out to prepare for the Second World War.
Before we talk about this time: do you notice the container in black and blue in the background? The graffiti commemorates FSV fan Freno, who died of a brain tumour on 20 August 2018 at the age of just 24.He will be remembered for his great commitment and the people who loved him. The banner in his memory can be found in front of Block 385 when you visit the stadium.

Back to National Socialism: 30 January 1933 not only had an impact on the political situation in Germany, but also on social life as a whole and therefore also on sport and club life at FSV Frankfurt.
Just two months after Hitler's appointment as Reich Chancellor, all Jewish club officials were removed from office. In addition to the president and ‘father’ of the Bornheimer Hang, Alfred J. Meyers, FSV lost around 10-15% of its members as a result of these measures, as it was particularly well established in the Jewish-dominated east of the city after the First World War. Most clubs and associations in the country came to terms with the new political situation relatively quickly, including FSV Frankfurt, which had a memorial stone placed on the club grounds with great pomp and circumstance. The original inscription "To our fallen 1914-1918" and the Iron Cross were removed after the war and replaced by the current inscription.

Later, while the Second World War had already broken out, the FSV also took part in the metal donation for the ‘Führer's’ birthday, for example, and sacrificed the trophies it had won for the German armaments industry in 1940.
