# Steinernes Haus / Bornfleck

*Markt 44*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/e887a59248baa2c3d2d177a21ba26ec5_original.png)

In 1464, Cologne merchant Johann von Melem (1433-1484) built the Bornfleck House. In contrast to the surrounding wooden buildings, his house was built of stone and so became known as the Steinernes Haus – the Stone House. Even today, the von Melem family’s coat of arms adorns the façade over the arched gateway. Their heraldic animal gave the house a third name, the Roter Krebs – the red crayfish. When the house caught fire in 1506, it was the guildhall of the furriers, a trade preparing or dealing in furs.

Reiffenstein praised the Stone House as “one of the most attractive buildings from the late Middle Ages”. He sketched the façade, as well as the interior and the gateway’s vaulted ceiling.

Badly damaged in the Second World War, the Stone House was reconstructed from 1959 to 1962. Since then it has been home to Frankfurt’s art association, founded in 1829 to show contemporary art.
