# Hauptwache

*An der Hauptwache*

Frankfurt places of mobility

![Carl Friedrich Mylius (1827-1916), Schillerplatz at the Hauptwache with waiting horse-drawn carriages, Frankfurt around 1865, HMF.C19977, public domain](../../images/d843426f3c7a70d61a85e42c8193dfa2_original.png)
*Around 1865 — Author: Carl Friedrich Mylius | License: Gemeinfrei; HMF, Foto: Horst Ziegenfusz | Description: Carl Friedrich Mylius (1827-1916), Schillerplatz at the Hauptwache with waiting horse-drawn carriages, Frankfurt around 1865, HMF.C19977, public domain*

The Hauptwache was already an important station in the 19th century. From the 1860s onwards, line B of the horse-drawn omnibuses connected it with Bornheim. In addition, horse-drawn carriages with taxi services were available at the train stations and in the city center. However, due to high costs, these early means of local transport were mainly reserved for tourists and businessmen and did not become a means of mass transport.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima; Sarah Roller*

In 1872, horse-drawn carriages were put “on rails.” This had the advantage that the horses could pull larger carriages faster due to the lower resistance. The first horse-drawn railroad line opened on May 19, 1872. It mainly served the urban middle class between the Hauptwache (then Schillerplatz) and Bockenheimer Warte. However, the horse-drawn railroad remained limited to a few districts in the city center.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima; Sarah Roller*

From the turn of the century onwards, the electrically powered streetcars gradually replaced the horse-drawn railroad. The “electric” was so popular that it was overwhelmed by the high volume of traffic in the 1920s. In 1928, 13 lines and 3,284 streetcars stopped at the Hauptwache every day. The streetcar remained the most important means of local transport in Frankfurt until the 1960s.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima; Sarah Roller*

The creation of Frankfurt’s subway and suburban railroad network restructured the city permanently and changed the way people moved around. Underground construction began in June 1963. The Hauptwache became a major construction site. The noise of heavy construction machinery became part of everyday life in Frankfurt, as did traffic diversions. Many so-called guest workers moved to Frankfurt and worked on the large construction sites that shaped the cityscape for decades.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima; Sarah Roller*

In October 1968, the city of Frankfurt celebrated the opening of subway line A1, which ran from Hauptwache to the residential area of Nordwestzentrum.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima; Sarah Roller*
