# Haus Fürsteneck

*Fahrgasse 9-11*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/43c311ad4ef1a7709e3b64217b7d936e_original.png)

In the mid-14th century, Johann von Holzhausen built Fürsteneck House. Together with the Steinernes Haus and the Leinwandhaus, Fürsteneck House is one of the city’s large secular Gothic buildings. Its design with its corner towers goes back to the 15th century.

In 1791, renovation work changed the appearance of the facade. The pointed arches on the ground floor were replaced with doors with horizontal lintels. This was how Reiffenstein saw and documented the house. In a different sketch, he reconstructed the façade before the renovation work.

The house had a magnificent main room with 17th-century wooden panelling and a stucco ceiling. In 1891, the wooden panelling passed to the Mitteldeutschen Kunstgewerbe-Vereins, an Arts and Crafts Association. Fürsteneck House was destroyed during the Second World War as was the wooden panelling, then in the Museum für Angewandte Kunst, Frankfurt’s applied arts museum.
