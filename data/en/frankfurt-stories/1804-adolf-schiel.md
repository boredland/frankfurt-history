# Adolf Schiel

*Golfstraße 17*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![Adolf Schiel (7th from left) with German officers during the South African War, 1899.](../../images/fcc6245ea8eb78ab38b06f4de88928b0_original.jpg)
*1899 — License: Adolf Schiel. 23 Jahre Sturm und Sonnenschein in Südafrika. Leipzig: Brockhaus, 1902, S. 384. | Description: Adolf Schiel (7th from left) with German officers during the South African War, 1899.*

Why did the shooting club in Golfstraße choose the officer Adolf Schiel as its patron saint? Essentially, this can be explained by the anti-British sentiment that was widespread in Germany at the time the club was founded in 1902. Adolf Schiel - born in Frankfurt in 1858 and raised near Stuttgart - had emigrated to the British colony of Natal in 1878 and from there to the British Republic of Transvaal. After the outbreak of the South African War in 1899, he formed a Free Corps of German emigrants that fought on the Burian side against Great Britain. In the German Empire, nationalist milieus in particular sympathised with the Boers because they regarded them as ‘Teutons’ related to the Germans and hoped for a weakening of Great Britain, which they saw as the greatest obstacle to the desired German ‘Weltgel-tung’.

Schiel was taken prisoner by the British a few days after the fighting began. After his release at the end of the war in 1902, he returned to Germany and died there in 1903 as a result of a war injury. Even before this, he published an autobiography, which attracted widespread attention in view of the attention paid to the Anglo-Bohemian conflict at the time.
