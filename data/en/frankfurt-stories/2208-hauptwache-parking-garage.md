# Hauptwache parking garage

*Kornmarkt 10*

Frankfurt places of mobility

![Willy Keim, Kornmarkt parking garage at Hauptwache with gas station, Frankfurt 1957, HMF.Ph12325, roebild](../../images/bef016bd76619f02ea15f2dab9076981_original.png)
*1957 — Author: Willy Keim | License: HMF, roebild | Description: Willy Keim, Kornmarkt parking garage at Hauptwache with gas station, Frankfurt 1957, HMF.Ph12325, roebild*

On September 18, 1956, the Kornmarkt parking garage opened at the Hauptwache. It was the first of its kind and still stands today. It initially offered space for 430 cars on four floors. It was designed by architects Max Meid and Helmut Romeik. At the beginning, parking in the garage cost 20 pfennigs per hour. Other needs of motorists were also catered for: a gas station and a “car care station” were also part of the parking garage. However, the parking spaces in Frankfurt's oldest parking garage are now too small for newer car models.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima, Nina Gorgus, Sarah Roller*

The number of cars rose rapidly in the post-war period. In 1960, there were over 120,500 registered vehicles in Frankfurt. That is almost four times as many vehicles as before the war. The historic city center was not prepared for the rapid increase in car traffic. At first, the great demand for parking spaces could not be met in the post-war period. At the end of the 1950s, there were only 4,000 parking spaces in Frankfurt.

It was not until 1966 that parking on the roadside was permitted. Frankfurt's parking garages, such as this one at the Hauptwache, were also intended to provide a remedy. In the year the first parking garage opened, plans were already in place to build 20 more “parking high-rises.” Ten years later, there were already nine parking garages with over 5,600 parking spaces in Frankfurt.

*Author: Ilyas Chhima, Nina Gorgus, Sarah Roller*
