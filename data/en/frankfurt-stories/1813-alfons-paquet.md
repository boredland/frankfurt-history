# Alfons Paquet

*Wolfsgangstraße 122*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

The much-traveled journalist and writer Alfons Paquet (1881-1944) lived in Frankfurt from 1910 after various other stations, at times at Wolfsgangstraße 122. His reporting and research trips led him to Siberia, Syria, Palestine, China, Japan, Sweden, Finland, Greece and Morocco as well as to the United States, Mongolia, Turkey and the Canary Islands. Contacts in diplomatic circles enabled him to travel to Moscow in 1918 as part of a German legation and exchange views with representatives of the Bolsheviks. 
In his travel reports and essays, Paquet critically discussed the imperialism of the European powers at the time. In principle, he was in favor of European domination of the world, but he saw the rivalries between the colonial powers at the time as a threat to peace in Europe.
