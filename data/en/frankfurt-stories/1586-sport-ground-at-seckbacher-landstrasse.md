# Sport ground at Seckbacher Landstraße

*Berger Straße 385*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![The crowded tribune on Seckbacher Landstraße](../../images/c556027b9041503896cf34ca6f0707f6_original.jpg)
*Description: The crowded tribune on Seckbacher Landstraße*

You're standing in front of the main entrance to the sports ground on Seckbacher Landstraße. (Fun fact: the house number corresponds to our block 385 in the stadium on the Bornheimer Hang, the home of the Ultras).
The sports ground is now home to SG Bornheim Grün/Weiß. The club has many men's and women's teams and it's always worth a visit, not least because, in addition to great football, there are often very tasty home-baked cakes. However, the history of the pitch is much longer than that of SG Bornheim.

The sports ground was the venue for FSV Frankfurt from 6 September 1908, as the previous pitch in Prüfling was no longer big enough for the steadily growing club.

After long negotiations, FSV was able to lease the land between Seckbacher Landstraße and Berger Straße, but this did not please the farmers in the neighbourhood. For one thing, there were precious apple trees all around, including on the pitch itself, which suddenly disappeared in a night-and-fog operation by brave FSV supporters. Secondly, the pitch did not have its own access at the beginning, which is why spectators had to walk across the orchardists' meadows to get to the pitch. The fact that at least the latter problem could be solved was thanks to the widow Clauer, who ceded part of her land to the football club. This gave the FSV its own access to the pitch, even if it was very narrow. A maximum of two people could walk on it side by side, which would be unthinkable by today's standards with up to 10,000 visitors. This is the entrance on the other side of the pitch on Seckbacher Landstraße.

The opponent in the opening match of the new facility was Phönix Karlsruhe, one of the original clubs of today's Karlsruher SC, which also wore the colours black and blue.

After FSV moved to the stadium on Bornheimer Hang in 1931, the pitch on Seckbacher Landstraße continued to be used by other clubs, such as ‘Hertha Weiß Blau’. In the post-war period, the old wooden stand was demolished by the destitute population and used as firewood for heating. Finally, SG Bornheim, which was founded in 1945, was allocated the pitch by the sports and bathing authority.
