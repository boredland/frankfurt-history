# House of the Textor family

*Große Friedberger Straße 20*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/d18212e190023f2f95a45ede95497c0f_original.png)

In 1714, the lawyer Christoph Heinrich Textor (1666-1716) had a house with a garden built for his family. Above the entrance door, he set the family’s coat of arms, a man with a fluttering hair ribbon and a sword in his right hand. His son was the lawyer and politician Johann Wolfgang Textor (1693-1771), grandfather of the writer Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749-1832). Goethe described his grandfather in his autobiography Dichtung und Wahrheit (“Poetry and Truth”): “He conversed little [...] and I do not remember ever having seen him angry.”

In the late 18th century, Reiffenstein’s father and grandfather lived here. When Frankfurt was bombarded by French troops in 1796, there was said to be “thousands of rifles” stored here, some of them loaded. When a cannonball hit the house and a fire broke out, the guns released a spectacular hail of bullets. The house was torn down in 1863.

*Author: Carina Koch*
