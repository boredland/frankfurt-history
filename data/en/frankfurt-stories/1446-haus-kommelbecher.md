# Haus Kommelbecher

*Kleinmarkthalle*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/fc4974aa96b9276503a7fbe77eccc83a_original.png)

Born in Kommelbecher House, Reiffenstein lived there until he was 23 years old. The house was in a traditional artisan’s quarter destroyed by fire in 1719. Afterwards, over 400 of the damaged houses were rebuilt.

Surprisingly, Reiffenstein only sketched his parents’ house once, long after he had moved out. His sketch shows a storeroom on the ground floor with beer mugs and tankards in the shelves – a reference to his parents’ alehouse. Once, Kommelbecher House had also been a lodging house for several craft trades.

In the Second World War, the Graubengasse lane was destroyed. In 1954, the Kleinmarkthalle – a covered market hall – was built on the site. Today, the greened square of Carl-Theodor-Reiffenstein-Platz on Hasengasse is close to where his parents’ house once stood.
