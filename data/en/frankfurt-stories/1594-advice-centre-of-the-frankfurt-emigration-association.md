# Advice centre of the Frankfurt Emigration Association

*Zeil 3 (building of today's Bürgeramt)*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![](../../images/c5a436a0f54f1ab559e4de27a46123d2_original.jpg)

In 1850, the ‘Anstalt zur Nachweisung von Arbeit’, a charitable organisation for free job placement, set up an office on the site of today's Bürgeramt. From 1850 to 1879, the Frankfurt ‘Association for the Protection of Emigrants’ also used the premises to inform those wishing to emigrate about the conditions in the United States, Australia, Brazil and other popular emigration destinations. In the destination countries, many emigrants endeavoured to form German emigrant colonies: Ethnically largely homogeneous settlements were intended to ensure the preservation of German culture, attract emigration from German countries and maintain a connection with the regions of origin. In the mid-1850s, anti-slavery activists distributed leaflets at the advice centre to sensitise those interested in emigrating to slavery in the Americas and to call for opposition to it.
