# Richard-Herrmann-Kiosk

*Berger Straße 191*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![](../../images/3f1180502f75c6ae767bfa4172beeb98_original.png)

You are standing here in front of the magazine and tobacco shop that our legendary captain of honour Richard Herrmann was able to open with the support of FSV at its original location in Berger Straße 171 (where the optician Fielmann is located today). When the shutter is closed, you can see a graffiti with Richard Herrmann's portrait.

The shop then moved to its current location in 1960. Richard Herrmann ran the shop during the week, later together with his wife Lilo, as well as training three times a week and playing matches at the weekend. His sons Horst and Rolf continued to run the shop until 2021.

Richard Herrmann played 320 times from 1947 to 1960 in the striped black and blue jersey for his FSV Frankfurt, scoring exactly 100 goals. He wore the jersey of the German national team eight times and was unfortunately also world champion in 1954. Yes, unfortunately! Because the team doctor at the time, Franz Loogen, administered ‘vitamin’ injections to the players at the tournament, which were not sufficiently sterile. As a result, several players fell ill with jaundice and Richard Herrmann died in 1962 at the age of just 39.
