# Eschenheimer Turm

*Eschenheimer Tor 1*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Eschenheimer Tor with city walls (reconstruction based on an old drawing from 1790), 1859, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0039](../../images/9dda62ba4b24cc177d4b62e9ff571980_original.jpg)
*1859 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Eschenheimer Tor with city walls (reconstruction based on an old drawing from 1790), 1859, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0039*

From 1346, the Eschenheimer Tor (Eschenheim Gate) was the northern entrance through the town’s fortifications. Its present form as a tower set on a square base with a gateway dates from 1426. Eschenheim Gate quickly became Frankfurt’s most famous landmark and survived the demolition of the city’s fortifications in the 1800s. Originally, the Karlstor, a customs and guard house, stood on its north side. It was torn down, at the latest, when horse-drawn trams were introduced in 1872. In the 19th century various plans were put forward to demolish the tower, as its position at the centre of a large street made traffic management difficult.

Reiffenstein sketched the tower and its interior. In his day, the tower was Frankfurt’s tallest building, though now it is overshadowed by many high rises. Today, this mix of old and new is seen as a characteristic feature of modern Frankfurt.

*Author: Carina Koch*
