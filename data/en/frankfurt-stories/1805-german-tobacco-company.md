# German Tobacco Company

*Platz der Republik 12*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![](../../images/10946706e295b6aa89bc2b83914cadce_original.jpg)

In 1912 at the latest, Frankfurt businessmen led by Josef Schloßmacher and Otto Lautz founded the Deutsche Tabakbau-Gesellschaft, which established tobacco plantations in the colony of Cameroon - more precisely, in the Mbanga region. In 1913, the company merged with business partners from Bremen, Heidelberg and Mannheim to form the Tabak- und Pflanzungs-Gesellschaft ‘Kamerun’, which Schloßmacher managed as a director. Although the plantations were lost to France during the First World War, the company continued to exist after the war, but did not take up any new business activities.

*Author: Felix Schürmann*
