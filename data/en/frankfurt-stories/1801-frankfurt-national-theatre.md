# Frankfurt National Theatre

*Börsenstr. 2–4 (Zara shop)*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![Interior view of the National Theater](../../images/af7a38f81062df5c8f5c9fb80490a546_original.jpg)
*around 1800 — License: HMF.C12746. | Description: Interior view of the National Theater*

Opened in 1782, the Comoedienhaus - renamed the ‘Frankfurter Nationaltheater’ in 1792 - developed into a centre of German-speaking theatre culture within a few years. After the beginning of the slave revolution on Saint-Domingue (today's Haiti) in 1791, the theatre staged several plays that dealt with slavery in the Atlantic world. These performances usually dealt with plantation slaves in the Americas or in Africa, who suffered exploitation, oppression and torture and even organised rebellions, sometimes were freed by whites and taken to German territories. On the part of the audience, the stage works provided the impetus for an examination of the rights and wrongs of slavery, the legitimacy of violent resistance and the realities of life for black people in the Americas.

*Author: Felix Schürmann*
