# Prüfling - the first home

*Im Prüfling 63*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![](../../images/7bba3302f5a0c04d6e084fbeb7e796a8_original.png)

You are now standing near FSV Frankfurt's first official match venue. When the club was founded on 20 August 1899, it initially played on Glauburgplatz and on what was then still undeveloped land between Rohrbachstrasse, Egenolffstrasse and Friedberger Landstrasse. In its founding year, FSV was then allocated the municipal playing field at Prüfling as a venue. The exact location of the pitch was probably a little further back on the grounds of the former Weidenborn School (now the Louise-von-Rothschild School). The following diagram shows the location of the playground and the development of the Prüfling neighbourhood from 1904-1913:

From here on the corner of Weidenbornstrasse/Prüfling, you have a similar view of the Zwiebelturm of the Johanneskirche as in the following drawing from our 1924 anniversary publication.

FSV Frankfurt did not yet have the luxury of its own pitch. The pitch was also used by gymnasts, for example. For every training session, the pitch was marked out and the goalposts brought along were set up.
FSV won the first public match on this pitch 5:1 against the Frankofurtia club. In the 1904/05 season, FSV won its first title here, the championship of the then Frankfurter Associationsbund (although the deciding match against Victoria - a predecessor club of Eintracht - was played in Hanau).

The pitch at Prüfling was anything but level. In 1905, an opposing team took advantage of this fact by complaining about the allegedly unplayable pitch after a defeat. This led to the match being scored in favour of the opposing team and the pitch being closed, although this was soon lifted. Unfortunately, it is not known who this bad loser was. However, it was quite common at the time to lodge a complaint after losing a match for the most adventurous reasons.

From 1906, the first club-owned football pitches with clearly demarcated playing areas were built in Frankfurt. Among the first clubs were Germania 94 and Hermannia, also from Bornheim. This gave these rivals a decisive advantage: from then on, they were able to charge spectators an entrance fee. For this reason, FSV increasingly gave up its right to play at home and voluntarily switched to the sports grounds of the opposing clubs for home matches.

From 1907, it was no longer possible for FSV to play its home games at Prüfling, as an association decree meant that from then on, matches could only be played on closed pitches. Due to the ever-closer development of the Prüfling neighbourhood, the club had to look for an alternative anyway and fortunately found one on Seckbacher Landstraße, where FSV was finally able to open its own sports ground in 1908. You can also stop by there later on in this tour.
