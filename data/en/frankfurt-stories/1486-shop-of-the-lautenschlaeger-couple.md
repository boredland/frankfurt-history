# Shop of the Lautenschläger couple

*Weckmarkt 13-15*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/ada5bd6bd63b45a9eddf92f294f9e0b8_original.png)

The booth on the north-east corner of the Weighing House belonged to master butcher Johann Adam Philipp Lautenschläger and his wife Katharina Elisabeth. Their booth led to a legal battle between the couple and the city council that dragged on for years.

In 1874, Reiffenstein noted the Weighing House had been demolished – apart from the north-eastern corner wall with the butcher’s booth. Even when the city archives were built on the site in 1877, the booth was still there. For the city, though, it not only obstructed the traffic, but was an eyesore on the new Archives building. Moreover, it represented a security risk for the valuable archive on the ground floor.

In 1874, the couple demanded 50,000 guilders for their booth - a demand the city rejected. Ultimately in 1891, they agreed to sell it for 8,000 guilders.
