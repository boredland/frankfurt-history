# Johanniterhof and church

*Fahrgasse 61 / Berliner Straße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Johanniterkirche, entrance gate, sacristy and surroundings (condition of 1839), 1871, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0184](../../images/e7d65b0c3ae05752de341a4892a67cab_original.jpg)
*1871 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Johanniterkirche, entrance gate, sacristy and surroundings (condition of 1839), 1871, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0184*

The house of the Knights Hospitaller was in Fahrgasse. They already had a courtyard and a church there by the mid-14 century. However, the construction work dragged on until the mid-16th century.

From 1801, the single-nave church interior served as a storeroom. When the city of Frankfurt acquired the complex in 1841, it also set up offices in the church. Reiffenstein sketched the church with the neighbouring stands and booths, torn down in 1845. Despite protests, the church was also demolished in 1874. Some of the architectural decorations are now in St. Joseph’s Church in Berger Strasse. The Historical Museum’s collection includes graveslabs from the Knights Hospitaller Church, as well as a weathercock and small sculptures. Today’s Museum of Modern Art stands approximately on the site of the Hospitaller’s Church of St. John.

*Author: Carina Koch*
