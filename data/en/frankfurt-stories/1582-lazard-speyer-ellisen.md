# Lazard Speyer-Ellisen

*Neue Mainzer Straße 23*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![The building at Neue Mainzer Strasse 23 was the headquarters of Lazard Speyer-Ellisen until 1908, when the bank moved to Taunusanlage 11.](../../images/798978ffee58a4cc2dc7a1e6b70f6d75_original.png)
*License: HMF.C16681. | Description: The building at Neue Mainzer Strasse 23 was the headquarters of Lazard Speyer-Ellisen until 1908, when the bank moved to Taunusanlage 11.*

Alongside Jacob S. H. Stern, Lazard Speyer-Ellisen was the second private bank in Frankfurt to operate on a large scale in the German colonial empire. Initially focussing on financial transactions with North America, from 1908 the bank became involved in the diamond management system of the South West African protectorate, the diamond processing cartel for diamonds from German South West Africa. As a member company of the South West African Mining Syndicate, it was also involved in the mining of diamonds. After the conquest of the colony during the First World War, the Union of South Africa took control of diamond production there in 1919.
