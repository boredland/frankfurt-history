# Spenglerlädchen

*Ecke Carl-Theodor-Reiffenstein-Platz*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/ac7d80c734d1d7732f98e64c83ea190b_original.png)

There were various shops in the vicinity of the gate in the Hasengasse lane. From the 16th century, this gateway provided access to the pits of building materials. The shops also included a lean-to metal workshop, which Reiffenstein sketched. Under the little protective roof, the tin goods on the windowsill illustrate the artisan products made there. This workshop produced sheet metal parts, for instance, for guttering or stoves.

In 1864, Reiffenstein emphasised that the interior “gave quite a cosy impression, not least because of the continuous glow of the furnace for the solder.” The smoke was extracted through a pipe over the window.

In 1856, he admitted the little workshop “ was not an architectural art work”. Nonetheless, he added, it offers a typical example of the old physiognomy of the town in his own lifetime. The little shop was torn down in 1856.
