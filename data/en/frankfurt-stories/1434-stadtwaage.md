# Stadtwaage

*Weckmarkt 13-15*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, weighing house and cathedral, 1837/1870, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0370](../../images/f4f3b9a8085fb8b293d77f7bdf0715e3_original.jpg)
*1837/1870 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, weighing house and cathedral, 1837/1870, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0370*

In 1503, construction work began on the late Gothic Weighing House, partly built on the site of the Old Jewish School. As Reiffenstein’s sketches show, the building had a high saddleback roof with a stepped gable. The façade was also decorated with Frankfurt’s coat of arms and imperial eagles. On the ground floor, two gateways opened onto a hall supported by three columns. The west wall was decorated by a crucifix, a figure of Christ crucified, and murals of Bartholomew and Charlemagne, Frankfurt’s patron saints.

The upper storey served as the municipal fruit store; in the 19th century, it housed the municipal archive. In 1873, to provide space for a new archive and museum, the Weighing House was torn down.

*Author: Carina Koch*
