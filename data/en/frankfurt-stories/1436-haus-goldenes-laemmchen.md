# Haus Goldenes Lämmchen

*Hinter dem Lämmchen 6 / Braubachstraße 25*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Hinter dem Lämmchen 6, Madonna at the corner of the house, 1878, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R1561](../../images/db219002155002a0cd3bfd1f4daf97ee_original.jpg)
*1878 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Hinter dem Lämmchen 6, Madonna at the corner of the house, 1878, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R1561*

Since 1438, the house here has been called the “(Golden) Lamb” and given its name to the narrow lane. During Frankfurt’s trade fairs, the building also served as accommodation. Reiffenstein noted that although the front house dates from the early 1700s, the rear houses were already standing in the late 1600s. The inner courtyard galleries had moveable wooden lattices or grilles. These served as screens for privacy or as blinds against bright sunlight.

The western corner of the house bore a Gothic statue of the Virgin Mary holding the infant Jesus. Since this statue dates from around 1460, it evidently belonged to a previous building on the site. In 1911, the Madonna statue entered the Historical Museum’s collection.

The building was left in ruins in the Second World War. It was rebuilt for Frankfurt’s “New Old Town” reconstruction project, completed in 2018. The new Madonna figure is also a copy dating from that time.

*Author: Carina Koch*
