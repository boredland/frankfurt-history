# The sports corner/ The "Sportseck"

*Im Prüfling 64*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![](../../images/a8b8687eeb09f68de78e0417288bc494_original.jpg)

‘All Bornheimer Pubs used to be FSV!’ (in hessian dialect:"Früher ware ja all Bernemer Kneip FSV!") But the Sporteck was until the end. This was due to owner Hans Bamberg's unwavering loyalty to the club. His family has always been FSV - ever since the club was founded. When his grandparents Heiner and Gretl Pflug took over the business in 1919, the direction was clear. However, the wonderful football wood carvings, which can also be seen in the photo, were already there before that.

The building was only leased and, as is so often the case, belonged to the Binding brewery. The Pflugs would have liked to have bought it back then, but Heiner was out hunting with his mates before the auction and had one or two too many drinks. By the time he got to the auction, the Binding had already been sold. But that didn't stop the plans for the pub. The Sporteck, located at Im Prüfling 64 on the corner of Rendeler Straße, was always the centre of celebrations, parties, anniversaries and everything that went with it. From the 1950s onwards, the team often came by before or after the game to eat and drink.
