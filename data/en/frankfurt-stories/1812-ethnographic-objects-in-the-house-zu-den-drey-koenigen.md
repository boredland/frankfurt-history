# Ethnographic objects in the house ‘Zu den Drey Königen’

*Zeil/corner of Große Eschenheimer Straße (Galeria Frankfurt building)*

In 1805, the Frankfurt painter Louise von Barckhaus-Wiesenhütten married the Dutch officer Willem Benjamin van Panhuys, who had exiled to the Electorate of Hesse during the turmoil of the Napoleonic Wars. Willem had inherited a plantation in Surinam from his first marriage, on which the couple lived intermittently from 1811 to 1816. In 1816, Willem held the office of Governor General of the colony for a few months. On the plantation on the lower Commewijne, Louise painted botanical motifs as well as some landscape views and groups of slaves. After Willem's early death in July 1816, Louise van Panhuys returned to Frankfurt and moved into her parents' house “Zu den Drey Königen”. She decorated the interior with her paintings as well as ethnographic and botanical rarities that she had brought back from Surinam, including a shell bracelet, a Bam-bus flute and a headband with feathers. She thus lent the interiors an "exotic" décor that soon attracted the curiosity of many Frankfurt scholars.
