# Salzhaus / Hohen Homberg

*Römerberg 27*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/df57d61c8150327ddcda16c2949bf83c_original.png)

The Salzhaus – Salt House – was first mentioned in written records in 1324, named after the merchants who traded salt there.

Reiffenstein’s sketch shows a decorated ground floor of red sandstone and several upper storeys with fine woodcarving. The elements depict, for instance, the four seasons, as well as flowers, fruit and lions. In his descriptions of the building in 1866, Reiffenstein praised the design in its entirety. He called the latticework over the doors a “masterpiece of the locksmith’s art” – an artisan craft which, apart from locks, made fine ironwork, fittings and other ornamental items.

In 1843, the city acquired the house so it could expand its city hall. The building was severely damaged in the Second World War. In 1951/52, a simplified new building was constructed over the old ground floor. A mosaic of the phoenix rising from the ashes recalls the reconstruction work in the post-war years.
