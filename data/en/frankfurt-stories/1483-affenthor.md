# Affenthor

*Affentorplatz 1, 2*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/862999aafe2106a081a1382330ea887f_original.png)

Since the 14th century, Sachsenhausen was surrounded by ditches and a wall with towers. The Affentor Gate was the southernmost entrance to Frankfurt. Reiffenstein sketched the square tower over the gate where, acting as a look-out, a guard could raise the alarm in case of fire.

Along with the city’s other fortifications, the Affentor Gate was torn down in 1810. Taking an old print as a model, Reiffenstein sketched the state of the gate during the demolition work. In 1810/11, in place of the medieval gate, Johann Friedrich Christian Hess constructed two neo-classicist customs and guard buildings. For a short time, the site became known as the Aschaffenburg Gate, but by 1814 its old name had been reinstated.

Today, the two Affentor buildings still stand facing each other. Only in the course of the 19th century did this area around Frankfurt became a popular and busy urban district.
