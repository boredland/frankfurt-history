# Alter Judenkirchhof

*Battonnstraße 47*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/300b131c252f52176bc401871095c50e_original.png)

The Old Jewish Cemetery is the second oldest of its kind in Germany. In 1836, Reiffenstein described it as the “most picturesque place in the entire city”. During his visit, he sketched the old gravestones surrounded by tall grasses and shrubs. The gravestones testify to the use of the cemetery as early as the 13th century. In 1828, when Frankfurt’s Main Cemetery was opened, the Jewish community was allotted a new cemetery there. In 1939, under the Nazi regime, the Jewish community was forced to sell the Old Cemetery to the city of Frankfurt. In early 1943, thousands of the gravestones there were destroyed. After the war, the restoration work on the cemetery was not completed until the end of the 1950s. Today’s Museum Judengasse borders the Old Cemetery, which can be viewed during a visit to the museum.
