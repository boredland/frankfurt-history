# Junghof

*Junghofstraße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/5a2cfe5c18dd49bf738bbc1e32be2789_original.png)

The Junghof courtyard is first mentioned in written records in 1361. As a typical urban courtyard, it comprised a range of buildings. The complex was separated by walls from the surrounding gardens, smallholdings and yards. From 1756, the west side of the courtyard was home to a small theatre where the young Johann Wolfgang Goethe (1749-1832) discovered his initial enthusiasm for the stage. As a trained theatre scene painter, Reiffenstein shared his passion.

From 1860, the Junghof was cleared for a new connecting road between the Rossmarkt and the Neue Mainzer Strasse. The simple old theatre was demolished. Its place was taken by the Saalbau, a large concert hall and ballroom. The modern architecture along Junghofstrasse illustrates the 19th-century shift from the old Frankfurt to the new, a change still evident in the high rises constructed today.

*Author: Carina Koch*
