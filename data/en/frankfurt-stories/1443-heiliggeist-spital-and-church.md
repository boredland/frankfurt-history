# Heiliggeist-Spital and church

*Saalgasse 19*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, old Heiliggeist-Spital, view of the courtyard, 1859, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0690](../../images/7c0884c44e8f9fd3770cd6bba28d798d_original.jpg)
*1859 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, old Heiliggeist-Spital, view of the courtyard, 1859, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0690*

The Hospital zum Heiligen Geist – Hospital of the Holy Spirit – was set on the riverbank to the east of the Holy Spirit Gate. Founded in the 13th century, this charitable hospice and infirmary was dedicated to the care of the poor and travellers in need. A church and the hospital hall were added in the 1460s. For these new buildings, prosperous Frankfurt families donated keystones decorated with their coats of arms, later sketched by Reiffenstein.

By the early 1800s, the original hospital no longer met the hygienic and medical standards of the day. As a result, work began in 1835 on a new hospital in Langestrasse. Despite protests, the city sold the old hospital to be demolished. Nonetheless, city architect Johann Friedrich Christian Hess (1785-1845) managed to save such art treasures as graveslabs, keystones from vaulted ceilings, and sculptures for the municipal collections (today’s Historical Museum).
