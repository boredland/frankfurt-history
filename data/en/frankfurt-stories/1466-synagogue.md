# Synagogue

*Kurt-Schumacher-Straße 43*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/7577b6802a73e2e756ea5194d5eae9ac_original.png)

Until 1603, Frankfurt had just one synagogue as a place of worship. It was built in 1461, when Frankfurt’s Jews were required to move into the newly-established Judengasse to the east of the city.

In 1874, Reiffenstein gave an account of the fire that destroyed a large part of the Judengasse in 1711. Although the main synagogue was built of stone, it also burnt to the ground. That same year a new building was constructed on the old foundations. The synagogue's roof timbering was set on fire during the city’s bombardment by French troops in 1796. The rebuild was demolished in 1854 and replaced the following year by a third synagogue.

This new main synagogue, designed by architect Johann Georg Kayser (1817-1875), was destroyed in the November Pogroms in 1938. Today, it is commemorated by a memorial plaque on the façade of Kurt-Schumacher-Strasse 41.
