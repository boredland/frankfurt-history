# Alte Brückenmühle

*Alte Brücke 2*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/20e8a21211c356481e6bdd332f8a237e_original.png)

In 1411/12, the Bridge Mill was built on the eastern side of Frankfurt’s Alte Brücke – Old Bridge. The mill was damaged numerous times by drifting ice, fires and during military action in wars. It was already rebuilt in 1417 after a fire.

When Swedish General Hans Vitzthum von Eckstädt took Sachsenhausen and the Bridge Mill in 1635, the mill caught fire and burnt down. Later, it was rebuilt again and a new mill constructed on the west side of the bridge. Reiffenstein sketched both these mills. In 1738, a new mill replaced the old one. In 1813, the canons of French troops left the gateway to the mill badly damaged.

The old Bridge Mill was ultimately demolished in 1852. The architecture of the 2006 Portikus exhibition hall on the last remaining Main Island recalls the old bridge mills.
