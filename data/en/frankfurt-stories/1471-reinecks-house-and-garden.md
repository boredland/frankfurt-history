# Reineck's house and garden

*Hasengasse 6*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/ec6bd175db21ce0148f524a04d06b675_original.png)

In 1719, a fire raged in the two narrows lanes of Hasengasse and Töngesgasse. The vaulted cellar of the 15th-century Reineck House survived, and was later sketched by Reiffenstein.

In 1730 the prosperous wine merchant Friedrich Ludwig von Reineck (1707-1775) had a new building constructed on the site of the old main house. From his belvedere on the roof, he had a view out across the entire town.

In 1822, the house and grounds passed to the city. From 1830, it housed the city Court of Justice. It was then used as a barracks from 1848. In the meantime, the garden, which lay in the old city moat, had run wild. Reiffenstein recalled how it was “a wonderful playground for us when we were children”. He reported on the demolition of the house and grounds from 1870 to 1879. Today, the site is home to shops and stores as well as Carl-Theodor-Reiffenstein-Platz, the square named after him.

*Author: Carina Koch*
