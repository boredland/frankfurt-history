# Klehn mosquito net factory

*Speicherstraße 12*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

![](../../images/a976fdf93c68a2176f3fdcaaf21b5808_original.jpg)

The establishment of German colonies in Africa and Oceania was not only hoped for by large companies, but also by small and medium-sized enterprises. Advertisements in colonial journals show that the Frankfurt entrepreneur J. Klehn ran a ‘mosquito net factory’ from 1896. At that time, there were hardly any treatment options for many tropical diseases; protection against mosquitoes and tsetse flies was crucial to avoid infection. According to Klehn's promise, settlers, administrative officials and travellers in German colonies and other tropical regions could have mosquito nets of various sizes and qualities sent to them from Frankfurt by post. This mail-order business was made possible by scheduled steamships, such as those operated by the shipowner Adolph Woermann between Hamburg and the German colonies.
