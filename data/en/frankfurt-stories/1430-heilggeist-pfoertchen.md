# Heilggeist-Pförtchen

*Saalgasse / Am Geistpförtchen*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Heiliggeist-Pförtchen from the outside (reconstruction after a drawing by Reiß), 1854, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0030](../../images/4aee1686d54eee26d440750bc4b4e982_original.jpg)
*1854 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Heiliggeist-Pförtchen from the outside (reconstruction after a drawing by Reiß), 1854, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0030*

The Holy Spirit Gate was the main entrance to the Heiliggeist-Spital – the Hospital of the Holy Spirit. It was constructed in 1454 in the existing city wall along the northern bank of the River Main. A series of small gates or archways between the Mainz Gate to the west and the Alte Brücke (Old Bridge) to the east provided access to the town: these included Leonard’s Gate next to St. Leonard’s Church, the Wooden Gate, Fahrtor Gate, Holy Spirit Gate, Butcher’s Gate at the Slaughter House, and Fisher Gate at the Old Bridge. Since these were all directly next to the river, they were often threatened by high water. Reiffenstein sketched all these gates, most of which survived into the 1840s. Since the Holy Spirit Gate was sold in 1797 and the site developed, Reiffenstein based his sketch on older depictions of the gate.
