# Haus zum Wedel

*Neue Kräme 1 / Braubachstraße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/01e6a1fb33f83eead3022ec1c1d9dee5_original.png)

The Haus zum Wedel was set on the very narrow Wedelgasse lane at the Römer (City Hall). In 1842, the grammar school in the former Franciscan monastery cloister on Paulsplatz square was torn down. This revealed the houses in the rear yard of the Haus zum Wedel. Reiffenstein sketched the buildings in a view from Paulsplatz square.

The Haus zum Wedel actually comprised three houses, acquired one after the other by the owners. The interiors were connected into a labyrinth of rooms, corridors and stairs epitomising the entangled, winding structure of houses in the Old Town.

When the house was demolished in 1866, the entrance to the narrow Wedelgasse lane was widened and modern houses were erected. In 1904, the lane disappeared entirely when the new access road of Brauchbachstrasse was constructed. Today, the former site of the Haus am Wedel is part of Paulplatz square.

*Author: Carina Koch*
