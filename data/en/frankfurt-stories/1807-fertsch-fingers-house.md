# Fertsch-Finger's House

*Paulsplatz near the Bridge of Sighs (Seufzerbrücke)*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

In the Fertsch-Finger House, which stood on Paulsplatz until 1900, a group of anti-slavery activists led by the lawyer Friedrich Wilhelm Carové organised the largest meeting against slavery and the slave trade in Frankfurt's history on 29 August 1850. The event, which was advertised through adverts, attracted a mass crowd; many interested people had to be turned away due to overcrowding. In addition to local activists, two freed slaves from the United States, James W. C. Pennington and Henry Highland Garnet, spoke. Pennington spoke about slavery-related legislation and Garnet about the continued oppression of free blacks in the United States. A group of women announced the formation of a committee to promote the renunciation of products of enslaved labour in Frankfurt. Pennington and Garnet welcomed this move and spoke favourably of the interest shown by the people of Frankfort in the fate of slaves. The meeting was to remain the greatest success in attracting the attention of the anti-slavery campaigners in Frankfurt. After Carové's death in 1852, the group appeared less frequently, probably also because state repression against liberal movements increased in the 1850s.
