# Goethe's birthplace

*Großer Hirschgraben 23*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/8660ca1231282b97dd531e02bc8beee9_original.png)

In his autobiography Dichtung und Wahrheit (“Poetry and Truth”), the writer Johann Wolfgang Goethe (1749-1832) describes the Frankfurt of his youth. In 1875, Reiffenstein published illustrations to Goethe’s book.

In 1733, Goethe’s grandmother had acquired two sixteenth-century houses. Just over twenty years later in 1755/56, they were converted into a large, spacious house. Reiffenstein sketched it before and after the conversion, showing the inner courtyard, the interiors and the view of the city walls from the window.

Before the conversion, the ground floor windows were fitted with the wooden lattices or grilles typical in Frankfurt, an intermediary between life inside and outside. As Goethe recalled in his autobiography: “The women sat behind [them] to sew and knit; the cook picked her salad there; female neighbours chatted with each other...”.

Today, Frankfurt’s Goethe House is a museum.
