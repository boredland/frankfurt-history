# Oeder Weg

*Frankfurt places of mobility*

![Unknown, Oeder Weg in summer 2022, Stefan Lüddecke, Mobilitätsdezernat Frankfurt](../../images/d1474160e30adaf68309c0e72f312254_original.png)
*2022 — License: Stefan Lüddecke, Mobilitätsdezernat Frankfurt | Description: Unknown, Oeder Weg in summer 2022, Stefan Lüddecke, Mobilitätsdezernat Frankfurt*

Despite the large number of cyclists and pedestrians, roads are often designed exclusively for the needs of car traffic. Poor visibility, high accident rates among cyclists, noise, and poor quality of life were the arguments used by the “Radentscheid” initiative to make concrete proposals for the conversion of roads from 2017 onwards. They collected 40,000 signatures for the petition. In 2019, the main demands were incorporated into a package of measures. Since 2021, Oeder Weg has also been redesigned to be “bicycle-friendly.” It is one of the most important north-south connections. The road is heavily trafficked, and at the same time, part of the road is also an important shopping street in the Nordend district.

*Author: Nina Gorgus, Sarah Roller*

The “bike-friendly side streets” include red markings at intersections, delivery and loading zones, restrictions on long-term parking, the expansion of public space through sidewalk extensions, greenery, and parking spaces for bicycles and e-scooters. The aim is to create a continuous network of bicycle paths that connects Frankfurt's districts and makes cycling smoother and safer.

*Author: Nina Gorgus, Sarah Roller*

Similar to the redesign of the Zeil into a pedestrian zone – almost 50 years earlier – not all Frankfurt residents agreed with the plan. Here, too, there were fears of traffic chaos and declining revenues for businesses. However, this redesign fits in with the premises of modern urban planning. Above all, holistic and sustainable ideas that focus on urban quality of life for everyone are in demand. This includes the redistribution of existing (road) space and equal rights for alternative means of transport to cars.

*Author: Nina Gorgus, Sarah Roller*
