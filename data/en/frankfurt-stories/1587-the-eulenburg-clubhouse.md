# The Eulenburg - Clubhouse

*Eulengasse 46*

125 years of FSV (1899-2024)

![](../../images/08d51e20add722686168eb0737eb6b18_original.png)

You are now standing in front of the Eulenburg, at least in front of where the well-known pub once stood. But the Eulenburg existed long before the FSV. It already existed when Bornheim was still its own village (until it was incorporated in 1877) and still had many green spaces.

The Eulenburg originally had its own fruit growing areas. At some point, the family began to press their own cider and serve it in the pub. This was quite common at the time. The pub business grew, the farm became smaller and in the end the Eulenburg was able to cater for 200 guests, many of whom were FSV fans. The owners of the pub had close ties to FSV and there was an FSV regulars' table in the Eulenburg for decades.

Officials and fans sat together here, drinking home-made cider and later also enjoying a snack. Eating in the pub was not so common in the early 1950s. Unfortunately, the regulars‘ table disappeared at some point due to age, until a younger generation came together in the early 2000s to form a new regulars’ table, which revitalised the wonderful connection between the Eulenburg and FSV Frankfurt. Legendary parties were celebrated there, FSV match scenes were re-enacted using sweets and songs were composed:

First round Eulenburg - second round pub -
today it's really going off in the Schöneberger -
maybe in the Spitze Eck - maybe in the Adler -
maybe the White Lily - a week's hangover -
European Cup, European Cup, European Cup, European Cup

[Original lyrics:

vielleicht die Weiße Lilie – eine Woche Kater –
Europapokal, Europapokal, Europapokal, Europapokal]
