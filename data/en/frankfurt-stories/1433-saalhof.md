# Saalhof

*Saalgasse 31,33*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Saalhof, wood-carved gable facing Saalgasse, 1863, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0669](../../images/95d43180f931d768afce62e66167cfe6_original.jpg)
*1863 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Saalhof, wood-carved gable facing Saalgasse, 1863, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0669*

With buildings from the 1100s to the 1800s, the Saalhof courtyard is the oldest structure in Frankfurt’s Old Town. Down the centuries, the Saalhof performed a range of functions – a Staufer royal palace, a customs office, accommodation for Frankfurt’s trade fairs, the residence of the Bernus and Leerse families, a painting gallery and a music school.

From 1841, architect Rudolf Burnitz (1788-1849) replaced parts of the medieval structures. At the same time, a new customs house was built to the west. The Rententurm – the Toll Tower – , the customs house, the Bernus and Burnitz buildings as well as part of the palace could be restored after the Second World War.

Today, these buildings house the Historical Museum. The collections include some objects Reiffenstein managed to save from Frankfurt’s Old Town before it was largely demolished in the 19th century.

*Author: Carina Koch*
