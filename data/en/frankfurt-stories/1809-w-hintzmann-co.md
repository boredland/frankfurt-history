# W. Hintzmann & Co.

*Stiftstraße 29–33*

Frankfurt and its colonial history

The Frankfurt merchant Wilhelm Hintzmann worked from 1881 as a commission agent for the German Africa Society and the German East African Society in Zanzibar. From the late 1890s, he ran his own trading company there, W. Hintzmann & Co., and opened branches in German East Africa and the then British-controlled Kenya. From the company headquarters in Stiftstraße, Hintzmann managed the import and export of textiles, cement, wire and rubber, among other things, and arranged deals for Indian merchants in East Africa. In 1912, Hintzmann's successors converted the company into a limited liability company under the new name Africana Handels-Gesellschaft, which continued business in East Africa until the outbreak of the First World War.
