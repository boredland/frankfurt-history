# Römer

*Römerberg 21*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![<p>Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Römer, door with iron grille on the Kaisertreppe (today the staircase to the wedding hall), 1865, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0542</p>](../../images/733383c0929831120355fea2e990437e_original.jpg)
*1865 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: <p>Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Römer, door with iron grille on the Kaisertreppe (today the staircase to the wedding hall), 1865, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R0542</p>*

The Römer has been Frankfurt’s city hall since the 15th century. The Haus zum Römer, the middle house of the Römer complex, dates from before 1322. It was acquired by the city of Frankfurt as the city hall together with, down the centuries, ten other residential buildings.

From 1356, Frankfurt was the town where kings and emperors of the Holy Roman Empire were elected. The seven electors met in the Electoral Chamber on the first floor of the Römer. From 1612 on, the adjacent Kaisersaal – the Emperors’ Hall – was used for a festive banquet after an imperial coronation. Reiffenstein sketched the staircase “the emperor would take to reach the Kaisersaal after his coronation”. The artist was also a member of the Römerbau Committee which worked to preserve the historical buildings.

After the Second World War, only the stone façades and ground floors of the medieval houses were still standing. Today, the building behind those façades dates from the 1950s. The Emperors’ Hall with its 19th-century portraits of the emperors has been reconstructed.
