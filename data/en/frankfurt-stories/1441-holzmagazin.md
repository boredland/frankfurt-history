# Holzmagazin

*Seehofstraße / Deutschherrnufer*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, wood depot in Sachsenhausen, tower no. 4, 1877, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF RS0201](../../images/fc04ca22aa4395be92222688b4448854_original.jpg)
*1877 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, wood depot in Sachsenhausen, tower no. 4, 1877, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF RS0201*

One of the town’s municipal timber yards was in Sachsenhausen on the southern bank of the river. It lay inside the city walls. To the north, it bordered the timber yard of the Commissioner of Woods and Forests. Previously surrounded by bleachfields, this area is now where three streets meet – Deutschherrnufer, Seehofstrasse and Wasserweg.

Although the old Sachsenhausen city wall had five towers, only the east tower was on this site. In 1849, Reiffenstein sketched the tower with its upper storey and roof, which was taken down in 1870. Seven years later, the entire tower was demolished.

Reiffenstein also sketched the city wall’s easternmost point, the round tower from the mid-15th century set between the two timber yards. With arrow slits and walls two metres thick, the fortified round tower seemed immensely strong.

*Author: Carina Koch*
