# Bleichgärten at Bleichstraße

*Katzenpforte*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, bleach houses and gardens at the Senckenbergischen Stiftung, corner at Bleichstraße and Radgasse, 1857, aquarelle, HMF C51047](../../images/b95a4e746772305db5b7b4884bb8a8f5_original.jpg)
*1857 — Author: Carl Theodort Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, bleach houses and gardens at the Senckenbergischen Stiftung, corner at Bleichstraße and Radgasse, 1857, aquarelle, HMF C51047*

The vast majority of the inner-city green spaces were bleachfields. The town map of 1811 marks the vast areas inside and outside the city required for bleaching cloth or fabrics. These areas were found to the east of the town, south along the Sachsenhausen riverbank, and on the northern edge of the city walls. In particular, Reiffenstein documented the northern bleachfields and houses at the old Senckenberg Foundation. The lyrical mood of these green areas, little used except for bleaching, attracted a number of artists.

In 1852, the Senckenberg Foundation acquired the adjacent plot on Radgasse lane. The bleach houses there were then demolished in 1866 and, five years later, the new Bürgerhospital built on their site. Today, the bleaching of textiles is only recalled in the name Bleichstrasse – literally “Bleach Street”.
