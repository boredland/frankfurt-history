# Haus Stadt Ulm

*Schäfergasse 9*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/0ec5596f64441d2c16816ab1320fcb6c_original.png)

We only know the Stadt Ulm house from Reiffenstein’s depictions. This complex of buildings lay to the north of the Zeil, near the Konstablerwache guard house. It stretched between Schäfergasse lane and the rear houses on Brönnerstraße 4-6 (formerly Hinter der Rose 7-8). The complex included a yard, stables and accommodation for carters and carriers bringing goods into the city. According to Reiffenstein, the railways ushered in the end of this means of transporting goods. The railways, though, not only replaced the traditional carrying trades, they also changed the Old Town’s urban and social structure.

The Stadt Ulm house was converted in 1915 and ultimately destroyed in the Second World War. Today, the site is occupied by a former department store.
