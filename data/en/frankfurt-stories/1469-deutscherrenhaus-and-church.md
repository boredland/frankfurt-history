# Deutscherrenhaus and church

*Brückenstraße 3, 5, 7*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/7efd14b87a31ff700d0aa7a9bc07e10b_original.png)

Around 1182, Kuno von Münzenberg (died 1207) founded a hospital and almshouse here. In 1221, this passed to the Order of Brothers of the German House of Saint Mary in Jerusalem, known as the Teutonic Order. The duties of the brothers of the Order not only included caring for the sick, but also prayer, divine services and pastoral care.

The original design of today’s church was finished in 1309. From the 14th to the 19th century, the monastery building known as the Deutschherrenhaus became a political meeting point for emperors, kings and princes.

In the 18th century, a new Deutschherrenhaus replaced the old one, the church was renovated in the baroque style, and the old portal set in front of a new entrance hall. In 1878, Reiffenstein criticised additional conversions and alterations; “there is no telling where this destruction of things from bygone eras will end”.

The building was damaged in the war and reconstruction work started in 1963. Today, it houses, among other things, the Icon Museum.
