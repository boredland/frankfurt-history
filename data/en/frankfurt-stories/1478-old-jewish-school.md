# Old Jewish school

*Today around Große Fischerstraße 28*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![](../../images/a7b7d50c26f7c2e835d7b623813755a1_original.png)

The old Jewish School was located in Frankfurt’s oldest Jewish quarter between the Cathedral and the River Main. In the pogroms in 1241 and 1349, the Jews living in Frankfurt were attacked and killed. The community’s synagogue was also damaged.

In 1462, Frankfurt’s Jews had to move into a new Jewish quarter on the eastern edge of the town. The city then took over the old synagogue. As a sign it was now the city’s property, the Frankfurt coat of arms with its imperial eagle was painted on the building’s façade. In his sketch, Reiffenstein reconstructed the badly damaged mural. He shows the eagle of Frankfurt’s arms flanked by two smaller coats of arms with imperial eagles.

In 1571, the old Jewish School was converted into a weighing house for copper. In 1873, before the building was demolished, Reiffenstein noted that the stone house is “totally in ruins with nothing left standing.”
