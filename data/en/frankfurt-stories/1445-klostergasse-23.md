# Klostergasse 23

*Battonnstraße*

Reiffensteins Frankfurt<br />(1820-1893)

![Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Klostergasse 23, back houses, opening of the Battonnstraße, 1884, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R1625](../../images/d43909d542d310e304d1a362af8a741d_original.jpg)
*1884 — Author: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein | License: HMF | Description: Carl Theodor Reiffenstein, Klostergasse 23, back houses, opening of the Battonnstraße, 1884, watercolored pencil drawing, HMF R1625*

The Klostergasse lane was east of Fahrgasse, and ran parallel to it. Starting in the Arnsburger Hof courtyard to the south, much of Klostergasse bordered the west side of the Dominican monastery. To the north, it led to the dead end of An der roten Badstube, which branched off the Fahrgasse. In the 1880s, this little lane was cut through when Battonnstrasse became the east-west extension to Schnurgasse, a project which involved tearing down a series of backyard houses. The demolition work left their yards visible for the general public, for instance, at the houses at Ellenbogengässchen 14 (left) and Klostergasse 23 (right), which Reiffenstein captured in a sketch. In 1884, he noted that “soon every trace [ of the Old Town] will be lost”.
