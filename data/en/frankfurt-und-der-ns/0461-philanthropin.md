# Philanthropin

*Hebelstraße 15-19*

![Photo of the "Philanthropin"](../../images/412da0376c365dbf16121e99ef7370da_original.jpg)
*ca. 1935 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-19667 | Description: Photo of the "Philanthropin"*

The so-called Philanthropin was located at Hebelstrasse 15–19 from 1908 onwards. The Jewish community had founded the school in 1804, and it would become one of the most well-known Jewish secondary schools in Germany. It was built by Georg Matzdorff and the engineer Ernst Hiller according to the most modern standards of the day. Boys and girls were taught separately, as is reflected in the architecture by the two entrances. In the 1920s the student body comprised 900 children and adolescents, both Jews and non-Jews. Under National Socialism, the circumstances of the school and those who attended it worsened. Starting in 1933, Jewish children and adolescents were forced to transfer there. At Frankfurt’s other schools they were subjected to anti-Jewish harassment by teachers and fellow pupils. By the end of 1938, it was the only school in Frankfurt Jewish pupils were allowed to attend. The Philanthropin existed until 1942; then the building was used as a reserve military hospital and emergency shelter until the end of the war. The building has served its original purpose again since 2006. Now it houses the Issac E. Lichtigfeld School.
