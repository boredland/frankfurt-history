# Stolen Place

*Kaiserstraße 37*

![Kaiserstraße 37](../../images/15232db3b15fa653f85b5d09f1b69695_original.jpg)
*before 1944 — Author: Postkarte | License: HMF | Description: Kaiserstraße 37*

In the rear building at Kaiserstrasse 37 was the temple of the Masonic Lodge "Zur Einigkeit", founded in 1742, in a purpose-built commercial building. The complex was built between 1894 and 1896 according to plans by architect Oskar Sommer. The Freemasons, who were persecuted and banned by the National Socialists, were forced to dissolve their lodge in 1935. The "General Masonic Museum of the Lodge of Unity" set up there was plundered by the National Socialists. The property was confiscated. The Gestapo deported some freemasons to concentration camps.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
