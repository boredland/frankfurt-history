# Naphtali Fromm

*Liebigstraße 27*

Journey to the past: Jewish life in Westend 100 years ago

![Liebigstreet 27 (today): Home of Naphtali Fromm](../../images/73249f351f0e0bb0cda3aa87a99042db_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Daniel Fricke | License: Evangelisch reformierte Gemeinde | Description: Liebigstreet 27 (today): Home of Naphtali Fromm*

Naphtali Fromm and his family lived here on the second floor. He was head of the synagogue at Unterlindau 23 for a long time.

Naphtali Fromm was born in Homburg in 1869, the son of Seligmann Pinchas Fromm. His ancestors were all rabbis. He decided to become a fruit wine manufacturer. He came to Chicago in 1893 to work on the jury of a fruit wine competition. In 1899 he married Rosa Krause, whose father was also a rabbi. A year later, their son Erich Fromm was born, who later became very famous.
Naphtali Fromm was always very committed to his religion. In 1919, he co-founded the Hermann Cohen Lodge, which was socially committed. He was president of the lodge from 1924 to 1925. He died on 29 December 1933 in Alsace. He only lived to see the beginnings of the Nazi dictatorship. His wife Rosa emigrated to New York in 1936. Naphtali Fromm himself did not become a rabbi, as his family would certainly have liked, but he was committed to the community and to working together. Now let's go to the Westend Synagogue
