# Jakob Horovitz

*Staufenstraße 33*

Journey to the past: Jewish life in Westend 100 years ago

![Jakob Horovitz](../../images/542ecb839e1cb6101f060a9d019b2463_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Daniel Fricke | License: ISG FFM S7P Nr. 6890 | Description: Jakob Horovitz*

Rabbi Jakob Horovitz lived in the house behind us around 90 years ago. Unfortunately, this is no longer the original house in which he lived, as many houses were destroyed during the war.

Jakob Horovitz was the rabbi of the synagogue at Unterlindau 23. What is special is that before him, his father Markus Horovitz was the first rabbi of the synagogue in Unterlindau. His son Jakob Horovitz continued the family tradition. When he became rabbi of the community in 1902, he was 29 years old. At that time, he already had a doctorate and had studied philosophy and oriental languages alongside his rabbinical training. As a scholar, he researched the story of Joseph and published his father's works. He not only wanted to preach about the good, but also to do the good. Anyone who needed help could come to him, and Jakob Horovitz tried to find a solution for everyone. He represented Jewish interests in the municipal school administration and ran the “Israelite Religious School” in Frankfurt. He also became deputy chairman of the General German Rabbinical Association and was involved in many Jewish organizations.

The memory of him and his family remains, as next year (2025) stumbling stones will be laid here for him and his family members.

*Author: Annika, Naemi, Paula, Clara*
