# Jewish training workshop

*Fischerfeldstraße 13*

![Photo of a Jewish training workshop](../../images/C27897_jpg_1700195052f811de780f0d25be6fb4a8_original.jpg)
*1932 — Author: Leonhard Kleemann | License: HMF C27897 | Description: Photo of a Jewish training workshop*

The “Jüdische Anlernwerkstätte”, an independent vocational school at Fischerfeldstrasse 13, enabled young people to learn a trade. Founded in 1933 by the Jewish Advisory Office for Economic Aid (“Jüdische Beratungsstelle für Wirtschaftshilfe”), the school also provided accommodation nearby to allow trainees from outside Frankfurt to attend as boarders. The primary objective was to prepare Jewish youth for emigration, since young people with a trade had better chances of obtaining a visa. In spring 1941, for example, the school trained 71 metal workers, a tinsmith, seven mechanics, 38 carpenters, nine shoemakers and 22 gardeners. There was also a course in book printing. In addition, the Advisory Office offered language courses in English, French, Spanish and Modern Hebrew.
