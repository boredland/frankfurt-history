TSV Schild Frankfurt became the largest club in the Schild Association. Many well-known athletes and well-educated trainers, sports medics and officials who had been expelled from their old clubs now became members. Schild was able to rent the sports field at the Buchrainweiher from the Jewish company Fredenhagen. Therefore, he did not have to constantly renegotiate with the Nazi authorities. An aquatic sports department was established next to the Licht- und Luftbad Niederrad. The many divisions of the club presented themselves at large sports events at the Buchrainweiher. The 1936 celebration is documented in cigarette pictures from the Jewish company Teneriffa-Zigaretten Frankfurt. From 1937 onwards, the purpose of sport was increasingly seen as physical and mental preparation for emigration. The last event was the autumn sports festival of the TSV Schild at the Buchrainweiher in October 1938. With more than 1,000 spectators, the sports festival was a great expression of the solidarity and unity of all Jewish athletes in Frankfurt.

*Author: Helga Roos, Sportkreis Frankfurt e.V.*
