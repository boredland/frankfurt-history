# Adlerwerke Memorial Site: Factory, forced labor, concentration camp

*Kleyerstreet 17*

![Lettering of the historical site Adlerwerke on the facade of the old factory building.](../../images/621f4f946c021519b587631bf9fb55e0_original.jpg)
*2023 — Author: Thomas Altmeyer | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Lettering of the historical site Adlerwerke on the facade of the old factory building.*

The “Adlerwerke Memorial Site: Factory, Forced Labor, Concentration Camp” opened in the former Adlerwerke in March 2022. This place of commemoration and education is dedicated to remembering the “Katzbach” concentration camp that existed on the Adlerwerke premises from August 1944 to March 1945 and the 1,616 inmates forced to work here. Approximately one third of the inmates perished in Frankfurt, others on the “death march” from Frankfurt to Hünfeld or in other concentration camps in the final days of the war.

The Adlerwerke Memorial Site documents and communicates the history of forced labor in Frankfurt. At the same time, it shows how the Nazi regime systematically robbed people of their rights, humiliated, and exploited them. This look at the divestiture of basic rights is intended to heighten visitors’ awareness of the present-day threats to democracy and human rights. The Historical Site focuses on a key question: How can the diverse society of today come to terms with the history of the German crimes of violence? The subject of labor is especially well-suited to this critical examination because it provides clear insights into the consequences of the abuse of power and the exploitation of individuals.
