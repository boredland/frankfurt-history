# Bomb shelter

*Grusonstraße 4*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Grusonstraße](../../images/5cb73548d169fc137e4814880c179a43_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Grusonstraße*

This was once the site of a bomb shelter that offered protection to train passengers travelling by way of the East station. It was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. It provided space for 1,000 persons on five floors. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using the shelters. A staircase led to this facility directly from the train platform. Construction work lasted from June 1941 to July 1942. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office lodged transients, refugees, and discharged members of the Wehrmacht (the German army during World War II) here. During the Cold War, the shelter was altered to offer protection from nuclear, biological, and chemical weapons. It was torn down in 2010.

Construction company: Ambrosius und Sohn
 Architect: unknown
