# Freiherrliche Carl von Rothschildsche public library

*Untermainkai 15*

![Photo of the Freiherrliche Carl von Rothschildsche public library](../../images/C12852a_jp_7f9581b15500b1d61192abbcc641e9a9_original.jpg)
*ca. 1890 — Author: Kühl & Co. | License: HMF C12852a | Description: Photo of the Freiherrliche Carl von Rothschildsche public library*

The Freiherrlich Carl von Rothschild’sche Public Library was located at this site. Today the building houses part of Frankfurt’s Jewish Museum. Baron Mayer Carl von Rothschild had purchased it in 1846 and commissioned the architect Friedrich Rumpf to design major alterations. The stairway and three prestigious salons, which are open for viewing in the museum, date from that period. The library moved into the building in 1895, having been founded by Hannah Louise von Rothschild in 1887 in memory of her father Mayer Carl. With the help of generous donations from the Rothschild daughters in Vienna, London, and Paris, the neighbouring building, no. 14, was purchased in 1905 and the library enlarged. The City of Frankfurt took over the library in 1928 and appointed Joachim Kirchner as head librarian. He joined the National Socialist German Workers’ Party in February 1933. In April, Mayor Friedrich Krebs instructed him to “purge” the municipal school and teacher libraries as well as the public libraries. Among other things, that meant that this library could now lend out books considered “un-German literature” only for scientific purposes. Kirchner also pursued the aim of changing its name to Library for Modern Languages and Music. All indications that the library owed its existence to the Rothschild family were removed, and thus also deleted from the collective memory of the town. When the library was dismantled in 1945, the holdings of the Freiherrlich Carl von Rothschild’sche Public Library were transferred to the Municipal and University Library at Bockenheimer Warte, where they are still accessible today.
