# forced labour camp

*Münchener Straße 56*

![Picture postcard with the "Hansa Royal"](../../images/82b32804865c6f0e4ce25e455211bfa6_original.jpg)
*before 1950 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S17 Nr. 00535-21 | Description: Picture postcard with the "Hansa Royal"*

The Voigt & Haeffner company lodged forced labourers in the Hotel Hansa Royal at Kronprinzenstrasse 56 (now Münchener Strasse). Altogether 500 forced labourers had their accommodations in this hotel, but it is unclear how many of them worked for Voigt & Haeffner. The company was a traditional Frankfurt business that had been founded in 1885 and had its headquarters at Hanauer Landstrasse 142–172. In addition to manufacturing electrical switchgear and switching devices it was involved in the development of heavy current technology. According to the Institut für Stadtgeschichte (Institute of Municipal History) it made use of several hundred foreign and forced labours on its own company premises as well as at other locations in Frankfurt. On 10 August 1943, a representative of Voigt & Haeffner inspected the midday and evening meals served in the camp and came to the following assessment: “Both meals were … inedible and were … thrown out.”
