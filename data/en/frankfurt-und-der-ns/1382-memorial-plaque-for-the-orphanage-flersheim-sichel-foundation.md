# Memorial plaque for the orphanage Flersheim-Sichel Foundation

*Ebersheimstraße 5*

![Memorial plaque for the orphanage Flersheim-Sichel Foundation](../../images/f96979fe67a0a80d0d3093fa8451f492_original.jpg)
*Author: Till Lieberz-Gross | Description: Memorial plaque for the orphanage Flersheim-Sichel Foundation*

The house of the Flersheim-Sichel Foundation at Ebersheimstrasse 5 served as a Jewish orphanage for boys from 1930. In 1939, with the help of James Armand and Dorothy de Rothschild, most of the children and the managing couple Steinhardt and their two daughters were rescued on a children's transport to England. The house was reoccupied, but closed and Aryanized in 1941 (subsequently used by the City of Frankfurt as a children's home for non-Jewish children). After the closure, the remaining Jewish children were sent to their parents or distributed to other homes in Frankfurt. The majority of these children and the home's staff were deported and murdered from 1941 (more than 80 people in total), including the director Julius Flörsheim, his wife and one son as well as the governess Käthe Casparius (1941). One of the rescued children, Uri Sella, then Ulrich Stobiecka, urgently wanted a memorial to the murdered children of the Flersheim-Sichel Foundation to be erected in addition to the memorial to the rescued Kindertransport children on the corner of Kaiserstrasse and Gallusanlage (within sight of the main railway station). Local Advisory Council 9 organized the realization of this wish together with the Cultural Office of the City of Frankfurt. The memorial stone was dedicated on 25 September 2019 by the Head of the Department of Culture and Science, Dr. Ina Hartwig.
