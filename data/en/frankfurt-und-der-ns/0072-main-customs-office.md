# main customs office

*Domstraße 1-5*

![Photo of the main customs office building](../../images/82493a04a6d52bd8447338fbb5386182_original.jpg)
*1927 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-4044 | Description: Photo of the main customs office building*

The Customs Offices worked closely with the Foreign Exchange Offices (“Devisenstellen”) which ensured their instructions were implemented. From May 1938, anyone planning to emigrate had to submit an application to the Foreign Exchange Office to take furniture, household goods, crockery, clothing, bedding or personal items and mementoes with them. The Foreign Exchange Office required prospective emigrants to provide detailed lists of removals goods and baggage. Since the Customs Offices meticulously checked these lists to ensure they were correct, their officers had to examine the removal goods before they were packed. Under Section 37a of the Foreign Exchange Act (“Devisengesetz”) which allowed the seizure of assets if “capital flight” was suspected, the officials could also confiscate objects, securities and shares, and freeze bank accounts.
