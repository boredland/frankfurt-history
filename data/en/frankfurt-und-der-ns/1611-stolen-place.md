# Stolen Place

*Bettinaplatz 59*

![Bettinaplatz 59](../../images/ab44b70bad0cde9bc9b4e54cddb1a795_original.jpg)
*License: ISG | Description: Bettinaplatz 59*

The property at Bettinaplatz 59 originally belonged to the widow Hella Bernecker, née Rosenthal, widowed Weil. As she was already living in exile in Switzerland at the time the purchase contract was signed, she had herself represented by a trustee. She had Argentinian citizenship at the time. Due to persecution, she had to sell her detached house with a plot area of 733 square meters to a non-Jewish neighbor and his wife in a purchase agreement dated 4 July 1938. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 57,000 Reichsmark and the sale price was 50,000 Reichsmark. The building authority and NSDAP economic advisor did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich. The purchase price had to be paid into a blocked bank account controlled by the currency regulation authority (“Devisenstelle”), to which the seller had no direct access.
