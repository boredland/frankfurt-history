# Forced labour camp

*Gutleutstraße 320*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

This was the location of one of the Max Gerner company’s three plants in which it exploited forced labourers. Primarily Russian and French prisoners of war were forced to work here. In 1942 the aircraft manufacturer had a forced labour workforce of 349. By the following year, the number had risen to 671—493 Russian prisoners of war, 164 women from the Soviet Union, and 14 Italian men. After aerial attacks in 1943, production was relocated to Odenwald Forest.
