# Office of the resis­tance figh­ter Lore Wolf

*Neuhofstraße 18*

![Title page of one issue of the “Proletarisches Volks-Gericht” newpaper](../../images/16f3476da072e8f68504561d4d3e029c_original.jpg)
*License: HHStAW, Best. 461 Nr. 15199/1 | Description: Title page of one issue of the “Proletarisches Volks-Gericht” newpaper*

In the third floor of this building, Lore Wolf kept an office: a room she had rented for 25 Reichsmark (RM) in the flat of a die-hard National Socialist. She introduced herself as a businesswoman from Wiesbaden, who needed a room for typing urgent letters. As nobody considered it possible that a party member would let a room to a communist, the cover was perfect. Between early 1933 and August 1934, this is where she wrote newspaper pages and flyers on wax matrices for duplication. The writings with their call to resistance were spread via concealed distribution points throughout the city and reached thousands of people.

In August 1934 the group was betrayed and many members were arrested. Lore Wolf managed to flee to the neutral Saar area with the help of pastor Eckert and other activists.

*Author: Susanne Thurnay (Studienkreis Deutscher Widerstand 1933-1945 e.V.)*
