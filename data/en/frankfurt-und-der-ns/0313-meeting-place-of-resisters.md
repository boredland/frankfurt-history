# meeting place of resisters

*Alt Praunheim 11*

![Meeting place for resistance fighters who sit together at the table, Alt Praunheim 11](../../images/2022111615_3741bd92472aa8c6b0b4c7a251d80631_original.jpg)
*2022 — Author: Katahrina Müller | License: VG Bild-Kunst, Bonn 2022 | Description: Meeting place for resistance fighters who sit together at the table, Alt Praunheim 11*

Social Democrats and Communists gathered at the Concordia restaurant, which was also called Bender-Schuch. There they played skat and discussed politics. They presumably also talked about news they had heard on foreign radio stations. They did not plan resistance operations. The Communists collected donations for the families of imprisoned fellow Communists. A Gestapo informer observed their evening skat gatherings. On 22 June 1942, the Gestapo raided the restaurant and arrested Albrecht Ege. On 18 November of that year, Ege was sentenced to death by the Kassel Higher Regional Court for preparing to overthrow the government, subversion of the national defence forces, and listening to “enemy” radio stations. The only evidence brought against him was that supplied by the Gestapo informer. The Preungesheim prison carried out the death sentence in January 1943. Two Communists were also brought before court and sentenced to long prison terms solely because of their participation in the skat gatherings. This case is an example of the Nazi regime’s brutal treatment of its political opponents.
 
 A Stolperstein for Albrecht Ege is found at Am Treutengraben 3 in Praunheim.
