# Stolen Place

*Bornheimer Landstraße 21*

![Bornheimer Landstraße 21](../../images/14aba1129f4c5b79c0a7955cd329dd83_original.jpg)
*2021 — Author: Martin Starl | License: ISG | Description: Bornheimer Landstraße 21*

The property at Bornheimer Landstraße 21, with a plot size of 332 square meters, originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Hermann Katz, who lived at Weberstraße 5. Hermann Katz had only acquired the house himself on October 30, 1936. Due to persecution, he was forced to sell the property to a non-Jewish buyer from Saarbrücken for 39,250 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated October 14, 1938. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 43,900 Reichsmark and the market value to 42,400 Reichsmark. The notary justified the difference to the purchase price with the condition of the property. The rental income amounted to 642 Reichsmark per month. The parties agreed to hand over the property on November 1, 1938, with the buyer and seller sharing the contract and brokerage costs as well as the real estate transfer tax. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy in favor of the Reich.
