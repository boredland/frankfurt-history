# Israelite orpha­nage

*Röderbergweg 87*

![Foto: Landesbildstelle, undatiert, HMF Ph06318](../../images/Ph06318_jp_2c66c1738c744ceac9dac43aee8631d4_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: unbekannt | License: Landesbildstelle Hessen | Description: Foto: Landesbildstelle, undatiert, HMF Ph06318*

This was the location of an orphanage and synagogue that had been endowed by Baroness Edmond de Rothschild. It was officially independent, but nevertheless counted among the welfare institutions of the Jewish Religious Association. The orphanage had been founded in 1876 and based at Röderbergweg 87 since 1903. Rosa and Isidor Marx had been the directors since 1918. More than 100 children lived here along with the orphanage staff. In November 1938, the SA (Storm Troopers) raided the home and brutally laid it to waste. All of the orphans over sixteen years of age were arrested and taken to the fair hall, then deported to Buchenwald and Dachau. Rosa and Isidor Marx set to work organizing the emigration of many of the children to the U.K. From 1940 onwards, the orphanage also housed a soup kitchen and a clothing depot which provided impoverished Jewish families with sustenance and clothing. As many as 500 meals were served here per day.
