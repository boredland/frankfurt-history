# Stolen Place

*Falkensteiner Straße 53*

![Falkensteiner Straße 53](../../images/b17e7726cb9015ffa5837c16d0709931_original.jpg)
*1983 — Author: Jutta Hofmann | License: ISG | Description: Falkensteiner Straße 53*

The three-storey detached house at Falkensteiner Straße 53 and a neighboring undeveloped plot at number 55 originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Adolf Neumann, who also lived there. He had the house with seven rooms, two bathrooms, a balcony and conservatory built around 1922. The land, which was laid out as an ornamental garden, covered a total of 581 square meters. His non-Jewish wife had been living separately from him in Friedrichsdorf since February 1935. Due to persecution, he was forced to sell his home to a non-Jewish Frankfurt lawyer and his wife for 28,500 Reichsmark in a contract dated December 27, 1938. The officially tax-assessed value of both properties together amounted to 35,000 Reichsmark and the market value to 29,000 Reichsmark. The buyers assumed the road clearance and road construction costs still to be paid for one of the plots. The contract costs and the real estate transfer tax were shared by the parties. The building authority approved the contract on June 28, 1939, and the Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich. The 34-year-old buyer belonged to the NSDAP and SS.
