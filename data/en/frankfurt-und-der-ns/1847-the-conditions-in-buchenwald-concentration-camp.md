# The conditions in Buchenwald concentration camp

*Corneliusstraße 15*

![Die Todesmeldung vom 19. November 1938 informiert über den Tod des Juden Julius Wohlfarth im Konzentrationslager Buchenwald. Als Todesursache wurde ein Lungenödem angegeben.](../../images/55a2f85fb2721b27951d974af31175c1_original.jpg)
*License: Arolsen Archives, 1.1.5.3 / 7434665/ | Description: Die Todesmeldung vom 19. November 1938 informiert über den Tod des Juden Julius Wohlfarth im Konzentrationslager Buchenwald. Als Todesursache wurde ein Lungenödem angegeben.*

Following the pogroms in November 1938, the National Socialists sent thousands of Jewish people to Buchenwald, including brothers Hugo, Theodor and Julius Wohlfarth. Even the journey to the Buchenwald concentration camp was brutal, with guards using clubs to beat the victims as they made their way from Weimar station to the camp. Anyone unable to keep up was shot. And when the new arrivals reached the camp gate, they were forced to stand to attention with a combination of threats and violence. Camp guards shouted orders and beat the prisoners for no reason. 
The prisoners lived in  overcrowded, unhygienic barracks with no clean water or heating. Many people had to sleep in extremely tight spaces, while their days were filled with forced labour, punishments, hunger and violence. Jewish prisoners were forced to work up to 15 hours a day under the most extreme conditions. 
One form of punishment was known as “tree hanging”. This involved tying a prisoner’s hands together and then hanging them from a tree or post by their wrists, so that their body weight was borne by their dislocated shoulders. Such brutality was commonplace in the concentration camp
