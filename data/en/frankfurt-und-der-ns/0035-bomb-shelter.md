# Bomb shelter

*Hadrianstraße 18a*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Hadrianstraße](../../images/1c8f5bcd6903858a2ca6d3798b2a9b18_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Hadrianstraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme” and offered protection to 1,051 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering them. Construction began in November 1940. 32 persons were forced to perform labour here, among them 20 prisoners of war. After the war, the shelter was used by the American military administration. In 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, it was refurbished to offer protection for one to two days in the event of an attack.

Construction company: Dykerhoff und Widmann KG (Dywidag), Wiesbaden
 Architects: Karl und Stephan Blattner, Frankfurt
