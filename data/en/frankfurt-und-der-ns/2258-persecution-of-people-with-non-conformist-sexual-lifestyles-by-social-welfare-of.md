# Persecution of people with non-conformist sexual lifestyles by Social Welfare Office and Care Office

*Braubachstraße 18-22*

![The building of the municipal health department also contained the welfare office and the nursing office. Photo: Historisches Museum Frankfurt](../../images/2ef9348d66148bf49479860dee5bd835_original.jpg)
*ca. 1936 — Author: Max Göllner | License: HMF, NS1366 | Description: The building of the municipal health department also contained the welfare office and the nursing office. Photo: Historisches Museum Frankfurt*

The municipal health authority building also housed the welfare office. Werner Fischer-Defoy (1880–1955, Nazi party member from 1929) headed both the social welfare office and the social maintenance office from 1933 to 1945. Defoy was an advocate of Nazi biopolitics. In his position, he actively facilitated the registration, persecution, and physical abuse of destitute, sexually nonconformist people such as Sophie Gotthardt and Otto Kohlmann. The so-called ”care office” (Pflegeamt) was subordinate to the social welfare office (Fürsorgeamt) and responsible for “persons with sexually transmitted diseases, those suspected of having a sexually transmitted disease, and morally endangered persons of both sexes.” It worked closely with the health authority. Primarily prostitutes and women leading nonconformist sex lives were affected by the social maintenance office measures. The office arranged for their examinations for venereal disease in the dermatological clinic and subsequently often their psychiatric examinations in the psychiatric clinic. Under the Nazi regime, however, the medical personnel of the psychiatric clinic was no longer simply to commit the persons in question to a sanatorium. Starting in 1934, they were also directed to arrange for the person’s sterilization on the basis of a “genetic assessment” under the “Law for the Prevention of Offspring with Hereditary Diseases.” To this end, the clinic transferred most of the persons in question to the Hadamar regional “mental sanatorium.” In many cases, already the medical personnel of the psychiatric clinic submitted the application for sterilization to the Frankfurt “hereditary health court.” This was the procedure followed in 1934 in the case of Sophie Gotthardt.
