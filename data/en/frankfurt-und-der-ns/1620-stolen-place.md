# Stolen Place

*Bockenheimer Landstraße 28*

![Bockenheimer Landstraße 28](../../images/0a832569711cd7d2ad71243b3f412a6d_original.jpg)
*1884 — Author: Römmler & Jonas | License: ISG | Description: Bockenheimer Landstraße 28*

The house at Bockenheimer Landstraße 28 originally belonged to "Hahn'sche Hausgesellschaft Bockenheimer Landstraße GmbH", which apparently had Jewish owners. It had two 8-room apartments, two 4-room apartments and two 3-room apartments. Due to persecution, the property had to be sold to Hochtief AG, Essen, for 95,000 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated May 6, 1941. The officially tax-assessed value had been calculated at 58,200 Reichsmark and the market value at 76,000 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 11,160 Reichsmark. The city's building authority criticized the purchase price as "overvalued" and reduced it to 76,000 Reichsmark. The Nazi authorities did not set a a compensatory levy to the German Reich. In 1939, the Nazi women's organization of the Gau had already shown interest in the property. However, for unknown reasons, it was not purchased.
