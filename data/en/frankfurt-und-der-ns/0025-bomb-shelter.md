# Bomb shelter

*Leunastraße 19*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Leunastraße](../../images/8a4c788e4fa5b15d9fe407aefe49b88d_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Leunastraße*

The bomb shelter at Charlottenweg 19 (now Leunastrasse 19) was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme for the employees of the IG Farben factory in Höchst. Production there was essential to the war effort. Construction began in March 1941. The shelter offered space for 941 persons on four floors. After the war, refugees were lodged in the facility. In September 1947, the bizonal administration housed construction workers in it. The term “bizonal” is a reference to the joint American-British administration. Around 1987—towards the end of the Cold War—the shelter was refurbished to offer protection for a few hours in the event of an attack. Today it serves the “Kulturbunker Höchst e.V.” as a clubhouse.

Construction company: unknown
