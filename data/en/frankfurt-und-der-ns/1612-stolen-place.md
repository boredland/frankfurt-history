# Stolen Place

*Bockenheimer Anlage 36*

![Bockenheimer Anlage 36](../../images/3bce4809ed6c4db9208ac0638422fe93_original.jpg)
*ca. 1910 — License: ISG | Description: Bockenheimer Anlage 36*

Due to persecution, the property at Bockenheimer Anlage 36, formerly belonging to the Jewish widow of the general practitioner Professor Dr. Carl Samuel Schott, Friederike Luise Schott, née Mayer or Meyer, had to be sold by contract on May 9, 1939 for the price of 62,000 Reichsmark. The buyer was the Reichsgruppe Industrie-Werkluftschutz, Hesse branch, which wanted to operate its office and the Hesse Industrial Air Raid Protection School here. The officially tax-assessed value of the property with a plot size of 1,567 square meters was 60,600 Reichsmark, the market value 66,200 Reichsmark. The apartment building had three 6-room apartments, two 3-room apartments and two attics as well as three garages, a 2-room apartment and a further two attics in the rear building. The annual rental income amounted to 9,492 Reichsmark. The buyer and seller shared the sales costs, including the land transfer tax. Any capital gains tax was borne entirely by the seller. According to the contract, the seller had to vacate the apartment she occupied in the property by June 1, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich. By contract dated September 1, 1939, the property was transferred to the Werkluftschutzschule Hessen e. V. association based in Frankfurt.
