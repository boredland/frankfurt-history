# "Zur Krone" Inn

*Alt-Fechenheim 95*

![The “Zur Krone” inn at Alt-Fechenheim 95.](../../images/isg_ffm_s7_0586b3bfdb6cca17de271d6f2a2ae918_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: Unbekannt | License: ISG FFM S7C Nr. 1998-42364 | Description: The “Zur Krone” inn at Alt-Fechenheim 95.*

Forced Labor Camp

Heinrich Wörner, a manufacturer of precision turned parts essential to the war effort, ran a forced labor camp in the dining room of a former inn. Known as “Zur Krone,” the inn was located at Vilbeler Landstraße 7. Between 1943 and 1945, the camp held 24 men from France taken to Germany as prisoners-of-war. From 1943, they were officially registered as civilian workers. In addition, one man from Holland was also held there. The men were monitored by the Wörner factory security officers. After work, they were allowed to move around freely.

From 1943 to 1944, Heinrich Wörner also operated a second labor camp a few paces further on, in two ancillary rooms in the Engel inn, Leinwebergasse 4. Here, the camp held 52 women and girls from the Soviet Union and, most probably, also from Poland. The women forced laborers from eastern Europe were not allowed to move around freely. On working days, the factory security officers took them en bloc to work at Vilbeler Landstraße 7 and then back again. They were neither allowed to leave the camp after work or on Sundays. Heinrich Wörner had a third forced labor camp directly next to the site of his factory. In total, 230 people, at least 134 of them women, had to work for him as forced laborers.
