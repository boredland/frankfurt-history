# Ebe family & Ebe leather store

*Eckenheimer Landstraße 84*

The Ebe family lived and ran the Ebe leather goods store on the premises of today's Weinstube in Nordend.

![<p>Ebe family ca. 1923</p>](../../images/49f6ab22065e629496543f66119ff253_original.jpg)
*ca. 1923 — Author: unbekannt | Description: <p>Ebe family ca. 1923</p>*

In 1921, Abraham Ebe opened his leather goods shop along with a workshop for the manufacture and repair of leather goods in Eckenheimer Landstraße 84. He and his wife Selda and their five children lived in this neighbourhood, where Jewish residents helped shape its infrastructure. All five children attended the Philanthropin, a Jewish school in Hebelstraße 14-16, which was close to the shop. The synagogues in Friedberger Anlage and Börneplatz were both within walking distance. On 29 October 1938, the Ebe family was taken from their apartment in Hanauer Landstraße 27 to the Polish border with 17 000 other Frankfurt Jews in possession of Polish passports, as part of the so-called "Polenaktion".

*Author: Dr. Hanne Straube*
