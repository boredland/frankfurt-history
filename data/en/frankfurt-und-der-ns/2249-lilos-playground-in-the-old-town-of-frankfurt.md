# Lilo’s Playground in the Old Town of Frankfurt

*Out and about in Frankfurt with Lilo*

![Lilo and Helmut (both in front) with their friends from Wollgraben.](../../images/8af955a13fe94786a82637a9385e0dc8_original.jpg)
*ca. 1936 — Author: unbekannt | License: Privat, Lilo Günzler: Endlich Reden, Henrich Editionen Frankfurt | Description: Lilo and Helmut (both in front) with their friends from Wollgraben.*

Lilo loved her home in the historical city center—or “old town”—of Frankfurt. She really felt very much “at home” there. In her memoirs, she wrote that the Wollgraben was both her home and her playground.
Wollgraben was the name of the street Lilo first lived on with her family. It was in the old town of Frankfurt. The street no longer exists. The map shows you approximately where Lilo’s house would be located today.
On Wollgraben there were a lot of children who Lilo was friends with. They often played jump rope or ball. Lilo wrote that they had very nice neighbors and everybody helped everybody.

“I hope it stays this nice forever,” Lilo wished. But it didn’t stay that way.

The family had to move, and Lilo’s brother Helmut was forced to leave the family and go and live in a home. Later the family had to leave the old town because of the war. They moved to a new housing development in Westend.
You can find out how life there was for Lilo in “Lilo Hides during the War.” Or you can take a break and come back to Lilo’s story another time.

*Author: Susanne Thimm*
