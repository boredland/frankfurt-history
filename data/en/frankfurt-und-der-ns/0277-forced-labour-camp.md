# Forced labour camp

*Seckbächer Gasse 13*

![Photo of a multi-storey building at Seckbächer Gasse 13, which the Degussa company used as a forced labour camp](../../images/9104be4253893d23efa373329d624a89_original.jpg)
*1915 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF C23275 | Description: Photo of a multi-storey building at Seckbächer Gasse 13, which the Degussa company used as a forced labour camp*

The Degussa gold and silver parting works operated six forced labour camps in Frankfurt, one of which was located at Seckbächer Gasse 13. Civilian workers from France were lodged here. After the occupation of France by the Wehrmacht (the German army under National Socialism), more than 2.3 million French persons performed forced labour in Germany. Some 1.2 million of them were prisoners of war. Many of them came to Germany as civilian workers. In other words, they were neither prisoners of war nor concentration camp inmates. They had been recruited voluntarily or forcibly and were put to work in private companies, government agencies, farms, and private households. Their employers also kept them under surveillance.
