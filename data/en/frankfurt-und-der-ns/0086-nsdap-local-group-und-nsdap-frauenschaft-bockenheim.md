# NSDAP local group und NSDAP Frauenschaft Bockenheim

*Leipziger Straße 44*

![Photo of the offices of the NSDAP local group and NSDAP Women's League Bockenheim](../../images/313292f7d2dfba7f75933a883655e50f_original.jpg)
*1907 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-20132 | Description: Photo of the offices of the NSDAP local group and NSDAP Women's League Bockenheim*

The Bockenheim chapters of the National Socialist German Workers’ Party and the National Socialist Women’s League had their offices at this site. The local chapters were subordinate to the district leadership. The district leader nominated party members for the office of local chapter leader. The local chapter leaders were then officially appointed by the regional leader. One local chapter oversaw approximately 1,500 households, as well as the cell and block leaders in its area. A block leader was responsible for 40 to 60 households, which he kept watch over and spied on. In other words, the local groups gathered information not only about party members in their neighbourhoods, but about everyone who lived there. Social control thus extended into the individual residential buildings. For Frankfurt the sources document 47 local chapter leaders, 670 cell leaders, and 3,630 block leaders.
