# Main Cemetery

*Eckenheimer Landstraße*

![](../../images/db745a604ff2de9036b2586cdfc4c0c9_original.jpg)
*Author: © Institut für Stadtgeschichte*

Since the early 1950s, the city of Frankfurt had been discussing the establishment of dignified memorials for the victims of National Socialism. The victims of all concentration camps as well as the domestic and foreign victims of National Socialist violence who died and whose remains were buried in Frankfurt cemeteries were to be commemorated in equal measure. The sculpture of Job, created by Gerhard Marcks, was erected in the forecourt of the gravesite, which was inaugurated in 1959 and is made of Main sandstone. It impressively symbolizes the problem of suffering, the meaning of which is not recognizable. The cemetery contains the mortal remains of 539 Germans who died in concentration camps, penal institutions or sanatoriums, 617 prisoners of war and forced laborers from the former Soviet Union, 126 Polish prisoners of war and forced laborers, 27 unidentifiable dead and 175 dead, mainly from the former Yugoslavia and the Baltic states. The initially general plaque text "To the victims of violence 1933-1945" was differentiated in the following years by further plaques commemorating the Frankfurt victims of political and racist terror, the murder of Sinti and Roma and the victims of science and "racial science".
