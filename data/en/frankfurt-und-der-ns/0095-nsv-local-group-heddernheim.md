# NSV local group Heddernheim

*Alt Heddernheim 30*

![Photo of the offices of the NSV local group Heddernheim](../../images/aa5079cffd9f5725d097934ba7c08142_original.jpg)
*1915 — Author: Abt Carl Andreas | License: HMF C23244a | Description: Photo of the offices of the NSV local group Heddernheim*

This was the location of the Heddernheim chapter of the National Socialist People’s Welfare, which was the second largest mass organization during the Nazi period. Even so, it offered its services only to those considered members of the so-called people’s community. No one else in need received its support. The People’s Welfare regarded the homeless and persons who relied on begging as competition for its own efforts to collect donations. During the war, it provided relief to refugees and persons who had been bombed out of their homes. To that end it also acquired the furniture and clothing of Jews who had been deported or had fled the country, and was involved in the auctions of goods thus stolen.
