# Bomb shelter

*Peter-Bied-Straße 60*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Peter-Bied-Straße](../../images/3a3963b540eedec3d6fd32e2c48237e6_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Peter-Bied-Straße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 971 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction began in March 1941. The interior was fitted with toilets and washbasins, kitchens, storage rooms, rooms for treating injured persons, etc. During the Cold War the shelter was refurbished to offer protection for a few hours in the event of an attack. Now privately owned, it has been a listed monument since 2012.
