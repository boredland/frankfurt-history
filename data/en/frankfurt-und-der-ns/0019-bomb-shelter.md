# Bomb shelter

*An der Schwarzbachmühle 20*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located on An der Schwarzbachmühle](../../images/05121597e1c33cd3fc2470e60f0e60b7_original.jpg)
*2007 — Author: Wolfgang Kamberger | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located on An der Schwarzbachmühle*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and served as a protective space for the residents of the Goldstein district. It accommodated 366 persons on two floors. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from using the shelters. Construction began in December 1940. As a way of protecting the bomb shelter from air raids it was made to look like the surrounding buildings. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. After the war, the Dellihausen forwarding company used the building to store valuable objects formerly belonging to evacuees and persons who had been bombed out of their homes. The shelter has been privately owned since 2007.

Construction company: unknown
 Architect: Johannes Schmidt.
