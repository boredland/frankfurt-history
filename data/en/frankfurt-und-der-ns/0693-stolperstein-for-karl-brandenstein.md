# Stolperstein for Karl Brandenstein

*Hattsteiner Straße 6*

![Stolpersteine for Karl Brandenstein at Hattsteiner Straße 6](../../images/2a9eb0f4d004f47cb3469bfd6bb29bba_original.jpg)
*2021 — Author: Initiative Stolpersteine Frankfurt am Main | Description: Stolpersteine for Karl Brandenstein at Hattsteiner Straße 6*

Karl Brandenstein (1907–1941)

 Karl Brandenstein was born in the Bonames district of Frankfurt in 1907, the youngest of four children. After his father’s death in 1921, he lived at Hattsteinerstrasse 6 in Rödelheim with his mother. He attended Körnerschule and Arndtschule. After leaving school, he took on various jobs—from 1923 to 1931 as a copper polisher at the Frankfurter Hof hotel, in various restaurants, and in 1938 as a warehouseman. On several occasions he was sentenced to minor fines, for example for bodily injury or violent conduct. From 1934 onward, he was known to the Frankfurt police for his homosexual relations.

On March 16, 1939, Brandenstein was arrested in the framework of a major police operation against homosexual men. Other detainees had informed on him. On June 10, 1939, the Frankfurt regional court sentenced him to six months in prison for consensual relationships under Section 175. In force from 1871 to 1994, Section 175 criminalized sexual acts between men and was severely tightened in 1935.

The court appointed Professor Rolf Hey to draw up an expert opinion. Hey pronounced Brandenstein “mentally inferior” and claimed that his homosexuality was hereditary. On that basis, the court ordered Brandenstein’s indefinite committal to a sanatorium.

On August 23, 1939, he was moved from the Preungesheim prison to the Eichberg sanatorium. His mother's plea for clemency came to naught. On February 19, 1941, he was transferred to the Hadamar regional sanatorium, one of the institutions participating in the so-called “euthanasia murders” referred to as “Operation T4”. Karl Brandenstein was murdered there at the age of 33. The cause of death was falsely given as “influenza and meningitis.”

The Stolperstein was initiated by Martin Dill and Heidi Stögbauer of the Frankfurt Stolperstein Initiative, funded by the Projektgemeinde Frankfurt, and laid in 2019.

Stolpersteine

A Stolperstein—literally “stumbling stone”—is a 10 x 10 x 10-cm concrete block with a brass plate mounted on the upper side. The brass plate is inscribed with the name of the person and brief information about their biography and persecution during the Nazi period. Learn more about the Initiative Stolpersteine Frankfurt: https://stolpersteine-frankfurt.de/en
