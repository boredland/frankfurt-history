# The Staufer Wall with former Judengasse

*Fahrgasse 92*

![Ein Stück der Stadtmauer Staufermauer aus dem 12. Jahrhundert. Teile der Mauer sind heute noch in der Fahrgasse in Frankfurt erhalten.](../../images/d41fa761b1636ea3681a1287c9f7f620_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Aliah Habl Corona | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Ein Stück der Stadtmauer Staufermauer aus dem 12. Jahrhundert. Teile der Mauer sind heute noch in der Fahrgasse in Frankfurt erhalten.*

The Staufermauer wall running along the Fahrgasse road in Frankfurt is one of the remnants of the city’s fortifications dating back to the twelfth century. Built during the time when the Hohenstaufen dynasty ruled the Holy Roman Empire, some parts of the wall and its arches have survived to the present day. The wall bordered Judengasse, the street that constituted the special quarter for Jewish people between 1462 and 1796. The street was only three to four metres wide and about 330 metres long. Today, the street named ‘An der Staufenmauer’ follows roughly the same route as the old Judengasse. Both living and social conditions were often very poor there, with people packed into tight and overcrowded quarters. Even so, it developed into one of Germany’s most important Jewish communities. Conditions in Judengasse grew even worse once Jews were allowed to live outside of ghettos, until it was finally demolished in the late nineteenth century.
