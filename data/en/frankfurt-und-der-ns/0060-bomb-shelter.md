# Bomb shelter

*Sonnenweg 21-23*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Sonnenweg](../../images/9d3f43a5af682279439d9d9bb2b269be_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Sonnenweg*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and served the residents of the Goldstein housing estate in Schwanheim. It provided space for 570 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered protection for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction began in October 1941. After the war, the Andreas Noris-Zahn company of Frankfurt stored chemicals here. The shelter underwent structural alterations and windows were added. Today the building is privately owned.
