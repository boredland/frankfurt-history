# NSV local group Höchst-Süd

*Bolongarostraße 107*

![Picture postcard with the offices of the NSV local group Höchst-Süd](../../images/3b3c9ef9a03b3946480713dda657bd1f_original.jpg)
*ca. 1905 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-22945 | Description: Picture postcard with the offices of the NSV local group Höchst-Süd*

This was the location of the Höchst-Süd chapter of the National Socialist Peoples’ Welfare, the second largest mass organization during the Nazi period. Among other activities, the People’s Welfare organized the Winter Relief Organization, which formally, however, was an independent entity. It offered its services only to members of the so-called people’s community; other persons in need were denied its support. It even regarded the homeless and persons who relied on begging as competition for its own efforts to collect donations. These persons were considered “asocial” and persecuted as such. During the war, the organization provided relief to refugees and persons who had been bombed out of their homes. To that end they also acquired the furniture and clothing of Jews who had been deported or had fled the country, and were involved in the auctions of goods thus stolen.
