# Rothschild Palace

*Bockenheimer Landstraße 10*

![Photo of Rothschild Palace in Bockenheimer Landstraße 10](../../images/C28607_jpg_5c3277d445d3ad104a76103dad192be8_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: Leonhard Kleemann | License: HMF C28607 | Description: Photo of Rothschild Palace in Bockenheimer Landstraße 10*

The private collector, banker, and art patron Maximilian Freiherr von Goldschmidt-Rothschild (1843–1940) came from the Jewish Goldschmidt-Kassel family. He lived with his wife Minna Caroline von Rothschild and their five children in the Rothschild Palais, a former country and summer house of Mayer Amschel Rothschild at Bockenheimer Landstrasse 10.
 
 Under pressure from the Nazis, Maximilian von Goldschmidt-Rothschild sold his property and the Rothschild Palais in Bockenheimer Landstrasse to the City of Frankfurt on 5 September 1938. He received 620,000 Reichsmarks (RM) for them, a price significantly below market value. Meanwhile 95 years of age, the collector was granted limited lifelong right of abode in his palace but had to pay 25,000 Reichsmarks per year in rent.
 
 In the wake of the pogroms of 9/10 November 1938, Goldschmidt-Rothschild was compelled to sell his private art collection of more than 1,500 objects to the City of Frankfurt on 11 November 1938. At 2,552,030 Reichsmarks, the price was once again far below value. It was transferred to an account to which he had no access. The artworks were distributed among the Museum für Kunsthandwerk (now Museum Angewandte Kunst), the Städtische Galerie (now Städel Museum), and the Liebieghaus. The palace itself was designated Museum für Kunsthandwerk, Department II and thus as a branch of the museum of the same name. The collection was made accessible to the public for a brief period.
