# Zeil and Wronker department store

*Zeil 85-93*

![Eine historische Aufnahme der Einkaufsstraße „Zeil“ in Frankfurt am Main, aufgenommen um die Jahrhundertwende. Kutschen, prächtige Gebäude und Straßenuhren prägen das Stadtbild.](../../images/28d69abc1129ac61be005e80b6e028dd_original.jpg)

In the 1930s, Jewish families owned about a third of all shops in Frankfurt. When the National Socialists seized power in 1933, Jewish stores were boycotted and the owners were forced to sell their businesses for much less than they were actually worth. One example was Kaufhaus Wronker, a department store on the Zeil shopping street located where the H&M clothing store now stands. Many Jewish shops were looted and destroyed during the November pogroms in 1938. Irmgard Walter-Zeising was there. She was very clear: “The merchandise was lying in the street and the shop windows were boarded up.” There are hardly any photos of the pogroms because the perpetrators did their best to keep these events from being documented. The pogroms not only resulted in financial loss for Jewish families, but for many they also brought violence, persecution and often death.
