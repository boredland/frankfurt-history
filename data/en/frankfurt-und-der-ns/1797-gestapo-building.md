# Gestapo Building

*Lindenstraße 27*

Journey to the past: Jewish life in Westend 100 years ago

![Former Gestapo Building](../../images/847d00196f8301dfde335b77babca6f6_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Daniel Fricke | License: Evangelisch reformierte Gemeinde | Description: Former Gestapo Building*

This building was the headquarters of the Secret State Police for the Wiesbaden administrative district from 1941 to 1944. The Gestapo in Frankfurt had previously been based in the so-called Siemens House in today's Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße near the Holbeinsteg. 
Jakob Horovitz was physically and mentally tortured in the worst possible way there and in the ‘Klapperfeld’ prison in the city centre around 86 years ago. The whole thing happened in 1938 because he was suspected of being a passport forger. He was therefore arrested by the Gestapo and was detained for three weeks and tortured in various ways. 
After the war, an acquaintance of Jakob Horovitz, who was also imprisoned, recounted what happened to them during his detention: Jakob Horovitz was standing in a room, and many spotlights were directed at him, blinding him. Gestapo officers ran around him, shouted at him, threatened and insulted him. This went on for a long time. Other prisonersften heard him screaming at night. Thanks to the efforts of the parish council, he was eventually released and fled to Holland, where he most likely died a few weeks later as a result of his imprisonment and torture.
