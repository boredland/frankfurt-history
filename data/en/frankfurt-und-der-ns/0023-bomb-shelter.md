# Bomb shelter

*Schmidtstraße 10*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Schmidtstraße](../../images/0d896e5bb739c9d0d45fe90b2704316c_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Schmidtstraße*

The bomb shelter at Boelckestrasse 10 (now Schmidtstrasse 10) was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 748 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction work lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. The façade was elaborately designed in the Romanesque style. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office lodged refugees here, as well as victims of Nazi persecution. The shelter has belonged to the City of Frankfurt since 1990.

Construction company: Diplom-Ingenieur Karl Kögel, Frankfurt
 Architect: Karl Olsson
