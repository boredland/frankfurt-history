# (Former) Synagogue Unterlindau

*Unterlindau 23*

Journey to the past: Jewish life in the Westend 100 years ago

![The Unterlindau 23 today](../../images/1b8765e83800971123f4eed00445654e_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Daniel Fricke | License: Evangelisch reformierte Gemeinde | Description: The Unterlindau 23 today*

A synagogue stood here from 1893.

But not right here, but in the backyard. In other words, it wasn't a super fancy building, it was quite inconspicuous. The building that stood on the street was a Jewish religious education school. That means you went to a school where there were only Protestant religious education classes. So you went to a different school for Jewish religious education, with your own certificate and so on.
That still exists today. I have a Jewish friend who goes to an external religious education program.

*Author: Jakob*

Due to the growing population of Jews in the Westend, the synagogue was almost doubled in size in 1926. Many synagogues were set on fire or completely vandalized during the Reich Pogrom Night of 1938. A service was about to begin in the synagogue at Unterlindau 23 when National Socialists marched in through the door and said that everyone had been arrested.

Despite the attacks, the synagogue was later - in contrast to other places - still in a condition where services could be held. Services were held there until at least 1941. Later, the synagogue was temporarily used as a camp for prisoners of war. The synagogue was destroyed by bombs in 1944.
After the war, all the remains were ruthlessly removed, which I find very sad, as this holy place had already been destroyed anyway and all the remains were thoughtlessly thrown away. That is also one of our reasons why we want to give this synagogue space and commemorate it.

*Author: Benjamin*
