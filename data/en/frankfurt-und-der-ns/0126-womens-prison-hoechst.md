# women's prison Höchst

*Hospitalstraße 18*

![Höchst Women's Prison](../../images/dccca9cbbd7385c51adc53edc05b0975_original.jpg)
*n. d. — Author: Unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Höchst Women's Prison*

During the years of the Nazi regime, the Höchst Prison held people on remand, in preventive detention, or serving a sentence. The men imprisoned here had been given sentences of up to three months, and were transferred from the Frankfurt, Höchst and Königstein courts. From mid-1939, this also functioned as a women’s prison. In 1943, a decree issued by the Reich Minister of Justice turned the prison into a so-called Special Penal Institution for Terms of Imprisonment for Fallen Women. The prison was overcrowded and the conditions inhumane. Originally designed for 112 prisoners, during the Second World War up to 240 inmates were held here. They provided forced labour for the munitions industry and the Wehrmacht, for example, at the VDM Heddernheim metal works or at the Alfred Teves factory.
