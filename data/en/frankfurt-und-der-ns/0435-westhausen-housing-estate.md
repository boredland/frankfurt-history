# Westhausen housing estate

*Geschwister-Scholl-Straße 43*

![Aerial photo of the Westhausen housing estate](../../images/9ac75c7230c92309ff54cf03483231d1_original.jpg)
*n.D. — Author: unbekannt | Description: Aerial photo of the Westhausen housing estate*

Those living in the Westhausen workers’ quarter largely supported Germany’s Social Democratic Party (SPD) or the Communist Party (KPD). The area was a target for Nazi attacks no less than twice. During the Reichstag elections in 1932, they tore down a red flag which the Communist Wilhelm Schuster had hung out of his window. At the time, he and his family had gone to the polling station. In spring 1933, the Nazis rounded up known Communists, Social Democrats and active Christians from their homes and took them to the waterworks close to the Ebelfeld School (now the Praunheimer Hohl area). There, they were tortured so badly that one worker died as the result of his injuries. As early as 1933, the Nazi city administration renamed many of the streets in the quarter after protagonists and places in German colonial history. After the end of the war, the residents campaigned to have the old street names reinstated. Now, some of the streets bear the names of Frankfurt’s resistance fighters. One street is named after Ludwig Landmann, once Frankfurt’s mayor, who was forcibly dismissed by the Nazis in 1933. However, none of the streets have been named after a Communist from that time.
