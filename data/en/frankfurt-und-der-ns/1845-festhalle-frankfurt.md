The Festhalle in Frankfurt is a central event location steeped in over one hundred years of history. Officially opened in 1909, it has played host to countless cultural, sporting and political events. During the November pogroms of 1938, the Festhalle served as a collection point for Jewish men before they were sent to concentration camps. The Festhalle suffered severe damage during the Second World War but was rebuilt afterwards. Today, it is a venue for international concerts, trade fairs and sporting events. The Festhalle represents cultural diversity, historical reflection and progress in the city of Frankfurt.
