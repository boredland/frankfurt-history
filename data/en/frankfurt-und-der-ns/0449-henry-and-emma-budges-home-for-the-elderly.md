# Henry and Emma Budge's home for the elderly

*Eschersheimer Landstraße 241-243*

![Photo of the Henry and Emmy Budge home for the elderly](../../images/Ph20590_jp_a9136f195c8f905c741b22152911bfaa_original.jpg)
*before 1941 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: HMF Ph20590 | Description: Photo of the Henry and Emmy Budge home for the elderly*

This was the location of the Henry and Emma Budge Foundation’s home for the elderly. The building had been built in 1920 and initially served as a convalescent home before becoming a home for elderly persons in 1928. It was open to Christians and Jews alike. Pursuant to an order by the Reich Minister of the Interior of 8 May 1938, the Jewish members of the foundation's board of directors had to resign and the home could only admit non-Jewish persons. The board refused to comply. On 23 April 1939, the district president in Wiesbaden ordered the foundation’s dissolution. The Jewish residents had to move out on 31 March 1939.
 The City of Frankfurt purchased the home and the property. The hereditary building rights no longer applied, and the building, complete with its furnishings, passed into municipal ownership after assumption of the mortgage in the amount of 680,145 Reichsmarks. The foundation was indemnified after the war. It has operated a home for the elderly in the Seckbach district of Frankfurt since 1968.
