# forced labour camp

*Berger Straße 133*

![Forced labor camp, Berger Straße 133](../../images/feffc9255ca59aa3dce46c0f32b0fe97_original.jpg)
*1985 — Author: Elisabeth Rehm | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2018-631 | Description: Forced labor camp, Berger Straße 133*

This was the location of the A. Klein restaurant, which was used to lodge civilian workers from Italy. It also housed forced labourers working for the Engelhard pharmaceutical manufacturer and the Peter Cornel juice press company. The building belonged to the Catholic congregation of the Church of St Joseph. Civilian workers were neither prisoners of war nor concentration camp inmates. In the summer of 1944, there were 5.7 million foreign civilian labourers working in private companies, government agencies, farms, and private households in the German Reich. Their employers also kept them under surveillance.
