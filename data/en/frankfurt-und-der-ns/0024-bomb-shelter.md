# Bomb shelter

*Brühlstraße 33-35*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Brühlstraße](../../images/7e5e3d672454a3ed77d15f9ac29c5e9a_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Brühlstraße*

This bomb shelter offered protection for 416 persons on three floors. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from using the shelters. Construction work lasted from November 1940 to April 1941. Of the 74 persons who performed forced labour on the construction site, 20 were prisoners of war. This was the first bomb shelter to be completed within the framework of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme”. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. After the war, the welfare office lodged refugees here. In 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, the shelter was refurbished to offer protection for a few hours in the event of an attack.

Construction company: Hochtief AG
 Architect: Karl Olsson
