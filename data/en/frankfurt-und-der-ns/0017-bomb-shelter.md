# Bomb shelter

*Alt Schwanheim 2a*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Alt Schwanheim](../../images/acdec9ce02500d6720a4b4b2fb096243_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Alt Schwanheim*

This bomb shelter offered protection to 330 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. This shelter was camouflaged as a barn by the shape of its roof. Construction work lasted from January 1941 to July 1942. At the entrance next to the stair tower, there is still a crest stone with an eagle and the year 1941 to this day. After the war, the building was used by the American military administration. Around 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, it was refurbished to offer protection for a few hours. It has been a listed monument since 2012 and today serves the “Musikzug Blau-Gold” as a clubhouse.

Construction company: FA Hofmann II (presumably Karl Hofmann II of Ortenberg)
 Architect: unknown
