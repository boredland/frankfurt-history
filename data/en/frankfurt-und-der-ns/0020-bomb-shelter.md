# Bomb shelter

*Bruno-Asch-Anlage 2*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located at Bruno-Asch-Anlage](../../images/6eccdb08f3afe4f480972ae6549b62a5_original.jpg)
*before 1943 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S17 Nr. 839-19 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located at Bruno-Asch-Anlage*

The bomb shelter at Bahnhofsanlage 2 (now Bruno-Asch-Anlage 2) was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme to offer protection to travellers at the Höchst railway station. It is seven storeys high and thus the highest bomb shelter in Frankfurt. It could accommodate 1,500 persons. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering the shelters. Construction began in February 1941. The shelter was severely damaged during the war. In 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, it was altered to provide protection from nuclear, biological, and chemical weapons. Today it is still used for purposes of civil defence. It has been a listed monument since 2005.
