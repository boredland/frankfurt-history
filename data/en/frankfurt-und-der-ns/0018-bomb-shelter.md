# Bomb shelter

*Am Gemeindegarten 18*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Am Gemeindegarten](../../images/a491e39db3211584e3440765b126f911_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Am Gemeindegarten*

This bomb shelter was deliberately built in the park. From the air it was meant to look like a villa to keep it from being bombed. The Griesheim chapter of the National Socialist German Workers’ Party was also located adjacent to the park. Construction on this bomb shelter, which was designed to accommodate 1,184 persons, got underway in September 1941. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from entering them. After the war, the Historisches Museum used this shelter as a storage space until 1957. At the time, museum employees discovered 67 Hanukkah menorahs and kept the find a secret. The menorahs had belonged to the collection of Sigmund Nauheim, which had been stolen from the Museum für jüdische Altertümer in 1938. The Historisches Museum was supposed to turn them over to the Jewish Restitution Successor Organization (JRSO) in 1951, but neglected to do so. It was not until 1987 that it gave the candelabra to the Jüdisches Museum (JMF)—which was founded in Frankfurt at that time—along with other religious objects. These objects form the core of the JMF’s present-day Judaica collection. The bomb shelter is now privately owned.
