# forced labor company

*Waldschmidtstraße 43*

![Forced labor company, Waldschmidtstraße 43](../../images/e65a5dc60a9a5ac89503979d3bc10fa7_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2010-55 | Description: Forced labor company, Waldschmidtstraße 43*

The Naxos Union abrasives and sanding machine factory at Waldschmidtstrasse 43 profited from forced labour. Between 1942 and 1944 the company operated a forced labour camp in the neighbouring building at Waldschmidtstrasse 33, which had a direct entrance to the factory grounds. The forced labourers came from all over Europe, the majority from the Soviet Union. In 1942 the camp lodged 3 Croatian men, 8 Polish men, 1 Czech man, 1 French man and 1 French woman, 16 Italian men, and 15 persons from other countries. In 1943 the number rose sharply to over 300. Now the camp population comprised 64 Russians (including 24 prisoners of war), 87 Ukrainians, 31 Poles and 24 Lithuanians, as well as 68 French forced labourers (including 17 prisoners of war). The Naxos Union operated altogether nine forced labour camps in Frankfurt, including one in the nearby Herder School at Wittelsbacher Allee 10.
