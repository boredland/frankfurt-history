# Forced labour camp

*Zeilweg (without number, possibly 42)*

![Photo of a machine hall belonging to VDM-Halbzeugwerke GmbH, on whose premises in Zeilweg (no number, possibly 42) there was a forced labour camp.](../../images/3a73caf00b696d274f4e706038a11460_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: Paul Wolff | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Wo Nr. 4414 | Description: Photo of a machine hall belonging to VDM-Halbzeugwerke GmbH, on whose premises in Zeilweg (no number, possibly 42) there was a forced labour camp.*

From 1941 onwards, there was a large forced labour camp with a population of 1,400 persons on the factory grounds of the VDM semi-finished products factory. These persons included 616 prisoners of war from France and 383 from the Soviet Union. Starting in 1942, this camp had its own residential register listing the forced labourers by name. It is now in the holdings of the Institut für Stadtgeschichte (Institute of Municipal History) in Frankfurt. During World War II, VDM and its subsidiaries (VDM-Halbzeugwerke and VDM-Luftfahrtwerke) operated altogether five camps, of which four were located in Heddernheim and Eschersheim.
