# The Hippodrome

*Kennedyallee / corner of Stresemannallee*

![<p>Opening of the 1936 Maccabi Indoor Sports Festival at the Hippodrome.</p>](../../images/ecb42acb72cbe081b0b0dd30282fee1f_original.jpg)
*1977 — Author: Robert Atlasz, Bar Kochba: Makkabi-Deutschland 1898-1938, Tel Aviv 1977 | Description: <p>Opening of the 1936 Maccabi Indoor Sports Festival at the Hippodrome.</p>*

The Hippodrome had been opened in 1899 on Forsthausstraße (today Kennedyallee / corner of Stresemannallee). It had a 95-meter-long and 25-meter-high hall with a gallery for spectators. Numerous riding, gymnastics and sports festivals were held here. The history of Maccabi sports emphasizes that Bar Kochba Frankfurt was the only Maccabi club in Nazi Germany that managed to organize three large sports festivals in the Hippodrom between 1934 and 1937. World-renowned athletes took part in the international indoor competitions in many sports. Several thousand visitors came to the sports festivals. During the Second World War, the Hippodrome was badly damaged and the ruins were blown up in October 1956.

*Author: Helga Roos, Sportkreis Frankfurt e.V.*
