# Forced labour camp

*Röderbergweg 122*

![frankfurthistory@stadt-frankfurt.de](../../images/1e0b7c79a351939d86554bd72a5c51a4_original.jpg)
*Author: Johann Friedrich Riese | License: HMF | Description: frankfurthistory@stadt-frankfurt.de*

Röderbergfelsenkeller Wilhelm Mank was a restaurant at Röderbergweg 122 that served as a camp for forced labourers who worked for Voigt & Haeffner. A manufacturer of switchgear and other electrical equipment, the company made use of several hundred forced labourers, who were lodged on the plant grounds and neighbouring land as well as in Fechenheim and here in Röderbergweg. In 1943 alone, at least 195 persons from Russia, 111 from Lithuania, 5 from Ukraine, 5 from Poland, 2 from Serbia, 25 from Croatia, 1 from Hungary, 1 from Slovakia, 167 from France, 16 from Belgium, 27 from the Netherlands, 39 from Italy, and 3 from Spain performed forced labour for Voigt & Haeffner. In 1946, the police reported that, in the period from 1941 to 1943, 7 persons from Bulgaria had were also forced to work for Voigt & Haeffner. This document is one of altogether 329 compiled by the Allies in their efforts to shed light on forced labour in Frankfurt and now in the holdings of the Arolsen Archives.
