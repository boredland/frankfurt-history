# Bomb shelter

*Friedberger Anlage 6*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Friedberger Anlage](../../images/3592fe3911f95ac10845613c8363351b_original.jpg)
*2014 — Author: Martin Starl | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2015-344 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Friedberger Anlage*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection for 1,385 persons on six floors. It was one of Frankfurt’s larger shelters. The city’s bomb shelters provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering them. Construction began in September 1941 but was never completed. French forced labourers were made to work on the construction site. After the war, books of the municipal and university library were stored here. In 2002, after a long struggle, the “Initiative 9. November e.V.” instituted a place of learning and remembrance in the shelter which is still in operation today. The City of Frankfurt erected a memorial in front of the building. The foundations of the synagogue are still in the ground.

Construction company: unknown
 Architect: Martin Leipold, Frankfurt
