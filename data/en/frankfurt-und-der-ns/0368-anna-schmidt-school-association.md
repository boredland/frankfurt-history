# Anna Schmidt School Association

*Mainzer Landstraße 37*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier%20fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

Käthe Heisterbergk had become the headmistress of the Anna Schmidt private girls‘ school in 1929. Under her oversight, the school was a place where the National Socialist doctrine never took hold. Neither the teaching staff nor the student body were willing to conform. In the place of the swastika flag, the school had its own flag with a depiction of an owl, the symbol of wisdom. Thanks to a specially designed school uniform, the students were able to circumvent the compulsory Nazi uniform. A Jewish teacher taught at the school until her emigration in 1939. And in 1945, two Jewish pupils were able to sit for their school-leaving examination here. Instead of engaging in open confrontation with the Nazis, Käthe Heisterbergk upheld the banner of humanist education and was thus spared more serious conflict.
