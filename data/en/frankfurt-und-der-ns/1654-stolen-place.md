# Stolen Place

*Friedberger Anlage 22*

![Friedberger Anlage 22](../../images/32ff33eb2e09f020ce1978eeb383c982_original.jpg)
*1914 — Author: Postkartensammlung Church | License: HMF | Description: Friedberger Anlage 22*

The property at the corner of Friedberger Anlage 22 / Palmstraße originally belonged to the two Jewish women Sara Sittha Mainz, née Stern, and Alice Oppenheimer, née Stern, who lived in the house at Friedberger Anlage 22 - until when is unclear. The property was 455 square meters in size. Sara Sittha Mainz wanted to emigrate into exile. Due to persecution, her representative had to sell the property to a non-Jewish lawyer's daughter from the neighborhood for 30,000 Reichsmarks under a contract dated 8 December 1938. The officially tax-assessed value was 25,900 Reichsmark and the market value 31,900 Reichsmark. The annual rental income was 4,644 Reichsmark. Contract costs and land purchase taxes were shared by the parties. The local NSDAP group in Sandweg expressed concerns about the purchaser because her parents had refused to "have the front garden railing on their property removed". They therefore refused to endorse the purchase agreement. The building authority nevertheless approved the purchase contract on May 19, 1939.
