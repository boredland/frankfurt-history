# Bomb shelter

*Germaniastraße 89*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Germaniastraße](../../images/7057fabbfda91dc614b8f5431f0b8739_original.jpg)
*1981 — Author: Joachim Sändig | License: HMF Ph17146 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Germaniastraße*

This bomb shelter was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 760 persons on four floors. Frankfurt’s air-raid protection facilities provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction began in December 1940. The main entrances are decorated with two medallion-shaped elements by the sculptor Carl Stock depicting a man and a woman on horseback. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office lodged unaccompanied child refugees here. Since that time the shelter has had windows. Today it is a listed monument and is used as a culture workshop by the Waggong e.V. club.

Construction company: Gebrüder Weil, Limburg
 Architect: August Härter
