# Hotel Viktoria

*Elbestraße 24-26*

![Photo of the Hotel Viktoria](../../images/8b3f34644789c19a1326f608a64680dc_original.jpg)
*1982 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2013-2261 | Description: Photo of the Hotel Viktoria*

The Hotel Viktoria belonged to the Jewish widow Therese Oberndorfer, née Bing. She had formerly resided in Bamberg, but by 1939 was already living in the Netherlands. She had leased the hotel. It had 51 hotel rooms, a restaurant, and three shops on its five floors. In the attic there were rooms for the personnel and a flat for the restaurateur.
 In 1939, Therese Oberndorfer was forced to sell the hotel property and furnishings to a non-Jewish construction engineer of Frankfurt below value. The leaseholder even lodged a complaint with the highest authority — the Reich Chancellery — but to no avail. The construction engineer purchased the property for 125,000 Reichsmarks (RM), of which the furnishings accounted for 15,000 RM. The engineer was a member of the National Socialist German Workers’ Party (NSDAP) and as such profited from the anti-Jewish policies of the Nazis. He dealt in properties that Jewish families had had to surrender in conjunction with Nazi persecution. He purchased the buildings at Rotteckstrasse 4, Mauerweg 30, Böhmerstrasse 6, Bethmannstrasse 52, and Eiserne Hand 3 — in some cases in partnership with an architect — and altered them before putting them up for sale. He also had changes made to the hotel and then sold it again.
