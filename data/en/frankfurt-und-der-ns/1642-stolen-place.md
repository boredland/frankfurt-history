# Stolen Place

*Falkensteiner Straße 37*

![Falkensteiner Straße 37](../../images/43d9be70dde47d684d95ea352b2a2abf_original.jpg)
*ca. 1925 — License: ISG | Description: Falkensteiner Straße 37*

The Jewish wife of a radiologist, Hilda Stilgebauer, née Lust, was forced by persecution to sell her ideal half of their 15-year-old detached house at Falkensteiner Straße 37 to her non-Jewish husband for 16,350 Reichsmarks under a contract dated November 7, 1940. Hilda Stilgebauer was already living in exile in Luxembourg when the contract was signed. At the same time, she sold him her shares in various properties belonging to both of them for 2,445 Reichsmark. The purchase prices corresponded to the officially tax-assessed value in each case. The building authority determined a moderate market value of 20,250 Reichsmark. The Reich Flight Tax incurred for the flight into exile and the Jewish property levy that the husband had paid were deducted from the purchase price. The remaining purchase price was to be paid in installments. The parties shared the costs of the contract, including the land transfer tax. The building authority approved the contract on October 2, 1941 and set a compensatory levy to the German Reich in the amount of 3,900 Reichsmark. Following an appeal by the buyer, the district president in Wiesbaden withdrew the decision on the compensatory levy.
