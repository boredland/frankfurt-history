# Stolen Place

*Feldbergstraße 42*

![Feldbergstraße 42](../../images/c24b8a4a58fdf0b70e38481c99966817_original.jpg)
*ca. 1964 — Author: Walter Schröder-Kiewert | License: ISG | Description: Feldbergstraße 42*

The property at Feldbergstraße 42 originally belonged to a Jewish merchant. The property comprised 488 square meters. The house contained four 3-room apartments, four 2.5-room apartments, two 2-room apartments and one 1-room apartment. The 83-year-old widower Albert Müller, who also lived there, had to have an offer to sell his apartment building to a non-Jewish factory owner couple from Weil der Stadt notarized on February 4, 1939 due to the persecution. The purchase price was to be 52,800 Reichsmarks. The officially tax-assessed value was 52,400 Reichsmark and the market value was 60,000 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 9,600 Reichsmark. The buyers were to grant the seller the right to continue to use the 3-room apartment on the first floor, which he occupied, even after the transfer on April 1, 1939. The rent was to be 90 Reichsmark per month. The costs for the contract, including the estate agent's commission, were to be borne by the buyers. The local NSDAP group in charge described the buyer as "politically unreliable". The NSDAP Gau economic advisor therefore refused to approve the contract. The building authority accordingly rejected the approval on May 27, 1939. The parties therefore canceled the contract. On June 7, 1939, Albert Mülller therefore concluded a new purchase contract with a non-Jewish engineer from Weil der Stadt, who acquired the apartment building for 52,800 Reichsmarks. The buyer paid the contract costs and the estate agent's commission. The parties agreed to hand over the property on July 1, 1939. The assessment of this buyer by the Weil der Stadt NSDAP local group was also negative: "he is politically worthless". Albert Müller did not live to see the purchase contract approved; he died on an unknown date. On June 19, 1940, the court-appointed guardian of the estate contacted the building authority to expedite the approval. This was finally granted by the building authority on July 2, 1940. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
