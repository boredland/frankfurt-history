# Stolen place

*Obermainanlage 8*

![Photo of the facade of the prayer room, Obermainanlage 8](../../images/e76e904667b1a6efdee57e261a26e22e_original.jpg)
*ca. 1955 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2008-124 | Description: Photo of the facade of the prayer room, Obermainanlage 8*

This property belonged to the businessman Moses Steinlauf, who had been born near Cracow in Galicia and is thought to have come to Germany with his family after World War I. In 1938, Steinlauf was forced to sell the property to non-Jews. He died in the Jewish community hospital in Gagernstrasse on 11 November 1939. His wife Bertha was deported and murdered in September 1942. His son Benno had been arrested by the Gestapo and sent to the Buchenwald concentration camp in July 1938. There he was classified as a political inmate for committing “currency offences”. The attempt to flee the country without applying to the foreign exchange control office for emigration was an example of such an offence. After nearly seven years in detention, he was liberated in the Sonnebergconcentration camp on 17 April 1945.
