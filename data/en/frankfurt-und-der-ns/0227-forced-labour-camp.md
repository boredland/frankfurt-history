# forced labour camp

*Hanauer Landstraße 528*

![Forced labor camp, Hanauer Landstraße 528](../../images/d520898a5c46c2c87a314c71e3330088_original.jpg)
*1931 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-29253 | Description: Forced labor camp, Hanauer Landstraße 528*

Between 1940 and 1945, the I.G. Farben-Industrie exploited altogether some 10,000 forced labourers from more than 20 countries in their three Frankfurt chemical plants in Höchst, Griesheim, and Mainkur. This was the only way of maintaining production that was vital to the war effort at a time when more and more employees were being called up for military service. The company operated over 20 forced labour camps in Frankfurt alone. Four of them were provenly located on the Mainkur factory grounds (formerly Cassella) at Hanauer Landstrasse 528. 22 Ukrainian and 2 Russian forced labourers were also lodged on one floor of the factory building at Alt-Fechenheim 30. Another camp was located a few doors away at Alt-Fechenheim 63. No information has come down to us, however, about the number of forced labourers housed there or their countries of origin. In 1940, 40 French prisoners of war assigned to forced labour were lodged in a third camp whose location is unknown. In 1942, 25 Italian forced labourers were registered there; the following year they were joined by 4 French and 48 Belgian forced labourers. Many other forced labourers also worked at the Mainkur plant. Their lodgings were located in Hydronstrasse and at Friedrichshafener Strasse 25 (now Cassellastrasse).
