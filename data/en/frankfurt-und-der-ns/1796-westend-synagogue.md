# Westend Synagogue

*Freiherr-von-Stein Straße 30-32*

Journey to the past: Jewish life in Westend 100 years ago

![The Westend Synagogue in the Freiherr-von-Stein Street today](../../images/b431d5fe1cf3eb7adc046bc1d30c9dd9_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Daniel Fricke | License: Evangelisch reformierte Gemeinde | Description: The Westend Synagogue in the Freiherr-von-Stein Street today*

Due to the growing Jewish population in the Westend, another synagogue was built between 1908 and 1910. Today around 600 stumbling stones commemorate Jewish families from this neighbourhood. The Westend was characterised by Jewish life. This synagogue is known as the Westend Synagogue. It is much better known than the synagogue in Unterlindau, as it is almost completely preserved. There were houses near the Westend Synagogue that were occupied by people loyal to the Nazi regime, which is why they were mainly only destroyed from the inside. The Westend Synagogue is the largest synagogue in Frankfurt. 
The synagogue is guarded by the police for security reasons. How sad that in this day and age, police protection has become more necessary than ever just so that people can practise their faith! Now we turn right from the view of the synagogue and take note of the many stumbling stones that lie along the way.
