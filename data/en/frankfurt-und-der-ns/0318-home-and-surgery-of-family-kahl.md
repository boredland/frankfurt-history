# Home and Surgery of Family Kahl

*Blanchardstraße 22*

![The house at Blanchardstraße 22 was the home and practice of the Kahl family](../../images/a2ed48fa1569b474707ab54d31a1d903_original.jpg)
*Description: The house at Blanchardstraße 22 was the home and practice of the Kahl family*

Margarete and Fritz Kahl, who rescued Jews, lived in this house. They took the risks related to their resistance activities despite the fact that they had children to take care of. Dr. Fritz Kahl was a physician and had his surgery in the house. In 1941, the couple collaborated with Heinz Welke, the pastor of the Church of the Holy Trinity, to create an illegal network in the Bockenheim district of Frankfurt. They helped people who were threatened with deportation to escape. One of the persons who joined them in these efforts was a detective on the staff of the city’s criminal investigation department who informed them of Gestapo operations in advance; another was a typesetter who forged documents and identification papers. Robert Eisenstädt was one of those who asked the network for help. The twenty-three-year-old had been deported to the Majdanek concentration and extermination camp in Poland in May 1942 and fled to Frankfurt from there in July of the same year. The Bockenheim network enabled him and his fiancée Eva Müller to flee to Switzerland. In 1944, the Evangelischer Verlag (publishing house) in Zürich published his report about his time in the camp as a way of informing the public about the Nazis’ murderous intentions.
 In 2006, the World Holocaust Remembrance Center Yad Vashem in Israel honoured the Kahls as “Righteous Among the Nations”.
