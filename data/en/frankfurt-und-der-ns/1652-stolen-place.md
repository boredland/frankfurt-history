# Stolen Place

*Friedberger Anlage 15*

![Friedberger Anlage 15](../../images/32bd144efdb916ac918e9ab85a2a1a2b_original.jpg)
*2012 — Author: Martin Starl | License: ISG | Description: Friedberger Anlage 15*

The villa at Friedberger Anlage 15, which was converted into a modern apartment building with small apartments in 1935 / 36, originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Leo Alsbacher from Ludwigshafen am Rhein and his wife Lina Alsbacher, née Schwab. The property covered an area of 403 square meters. The two-storey house contained five 2-room apartments and one 1-room apartment. Due to the persecution, they had to sell their house to a non-Jewish mechanic from Stuttgart-Stammheim for 39,000 Reichsmark in a contract dated February 16, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value was 25,400 Reichsmark and the market value 39,500 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 5,048 Reichsmark. The contract costs, including land transfer tax and estate agent's commission, were shared by the parties. The building authorities approved the purchase contract on July 19, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of rent increases.
