# Frankfurt Angel

*Klaus Mann square*

Klaus-Mann-Platz

![](../../images/7731ce13793f47c185524329d1afc641_original.jpg)
*Author: © Institut für Stadtgeschichte*

In December 1994, the "Frankfurt Angel" memorial was unveiled on Klaus-Mann-Platz. It commemorates the suffering of homosexuals during the Nazi era. In 1935, the existing §175 of the penal code was tightened considerably along with police registration and persecution. Thousands of homosexuals were eventually deported to concentration camps and marked as a group by a pink corner on their jacket. Although there was no legal threat of punishment for lesbian love, the Nazi authorities also persecuted women under other pretexts.
Criminal prosecution did not end with the Nazi regime. It was only relaxed in the 1970s and completely abolished in 1994 with the abolition of §175.
The memorial was erected by the "Initiative Mahnmal Homosexuellenverfolgung e.V." and designed by Rosemarie Trockel.
