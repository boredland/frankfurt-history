# Forced labour camp

*Kettelerallee 72*

![Picture postcard with exterior view of the Bornheimer Ratskeller](../../images/47a8ae916b95f6df5596b637b741e8f6_original.jpg)
*1907 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF / Sammlung Church C80203 | Description: Picture postcard with exterior view of the Bornheimer Ratskeller*

The Bornheimer Ratskeller at Nussberg 72 (now Kettelerallee) served as a camp for forced labourers who worked for the Lurgi Werkstätten in the Riederberg industrial zone. In 1942, the company made use of 29 French forced labourers. In 1945, 4 Italian forced labourers who worked for the Peter Cornel apple juice, grape juice, and apple wine press house were lodged here. In 1946, the 6th police district reported to the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration (UNRRA) that 28 forced labourers from France had been lodged here from February 1942 to March 1945. Frankfurt had been liberated by Allied troops on 29 March. The UNRRA was in charge of registering persons from the member states of the U.N. (founded in October 1945), looking after them, and helping them return to their home countries.
