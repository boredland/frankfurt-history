# forced labour camp

*Eulengasse 46*

![Picture postcard with the restaurant "Zur Eulenburg" in Bornheim](../../images/0533dc953124078640858853d1af85b9_original.jpg)
*1901 — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80372 | Description: Picture postcard with the restaurant "Zur Eulenburg" in Bornheim*

In the Katharina Jockel guesthouse at Eulengasse 46 there was a camp for forced labourers used by the August Klüser Nica Nickelindustrie company. Initially, in the autumn of 1942, it accommodated 4 persons from Eastern Europe. From then on, the number increased. In 1943 there were 12 Ukrainian men as well as an unknown number of persons from Italy and France living here. In 1946 the 6th police district reported that 4 Frenchmen had also been lodged here in the period from 1 July 1942 to March 1945. They were liberated by the U.S. Army on 29 March 1945 and taken back to France. This document is one of altogether 329 compiled by the Allies in their efforts to shed light on forced labour in Frankfurt and now in the holdings of the Arolsen Archives.
