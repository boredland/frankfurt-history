# Bomb shelter

*Petterweilstraße 68*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Petterweilstraße](../../images/687414fb295657237a660387c429222c_original.jpg)
*1981 — Author: Joachim Sändig | License: HMF Ph17181 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Petterweilstraße*

This bomb shelter was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 761 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction began in August 1941. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office and then the Evangelical welfare organization used the shelter for lodgings and as a storage space for relief supplies from abroad. Around 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, it was refurbished to offer protection for a few hours in the event of an attack. The building presently serves as a storage space.

Construction company: W. Franz Hoch- und Tiefbau, Hanau
 Architect: unknown
