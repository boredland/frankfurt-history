# Memorial plaque for the victims of "Katzbach" Concentration Camp

*Kleyerstraße 17*

![The memorial plaque for the prisoners of the Katzbach concentration camp.](../../images/906f3aee93b401806cc729e9601c9e19_original.jpg)
*2023 — Author: Thomas Altmeyer | License: gemeinfrei | Description: The memorial plaque for the prisoners of the Katzbach concentration camp.*

Commemorative Plaque at the Adlerwerke
Hard-Earned Commemoration

During the Nazi period, the Adlerwerke was a prominent armaments manufacturer. The company produced war vehicles as well as weapon and ammunition parts. To this end, it exploited forced laborers, prisoners of war, and concentration camp inmates. In August 1944, a subcamp of the Natzweiler concentration camp in Alsace was established here under the code name “Katzbach.” 1,616 inmates, primarily Poles, were interned here at close quarters and under inhumane conditions. They were exploited for their labor and exposed to air raids without protection. 527 of them died of illness, exhaustion, or undernourishment. Many were murdered by the guard units and buried in Frankfurt’s Main Cemetery. Shortly before the arrival of the U.S. Army in late March 1945, the surviving forced laborers were forced to set out for the Buchenwald and Bergen-Belsen concentration camps.

The commemorative plaque was mounted in the 1990s on the initiative of the works council at what was, by then, called the “Triumph-Adler” company. Its unveiling took place on December 14, 1993 in the presence of former camp inmates who had come on invitation from the works council as well as Ernst Kaiser and Michael Knorn. The two teachers had researched the history of the “Katzbach” concentration camp. The plaque then disappeared for several years during protracted construction work on the factory building. In 1998, it was installed on the façade facing Kleyerstrasse, where it can still be seen today.
