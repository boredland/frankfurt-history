# Bomb shelter

*Goldsteinstraße 302*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Goldsteinstraße](../../images/a56876dfe1b6b5aaa8b0b00e2b6ec584_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Goldsteinstraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and provided protection to 366 persons on two floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction began in December 1940. As a way of camouflaging this shelter, it was designed in the style of the low buildings nearby. The original exterior as well as the interior fittings have survived partially intact. At the south entrance is a keystone with a depiction of a mother with four children and the inscription “Erbaut 1941” (Built in 1941). After the war, the Archäologisches Museum and the Freies Deutsches Hochstift used the building as a storage depot. It has been a listed monument since 2012 and is now used for storage and as an event space.

Construction company: unknown
 Architect: Johannes Schmidt
