# Forced labour camp Maschinenwerke Frankfurt

*Schwedlerstraße 2-4*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier%20fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

Here Kolb, Rieber & Co. exploited the manpower of forced labourers who were presumably lodged on the company grounds. One of them was Bronius Silkai, a forced labourer from Lithuania. He worked in the machine factory from 1942 to 4 May 1944, when he was arrested by the Gestapo. The exact reason for his arrest is not known. After being interrogated and tortured by the Gestapo, he was committed to the “work education camp” in Heddernheim. On 15 July of the same year, he was moved to the “auxiliary hospital“ of the Kelsterbach “transit camp” and then deported to the Hadamar killing facility, where he was murdered on 13 November 1944. Forced labourers from Eastern European countries were systematically murdered in Hadamar from 1943 onwards. The murders were often carried out on grounds that the person had tuberculosis or was mentally ill. Bronius Silkai was a victim of this practice. An inflammation of the parotid gland was given as the cause of death.
The company grounds were completely destroyed by bombs on October 4, 1943 and subsequently rebuilt in makeshift fashion.
