# forced labour camp

*Eifelstraße 2*

![Picture postcard with exterior view and catering area of the restaurant "Zur schönen Aussicht" in Schwanheim](../../images/f03abf78c00b5f62412e2defe55ca1e7_original.jpg)
*around 1917 — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80404 | Description: Picture postcard with exterior view and catering area of the restaurant "Zur schönen Aussicht" in Schwanheim*

The I.G. Farben-Industrie, a manufacturer of dyestuffs, made use of forced labourers in its three Frankfurt chemical plants, Höchst, Griesheim, and Mainkur, from March 1940 onwards. The company operated more than twenty forced labour camps in Frankfurt. In 1943, a barracks camp for forced labourers working in the Griesheim plant was erected on the grounds of an inn at Eifelstrasse 2. Between 1943 and 1944, 21 foreign forced labourers were lodged here. By the end of 1945, I.G. Farben had exploited altogether some 10,000 forced and civilian labourers from more than 20 countries in Frankfurt alone. This was the only way of maintaining production that was vital to the war effort at a time when more and more employees were called up for military service. Among the forced labourers were prisoners of war as well as civilian workers who had been recruited voluntarily or forcibly in the occupied countries of Western Europe, but also many who had been deported to Frankfurt from Eastern Europe. Civilian workers were forced labourers who were neither prisoners of war nor concentration camp inmates.
