# forced labour camp

*Fahrgasse 10*

![Forced labor camp, Fahrgasse 10](../../images/71d707ec2322c5105412c41f0214393f_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: Rainer Emanuel | License: HMF, Ph06493 | Description: Forced labor camp, Fahrgasse 10*

Civilian workers were lodged in Klostergasse (now Fahrgasse). Widespread use was made of forced labourers—in business establishments from the small-scale shop to the large corporation, as well as on farms and in private households. In Frankfurt, the average number of forced labourers at any given time during the war years was approximately 25,000. Among them were many civilians who had been recruited abroad voluntarily or forcibly or simply deported. In the summer of 1944, 5.7 million foreign civilian workers were being exploited in the German Reich.
