# Stolen Place

*Feldbergstraße 21*

![Feldbergstraße 21](../../images/3276ca0e25fdaca29dd55ba7a57222de_original.jpg)
*1891 — Author: Keller | License: ISG | Description: Feldbergstraße 21*

The tenement at Feldbergstraße 21 / corner of Freiherr-vom-Stein-Straße originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Max Buchwalter. The property covered 434 square meters. There was a 7-room apartment on the first and second floor, which could only be partially rented out. In the future, it was to be converted into smaller apartments. At the time of the sale, Max Buchwalter was in exile in Bern, Switzerland. Due to persecution, he had to sell his property through his authorized representative to a non-Jewish Frankfurt medical councilor for the price of 43,000 Reichsmarks in a contract dated February 7, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 48,200 Reichsmark and the market value 44,000 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 7,140 Reichsmark. The contract costs, land transfer tax and estate agent fees were shared by the parties. The building authorities approved the contract on April 24, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
