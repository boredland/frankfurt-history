# Remand Prison

*Hammelsgasse 6-10*

![Courtyard of the remand centre/court prison, Hammelsgasse 6-10](../../images/Ph30608_jp_8a188b36527979d4499108f9b37b51f2_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: Gisa Hillesheimer | License: HMF, Ph30608 | Description: Courtyard of the remand centre/court prison, Hammelsgasse 6-10*

From 1933 to 1945, the Hammelsgasse remand and court custody prison held six to ten people persecuted by the Nazis for racist motives, or because of their sexual orientation, or political or religious convictions. This group also included, for instance, Jehovah’s Witnesses. Originally designed for 280 inmates, the prison held an average of 440 people between 1935 and 1938. Many prisoners died from the inhumane conditions as well as the effects of the torture they suffered at the hands of the Gestapo. At least seven cases are known of people committing suicide. During an air raid on 29 June 1944, a bomb hit the prison directly killing 16 people held in a communal cell. The prison was cleared on 26 March 1945 – a few days before the US troops arrived. On foot and by train, 72 prisoners were forcibly taken to Bamberg. Today, there is a memorial plaque on Building E at the Hammelsgasse District Court.
