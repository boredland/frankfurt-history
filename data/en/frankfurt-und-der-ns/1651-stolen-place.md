# Stolen Place

*Frankenallee 99*

![Frankenallee 99](../../images/9aa67d0e7fe892a8e8d88fbfc10ac94c_original.jpg)
*ca. 1928 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Frankenallee 99*

The four-storey residential and commercial building at the corner of Frankenallee 99 / Günderodestraße originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Selig Nussbaum. He lived at Westendstraße 28 at the end of 1938 and was in exile in mid-1941. The property covered an area of 550 square meters. On the ground floor and first floor there were two stores with associated apartments. There were also apartments on the upper floors. The annual rental income amounted to 9,003 Reichsmark. Due to the persecution, he had to sell his house to a non-Jewish Frankfurt doctor for 49,300 Reichsmark in a contract dated November 18, 1938. The latter had also acquired the properties Hölderlinstraße 27, Westendstraße 28, Goetheplatz 6 and 8 from Selig Nussbaum. The building authority held out the prospect of approving the purchase contract on payment of a compensatory levy in favor of the Reich. However, the buyer apparently withdrew from the contract, as Selig Nussbaum had to sell the building again by a proxy on June 25, 1941 at a price of 68,000 Reichsmarks: this time to a non-Jewish chemist from Rudolstadt and his wife. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 45,800 Reichsmark and the market value to 58,500 Reichsmark. The costs of the contract, including land transfer tax and estate agent's commission, were borne by the buyers. The building authority approved the contract on November 21, 1941 with the condition that the costs were to be divided and the purchase price reduced to 60,000 Reichsmark. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
