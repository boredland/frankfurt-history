# Bomb shelter

*Schleifergasse 5*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Schleifergasse](../../images/b59f1810392ceed5ff7fa2e2966791ca_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Schleifergasse*

This bomb shelter was part of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme”. It was made to look like a barn to protect it from Allied aerial attacks. The air-raid doors were moreover concealed behind wooden doors. The shelter offered protection for 434 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt could accommodate a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction work lasted from November 1940 to July 1942. In 1940, 30 persons were forced to work on the construction site. Built in part on the foundations of the synagogue, the shelter has been a listed monument since 1989. A new place of remembrance is to be established here.

Construction company: ARGE (Arbeitsgemeinschaft) Jobst & Milch
 Architect: Friedrich Karl Kur
