# Blanka Zmigrod

*Kettenhofweg/Niedenau*

![](../../images/be21b4063da90479fc5c97c0c6529915_original.jpg)
*Author: Franziska Mucha | License: HMF*

On February 23, 2021, about 100 people from Frankfurt met at the intersection of Kettenhofweg and Niedenau to commemorate the murder of Blanka Zmigrod. This commemoration was followed by the launch of a petition for the erection of a memorial to Blanka Zmigrod by Ruben Gerczikow. The petition was signed by almost 40,000 people. The memorial plaque was installed a year later. Blanka Zmigrod was born in Chorzów, Poland, on January 22, 1924. She survived the Shoah despite several stays in four concentration camps, including Auschwitz. After 1945, Zmigrod emigrated to Israel, from where she returned to Germany as early as 1960. She found her new home in Frankfurt's West End. Blanka Zmigrod was murdered on February 23, 1992 at the intersection of Kettenhofweg and Niedenau by a Swedish right-wing terrorist who shot her in the head. After the crime, he grabbed her handbag and fled. In Sweden, the perpetrator had already shot eleven people with a migration history between August 1991 and January 1992, for racist motives.
