# Legalised Robbery: How Aron Goldschmidt lost his house.

*When lines are crossed!*

![Sandweg 38](../../images/8eecdcebbadc356da481cf07f6f0b2ef_original.jpg)
*1960 — License: http://www.frankfurt-nordend.de/Images/1960_sandweg_mousonstr.jpg | Description: Sandweg 38*

The former master butcher Aron (called Adolf) Goldschmidt, born on January 4, 1872 in Breitenborn, lived in the corner house between Sandweg 38 and today's Mousonstraße (then Gwinnerstraße) with his wife Sophie Goldschmidt, born as Katz. In an address book from 1935, he is described as a “salesman”, perhaps because of the ban on slaughtering that had been in force for Jewish people since 1931/32. 
 
When Aron Goldschmidt offered his property at Sandweg 38 for sale for the first time in February 1939, he was not acting of his own free will. In order to obtain the assets of their Jewish fellow citizens, the National Socialists had set up a perfidious system to bureaucratically conceal their brutal plunder.

Jewish property and other assets had to be sold within a certain period of time according to the “Decree on the Use of Jewish Property” (December 3, 1938). The sale had to be approved by the responsible “foreign exchange office”. This had to ensure that the sale of Jewish property primarily filled the coffers of the German Reich and that emigrants did not take any valuables or foreign currency abroad (so-called Aryanization/dejudaization of Jewish assets).

The first prospective buyer was a farmer from Kefersheim in Rhineland-Palatinate. This village had to make way for the “Baumholder” military training area in 1939 and it is likely that the house at Sandweg 38 was to be purchased with the compensation money received. However, the sale was prohibited by the Frankfurt building authority on November 14, 1939 for unknown reasons. 
 
 A second purchase contract was notarized on September 28, 1939 with Friederike Küssner (“innkeeper”) and submitted to the Frankfurt am Main customs investigation office for approval on October 5, 1939. The purchase price was 47,500 RM, to be paid less a mortgage of 21,000 RM. The notary asks to be informed whether “an order has been issued against Goldschmidt” or “any measures are to be taken”.
 
On November 23, 1939, Aron Goldschmidt stated in a “self-disclosure” that he and his wife had a credit balance of 16 RM and property worth 26,500 RM. The foreign exchange office deducted a total of RM 10,691.95 from these assets for “all debts including the unpaid Reich flight tax and Jewish property tax. At this point, Goldschmidt no longer had any income of his own, because boycott campaigns and bans on practicing a profession had made it impossible for Jews to work since 1933. He estimated his current monthly expenses at 355 RM.

On November 30, 1939, Goldschmidt informed the buyer, Friederike Küssner, that he was only allowed to accept payments on his “restricted security account” and that cash payments to him (not even via third parties) were no longer permitted. The foreign exchange office informed him on December 4, 1939 that he was allowed to dispose of a monthly allowance of 350 RM. The sale was approved by the Frankfurt building authority on December 16, 1939. 
 
After the purchase price had been transferred to the blocked account on February 9, 1940, Aron Goldschmidt asked the foreign exchange office on February 12 to pay him RM 3,000 of it, as he had debts to settle. He repeated this request on March 16 as the “money recipients were pressing him for payment” and, as requested by the foreign exchange office, enclosed proof from his creditors. On April 3, 1940, the payment of the amount to Goldschmidt was approved by the foreign exchange office.

In September 1941, Goldschmidt informed the foreign exchange office of his new address at Baumweg 34. A year later, on 29 September 1942, a message from the foreign exchange office was sent to Goldschmidt's bank stating that, according to the order “on the basis of the Gestapo lists of evacuated Jews”, the “assets of Jews evacuated to the East” had been “confiscated for the benefit of the German Reich”. Below this, the names of Aron Goldschmidt and Sophie Goldschmidt are handwritten with their respective first names Israel and Sara and their last address, Baumweg 34. Also handwritten below this: The security order has been completed.

According to the victim database holocaust.cz, the couple was murdered by the National Socialists in the Theresienstadt ghetto shortly after their deportation from Frankfurt on 2 September 1942. Aron Goldschmidt died there on December 19, 1942, his wife Sophie Goldschmidt, née Katz, on February 9, 1943.

*Author: Lilly Breitkreutz, Dominic Diehr, Lawrence El Salim, Niklas Pieck*
