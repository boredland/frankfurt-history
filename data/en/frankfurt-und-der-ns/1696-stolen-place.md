# Stolen Place

*Kelsterbacher Straße 2-6*

![Kelsterbacher Straße 2-6](../../images/a02e0bb950aaa98de9ecb82443ebeccc_original.jpg)
*before 1941 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: HMF | Description: Kelsterbacher Straße 2-6*

With a contract dated October 3, 1940, the city acquired the developed property named "Frauenhof" with an area of 6,090 square meters from the bank director Ludwig Arnold and Prof. Dr. Albert Hahn for the price of 40,000 Reichsmark, who had to sell this property due to persecution. Later, the property at Kelsterbacher Straße 2-6 was used as a camp for civilian workers employed by the Mitteldeutsche Kettenfabrik Fritz Emmerich. The city's building authority, represented by the property management department, was listed as the buyer. The site was intended as a youth hostel.
