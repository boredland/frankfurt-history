# Home of Hans Lutz, member of the resistance

*Böttgerstraße 29*

![Apartment of resistance fighter Hans Lutz, Böttgerstraße 29](../../images/c130cc7cc71154662634e337f86a6ecd_original.jpg)
*1981 — Author: Joachim Sändig | License: HMF, Ph17152 | Description: Apartment of resistance fighter Hans Lutz, Böttgerstraße 29*

The plumber Hans Lutz (b. 1904) lived at Böttgerstrasse 29. He worked for the Vereinigte Deutsche Metallwerke (VDM) metalworks company, which was located on the grounds of the former Heddernheimer Kupferwerke (copper factory). Lutz was a member of the so-called Funken group, which published the resistance newspaper Der Funke: Informationsorgan der freien Gewerkschaften Süddeutschlands (The Spark: Information Organ of the Free Trade Unions of Southern Germany). He and the like-minded Georg Bender and Otto Meyer organized the newspaper’s distribution in Frankfurt. He also carried out courier services from Frankfurt to Switzerland and gathered information about the situations in companies for the purpose of passing it on to members of the resistance in exile. The group’s cover was blown when the Gestapo discovered the distribution network in 1938. On 14 October 1938 the People’s Court sentenced Hans Lutz and Georg Bender to ten years in prison, Otto Meyer to four years.
