# Stolen Place

*Fichtestraße 18*

![Fichtestraße 18](../../images/ed78cae1fb279179964da305c222d873_original.jpg)
*2012 — Author: Martin Starl | License: ISG | Description: Fichtestraße 18*

The three-storey residential building with courtyard and garden at Fichtestraße 18 originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Leo Alsbacher from Ludwigshafen am Rhein and his wife Karoline, née Schwab. The property covered an area of 331 square meters. The house contained one 6-room, one 5-room, one 4-room and one 2-room apartment as well as two 1-room apartments. The property was encumbered with a security mortgage from the Ludwigshafen tax office for any Reich Flight Tax amounting to 2,000 Reichsmark. Due to the persecution, the couple had to sell their property to a non-Jewish buyer from Würzburg for 29,000 Reichsmark in a contract dated March 22, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 27,600 Reichsmark and the market value to 30,900 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 5,436 Reichsmark. The parties shared the contract costs including land transfer tax and estate agent's commission. In July 1939, the buyer wanted to withdraw from the purchase on the grounds that the house had been "cleared for Jews, as a result of which Jews were moving in and out, which alone had a very detrimental effect on the order and cleanliness of the house with regard to vermin" and asked the building authority to refuse to approve the contract. The building authority pointed out that the seller would have to give his notarized consent. However, he refused to do so. In August 1939, the buyer then asked for accelerated approval of the contract. It can only be assumed that she had speculated on an officially ordered reduction in the purchase price due to vermin infestation. The building authorities finally approved the contract on 2 September 1939. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
