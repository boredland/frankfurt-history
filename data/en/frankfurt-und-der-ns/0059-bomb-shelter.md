# Bomb shelter

*Schmickstraße 18*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Schmickstraße](../../images/660fc0c59d7a59713688c9acdf559743_original.jpg)
*ca. 1943 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7B Nr. 1998-3775 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Schmickstraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 496 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction work lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. According to the construction site map, there was a hut on the site for prisoners of war who performed forced labour in the building project. After the war, the welfare office used the facility to lodge persons who had been bombed out of their homes. Around 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, the shelter was renovated. Today it serves as a “Kulturbunker“ and provides studios for artists as well as rehearsal rooms for musicians.

Construction company: Gebrüder Heunisch, Frankfurt
 Architect: Martin Leipold, Frankfurt
