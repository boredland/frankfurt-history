# Stolen Place

*Annastraße 12*

![Annastraße 12](../../images/9cb4340d5f83ebde6ab172dfcc9cd159_original.jpg)
*1983 — Author: Jutta Hofmann | License: ISG | Description: Annastraße 12*

The property at Annastrasse 12 originally belonged to Eugen Hess, formerly in his own house at Holzhausenstrasse 23-25. Eugen Hess was the owner of the export company Ph. Deutsch, Neue Mainzer Strasse 60. The Jewish owner, who had already emigrated to London in 1940, had to sell the chauffeur's house with garages and a 4-room apartment to the tenant of the house for the price of 9,000 Reichsmarks under a contract of sale dated April 1, 1940. The tenant had worked as a chauffeur and housekeeper for Eugen Hess from 1920 to April 1, 1938. The house at Annastrasse 12 had been built in 1922 specifically for his needs. After a lengthy back and forth, the authorities decided not to impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich because they recognized the favourable purchase price as a loyalty bonus for many years of service.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
