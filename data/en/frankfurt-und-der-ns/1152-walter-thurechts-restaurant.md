# Walter Thurecht's restaurant

*Großen Fischergasse 14*

![](../../images/7e2de8b2806345d122cce83cb75de7bf_original.jpg)

The bar at Grosse Fischergasse 14 had been in existence since 1899. (The street itself no longer exists.) Walter Thurecht took it over in 1934. He expanded it to include the building next door at Grosse Fischergasse 12. The licensing records contain evidence of Thurecht’s discrimination and denunciation going all the way back to the year he opened his establishment. For example, he was subjected to discrimination by the “Reich Unity Association of the German Catering Industry” (Reichseinheitsverband des deutschen Gaststättengewerbes). In 1937 he was arrested for violating Section 175. This law made male homosexuality punishable. Walter Thurecht’s father Max Thurecht wanted to manage the establishment while his son was in pre-trial detention but received no permit to do so. Instead, Max Pfefferle took charge of it in 1938. Pfefferle already had experience managing bars in the vicinity of the stadium.
