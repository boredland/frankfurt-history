# Bomb shelter

*Mühlgasse 3-5*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Mühlgasse](../../images/016e24264977fd52acd228f07f3883ab_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Mühlgasse*

This was the site of a bomb shelter that was part of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme” and provided protection for 412 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction work lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. This was the only bomb shelter in Frankfurt to be hit hard during the air-raid attack of 12 September 1944. On that occasion, 172 persons died and 90 were injured. The upper section of the Leipziger Strasse was also severely impacted. Afterwards, hundreds of Bockenheim residents protested at the bomb shelter with shouts of “Down with the war” and “No more war”. The shelter was repaired as late as 1944, and torn down in 2007.

Construction company: Diplom-Ingenieur Karl Kögel, Frankfurt
 Architect: Karl Christoph Gräf, Frankfurt
