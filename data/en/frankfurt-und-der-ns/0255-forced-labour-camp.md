# Forced labour camp

*Gwinnerstraße 42*

![Photo of a building belonging to Lurgi Werkstätten GmbH, which operated a forced labour camp on its factory premises](../../images/ddb3f71c2e1766b5d85109f1678c4686_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-30046 | Description: Photo of a building belonging to Lurgi Werkstätten GmbH, which operated a forced labour camp on its factory premises*

Between 1943 and 1945, the Lurgi Werkstätten, an operation for coal refinement, gasification, and fuel synthesis, lodged female forced labourers from Ukraine in two rooms on its factory grounds at Mousonstrasse 42 (now Gwinnerstrasse). The 6th police district reported this to the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration (UNRRA) in 1946. The UNRRA was in charge of registering persons from the member states of the U.N. (founded in October 1945), looking after them, and helping them return to their home countries. This was only one of altogether three camps in which the forced labourers assigned to Lurgi were housed. Other sources assume that 33 inmates from the Preungesheim prison were also put to work in armaments production at Lurgi.
