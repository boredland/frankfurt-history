# Stolen Place

*Fahrgasse 85*

![Fahrgasse 85](../../images/a1494896f691fa91c0aea1c1a5764c66_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Fahrgasse 85*

The residential and commercial building at Fahrgasse 89 originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Adolf Beckhardt, who was living in exile in New York at the time of sale, and Ludwig Beckhardt, who lived at Zeppelinallee 63. The property covered an area of 141 square meters. There was a store on the first floor and presumably apartments on the three floors above. Due to the persecution, they had to sell their house to a non-Jewish buyer from Marbach am Neckar and his wife for 21,800 Reichsmark in a contract dated April 29, 1940. The officially tax-assessed value was 21,800 Reichsmark and the market value was 23,900 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 4,260 Reichsmark. The costs of the contract and the land transfer tax were borne by the buyers. The estate agent's commission was shared between the parties. The building authority approved the contract on September 20, 1940 and reserved the right to set a compensatory levy in favor of the Reich in the event of a rent increase. The buyer had been a member of the NSDAP since May 1, 1933 and acted as a block administrator for the NSV.
