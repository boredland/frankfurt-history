# A Street for Lilo

*Out and about in Frankfurt with Lilo*

![Lilo, Gerdi, and Helmut reunited at Christmas in 1945](../../images/32ae84bdfc254152e97322d424162e78_original.jpg)
*1945 — Author: unbekannt | License: Privat, Lilo Günzler: Endlich Reden, Henrich Editionen Frankfurt | Description: Lilo, Gerdi, and Helmut reunited at Christmas in 1945*

Today there’s a Lilo Günzler Street in Frankfurt. It’s in the Schwanheim district. Günzler was Lilo’s last name after she married her husband Hans in 1957.
In 1965, the Günzlers moved to Schwanheim and Lilo joined various groups, for example the history club and the church congregation. And she had a job taking care of children at a school.
For a long time, she never talked about what had happened to her and her family during the Nazi period and the war.
In the early 1990s, Lilo’s husband died, and Lilo began to think more about her life story again.
Lilo first told her story in public about 15 years later, in 2005. From then on, she visited school classes and wrote a book about her memories. Talking helped her learn to deal with the horrible time she had been through.
Lilo died in 2020 at the age of 87.
The street in Schwanheim was named after Lilo in 2021 to honor her work and remember her story.
And it’s important to remember Lilo’s story. It stands for the stories of many other children who are today forgotten and can no longer tell their stories. 

Have a further look around the app. In it, you’ll find stories about other children and how they survived the Nazi period.

*Author: Susanne Thimm*
