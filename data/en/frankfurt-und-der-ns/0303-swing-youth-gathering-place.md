# swing youth gathering place

*Gallusanlage 2*

![Outdoor area of the ‘Rumpelmayer’ café](../../images/232904ac707c75cce2a6cf6ad5898ced_original.jpg)
*ca. 1940 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2003-126 | Description: Outdoor area of the ‘Rumpelmayer’ café*

Café Rumpelmayer was located not far from the theatre in Adolf-Hitler-Anlage (now Gallusanlage). It was a popular gathering place for actors and theatre critics. Young members of the swing scene also frequented it, among them, for example, the “Club der Kameruner”. Swing fans set themselves apart from the Hitler Youth and the League of German Girls with their clothing and hairstyles: The boys often had shoulder-length hair and wore wide, bellbottom trousers, patterned sports jackets, and hats pushed down over the brow. The girls wore makeup and clothes that accentuated their figures. Boys and girls moreover spent their free time together, thus resisting the strict gender segregation of the National Socialist youth organizations.
