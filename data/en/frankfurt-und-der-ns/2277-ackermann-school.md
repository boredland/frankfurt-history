# Ackermann school

*Ackermannstreet 37*

Ackermannstraße 37

![The Ackermann School damaged in a bombing raid.](../../images/Geschichts_99bbd18f13c2fd80bcb991fd431ce83a_original.jpg)
*1945 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Geschichtswerkstatt Gallus e.V. | Description: The Ackermann School damaged in a bombing raid.*

A women’s forced labor camp in a school

The Ackermann School housed a camp for women forced laborers from Czechoslovakia. The conditions for Czechoslovak forced laborers were somewhat better than for Polish forced laborers or people classified as “Ostarbeiter” (“Eastern workers”). They did not have to wear material badges on their clothing and, to an extent, were allowed to take part in social life.

Blažená Urbanová from Czechoslovakia had to work as a forced laborer from 1942 to 1945. In 1999/2000, she applied for compensation. When she sent in her completed questionnaire to the “Ausgegrenzte Opfer” (Marginalized Victims) Working Group in Frankfurt, she also included an envelope with several photos. There is no record of who took the photographs, or whether Blažená Urbanová herself can be seen in any of them. Nonetheless, they show the daily life of the women in the Ackermann School camp and their fellow forced laborers. Sometimes working 12-hour shifts, they were all deployed in Röder Präzision, a company in the aircraft industry.

*Author: Projekt „Tschechische Zwangsarbeiter*innen in Frankfurt“ am Gymnasium Römerhof / Geschichtsort Adlerwerke*
