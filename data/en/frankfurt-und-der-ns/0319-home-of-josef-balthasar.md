# Home of Josef Balthasar

*Baseler Straße 21*

![Drawing of Josef Balthasar's attic flat](../../images/4414c0200c253844514f919b0dffed5a_original.jpg)
*2022 — Author: Katharina Müller | License: VG Bild-Kunst, Bonn 2022 | Description: Drawing of Josef Balthasar's attic flat*

The tailor Josef Balthasar lived at Scharnhorststrasse 21 (now Baseler Strasse). In 1942 he hid the couple Sally and Hanna Goldschmidt in the attic of his house. On 20 May 1942, they had received a summons to report for deportation by mail. The reason for their persecution by the Nazis was their Jewish descent. They removed the yellow stars from their clothes and fled. After hiding in Balthasar’s home for thirteen weeks, they fled to the Swiss border. When they failed in their attempt to enter Switzerland, they returned to Balthasar. He was afraid the Goldschmidts would be discovered, however, so they changed hiding places several times. In September 1942 they were back with Balthasar in Scharnhorststrasse. Finally an underground organization in Strasbourg helped the couple flee to Switzerland on a freight train. There they lived in refugee homes and various camps. In 1946 they emigrated to the U.S.
