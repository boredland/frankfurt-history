# Kindertransporte (Children’s Transports) and Orphans' Roundabout

*Gallusanlage*

![Das Denkmal „Waisenkarussell“, das an die jüdischen Kindertransporte während der Nazi-Zeit erinnert in Frankfurt am Main.](../../images/3a0d5ce44aa062cfc48a424c900f2b18_original.jpg)
*2024 — Author: Victoria Schneider | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Das Denkmal „Waisenkarussell“, das an die jüdischen Kindertransporte während der Nazi-Zeit erinnert in Frankfurt am Main.*

In November 1938, several countries agreed to accept Jewish children from Germany in order to save them from persecution and death. The children were able to bring very little with them and had to leave their families behind. Their farewells were sad and painful affairs. Ruth Maier wrote in her diary that parents were crying on the platforms in the station while small children called out for their parents. The children had to adapt to life in different countries and learn the languages of their new foster families. After the war, many of them found out that their parents had died. These transports saved the lives of many children but also marked a negative turning point in many of their lives.

*Author: Laura Schmidt*
