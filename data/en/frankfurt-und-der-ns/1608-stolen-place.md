# Stolen Place

*Beethovenstraße 18*

![Beethovenstraße 18](../../images/cb2df661df41d9aa5e010bd8e68f65d9_original.jpg)
*License: ISG | Description: Beethovenstraße 18*

The property at Beethovenstraße 18 originally belonged to the widow Else Heilbut, née Bachmann. Due to persecution, she had to sell her apartment building with two 6-room apartments, a 5-room apartment, a 3-room apartment as well as a janitor's apartment and attics with a plot area of 596 square meters to a non-Jewish Frankfurt architect for 47,500 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated 31 October 1940. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 43,900 Reichsmark. The purchase price had to be paid into a blocked bank account at a foreign exchange bank, to which the seller had no direct access. The price inspection office for properties at the city's building authority set a compensatory levy to the German Reich in the amount of 2,600 Reichsmark. Mrs. Heilbut was already living in exile when the contract was concluded. Together with a partner, the buyer also acquired half of the houses Varrentrappstraße 67 and Parkstraße 16, which had formerly belonged to Jews, which they resold on July 1, 1939 after renovations.
