# Unemployment Aid

*Hohenstaufenstraße 8*

![Photo of the unemployment aid building](../../images/9c952578c89882c02983d017d69e0b65_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2001-155 | Description: Photo of the unemployment aid building*

Here the SA (“Sturmabteilung”), the paramilitary wing of the Nazi Party, established a home for the unemployed and homeless members of the SA and the SS (“Schutzstaffel”). The SA homes with their reasonably priced rents and food were an enticement and, with around two thirds of Germany’s six million unemployed receiving no unemployment benefits, one which many had to rely on. From early 1932, around 300 unemployed people were provided with food here every day. In April 1932, the home was closed after the emergency decree to secure state authority was issued banning the armed and violent SS and SA organisations. However, the SA had well-known supporters such as Crown Prince Wilhelm, the oldest son of the former Kaiser Wilhelm II. After the government under Heinrich Brüning fell in May 1932, the new Reich Chancellor Franz von Papen lifted the ban in June that same year. On 30 January 1933, the staunch right-wing conservative Papen was appointed as vice-chancellor under Adolf Hitler.
