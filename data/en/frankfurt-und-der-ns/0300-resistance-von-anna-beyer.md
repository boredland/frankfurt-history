# resistance von Anna Beyer

*Eiserner Steg*

![Resistance action by Anna Beyer, Eiserner Steg](../../images/c7915bf9ff03f02d4d715689bfa71171_original.jpg)
*2022 — Author: Katharina Müller | License: VG Bild-Kunst, Bonn 2022 | Description: Resistance action by Anna Beyer, Eiserner Steg*

The resistance activist Anna Beyer had a suitcase especially primed to print the words “Down with Hitler” on the pavements of different streets in Frankfurt. It was Helmut von Rauschenplat who had come up with the plan. Rauschenplat was the head of the German branch of the Internationaler Sozialistischer Kampfbund (ISK; International Socialist Militant League), of which Anna Beyer was a member. Mounted on the bottom of the suitcase was a sponge saturated with a special ink. Thanks to its chemical composition, the letters only became visible after a while. One location of this protest campaign was the Eiserner Steg, which was already in use as a pedestrian bridge back then. In 1936, Anna Beyer opened a vegetarian restaurant in Steinweg in Frankfurt with the aim of using its earnings to finance ISK resistance activities. She had adopted the idea from the vegetarian restaurant of the ISK in Cologne after a visit there.
