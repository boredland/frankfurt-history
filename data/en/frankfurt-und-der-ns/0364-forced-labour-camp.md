# Forced labour camp

*Dieselstraße 30-40*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier%20fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

The Matra Works, an aircraft and armaments manufacturer, operated a forced labour camp at Dieselstrasse 30–40. Until 1942, there was an internment camp for Sinti and Roma on an adjacent lot at the corner of Karl-Benz-Strasse and Dieselstrasse. After the departure of the Sinti and Roma, the Matra company used that camp to lodge its forced labourers. At the end of September 1943, 13 Russians and 43 Belgians had their lodgings here in three barracks. For 1943, 10 Russians, 35 Lithuanians, 102 Belgians (including 21 women), 1 Croatian, 1 Dutchman, and 77 French (including 23 women) were registered here. The camp was in existence until 1944. The Matra Works also lodged forced labourers at Niddastrasse 45.
