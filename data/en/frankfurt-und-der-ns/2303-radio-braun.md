# Radio Braun

*Idsteiner Street 91*

![Braun Radio, Idsteiner Street 91](../../images/d311f81f6f96f49f417e9c2cd46b2bfd_original.jpg)
*1946 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Braun Archiv | Description: Braun Radio, Idsteiner Street 91*

Forced Labor Camp

The Max Braun company, a specialist producer of radios and sound equipment, operated a barrack camp for “Eastern workers” on its premises at Idsteiner Straße 91. During the Second World War, seventeen Russians and twenty-three Ukrainians, all women, had to live there as forced laborers.

The Braun family company was founded in Bockenheim in 1921. Initially with four employees, it was based on the third floor in a rear house at Jordanstraße 2. Five years later, it moved to Kiesstraße 41-45 with a staff of twenty, rapidly growing to a workforce of 180.

In 1928, the Max Braun company relocated to Gallus, a new neighboring city district. There, 400 people worked on the five floors of its newly built factory at Idsteiner Straße 91. A second factory was soon opened at Mainzer Landstraße 196, and by 1938 the company had around 1,000 employees dealing with orders and producing electrical devices.

When factory workers were conscripted by the Wehrmacht for the Second World War, Max Braun also tried to obtain foreign workers from the Labor Office – which required proof of the requisite accommodation. Given the scarcity of building materials and labor, this was difficult. Fifty-two French and forty-four Flemish workers could be accommodated in the Volkshaus Sossenheim, Siegener Straße 22. For the other approximately 400 forced labourers, both men and women, there is no clear record of where they were accommodated.

However, the euthanasia crime on a Ukrainian forced laborer is clearly documented.

From November 1942 until 15 August 1944 when she was nearly twenty-four years old, Anna Melnyk also worked in the barracks on the premises at Idsteiner Straße 91. On 16 August 1944, she was taken to the Hadamar psychiatric hospital and killing center and murdered there. The young woman may have become ill with tuberculosis or suffered from depressions in the face of her forced and degrading situation.

*Author: Christine Hartwig-Thürmer*
