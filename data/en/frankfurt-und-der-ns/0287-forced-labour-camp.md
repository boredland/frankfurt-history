# Forced labour camp

*Vilbeler Landstraße 7*

![Photo of a machine hall of the Heinrich Wörner company, which operated its own forced labour camp on its premises at Vilbeler Landstraße 7](../../images/5eeee577ca6c4409c906b787af84212f_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: Max Göllner | License: HMF Z.2005Göllner000007 | Description: Photo of a machine hall of the Heinrich Wörner company, which operated its own forced labour camp on its premises at Vilbeler Landstraße 7*

The Heinrich Wörner company, a manufacturer of precision turned parts, operated a camp of its own on its factory grounds at Vilbeler Landstrasse 7 from 1943 to 1945. The forced labourers were housed in barracks. In the autumn of 1942, 54 forced labourers from Russia, including 31 women, had their lodgings here, in 1943 76 persons from Ukraine, among them 30 women, as well as 1 Belgian, 1 Dutchman, 1 German, and 21 women from Russia. The Wörner company operated altogether three forced labour camps in Frankfurt during the war years.
