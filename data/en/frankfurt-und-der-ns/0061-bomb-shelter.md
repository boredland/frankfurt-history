# Bomb shelter

*Thudichumstraße 9-11*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Thudichumstraße](../../images/2f55072a0c200773773127f2d13602c5_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Thudichumstraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and served to protect residents of Rödelheim. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from using the shelters. This one offered space for 979 persons on four floors. That made it one of the city’s larger air-raid protection facilities. Construction work lasted from August 1941 to May 1943. At that point, however, only the shell construction was complete; the building still lacked heating, ventilation, and interior fittings. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. After the war, this shelter served as a collecting point for clothing and commodity donations for refugees and other persons in need. It was torn down in 2016, and a nursing home was built on the site.

Construction company: Joseph Kunz & Sohn, Frankfurt-Höchst
 Architect: Friedrich Karl Kurz
