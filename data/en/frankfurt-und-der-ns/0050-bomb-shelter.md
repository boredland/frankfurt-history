# Bomb shelter

*Mörfelder Landstraße 128*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Mörfelder Landstraße](../../images/b026bedcc6563e5de19ec17f5242acb0_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Mörfelder Landstraße*

This bomb shelter was built for the personnel of the Reich Railway, the fire brigade, and the rescue service. It provided space for 418 persons on three floors. It was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. Construction work lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. In 1940, 30 persons were forced to work on the construction site. After the war, the welfare office used it to lodge pregnant and elderly refugees. Towards the end of the Cold War the shelter was refurbished to provide protection for a few hours in the event of an attack. Today it is used by the fire brigade. A large proportion of the original façade is still intact; changes have been made only on the south side.
 Construction company: ARGE (Arbeitsgemeinschaft) Groß-Frankfurt
 Architect: Hans Martin Born
