# University Dermatology Clinic

*Eschenbach-Straße 14 (main entrance to the former municipal hospital)*

![The building of the University Dermatology Clinic at Sachsenhausen Municipal Hospital. Photo: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt](../../images/d49105c9bd0ec69f14353b342636a17a_original.jpg)
*unkown — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt, S7A2002 75 | Description: The building of the University Dermatology Clinic at Sachsenhausen Municipal Hospital. Photo: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt*

One of the subdivisions of the municipal Sachsenhausen Hospital opened in 1884 was a clinic for “skin diseases and syphilis.” This clinic served “specifically for the purpose of admitting girls with venereal diseases who had been committed by the police.” In the Weimar Republic, a pattern evolved in how the authorities dealt with prostitutes and the prostitution of suspicious women. The “care office” (Pflegeamt) had them taken to the dermatological clinic to be examined for venereal disease, from there to the psychiatric clinic for psychiatric examination, and from there, in almost all cases, to the Hadamar regional “mental sanatorium” for “discipline through work.” Under Nazi rule, the treatment of such persons grew harsher. The National Socialists degraded prostitutes and women leading nonconformist sex lives as “asocial.” As in the Weimar Republic, they continued to have these women committed to the dermatological clinic for examination, from there to the psychiatric clinic, and nearly always from there to the Hadamar “sanatorium.” Now, however, the medical personnel of the psychiatric clinic were also under orders to arrange for the women’s sterilization. The social maintenance office took the necessary steps to have both Sophie Gotthardt and Otto Kohlmann sterilized, including their committal to the Hadamar regional sanatorium.

*Author: Frauke Steinhäuser*
