# Publishin House of Deutsche Arbeitsfront GmbH Gaufiliale Hessen-Nassau

*Roßmarkt 12*

![Picture postcard with the Roßmarkt](../../images/d219c62bb3c53495ab26b566c657cec5_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80384 | Description: Picture postcard with the Roßmarkt*

In 1940, this was the location of a branch of the German Labour Front publishing company, which had its headquarters in Berlin. On 10 May 1933, the Labour Front had supplanted the trade unions that until then had represented workers’ interests. Thousands of trade union members were persecuted, tortured, and murdered. The aim of the Labour Front was to keep control over employees and form them in the National Socialist spirit. It was one of the Nazi organizations with the highest number of members, and accordingly influential. With a turnover of some two million reichsmarks, it developed to become one of the largest German companies. The bookselling and publishing trades were among the sectors in which the German Labour Front was active.
