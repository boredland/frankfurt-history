# Stolen Place

*Battonstraße 70 und 70a*

![Battonstraße 70 und 70a](../../images/d54ab99585733e092f3a75cd6178c74d_original.jpg)
*ca. 1941 — License: ISG | Description: Battonstraße 70 und 70a*

The properties at Battonstrasse 70 and 70a originally belonged to the Swiss Salomon Mayer. One of the buildings served as an apartment building with ten 3-room apartments, eight 2-room apartments and one 1-room apartment. House number 70a housed six stores as well as the "Hotel König von England", which apparently accommodated permanent tenants. Due to persecution, Mayer had to sell his houses with a plot size of 582 square meters to a non-Jewish merchant from Brebach / Saar for the price of 160,000 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated 1 November 1938. The officially tax-assessed value of the property amounted to 145,000 Reichsmark. According to the local NSDAP group in Brebach, the buyer belonged to the NSDAP and was "politically reliable". The buyer successfully defended himself against the compensatory levy to the German Reich of 15,000 Reichsmark imposed by the building authority. The purchase price had to be paid into a blocked bank account controlled by the currency regulation authority (“Devisenstelle”), which the seller could not dispose of directly.
