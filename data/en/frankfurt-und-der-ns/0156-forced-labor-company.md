# forced labor company

*Wendelsweg 64*

![Forced labor company, Wendelsweg 64](../../images/3bccbf47f9b782876394da9e9b5f83b7_original.jpg)
*ca. 1900 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-29407 | Description: Forced labor company, Wendelsweg 64*

The Henninger brewery, which was based at Wendelsweg 64, made use of forced labourers. In 1942, 10 Polish women, 4 Polish men, 1 Ukrainian, 1 Frenchman, 3 Belgians, and 1 Dutchman were registered as working for the company, in 1943 2 Russian men, 17 Ukrainian women, 2 Ukrainian men, 4 Polish women, 10 Polish men, 5 Lithuanian women, and 5 Lithuanian men. It is not known where they were lodged. In August 1943, however, the company verifiably housed 2 forced labourers from Western Europe in a jointly used camp at Allerheiligenstrasse 26.
