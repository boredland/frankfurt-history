# New synagogue

*Conrad-Weil-Gasse*

![Devastated interior of the synagogue.](../../images/4807963eaab2604ffd66b929ee83c7d4_original.jpg)
*License: HMF | Description: Devastated interior of the synagogue.*

The New Synagogue of the Jewish community of Bergen-Fechenheim, located in Erbsengasse, was consecrated in 1854. It was the vibrant cultural centre of Jewish life in Bergen. In existence since the early eighteenth century, the community counted some 250 members by 1800. By 1932, the number had declined to 190 persons belonging to 25 families. The population continued to decrease under the Nazi persecution measures that began the following year.
 
 During the 1938 November Pogroms, the synagogue was laid to waste by the Nazis. On the morning of 10 November, 16 Jewish men were arrested and deported to Buchenwald. Jews died in Fechenheim as a consequence of the brutal attacks. Salomon Strauss, for example, is known to have died of heart failure after such an assault. Today a small permanent exhibition at Marktstrasse 30 commemorates Jewish life in Bergen-Enkheim.
