# Julius-Munk-Anlage

![View of the Julius Munk complex.](../../images/94540682303b810f983bc097817c82c8_original.jpg)
*2022 — Author: Thomas Altmeyer | License: gemeinfrei | Description: View of the Julius Munk complex.*

A Sports Ground as a Place of Remembrance for a Doctor

On October 8, 2016, the sports ground and green area between Kleyerstrasse, Schwalbacher Strasse, and Weilburger Strasse were named after Dr. Julius Munk. After Austria’s annexation to the German Reich, Munk had been subjected to persecution for being Jewish. He fled to France and joined the resistance. Following his arrest, Dr. Munk was sent to the “Katzbach” concentration camp as an inmate. There he helped many of his fellow inmates. Shortly after his liberation he died of the consequences of his imprisonment.

Until 1939, this site was home to the buildings of four companies whose owners were persecuted as Jews under the Nazi regime. In the years 1936 to 1938, they were compelled to sell their companies to the Adlerwerke below value or were dispossessed. The Nazis referred to this practice as “Aryanization.” Later there were Adlerwerke factory buildings and two barrack camps for the company’s forced laborers on the site.
