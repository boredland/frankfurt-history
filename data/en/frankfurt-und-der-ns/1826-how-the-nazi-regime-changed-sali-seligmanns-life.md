# How the Nazi regime changed Sali Seligmann's life

*When lines are crossed!*

![Signature of Sali Seligmann from 1956.](../../images/751114ddcc4a5a9e2dcf6037aab920e9_original.jpg)
*1956 — Author: Andreas Schanne | License: HHStAW, 519/320560 ODER HHStAW, 518, 58809 | Description: Signature of Sali Seligmann from 1956.*

This house has an important and tragic history. It originally belonged to Sali Seligmann, born on September 8, 1882, a Jewish citizen of Frankfurt. During the National Socialist era, however, he had to give up his home and flee to protect his family. Sali was held in the Dachau concentration camp from November 14, 1938 to December 21, 1938, because of his Jewish faith. After his time in the concentration camp, he was forced to sell his residential and business property in Frankfurt am Main to Heinrich and Kurt Jammer for 57,500 Reichsmark on February 9, 1939, far below its value. This forced sale was part of the systematic ‘Aryanisation’ („Arisierung“) through which Jewish citizens were expropriated.

On August 13, 1940, Seligmann also received a tax assessment for the so-called Reich Flight Tax („Reichsfluchtsteuer“), which was imposed on all Jewish citizens who emigrated. His assets were assessed at 4,172 Reichsmark, of which a quarter - 1,043 Reichsmark - was taken from him as tax. This tax measure was a further barrier designed to make it more difficult for Jewish people to emigrate to a safe foreign country and to further reduce their already severely limited assets.

Shortly afterwards, on September 23, 1940, Sali and his family (two other people, names unknown) finally fled by ship from Genoa to New York. In New York, Seligmann campaigned for compensation for the professional and economic damage he had suffered between January 1, 1936 and September 8, 1952. After a long legal battle, he was finally awarded compensation totalling 25,000 Deutsche Mark. Sali lived on Creston Avenue in New York until 1985. After that, his trail disappears. Details about the rest of his life and information about his death are not known.

*Author: Laura Wand, Andreas Schanne, Lukas Seeberger, Yanaphon Sanitsom*
