# Remembrance of Paul Arnsberg

*Paul Arnsberg Platz*

![](../../images/5c5a2d08cef9f88a7aa325b440d9c3a2_original.jpg)
*Author: © Institut für Stadtgeschichte*

Paul Arnsberg was born in Frankfurt am Main in 1890 to Jewish parents. After completing his doctorate in law, he joined the judiciary, was dismissed after Hitler came to power and fled to Palestine in 1933; 25 years later, he returned to the city of his birth.
Arnsberg was passionate about journalism and history. As early as the 1920s, he wrote for the Frankfurter Zeitung and was active in the Frankfurt Jewish community. In exile, he married Rosl Abramowitsch and founded the largest distributor of newspapers and books in Israel.
He returned to Frankfurt in 1958 and worked as a journalist again, from 1960 for the Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung. He was a board member of the Jewish community and worked to promote understanding between Germans and Jews. As a historian, Arnsberg became known above all for his documentation of the history of Jews in Frankfurt and Hesse. Pauls Arnsberg died in 1978.
As part of the restructuring of the Ostend, a square was named after Paul Arnsberg in 2010 and a stainless steel memorial column was installed for the namesake the following year. It was designed by the sculptor Clemens M. Strugalla. A simple relief shows a portrait of Arnsberg with biographical details, stylized motifs of former Jewish life in the Ostend and Jewish festivals.
