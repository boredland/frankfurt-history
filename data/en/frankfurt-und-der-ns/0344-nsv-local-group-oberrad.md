NSV local group Oberrad

The Oberrad branch of the National Socialist People’s Welfare organisation (NSV) was also located here. After the German Labour Front, the People’s Welfare was the second largest mass organization during the Nazi period. Even so, its services were solely reserved for those considered “Volksgenossen”, members of the so-called people’s community, and no one else in need received its support. The People’s Welfare viewed the homeless and street beggars as competitors in its own efforts to collect donations, and they were branded as “asocials” and persecuted. During the war, the People’s Welfare provided relief to refugees and persons bombed out of their homes. To this end, it also acquired the furniture and clothing of Jews who had fled the country or been deported, and was involved in the auctions of these looted goods.
