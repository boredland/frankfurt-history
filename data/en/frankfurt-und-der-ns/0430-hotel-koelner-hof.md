# Hotel "Kölner Hof"

*Mannheimer Straße 5*

![](../../images/NS0880_jpg_aa571b061cf0837456557ddb56c5b6e2_original.jpg)

Even in the late 1800s, the Hotel Kölner Hof at Gneisenaustrasse 1-5 (now Mannheimer Strasse 5) was a meeting point for antisemites. In 1895, when proprietor Hermann Laass publicly announced that Jewish guests were not welcome in his hotel, he was also encouraging guests hostile to or prejudiced against Jewish people. These included, for instance, Frankfurt’s National Socialists, who met here regularly in the 1920s. From 1924 to 1928, Hermann Laass was a member of Frankfurt’s city council assembly for the far-right German National People’s Party (DNVP). In 1933, he then joined the Nazi Party and had a massive swastika set on the roof of his hotel.
