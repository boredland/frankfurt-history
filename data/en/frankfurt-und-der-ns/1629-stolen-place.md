# Stolen Place

*Ebersheimstraße 5*

![Ebersheimstraße 5](../../images/b1ef69f20e06bffd371b4dfcb88dca75_original.jpg)
*ca. 1950 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Ebersheimstraße 5*

At Ebersheimstraße 5, there was a Jewish children's home founded in 1863 for boys from socially disadvantaged families from Frankfurt and the surrounding area, who also received education here. The owners were the Julius and Amalie Flersheim Foundation and the Ignatz and Anna Sichel Foundation. However, the property itself was a modern new building from 1929. With a contract dated August 26, 1942, the city acquired the educational home for Israelite boys with a property area of 1,800 square meters from the Reich Association of Jews in Germany based in Berlin-Charlottenburg, in which the Jewish community and the Israelite religious community had been forcibly united, for the price of 125,000 Reichsmarks. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 49,600 Reichsmark and the market value 130,000 Reichsmark. The property was to continue to serve as a children's home. However, the Jewish residents long since had to leave their home. After the November 1938 pogrom, 21 children were able to flee into exile in Britain the following spring with the help of Braon James de Rothschild. Ten children had to remain in Frankfurt. Their fate is unknown. The Gestapo deported the teacher Julius Flörsheim, his wife and the teacher Käthe Casparius to the Lodz ghetto during the first major deportation from Frankfurt on October 19, 1941. The official handover of the property was to take place on July 1, 1942. In fact, the welfare office had already been using the building as a children's home for some time. On September 7, 1942, the building authority asked the district president for accelerated approval, citing the imminent deportation of the Reich Association's representative Dr. Arthur Kauffmann. The district president then granted his approval on September 15, 1942.
