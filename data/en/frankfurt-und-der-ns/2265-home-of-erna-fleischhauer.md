# Home of Erna Fleischhauer

*Wolfsgangstraße 123*

![The house at Wolfsgangstraße 123 no longer exists. Parkstraße 20 now stands on the site.](../../images/b8e5b2e76a8073a4862d03845245f98c_original.jpg)
*2025 — Author: Jasmin Klotz | License: gemeinfrei | Description: The house at Wolfsgangstraße 123 no longer exists. Parkstraße 20 now stands on the site.*

The House Search

In 1938, the “Central Reich Office for Combating Obscene Images, Writings, and Advertisements” directed the Frankfurt criminal investigation department to search the apartment of Erna Fleischhauer. Erna was a trans woman who lived at Wolfsgangstrasse 123.

The search took place in connection with criminal proceedings against Hella Knabe. During a search of Knabe’s home, a letter from Erna Fleischhauer had been found in which she inquired about the price of white lace lingerie. Hella Knabe was known for helping trans and/or gender-nonconforming persons by obtaining their preferred clothing for them. She also took orders for items like hormone creams, books, shoes, and chastity belts.

During their search of Erna Fleischhauer’s home, however, the officers did not find any of the type of clothing perceived by society as feminine. All they found was the banned book, Das Lasterhafte Weib (The Dissolute Woman). Published by Agnes Esterházy, the book is in part about lesbian love and trans women. The foreword was written by Magnus Hirschfeld. After the police had confiscated the book, Erna Fleischhauer was interrogated.

Erna Fleischhauer’s interrogation

During the interrogation, Fleischhauer lied to the officers. She claimed that she had no contact to trans women. She also said that she had had no further contact to Hella Knabe. She asserted that she had ordered the lace lingerie for a young woman from a wealthy family. She had had a sexual relationship with the woman but did not want to say her name on account of the woman’s social standing.

The criminal file is today in parts extremely difficult to read. The interrogation minutes are fragmentary. The police seem to have believed Erna Fleischhauer’s claims, particularly as they had found only the banned book among her possessions. Despite extensive research, it has never been determined where Erna Fleischhauer kept her feminine clothing.

The expropriation of the house

A Nazi agency called the “Main Trustee Office East” was founded in 1939. Its purpose was to expropriate and manage the property of Polish citizens and the Polish state after German troops invaded Poland. The so-called “Special Department of the Old Reich” also dispossessed Jews from Eastern Europe who lived in Germany.

The agency also seized the house at Wolfsgangstrasse 123 and managed it. It has not been possible to establish the original owner’s identity. It is also not known whether Erna Fleischhauer was aware of the expropriation and if so to what extent. By December 20, 1944 at the latest, she was living at Schwindstrasse 14.
