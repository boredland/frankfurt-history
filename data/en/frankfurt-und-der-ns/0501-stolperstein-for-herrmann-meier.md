# Stolperstein for Herrmann Meier

*Am Rödelheimer Wehr 4*

Hermann Meier (1902–1943)

Hermann Christian Meier was born in Frankfurt am Main on December 25, 1902 and christened as a Protestant. He worked as a locksmith. In August 1941, he was sentenced to an eighteen-month prison term for “fornication with a man” under Section 175 of the German Criminal Code. He had engaged in a consensual relationship with a 19-year-old. In force from 1871 to 1994, Section 175 criminalized sexual acts between men and was severely tightened in 1935. After serving his sentence, however, Meier was not released but placed in “preventive custody” by the Frankfurt criminal investigation department. In 1942, he was transferred to the Buchenwald concentration camp. There the SS categorized him as a “homosexual.” He received the inmate number 4031. He was lodged in Block 30 and, like many other homosexuals, assigned to extremely heavy labor in a penal detachment in the quarry. Hermann Meier died in Buchenwald on September 15, 1943, allegedly of pneumonia.

The Stolperstein was initiated by gab magazine and the Initiative for a Memorial to the Persecution of Homosexuals and laid in 2008.

Stolpersteine

A Stolperstein—literally “stumbling stone”—is a 10 x 10 x 10-cm concrete block with a brass plate mounted on the upper side. The brass plate is inscribed with the name of the person and brief information about their biography and persecution during the Nazi period. Learn more about the Initiative Stolpersteine Frankfurt: https://stolpersteine-frankfurt.de/en
