# Bomb shelter

*Lortzingstraße 15*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Lortzingstraße](../../images/492d44a96576839671aeb79ad8856f49_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Lortzingstraße*

This was the site of a bomb shelter that provided protection for 771 persons. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering them. This shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. Construction work on it lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. In form and style, the building was designed to look like the surrounding structures as a way of camouflaging it. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office lodged refugees here. From 1995 to 2016 the shelter housed the private Explora Museum. The owner lived on the top floor. In 2021 it was sold and torn down.

Construction company: A. u. K. Dietrich, Frankfurt
 Architect: Franz Hufnagel, Frankfurt
