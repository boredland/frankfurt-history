# The vanished prison

*Hospitalstraße 18*

![](../../images/7d3426b7ff800130419088c39fd0715b_original.jpg)
*Author: Günter Thorwarth | License: Günter Thorwarth*

The former Frankfurt/Höchst prison was built in 1912 and demolished in 2014. During the National Socialist era from 1933-1945, it served as a "special prison for women who had fallen from grace". Among the prisoners at the time were many Eastern European women and at least 22 Jehovah's Witnesses.

Immediately after the end of the war, the American photojournalist Margaret Bourke-White visited the prison. She wrote, among other things, that an "Anti-Fascist Defense Committee" had imprisoned some National Socialists, including Prince August Wilhelm.

 

Further information can be found here: https://www.stadtteil-historiker.de/
