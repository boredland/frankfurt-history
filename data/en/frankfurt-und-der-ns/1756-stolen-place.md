# Stolen Place

*Schöne Aussicht 16*

![Schöne Aussicht 16](../../images/1f242c55c339a733421fda92fd029c87_original.jpg)
*ca. 1935 — Author: BLDAM | License: HMF,  Bildarchiv, Neg.Nr. 42 b 26/6438.1 | Description: Schöne Aussicht 16*

With a purchase contract dated March 29, 1939, the city acquired the developed property with an area of 787 square meters from the Jewish merchant Moritz Sachs(-Fuld) for the price of 71,300 Reichsmark. The building, a classicist palace, was known as the "Schopenhauerhaus" because the philosopher Arthur Schopenhauer had died in the first floor apartment in 1860. The city established the Schopenhauer Museum there immediately after the purchase. The memorable building was destroyed to the ground on March 22, 1944 during an air raid by the Royal Air Force.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
