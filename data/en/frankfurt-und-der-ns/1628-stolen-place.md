# Stolen Place

*Corneliusstraße 9*

![Corneliusstraße 9](../../images/aba2a2e0c540a076dc58b9a60e375181_original.jpg)
*before 1950 — Author: Postkartensammlung | License: ISG | Description: Corneliusstraße 9*

The property at Corneliusstrasse 9 belonged to the estate of the widow of master butcher Wilhelm Wolfgang. Cornelia Wolfgang, née Emden, who was persecuted for anti-Semitism, died on November 14, 1940. Her daughter was to inherit the property. For this reason, the Reich Flight Tax Office of the Frankfurt-West tax office had a security mortgage of 15,000 Reichsmark registered on the property in 1941 to secure the increased Reich Flight Tax due to the inheritance. The standard value of the residential building amounted to 50,400 Reichsmark. The municipal building authority approved the registration of the security mortgage.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
