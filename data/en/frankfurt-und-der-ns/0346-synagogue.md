# Synagogue

*Hermesweg 5-7*

The Löb Elias Reiss synagogue was located in the back yard building at Hermesweg 5-7 (now Julius-Heymann-Strasse 10-12). Inaugurated in 1887, it belonged to the Jewish religious school which operated a Torah study hall here. Founded in 1782 by a bequest from Löb Elias Reiss, the synagogue was initially located in the “Zum weißen Schwanen” house in the Judengasse. The new synagogue building in Hermesweg could seat 118 worshippers. The marble Torah Ark and the ornaments for the seven to eight Torah rolls came from the large main synagogue in Börnestrasse. The valuable furnishings included a silver goblet, a lectern of fine wood and a large candelabra as well as two Persian carpets donated by the Rothschild family. The religious school’s lecture hall had a grand piano and a library of around 500 volumes. On 10 November 1938, members of the Hitler Youth and the SA (Storm Troopers) desecrated this place of worship. They smashed the synagogue’s windows, threw ritual objects into the garden and set them on fire. A fire was also started in the building itself, but it could be quickly extinguished. Since the damage here was limited compared to the destruction of Frankfurt’s large synagogues in the November Pogroms, services could be held in Hermesweg until May 1941. Even in late October 1941 when the first large deportations from Frankfurt began, a wedding announced in the weekly Jüdisches Nachrichtenblatt was celebrated here. In May 1942, a Jewish home for the elderly (“Versorgungsanstalt für Israeliten”) moved into Hermesweg 5-7. Just a few months later in August 1942, the home’s residents were deported to Theresienstadt.
