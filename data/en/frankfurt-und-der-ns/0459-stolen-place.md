# Stolen place

*Ulmenstraße 20*

![Family residence Goldschmidt-Rothschild, Ulmenstraße 20](../../images/64fd1fcd03774f0c1751865baa4c3803_original.jpg)
*ca. 1882 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-13284 | Description: Family residence Goldschmidt-Rothschild, Ulmenstraße 20*

In 1889, the building at Ulmenstrasse 20 belonged to the banker Maximilian Benedikt Hayum von Goldschmidt-Rothschild. In the early 1880s, Marks John Livingston (originally Markus Löwenstein) had had it built as a stable. To this day the building is still called the Livingston Stable. In 1941, von Goldschmidt-Rothschild was forced to sell it to the Schleussner Photo Works. After World War II it was used briefly as a revue theatre, then served as a warehouse. Today it houses a restaurant.
