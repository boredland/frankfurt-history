# NSV local group Dornbusch

*Hansaallee 150*

![Photo of the "Haus der Jugend Dornbusch" (NSV local group Dornbusch)](../../images/8136e65dc5242c87ad33f470433e414e_original.jpg)
*before 1941 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: HMF Ph34715 | Description: Photo of the "Haus der Jugend Dornbusch" (NSV local group Dornbusch)*

This was the location of the Haus der Jugend (House of Youth), the most modern youth hostel in the 1930s in the south of the German Reich. Designed by architect Franz Thyriot, the hostel was constructed in around 1929. It was based on the architectural style in the “New Frankfurt” public housing programme, co-initiated by city planning commissioner Ernst May in the 1920s. The hostel’s opening ceremony on 1 June 1930 saw scenes of brawling between members of socialist youth associations and the Hitler Youth. With the police taking rubber truncheons to separate the two groups, the representatives of the Catholic youth movement, the German Scouts, and the Bündische Jugend, an umbrella organisation of free youth movements, all left the event, and the inauguration celebrations were cut short. A youth hostel until 1939, the building was seized by the Wehrmacht after the war broke out. Ultimately, it housed the Security and Aid Service (SHD) which functioned as a “firefighting police force” (“Feuerlöschpolizei”). After the end of the war, the American military administration used the building as a facility for cultural and social events until their troops left in the 1990s. In 1996, the Frankfurt Youth Council moved in here together with the non-profit Anne Frank association for youth work, founded in 1994 by committed Frankfurt citizens inspired by the project “In the Footsteps of Anne Frank”. In 2013, the association was renamed the Anne Frank Educational Centre. The building also houses many other cultural and social initiatives, such as the “Rathaus für Senioren” (Senior Citizens’ Town Hall).
