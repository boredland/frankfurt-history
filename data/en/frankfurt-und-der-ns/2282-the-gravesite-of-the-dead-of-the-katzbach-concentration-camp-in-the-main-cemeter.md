# The Gravesite of the Dead of the “Katzbach” Concentration Camp in the Main Cemetery

*Main Cemetery*

![In 2025, a glass memorial stele was erected. It bears the names of all 527 prisoners of the Katzbach concentration camp who were murdered in Frankfurt.](../../images/87f28160c11ef27d7b829c921959edab_original.jpg)
*2025 — Author: Maciej Rusinek | License: Privat | Description: In 2025, a glass memorial stele was erected. It bears the names of all 527 prisoners of the Katzbach concentration camp who were murdered in Frankfurt.*

A Polish Grave of Honor

This is the site of the mass grave for the dead of the “Katzbach” concentration camp in Frankfurt’s Gallus district. The U.S. military administration had the dead of the “Katzbach” concentration camp transferred to a mass grave in 1948. The Americans commissioned the Polish Catholic Mission to tend the grave and erected a stone cross on it. The cross bears the inscription “Ofiarom niemieckich obozów koncentracyjnych 1948 – Rodacy” (To the victims of the German concentration camps 1948. The compatriots). The Polish inscription makes the link to Polish history clear.

The majority of “Katzbach” concentration camp inmates were Polish men who had been deported to Germany in the context of the Warsaw Uprising of 1944. In 1972, the State of Hesse had the grave lined with stone slabs. They bore the names of the dead of the “Katzbach” concentration camp. However, many of the names were missing or misspelled.

In 1986, the Polish association “Bund der Polen Zgoda” donated a commemorative plaque that was laid on the gravesite. The inscription in Polish and German reads “Ofiarom niemieckich obozów koncentracyjnych 1948 – Rodacy” (To the victims of the German concentration camps 1948. The compatriots). In 1997, the LAGG association installed a new memorial stone which not only names the victims, but also those responsible for the crimes committed in the “Katzbach” concentration camp:

In remembrance / Here lie 528 people. / They died in the ADLERWERKE in Frankfurt am Main between August 1944 and March 1945. / They were annihilated by labor, forced labor. / They starved to death, died of exhaustion, of untreated illnesses, were beaten to death. / They died in the middle of Frankfurt under the oversight of the SS, the company management, the Dresdner Bank, and the City. The ADLERWERKE was an outpost of the Natzweiler concentration camp. / The womb is fertile still from which it crawled. Bert Brecht.

In 2025, on the initiative of the LAGG association and the Adlerwerke Historical Site, a glass stele was erected bearing the correctly researched names of all inmates murdered in the “Katzbach” concentration camp. This makes it possible to pay tribute to each individual. To this day, the mass grave is a place of commemoration of the Warsaw Uprising of 1944, especially for Frankfurt’s Polish residents.

*Author: Geschichtsort Adlerwerke: Fabrik, Zwangsarbeit, Konzentrationslager*
