# Bomb shelter

*Eppenhainer Straße 8*

![Photo of a bomb shelter in Eppenhainer Straße](../../images/a05049996b61646eb038f79e68493ab9_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter in Eppenhainer Straße*

This bomb shelter was part of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme” and offered protection to 414 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction work lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. 36 persons were forced to work on the construction site; 10 of them were prisoners of war. After the war, the shelter was used as a hotel, as many hotels had been destroyed. Around 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, it was refurbished to provide protection for a few hours in the event of an attack. It has been a listed monument since 2012.

Construction company: ARGE (Arbeitsgemeinschaft) Junior
 Architects: Karl und Stephan Blattner, Frankfurt
