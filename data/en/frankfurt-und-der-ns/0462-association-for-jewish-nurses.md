# Association for Jewish nurses

*Bornheimer Landwehr 85*

![Association for Jewish Nurses, Bornheimer Landwehr 85](../../images/815aa0eb1a6a26e7db878b8326f66fb2_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-26866 | Description: Association for Jewish Nurses, Bornheimer Landwehr 85*

The Association of Jewish Nurses had its offices at this site. Nurses of Frankfurt had founded this professional association in 1893. In 1933 it had a membership of 47 nurses and 14 pupils, of whom 21 had their lodgings in the building at Bornheimer Landwehr 85. In April 1939, the association was forced to sell the building to the city for 900,000 Reichsmarks, after which it was rented out to the Jewish community for three years. Then the property was transferred to the Hospital zum Heiligen Geist. Like other Jewish organizations, the nurses’ association was dissolved in early 1940. In November 1940 the Gestapo evicted the remaining residents, who were forced to move to the Jewish Hospital. Initially the building was then used by the university clinic. Several of the nurses verifiably escaped to the U.S. However, 99 of them did not manage to flee but were deported to the Terezín concentration camp or one of the extermination camps. The building was destroyed completely by an air raid in October 1943.
