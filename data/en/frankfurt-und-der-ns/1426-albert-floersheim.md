# Albert Flörsheim

*5 Places – 5 Stories*

![](../../images/56d4176ed426d52694f3a3477f8328fc_original.jpg)
*License: HMF, C27958+001.tif*

The Albert Flörsheim wine shop was located in Fischerfeldstraße 3b and 5. 
He and his family, who were Jewish, lived in Oberweg 4. His wine shop was well-respected and active throughout Europe. After the National Socialists came to power, his sales went down drastically. Therefore he had to dismiss his employes. 
In the year 1935 he was diagnosed with depression and underwent psychological treatment. After anti-Jewish laws made it impossible to operate his wine shop, he sold it and all his properties in 1938. 
Before the family fled to America, they had to pay compulsory charges on their assets. Albert Flörsheim never fully recovered from these events, and in 1940 he took his own life. In 1958 his kids sued Germany for damages, but the case was dismissed in the year 1962.

*Author: Laurenz Schramm, Konrad Pflüger, Benedikt Rahlwes*
