# Stolen Place

*Ginnheimer Landstraße 40-42*

![Ginnheimer Landstraße 40-42](../../images/ec82042e88c5fda75d894a4484a4509a_original.jpg)
*ca. 1910 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Ginnheimer Landstraße 40-42*

The "Erziehungsverein Frankfurt" ran the Westendheim reform school at Ginnheimer Landstraße 40-42, which was intended to protect and care for "at-risk young people" from neglect and was known as the "Perlenfabrik". In April 1933, the SA occupied the building, used it temporarily as a "wild" concentration camp and later as the headquarters of SA Standarte 63 and, from 1942 until the liberation in March 1945, as an SA group school in Hessia.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
