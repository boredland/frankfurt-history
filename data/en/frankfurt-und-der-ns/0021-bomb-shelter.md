# Bomb shelter

*Bebraer Straße 10*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Bebraer Straße](../../images/a657000d4897ae74fc55642b1012c564_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Bebraer Straße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. The residents of the Fechenheim district and persons travelling by way of the Mainkur railway station found protection from air raids here. The shelter was designed to look like the buildings in the industrial zone and was therefore difficult to recognize from the air. It offered shelter to 900 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt could accommodate a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction, which was carried out by the Philipp Holzmann AG company of Frankfurt, began in November 1941. Under National Socialism, Holzmann profited substantially from forced labour. Today this shelter is privately owned.
 Construction company: Philipp Holzmann AG 
 Architect: unknown
