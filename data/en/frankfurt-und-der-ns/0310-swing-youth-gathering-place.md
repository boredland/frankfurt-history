# swing youth gathering place

*Herxheimerstraße 10*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Herxheimer Straße](../../images/5a1f8122579b64515ab57c083299e1e8_original.jpg)
*1947 — Author: Fred Kochmann | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Ko Nr. 1668 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Herxheimer Straße*

This was a gathering place of the “Club der Kameruner”, a group of young people who listened and danced to swing. Swing is a style of jazz developed in the 1920s in the USA, primarily by black musicians. The National Socialists accordingly rejected it. Starting on 12 October 1935, there was a ban on broadcasting jazz on the radio. Swing fans set themselves apart from the Hitler Youth and the League of German Girls with their clothing and hairstyles: The boys often had shoulder-length hair and wore wide, bellbottom trousers, patterned sports jackets, and hats pushed down over the brow. The girls wore makeup and clothes that accentuated their figures. Boys and girls moreover spent their free time together, thus resisting the strict gender segregation of the National Socialist youth organizations.
