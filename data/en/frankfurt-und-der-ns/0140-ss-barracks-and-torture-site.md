# SS-barracks and torture site

*Hermesweg 10*

![Photo of an SS barracks and place of torture located in the former Klinger school](../../images/84919d5ac7bdf0cc613a4cdf4b35b2c2_original.jpg)
*n.D. — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF / Sammlung Church C80408 | Description: Photo of an SS barracks and place of torture located in the former Klinger school*

On April 24, 1933, Friedrich Krebs (acting mayor of Frankfurt) allowed the SS to use the former Klingerschule building. The SS housed several Hessian and Frankfurt offices here and trained its members militarily on the school grounds. In the cellars, the SS, the Schutzstaffel (Protection Squad) of the National Socialist German Workers' Party (NSDAP), tortured political opponents until 1937. They were accused of high treason.

The communist Karl Geist recalled his interrogation in July 1933:
"While four SS men held me by my arms and legs, two others beat me with a steel rod. The seventh SS man wrapped a leather noose around my neck. Whenever I fainted, they revived me by pouring a bucket of cold water over me. ... Beaten to the point of being unable to walk ... I lay in my cell in the police prison for days ... the fear ... - Would I survive further abuse in my condition?"
(Karl Geist, statement in the compensation proceedings in 1967 (abridged))

Those tortured here usually faced years of imprisonment in prisons or camps. The building was used as a business school from 1939 until it was destroyed in the war in 1944. Between 1941 and 1943, Frankfurt bailiffs auctioned off the household goods of Jewish families who had been deported in the gymnasium. The proceeds from the auctions went to the Gestapo and the Frankfurt tax office.
