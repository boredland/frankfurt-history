# Stolen Place

*Alt Rödelheim 23*

![Alt Rödelheim 23](../../images/4a9331d5bc2bed278a9733dc1d87c7d1_original.jpg)
*ca. 1920 — License: ISG | Description: Alt Rödelheim 23*

The property Alt Rödelheim 23 had originally belonged to the two widows Mina Nelken, née Bertuch, and Lina Meisels, née Nelken, both in Breslau, and the commercial employee Ludwig Nelken in New York. Due to persecution, the Jewish owners were forced to sell the apartment building to a non-Jewish carpenter's wife from Sossenheim for 30,800 Reichsmarks in a contract dated March 24, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 31,800 Reichsmark. The buyer's parents paid for the house and reserved the right to use it for life. The sellers all managed to flee into exile. The authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
