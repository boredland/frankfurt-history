# Forced labour camp

*Schmidtstraße 45*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

The Max Gerner aircraft manufacturer had its Plant III at Boelckestrasse 45 (now Schmidtstrasse). The company exploited forced labourers at this site. The Soviet prisoners of war presumably had their lodgings in a barracks camp on the factory grounds, having probably been assigned to Gerner by the Stalag IX B Wegscheide POW camp. Altogether 349 forced labourers were registered with the company in 1942. In 1943 the number rose to 671, of whom 493 were Soviet prisoners of war, 164 were women from the Soviet Union, and 14 were Italian men. Only little is known about their lodgings. The Gerner company also housed some of its workers in Richthofenstrasse and at Gutleutstrasse 394, but precise numbers are available only for Richthofenstrasse. In 1943, after its factories were damaged during aerial attacks, the company moved a part of its operations to Odenwald Forest. According to first-hand accounts, two of the factory workers came to the aid the Soviet forced labourers. Karl Schreiber reportedly gave them food and cigarettes. The Communist Georg Michel is said to have tried to inform them about the location of the front line. Michel was arrested by the Gestapo and sent to the front even though the charges against him were never proven.
