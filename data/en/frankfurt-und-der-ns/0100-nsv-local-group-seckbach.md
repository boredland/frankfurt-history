# NSV local group Seckbach

*Wilhelmshöher Straße 172*

![Photo fo the offices of the NSV local group Seckbach](../../images/b1ace9d4320e76bfffa0c67a291666cf_original.jpg)
*1932 — Author: Hannah Reeck | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-25447 | Description: Photo fo the offices of the NSV local group Seckbach*

The Seckbach chapter of the National Socialist Peoples’ Welfare was located in one of the buildings of the Zentgrafen School. Records in the board of education archives show that the welfare organization set up a private kindergarten here in 1941. The war economy now needed mothers as labourers, and in that context the People’s Welfare kindergartens came to play an important role. Crafting play weapons, playing war, and practising marching were all part of everyday life in these kindergartens. This shows how in particular little boys were introduced to their later role as soldiers. A prominent pioneer in kindergarten education under National Socialism was the physician Richard Benzing. In 1942 he suggested that small children spend nine hours a day out of doors all year long to toughen and strengthen them.
