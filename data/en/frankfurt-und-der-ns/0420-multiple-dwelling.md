# Multiple dwelling

*Schillerstraße*

![](../../images/isg_ffm_s7_61996f18f903475a7fd5d3768a7c467a_original.jpg)

The apartment building in Quinckestrasse 25 (now Königswarter Strasse) belonged to Rahel Mainz (1883-1947). She worked as a secretary and lived on the ground floor. Willy Mainz (1877-1953), a bank employee, lived on the first floor with his wife Berta, née Schwab. He had trained from 1895 to 1901 in the renowned Rothschild & Söhne bank in Frankfurt. Until 1931, he worked in Frankfurt for the Deutsche Bank and Disconto-Gesellschaft. Most likely, Willy Mainz was Rahel’s older brother. He was chairman of the board of trustees at the yeshiva at Theobaldstrasse 6, an orthodox school devoted to the study of rabbinic literature. Aged 61, he was arrested there by the Gestapo during the November Pogroms and taken to the Buchenwald concentration camp. Today, his file card with his inmate number 21430 is stored in the Arolsen Archives. The conditions in the Buchenwald concentration camp were catastrophic, and 61 people from Frankfurt died there. Although most of the Jewish men were released, they had to agree to leave Germany immediately. Their relatives were forced to transfer money for the men’s return journey from the camp, payments which often went into the coffers of the SS (Schutzstaffel). As Willy Mainz’s card shows, his wife made several transfers of money. After Willy Mainz was released at the end of November, he emigrated with his wife to Palestine (now Israel) via London. They left Germany on 4 December 1938 and arrived at their destination on 10 January 1939. Willy Mainz died in Tel Aviv in 1953. In 1938, Rahel was forced to sell her house to a non-Jew. She then emigrated to England and died in Maidenhead in 1947.
