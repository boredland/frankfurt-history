# Israelite Women's and Men's Health Insurance Fund

*Rechneigrabenstraße 18-20*

![Photo of an interior space at Rechneigrabenstraße 18-20](../../images/isg_ffm_s7_331ec944dc19ad48c2ce4830b5c2cf30_original.jpg)
*ca. 1938 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-18992 | Description: Photo of an interior space at Rechneigrabenstraße 18-20*

This was the location of the neo-classical twin buildings of the Hospital of Jewish Health Insurances, designed by the architect Rudolf Burnitz and constructed in around 1827. From 1829, house number 18 served the Jewish insurance companies for men, while from 1831 house number 20 provided care for the Jewish health insurance companies for women. From around 1923, the hospital complex became an old people’s home. 
 
 The hospital’s construction was generously funded by the Rothschild family, who also donated valuable religious and ritual objects to the hospital synagogue. Set between the staircases of the twin buildings, the synagogue had enough space for 77 men and 44 women. In Frankfurt dialect, it was called the “Kippeshdub” – derived from the Kupot, the mutual aid burial societies which took care of the sick and dying. In addition to the large prayer room, the synagogue also had a library with around 2,000 volumes of Hebraica and Judaica. Although the synagogue survived the November Pogroms unscathed, some especially rare pieces donated by the Rothschild family were kept in the Museum of Jewish Antiquities, looted and destroyed in those antisemitic riots. In 1939, the land and property at Rechneigrabenstraße 18-20 was forcibly sold to the city of Frankfurt. Under the planned “Altstadtgesundung” (‘Old Town recovery’) project to tear down and rebuild parts of the historic city centre, the old hospital buildings were to be demolished.
 
 Until September 1942, the hospital was used as a “ghetto house” and a collection point for deportations. As the Rechneigrabenstraße 18-20 compensation file shows, the SS destroyed and plundered the building, synagogue and library on the day of the last transport. The Jewish Restitution Successor Organization (JRSO), designated as the official custodian of heirless Jewish property, was active in the American zone from 1948 and registered a claim for compensation. The price of just the antiquities, furniture and ritual objects destroyed and looted by the SS was put at DM 965,720. However, in 1961 the compensation authority only agreed to pay the JRSO DM 120,000.
