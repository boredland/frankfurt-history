# Tax Office Frankfurt-Outer District Real Estate Office and Asset Disposal

*Hochstraße 18*

![Foto: Unbekannt, ca. 1920, Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-8853](../../images/d1e962510cda53b5f574fcadc8981ec6_original.jpg)
*ca. 1920 — Author: unbekannt | Description: Foto: Unbekannt, ca. 1920, Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-8853*

The Frankfurt-Suburbs tax authorities played a key role in looting citizens persecuted as Jews and set up an “Asset Utilisation Office” (“Vermögensverwertungsstelle”) as a department dedicated to this task. It collected, for example, the lists of property and assets Jews were required to register in detail under the ordinance passed in April 1938. The lists not only covered bank balances and deposits, but also insurances, stocks and shares, real estate and freehold property. This measure, later also implemented in occupied France, was just the first step on the road to expropriate those persecuted as Jews. The tax offices cooperated with other authorities, passing on information so that assets and property on these lists could be confiscated and expropriated. In some cases, the property seized had been left in store with haulers or forwarding agents by those forced to emigrate. Mainly, though, the assets and property belonged to deportees. Their household goods, furniture and art works were sold at public auctions – and so the general population also benefited from this systematic looting and expropriation. The profits from the auctions flowed into the Nazi regime’s coffers.
