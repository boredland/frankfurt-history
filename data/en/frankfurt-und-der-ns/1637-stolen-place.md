# Stolen Place

*Fahrgasse 150*

![Fahrgasse 150](../../images/9285e4799c40303e3187a896fb4fb945_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Fahrgasse 150*

The residential and commercial building at Fahrgasse 150 / corner of Allerheiligenstraße 91-93 originally belonged to the Jewish lawyer Dr. Adolf Eisenmann, Lilly Simon, née Eisenmann, and the Jewish widow Clementine Schaumberger, née Eisenmann. The first two sellers lived in Berlin. Mrs. Schaumberger lived at Wolfsgangstraße 16, where the property had an area of 280 square meters. The ground floor of the semi-detached house was occupied by six stores, the second floor was used by Deutsche Bank as a depository. There were eight apartments and three attics on the upper floors. Due to persecution, they had to sell their house to a non-Jewish Frankfurt manufacturer of sugar cubes and his wife under a contract dated December 5, 1938 for 141,500 Reichsmarks. The officially tax-assessed value was 141,500 Reichsmark and the market value 156,500 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 27,600 Reichsmark. The contract costs, land transfer tax and estate agent's commission were shared by the parties. The district president of Wiesbaden approved the contract on February 9, 1939, subject to a compensatory levy to the German Reich. The building authority waived the compensatory levy, but reserved the right to increase the rent.
