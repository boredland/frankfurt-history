# forced labour camp

*Große Friedberger Straße 18-20*

![Picture postcard with the Hotel Drexel](../../images/bfa1c860c02e856b78bb1b31ed0dfa51_original.jpg)
*around 1935 — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80378 | Description: Picture postcard with the Hotel Drexel*

In 1942, Hotel Drexel at Grosse Friedberger Strasse 18 made use of at least 1 female civilian worker from Ukraine. Civilian workers were recruited voluntarily or forcibly from the occupied countries in Western and Eastern Europe. Many Eastern Europeans were brought to Germany against their will and forced to perform labour. Civilian workers were forced labourers who were neither prisoners of war nor concentration camp inmates. Nazi racism led to differences in how forced labourers of differing origins were paid and treated. A distinction was made between “Western workers” and “Eastern workers”. Persons from Eastern Europe received only ten per cent of the wages of a German worker and were not provided any form of occupational protection.
