# Hos­pi­tal "Gumpertz’sches Sie­chen­haus"

*Röderbergweg 62-64*

![Floor plan of the hospital "Gumpertz'sche Siechenhaus" (Gumpertz's infirmary)](../../images/dd9491ad67673148f0cbacbb9e36804a_original.jpg)
*ca. 1900 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S8-2 Nr. 767 | Description: Floor plan of the hospital "Gumpertz'sche Siechenhaus" (Gumpertz's infirmary)*

Röderbergweg 62–64 was the site of a Jewish hospital known as the Gumpertz’sches Siechenhaus. Founded in 1888 by the Jewish donor Betty Gumpertz, it treated chronically ill and bedridden persons in need. It also housed a nursing facility for the sick, elderly, disabled, and poor. During the 1938 November Pogroms, the hospital provided protection to many Jewish families. In late September 1940, its provider institution— the Gumpertz’sches Siechenhaus and Minka von Goldschmidt-Rothschild Foundation — was incorporated into the compulsory Reich Association of Jews in Germany. In late 1941, the Gestapo ordered the evacuation of the hospital within one day. The patients were taken to the Jewish hospital in Gagernstrasse and from there deported to the Terezín concentration camp in 1942. After the evacuation, the City of Frankfurt purchased the building below value. The property was to be used as a hospital and home for the elderly. Following the liberation, the partially destroyed building housed an infirmary for displaced persons.
