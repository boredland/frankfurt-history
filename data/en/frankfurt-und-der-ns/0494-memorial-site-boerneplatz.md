# Memorial Site Börneplatz

*Battonnstraße 47*

![Memorial site Börneplatz](../../images/e0b4f881ba21d35e11ce265fff830730_original.jpg)
*Author: Norbert Miguletz | Description: Memorial site Börneplatz*

11,908 names, 11,908 fates: the Börneplatz memorial commemorates Frankfurt's Jewish community, which was destroyed under National Socialism. It is located in the eastern part of the city center between the oldest Jewish cemetery in Frankfurt and the rear of the building that also houses the Judengasse Museum.
The frieze on the outer wall of the Old Jewish Cemetery forms the center of the memorial, which opened in 1996. 11,908 name blocks commemorate all known Frankfurt victims of the Nazi extermination policy. Visitors to the memorial can lay small stones here in accordance with Jewish mourning rituals. The adjacent Jewish cemetery has been given two modern metal gates, on which Beth HaChaim ("House of Life") is written in Hebrew letters.

A large stone cube stands on the actual square, surrounded by a grove of plane trees. It consists of the remains of the foundations of the former ghetto. The ground of the square is covered with gray gravel stones. The floor plan of the former Börneplatz synagogue is marked by metal rails on the ground. It was vandalized in 1938 during the November pogrom. A memorial plaque to the destroyed synagogue, which had already been unveiled nearby in 1946, was installed on the rear wall of the Stadtwerke building.
A few meters next to the stone cube, five street signs commemorate the changing names of the square: it was first called Judenmarkt, from 1886 Börneplatz, and in 1935 it was renamed Dominikanerplatz because of its Jewish namesake (Ludwig Börne). It was not renamed Börneplatz again until 1978. It has been renamed Neuer Börneplatz since 1996.
