# Stolperstein for Elias Oppenheimer

*Wöhlerstraße 6*

Elias Oppenheimer (1886–1942)

Elias Oppenheimer was born in Kirchheim in the state of Rhineland Palatinate in 1886. His father died when he was young, and he lived alone with his mother, a tradeswoman. Elias Oppenheimer was a Jew. He worked as a tradesman and for the auxiliary postal service for a time, then took ill and became disabled. In 1937 he moved into the Jewish home for the elderly at Wöhlerstrasse 6 in Frankfurt. On September 18, 1941, he was arrested after being reported for alleged “indecent acts.” The Reichbahn inspector Schwarz claimed that Elias Oppenheimer had touched him on a tram. Although Oppenheimer denied the accusations, the district court of Frankfurt sentenced him to five months in prison under Section 175 of the German Criminal Code. In force from 1871 to 1994, Section 175 criminalized sexual acts between men and was severely tightened in 1935.

After serving his prison term in Preungesheim, Oppenheimer was put in “protective custody” and committed to the Buchenwald concentration camp in May 1942. There the SS assigned him to the inmate categories “Jew” and “homosexual.” He was murdered just a few days after his arrival. He was 56 years old.

 The Stolperstein was initiated by Martin Dill and Heidi Stögbauer of the Frankfurt Stolperstein Initiative, funded by Hans-Peter Hoogen, and laid in 2019.

Stolpersteine

A Stolperstein—literally “stumbling stone”—is a 10 x 10 x 10-cm concrete block with a brass plate mounted on the upper side. The brass plate is inscribed with the name of the person and brief information about their biography and persecution during the Nazi period. Learn more about the Initiative Stolpersteine Frankfurt: https://stolpersteine-frankfurt.de/en
