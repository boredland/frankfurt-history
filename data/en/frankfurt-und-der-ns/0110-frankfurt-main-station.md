# Frankfurt main station

![Photo of the station building at Frankfurt Central Railway Station](../../images/a1d39718701a7832716493eab9a26074_original.jpg)
*1937 — Author: Wolff & Tritschler | License: HMF Ph04243 | Description: Photo of the station building at Frankfurt Central Railway Station*

The Main Station at Hindenburg-Platz (now Am Hauptbahnhof) served as the point of departure for numerous deportations. On 28 and 29 October 1938, the police arrested Jews of Polish nationality living in Frankfurt and deported them to Beuthen—a no-man’s-land between Germany and Poland.
 
 After the mass deportations of the years 1941 and 1942, there were eighteen further deportations that—with two exceptions—departed from Frankfurt Main Station. Between March 1943 and 15 March 1945, there were, again and again, instances of individuals or small groups being transported to the Auschwitz, Terezín, Buchenwald, and Ravensbrück concentration and extermination camps. Among these persons were, for example, Jews who until then had been protected from deportation by their marriage to a non-Jew. 
According to one report: 
“I witnessed one of these transports on 2 May 1944, and noted that the victims had been herded by 6 to 8 uniformed policemen with police dogs from the station precinct headquarters at track 1 along the concourse to track 18, walking in rows of four … My wife was also compelled to go the path of suffering to Auschwitz. She never returned…. The transports took place every week on Monday at the same time and in the same way.” (Krause-Schmitt, S. 68f; Monica Kingreen, Nach der Kristallnacht ..., S. 384.)
