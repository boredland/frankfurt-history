Emanuel Rothschild: A life marked by persecution and survival

Emanuel Rothschild (1895–1975) ran a successful textile business in Frankfurt before his life was torn asunder by the National Socialists. He survived the persecution by going into hiding.

Emanuel Rothschild was born in Hörstein on 21 June 1895. On leaving school, he began an apprenticeship in Frankfurt before opening his own textile shop in 1919. In 1932, he married Elisabeth Fanny Groh, a Protestant woman who owned a silverware shop and was also a singer.

Life after 1933

When the Nazi Party seized power in 1933, Emanuel was forced to close his business. He and Elisabeth lost their customers and she was forbidden to perform in public. In 1938, Emanuel was arrested and sent to Dachau concentration camp, where he and many other Jewish men were abused and humiliated. After three months, he was released.
From 1941, Emanuel was required to wear a Star of David. Four years later, he went into hiding in Frankfurt and managed to survive the Holocaust. Emanuel died in 1975 and Elisabeth in 1986.
