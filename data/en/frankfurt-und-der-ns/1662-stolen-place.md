# Stolen Place

*Große Friedberger Straße 1*

![Große Friedberger Straße 1](../../images/1b2335cd72134b0c97c2ed7fa83ecd52_original.jpg)
*1902 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Große Friedberger Straße 1*

The residential and commercial building at Große Friedberger Straße 1, corner of Zeil 70, had belonged to the husband of Gertrude Schloss, née Rosenthal, since 1932. He had a mortgage on the building and acquired it when the owner company founded in 1849, a tobacco manufacturer and cigar store, went into liquidation. The company had erected the building itself in 1889. Gertrude Schloss, now a widow, inherited the property. She herself lived at Voelckerstraße 17. Due to persecution, she had to sell the property to the owner of the non-Jewish Frankfurt company, which remained a tenant in the building, for the price of 50,000 Reichsmarks in a contract dated December 12, 1938. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 40,000 Reichsmark and the market value to 57,300 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 8,400 Reichsmark. The contract costs and the real estate transfer tax were shared by the parties. The seller asked for the contract to be approved quickly because she had to pay the Jewish property levy and the tax office had only deferred this until the end of March 1939. She did not have any other liquid funds to pay it. The building authorities approved the contract on August 16, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
