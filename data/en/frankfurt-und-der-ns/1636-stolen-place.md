# Stolen Place

*Fahrgasse 116*

![Fahrgasse 116](../../images/0bb6167315cc467ddd789513e591b9f3_original.jpg)
*ca. 1940 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Fahrgasse 116*

The Jewish private citizen Alfred Netter had inherited a one-third co-ownership share in the approximately 200-year-old property at Fahrgasse 116 from his wife, who had died in 1935. Alfred Netter lived at Eppsteiner Straße 42 / II in 1941. The property at Fahrgasse 116 measured 149 square meters. The house consisted of a store on the first floor, commercial premises and apartments on the upper floors. Due to persecution, he had to sell his co-ownership share to the non-Jewish owner of the company, which had rented the store on the first floor of Fahrgasse 116 for many years, for the price of 10,700 Reichsmark in a contract dated July 28, 1941. The officially tax-assessed value of the co-ownership share was 11,433 Reichsmark and the market value was 13,333 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 6,060 Reichsmark. The buyer assumed the costs from the contract office for the real estate transfer tax. Of the purchase price, 2,833 Reichsmark had to be paid to the Frankfurt-Ost tax office to secure the Reich Flight Tax. The almost 70-year-old seller asked for the purchase contract to be approved quickly because he was urgently dependent on the proceeds from the sale for his livelihood. The building authority approved the contract on September 18, 1941, but the Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich. According to the Arolsen Archives, the Gestapo deported Alfred Netter on September 16, 1942.
