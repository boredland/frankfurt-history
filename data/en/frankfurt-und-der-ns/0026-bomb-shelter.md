# Bomb shelter

*Eberstadtstraße 35*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Eberstadtstraße](../../images/b8c1cdf597db72d3585970d67bbaa276_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Eberstadtstraße*

This bomb shelter offered protection to 878 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt could accommodate a total of only 35,000 residents. The others fled into the cellars of their houses or to the public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering them. Construction work lasted from March 1941 to July 1942. The architect Franz C. Throll ordered a large figure of a golden eagle with a laurel wreath and a swastika for the north façade. He was criticized for doing so because in 1942, the third year of the war, it was not permitted to use more material than absolutely necessary. After the war, the swastika was covered with cement. This bomb shelter is still serviceable today as a protective space for one to two days. It has been a listed monument since 2012.
 Construction company: Wayss & Freytag AG, Frankfurt
 Architect: Franz C. Throll
