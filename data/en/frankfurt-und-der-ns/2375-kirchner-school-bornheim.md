# Kirchner School Bornheim

*Berger Straße*

![](../../images/aee2fcb98f23da009c4ac1c0a17de7ec_original.png)

Kirchnerschule Bornheim

The Kirchner School originated as the Bornheimer School, which opened on November 1, 1865 as a Volksschule (eight-year grammar school). Owing to the rapidly rising number of pupils, the main building was soon too small, and a second building was added at the rear in 1870. At the time, there were ten classes. Today, both buildings are listed as protected monuments. The school received its present-day name, Kirchner School, in 1890 in commemoration of Anton Kirchner (1779–1834), a Protestant minister, teacher, and school reformer. It was damaged by bombing during World War II, but resumed operation in 1945. It has been a Grundschule (four-year elementary school) since 1965.
