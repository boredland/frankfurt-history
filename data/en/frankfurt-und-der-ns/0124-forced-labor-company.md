# forced labor company

*Frankenallee 25*

![Forced labour camp, Frankenallee 25](../../images/a1719d2dc40963731c31e20f1d88d188_original.jpg)
*1935 — Author: Paul Wolff | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Wo Nr. 2778 | Description: Forced labour camp, Frankenallee 25*

During the National Socialist period, the August Osterrieth printing company profited from forced labour. Among the forced labourers working here were Jews, persons from countries occupied by the German Reich in the course of World War II, and women from the forced labour camp for Sinti and Roma in Dieselstrasse. In 1943, the company moreover made use of forced labourers from Russia (50 persons) and from countries such as France, Belgium, and the Netherlands (16 persons). They were lodged in a camp in Reuterweg.
