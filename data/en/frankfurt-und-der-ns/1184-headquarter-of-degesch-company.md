# headquarter of DeGeSch company

*Weißfrauenstraße 9*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier%20fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

headquarter of DeGeSch company

The DeGeSch company had its headquarters in this building from 1920 until the bombing of Frankfurt's old town in 1944. The abbreviation DeGeSch stands for "Deutsche Gesellschaft für Schädlingsbekämpfung". The chemical company is best known for "Zyklon B," the poison gas used by the Nazi regime to murder hundreds of thousands of people in the death camps. DeGeSch held the patent for the poison. The largest shareholder of DeGeSch was Degussa, the Deutsche Gold- und Silber-Scheideanstalt. It belonged to I.G. Farben AG, an association of the largest German chemical companies headquartered in Frankfurt. All German chemical companies were involved in war crimes: they knowingly profited from the production of Zyklon B and other goods needed for the war. I.G. Farben also operated company-owned concentration camps and used many forced laborers* in their companies. The headquarters of I.G. Farben AG, built between 1928 and 1931 in Frankfurt's Westend district, has been the campus of Goethe University since 2001. As a result of discussions about the company's involvement in the Holocaust, a memorial - the Nobert Wollheim Memorial - and a historical documentation were erected on the site. On the initiative of students, there is now a memorial in front of the former I.G. Farben headquarters commemorating the corporation's forced laborers.
