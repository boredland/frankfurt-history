# Forced labour camp

*Diesterwegplatz 51-53*

![Photograph of a station concourse at Diesterwegplatz, where the Deutsche Reichsbahn maintained a forced labour camp](../../images/12a3b11f3546287adc2831e744e456eb_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Friedrich Lauffer | License: HMF Ph11533 | Description: Photograph of a station concourse at Diesterwegplatz, where the Deutsche Reichsbahn maintained a forced labour camp*

The German Reich Railway operated a camp for forced labourers at this site. The Reich Railway had altogether eleven camps at its disposal in Frankfurt. The track-laying crew 1102 was stationed at Hedderichstrasse 53 (now Diesterwegplatz) near the city’s South Station. This group consisted of 77 Czech and 16 German forced labourers who had to perform heavy physical labour, for example in the repair of tracks damaged by bombing. The camp was set up in 1943.
