# Goethe Gymnasium

*Beethovenstraße 7B*

![Klassenfoto des Goethe Gymnasiums um 1925-1933. Es handelt sich hier um die Schulklasse von Hans Bauer.](../../images/df9b4be86b969267952cc929042414e8_original.jpg)

In the 1920s, many Jewish boys attended Goethe-Gymnasium in Frankfurt, a prestigious boys’ grammar school that offered a good education. In an interview, Hans Bauer reported that, of the 36 children in his class at the time, 16 – i.e. nearly half – had been Jewish. He saw the teaching staff of Goethe-Gymnasium as “relics from the time of the Kaiser” who had no respect for the new democratic republic that had just been established. This posed a challenge for the students, including Hans Bauer himself. After the National Socialists seized power in 1933, many Jewish students left the grammar school, including Hans Bauer, who left in September 1933 at the age of 15. Looking back, he says that his education at the Goethe-Gymnasium grammar school had been beneficial for, among other things, his carpentry apprenticeship.

*Author: Victoria Schneider*
