# Moritz and David Kulp

*5 Places – 5 Stories*

![Obermainanlage 29 (today)](../../images/3dc9020a52a9fbdbd95c5b8c50b93b8d_original.jpg)
*2023 — Author: Gabriel Olbrich | Description: Obermainanlage 29 (today)*

This house belonged to the Jewish brothers Moritz and David Kulp. It was foreclosed on December 20, 1940. Moritz and David Kulp were forcibly placed into the Jacoby's Heil- und Pflegeanstalt in Sayn, today Bendorf, on August 20, 1942. This institution was managed by Jews. Two days later, Moritz Kulp, aged 54, died in the institution due to an unknown cause of death. His brother David Kulp was deported on June 14, 1942, and likely died later in one of the extermination camps in Eastern Europe. 
All caregivers and patients were also deported during the course of the year. 
The house was auctioned off to Karl Nagel and his wife for 27,300 Reichsmarks.

*Author: Erik Hartmann, Gabriel Olbrich, Sophia Zücker*
