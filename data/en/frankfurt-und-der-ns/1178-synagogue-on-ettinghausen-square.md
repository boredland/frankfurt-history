# Synagogue on Ettinghausen Square

*Höchst market*

Höchster Markt

![](../../images/27d3a868bfea60960d2a6bc1393348e8_original.jpg)
*Author: © 2010 Architectura Virtualis GmbH, Kooperationspartner der Technischen Universität Darmstadt*

Since 1961, a plaque at the site of the former Höchst Synagogue has commemorated the devastation and subsequent demolition of the synagogue. The plaque was renewed in 1981 and replaced in 2018 by a plaque with a more extensive text.

The synagogue was consecrated on December 14, 1905. The architect of the slate-roofed brick building was Sigmund Münchhausen from Cologne. The façade with two corner towers faced east towards the Höchster market square. A mikvah fed by the groundwater of the Liederbach stream with a basin and a room for the prayer leader were built at the rear. The synagogue offered 84 seats for men and 54 seats for women in the gallery. It replaced a previous building from the 19th century, which had become too small for the growing community.

On the morning of November 10, 1938, SA men from Höchst set fire to the synagogue, which burned down completely at noon on the same day after another arson by Höchst residents. The burnt-out ruins were demolished in the spring of 1939. It was replaced by the air raid shelter that still stands today.

Since its naming in 2008, the square in front of the bunker has commemorated the Ettinghausen family, one of the oldest Jewish families in Höchst, who were active in many ways in local politics, society and business. In 2010, two media viewers were installed on the square, which simulate the exterior view of the church and its interior design in three dimensions.
