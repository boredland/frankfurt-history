# forced labour camp

*Assenheimer Straße 38-40*

![Forced labor camp, Assenheimer Straße 38-40](../../images/f88e51560dc0bd254adb39f5691e47b9_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-24687 | Description: Forced labor camp, Assenheimer Straße 38-40*

The Körner School (now Michael Ende School) served as a lodging for prisoners of war assigned to clean up the damage caused by aerial attacks on the city. Prisoners of war were under the control of the Wehrmacht (the German army under National Socialism) and were initially held in camps referred to as “Mannschaftsstammlager” or “Stalag”. There they were assigned to so-called labour detachments that lodged and worked outside the camp. The POWs housed in Körner School had previously been interned in the Stalag IX B Wegscheide. Three school pupils defied the absolute ban on contact. In their school reports they were reprimanded for "loitering around prisoners despite prohibition". One of the pupils reportedly obtained food for the Russian prisoners of war.
