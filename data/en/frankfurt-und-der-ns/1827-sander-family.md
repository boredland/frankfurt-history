# Sander family

*When lines are crossed!*

![Salomon Sander (left side) and Lina Sander (right side) in Frankfurt am Main](../../images/b5f2017900bebbeda3217986bdf8b2bf_original.jpg)
*License: Initiative Stolpersteine Frankfurt am Main | Description: Salomon Sander (left side) and Lina Sander (right side) in Frankfurt am Main*

In 1899, Lina and Salomon Sander acquired the property at Querstraße 1, a large corner building with ten rented flats and five shops, in which they also lived. Salomon ran a chemist's shop there, which he later sold to the Erbes family, who also lived in the building, presumably for reasons of age.

At the beginning of 1940, the elderly couple moved to Heidelberg. Their house was forcibly sold, with the Erbes family also taking over the Sander's flat. According to their granddaughter, Lily Gutmann-Fuld, the Erbes family remained well-disposed towards the Sanders until the end. Later, Lina and Salomon returned to Frankfurt and lived first in Liebigstraße and finally at Sandweg 14.

They were forced to pay a ‘Jewish property levy’ of 16,855.73 Reichsmark and also concluded an obligatory ‘home purchase contract’ for 16,797 Reichsmark. Such contracts deceived the signatories into believing that they were securing their old-age provision for a retirement in a home in Theresienstadt. After their deportation, they died there.

*Author: Anika Gehbauer, Pavel Meyer, Sara Röthe, Nora Schauer*
