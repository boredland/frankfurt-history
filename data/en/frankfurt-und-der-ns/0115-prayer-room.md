# Prayer room

*Obermainanlage 8*

![Photo of the facade of the prayer room, Obermainanlage 8](../../images/e76e904667b1a6efdee57e261a26e22e_original.jpg)
*ca. 1955 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2008-124 | Description: Photo of the facade of the prayer room, Obermainanlage 8*

This was the location of a prayer room known from 1920 onwards as the Berger’sche Schul. It had previously been called Beth Hamidrasch (House of Study). So-called “Betstuben” like this one were small, simply furnished synagogues. They served as places of prayer, discussion, and learning. In 1938 there were still some 40 prayer rooms in Frankfurt. The Berger’sche Schul was one of the most important “Betstube” for Jews who had immigrated to Frankfurt from Eastern Europe. Many of them lived in the Ostend district. The prayer rooms were often operated by private persons or small associations to give themselves and other believers a place to pray near their homes. The property at Obermainanlage 8 belonged to Moses Steinlauf, who was also on the board of the synagogue association.
