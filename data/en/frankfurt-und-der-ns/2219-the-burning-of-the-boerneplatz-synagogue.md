# The Burning of the Börneplatz Synagogue

*Out and about in Frankfurt with Lilo*

![](../../images/8cf56ebb6ba19c35252ff01612c03383_original.jpg)

On November 10, 1938, Lilo was on the way to kindergarten with her mother. She was especially looking forward to her day.
Since Lilo and her family lived in Frankfurt’s old town, they passed by the Börneplatz Synagogue on their way to the kindergarten.
The synagogue was on fire. Lilo’s mother was immediately shocked and terrified and knew that she and her children were in danger. Instead of going to kindergarten, Lilo had to go home again. And to be on the safe side, Helmut, her big brother, was picked up from school.
Already before that day, the family had thought a lot about the hostility towards Jews under the Nazi regime and how that hostility affected them. What happened on that day showed them how great the danger really was and that the family had to take good care of itself.

November pogroms

On the night of November 9, 1938, pogroms took place all over the German Reich. Pogroms are violence and destruction directed against people, in this case Jews. Especially synagogues—Jewish houses of worship—were destroyed and set on fire. But other Jewish institutions and shops were also destroyed. All in all, it was a huge act of violence against Jews that took place in public, and hardly anyone did anything to stop it.
