# Forced labour camp

*Kleyerstraße 79-87*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier%20fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

The telecommunications company Telefonbau & Normalzeit made use of forced labourers from Eastern Europe starting in 1942. The enterprise had originally belonged to Harry Fuld, a Jew, before being expropriated and renamed. The forced labourers were put to work in the factories at Mainzer Landstrasse 132–142 and Kleyerstrasse 79–87. In April 1942 the company made use of 53 Lithuanian forced labourers. By September 1942 the number had risen to 66 Russian and 43 Lithuanian men, by 1943 to 140 women and 89 men from Ukraine as well as 2 women and 1 man from Russia. Some were lodged on the factory grounds, others on the post office sports field in Sondershausenstrasse.
