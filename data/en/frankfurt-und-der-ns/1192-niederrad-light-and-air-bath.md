# Niederrad Light and Air Bath

*Niederräder Ufer 10*

![Copyright: Nachlass J. Bendorf](../../images/18383845f2b77a3f2c969048d40c6aee_original.jpg)
*Author: Julius Bendorf | Description: Copyright: Nachlass J. Bendorf*

The Strandbad Niederrad was a very popular river swimming pool on the Main Island, which had been created by the construction of the locks. It had been opened in 1900. In April 1936, the city of Frankfurt decreed that the Jewish population could no longer visit any public swimming pool - except for the Strandbad Niederrad. It became one of the most important places of refuge for Frankfurt's Jews. The TSV Schild Frankfurt succeeded in building a boathouse right next to the bath. Water sports departments were founded. Training took place on the Main, and competitions were held in swimming and in canoeing and rowing. November 1938 marked the end: the Jewish sports clubs were banned and the Jewish population was no longer allowed to visit any public baths. In Main 1939 the SA took over the bath. Since 1994, a plaque on the site of today's Licht- und Luftbad Niederrad commemorates the expulsion of the Jewish population from there.
