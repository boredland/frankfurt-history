# Bomb shelter

*Herxheimer Straße 10*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Herxheimer Straße](../../images/5a1f8122579b64515ab57c083299e1e8_original.jpg)
*1947 — Author: Fred Kochmann | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Ko Nr. 1668 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Herxheimer Straße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 1,201 persons on four floors. It was one of Frankfurt’s larger shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from using the air-raid protection facilities. Construction began in August 1941. The building was decorated with numerous elements made of red Main sandstone. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. After the war, the Dellihausen forwarding company used the building to store valuable objects formerly belonging to evacuees and persons who had been bombed out of their homes. In 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, the shelter was refurbished to offer protection for one to two days in the event of an attack.

Construction company: Jakob Noll, Frankfurt
 Architect: Karl Olsson
