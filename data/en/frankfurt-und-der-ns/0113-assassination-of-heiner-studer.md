# Assassination of Heiner Studer

*Bockenheimer Warte*

![](../../images/20ec5b0caa2b41b38177c4980698de2f_original.jpg)
*Author: © Katharina Müller, VG Bild-Kunst, Bonn 2022*

assassination site

On 19 February 1933 at the Bockenheimer Warte tower, armed SA members lay in wait for Heiner Studer, one of the leading figures in a militant anti-fascist alliance (“Kampfbund gegen den Faschismus”). Studer drove his motorbike past the tower. His pillion passenger was Johann Kestler, active in distributing the “Arbeiter Zeitung” (“Workers’ Newspaper”). The SA opened fire – and left Johann Kestler with fatal injuries. The Nazis tried to suppress reports of the murder in the “Arbeiter Zeitung”. With the Nazi Party in power in the city administration from mid-March, no legal steps were ever taken in Kestler’s murder. The police only opened an investigation into the case in 1945, after the end of the war.
