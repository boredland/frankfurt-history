# Bomb shelter

*Engelsplatz 38*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located at Engelsplatz](../../images/b7afdaaea4579e376f65e211c1e2d634_original.jpg)
*2014 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located at Engelsplatz*

This bomb shelter was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 420 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering them. Construction work lasted from November 1940 to July 1942. In 1940, 46 persons are known to have performed forced labour here; 23 of them were prisoners of war. After the war, the Ethnologisches Museum (now the Museum der Weltkulturen) and the Frobenius-Institut used it as a depot. During the Cold War, it was refurbished to provide protection for a few hours in the event of an attack. It has been a listed monument since 2012 and is used as a clubhouse.

Construction company: Züblin AG
 Architect: Karl Olsson
