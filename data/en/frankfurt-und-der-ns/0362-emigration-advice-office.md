# Emigration Advice Office

*Braubachstraße 30-32*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

With Adolf Hitler's appointment as Reich Chancellor on January 30, 1933, more and more anti-Jewish laws gradually came into force and National Socialists made life increasingly difficult for Jews. This also applied to Frankfurt, where the Jewish community had been large and firmly integrated into the city's society for hundreds of years. Those who had the opportunity tried everything to leave Germany and build a new life elsewhere. Many Jews prepared to emigrate in the hope of being exposed to less hostility towards Jews in another country. For them, the Emigration Advisory Service, which was located here at Braubachstrasse 30-35, became increasingly important, advising people on how to start a business abroad and providing expert opinions on whether the amount needed to start a business abroad was available. Another institution that provided assistance in setting up a business abroad was the Jewish Training Center at Fischerfeldstrasse 13, which was established by the Jewish Counseling Center for Economic Assistance. Here, until 1941, Jewish young people could learn a trade in order to be able to work abroad. Closely connected with this was the idea of establishing a Jewish state of one's own in another country, for example in Palestine, where one was protected from anti-Semitism. Municipal authorities such as the Emigration Advisory Service implemented the Nazis' policy of exclusion and persecution at the communal level, even to the point of organizing emigration. The Frankfurt Health Department played a special role in Nazi policy. You will Website about this at the next stop.
