# Stolen Place

*Fahrgasse 36*

![Fahrgasse 36](../../images/2c751c7b8903569a9917b246913ec421_original.jpg)
*ca. 1940 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Fahrgasse 36*

The half-timbered houses at Fahrgasse 36 / corner of Klostergasse 9 from the 17th or 18th century originally belonged to the company J. C. Foltz-Eberle oHG, owned by the Jewish merchants Richard Wolff and Markus Wolff. The property covered 292 square meters and had a guest right. The first floor of Fahrgasse 36 housed a restaurant and the landlord's 3-room apartment. On the four upper floors above were three 3-room, five 2-room and one 1-room apartments. In the two-storey building at Klostergasse 9 there was a workshop, three 3-room and three 1-room apartments. Due to the persecution, a business consultant without sufficient legitimation sold the building to Henninger Bräu AG for 40,800 Reichsmark in a contract dated January 12, 1939. However, at least 11,800 Reichsmark was to be withheld from the purchase price for repairs. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 40,800 Reichsmark and the market value to 22,600 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 7,200 Reichsmark. The contract costs and the estate agent's commission were shared between the parties. However, in March 1939, a trustee appointed by the District President of Breslau for the entire assets of J. C. Foltz-Eberle did not recognize the purchase contract. However, the district president had already approved the contract on February 10, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich despite the considerable difference between the officially tax-assessed value and the sales price because considerable repair work was necessary. However, it was reserved in the event of a rent increase.
