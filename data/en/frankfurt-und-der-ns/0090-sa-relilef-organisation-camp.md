# SA Relilef Organisation Camp

*Rödelheimer Landstraße 5-19*

![Aerial photo of an SA Relilef Organisation Camp in Rödelheimer Landstraße](../../images/c46382dfe5bf2fc931834d0b42a5670d_original.jpg)
*ca. 1926/27 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-19802 | Description: Aerial photo of an SA Relilef Organisation Camp in Rödelheimer Landstraße*

The “Sturmabteilung”, or Storm Troopers, were a branch of the National Socialist German Workers’ Party. They were armed and prepared to use violence. In the Weimar Republic they had fought street battles with their political opponents, carried out attacks, and tortured people. When the process of forced conformity to National Socialist ideology was completed in 1934, the SA lost its original raison d’être. Back in 1933, however, its leadership had already begun establishing training camps throughout the Reich for so-called Old Fighters, the majority of whom were unemployed persons. Each camp lodged 300 men and provided them with ideological, vocational, and athletic training. The aim was to get them off the streets and thus avoid unrest. The German Reich spent altogether 73 million reichsmarks on the programme. From the winter of 1936/1937 onwards, the so-called Hilfswerklager camp of SA Brigade 49 (Frankfurt) was located here, on the grounds of the former Bockenheim artillery casern. The camp had previously been in Höchst.
