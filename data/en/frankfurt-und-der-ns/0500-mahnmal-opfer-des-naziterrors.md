# Mahnmal "Opfer des Naziterrors"

*Paulsplatz 11*

![Drawing of the memorial to the Victims of Nazi Terror](../../images/65292502930d5749ddeed235138c59d6_original.jpg)
*Description: Drawing of the memorial to the Victims of Nazi Terror*

Memorial

There has been an official memorial to the National Socialist crimes in Frankfurt am Main since the mid-1950s. In 1959, the city councillors and magistrate decided to erect a memorial at a central location in the city to commemorate the fellow citizens who had resisted the Nazi terror at the risk of their own lives and following only their conscience. According to the will of the parliamentarians, the memorial, which was awarded in a competition among sculptors, was to be erected on the western staircase of St. Paul's Church facing the busy Berliner Strasse. In 1964, the prize-winning design by the Munich sculptor Hans Wimmer, which depicts a kneeling figure, was realized. According to the jury, the shell limestone figure raising its bound hands to the sky not only expresses "man's subjection to terror", but also "a rebellion to the end". As suggested by the jury, 53 names of the most important concentration, labor education and internment camps as well as ghettos are engraved on the sandstone base in alphabetical order. A spelling mistake was corrected with the repair at Riga-Salaspils.
