# Stolen Place

*Brückhofstraße 10*

![Brückhofstraße 10](../../images/b75953e7a8c15e6fb7ec3fb739671757_original.jpg)
*1939 — Author: Fried Lübbecke | License: ISG | Description: Brückhofstraße 10*

The property at Brückhofstraße 10, with a plot area of 165 square meters, originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Albert Aaron Goldberg, who lived at Staufenstraße 30 at the time of sale. The building comprised two stores, one 3-room, three 2-room and three 1-room apartments. The rental income amounted to 252 Reichsmark per month. Due to persecution, he had to sell his property to a non-Jewish woman from Frankfurt for 15,000 Reichsmark in a contract dated July 7, 1941. The officially tax-assessed value was 22,600 Reichsmark and the market value was 15,300 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 3,024 Reichsmark. The costs of the contract, including land transfer tax, brokerage fees and deletion costs, were borne by the buyer. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy in favor of the Reich.
