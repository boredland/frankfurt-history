# Forced labour camp

*Röderbergweg 270*

![Aerial view of Café Röderbergschlösschen at Röderbergweg 270, where a forced labour camp was operated](../../images/ef6720a2939ddb9907c93985c5210280_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-14005 | Description: Aerial view of Café Röderbergschlösschen at Röderbergweg 270, where a forced labour camp was operated*

A forced labour camp went into operation in the Café Röderbergschlösschen at Röderbergweg 270 in 1942. Two Frankfurt companies both based in Weismüllerstrasse in the east harbour lodged labourers here. In 1942, the Heinrich Hartherz pickle and sauerkraut factory made use of 25 forced labourers from Russia, in 1943 53 from Ukraine, and in 1944 24 women of unknown nationality. The Claus Meyn glass roof factory, which was on the neighbouring premises, made use of 25 forced labourers from Russia in 1942, 26 from Ukraine in 1943, and 20 from Ukraine as well as 10 from Poland in 1944. The living conditions in the “Röderbergschlösschen” were extremely inhumane. After the café was damaged during an aerial attack, the male forced labourers were lodged on the factory grounds of the Claus Meyn company at Weismüllerstrasse 16–18. The location of the women’s accommodations is unknown.
