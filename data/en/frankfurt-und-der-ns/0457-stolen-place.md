# Stolen place

*Liebigstraße 27b*

![Grünbaum family residence, Liebigstraße 27b](../../images/f688a039edc758b0b350df0f8d47c2cb_original.jpg)
*1884 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-13178 | Description: Grünbaum family residence, Liebigstraße 27b*

The building at Liebigstrasse 27b originally belonged to Fritz Bieler. He was subjected to persecution by the Nazis. In 1939, the house was put up for compulsory auction and sold.
 The couple Elise and Meir Grünbaum also lived here. During renovations in the summer of 1986, Inge Geiler—who lived in what had once been the Grünbaums’ flat—found photos, front pages of the Jüdisches Nachrichtenblatt, postcards, 47 letters, and other papers in the wall panelling. They document the final months in the lives of the Jewish couple. The Grünbaums, both in their eighties, lived in their flat until August 1942, when they were deported to Terezín. Meir Grünbaum gave expression to his despair on little slips of paper and receipts. “You would never believe how unhappy I am. I was always such an honest man. And now this is the life I have.” And: “I wish I had never come into the world.” He had once worked for the Dresdner Bank and been a respected citizen of the City of Wiesbaden, where he lived with his wife. They were forced to move from Wiesbaden to Frankfurt in 1941. In 2008, Inge Geiler officially turned over the Grünbaum documents to the Institut für Stadtgeschichte in Frankfurt. In 2012 she published the results of her research on the family in a book entitled “Wie ein Schatten sind unsere Tage”: Die Geschichte der Familie Grünbaum.
