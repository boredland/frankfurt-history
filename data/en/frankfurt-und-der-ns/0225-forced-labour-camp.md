# forced labour camp

*Hanauer Landstraße 142-172*

![Picture postcard with aerial view of the Voigt &amp; Haeffner industrial and company plant](../../images/2fd17422f42dba499a178fb0e386bfbc_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80393 | Description: Picture postcard with aerial view of the Voigt &amp; Haeffner industrial and company plant*

Voigt & Haeffner, a factory specializing in heavy current electrical equipment, was based at Hanauer Landstrasse 142–172 and lodged forced labourers in barracks on the company grounds. More than 200 forced labourers, primarily from Russia and France, were housed here in 1942. In 1943 and 1944 the company put up a temporary tent camp for forced labourers on its premises at Hanauer Landstrasse 121. This camp accommodated 75 men and 151 women. The company operated three further camps in Frankfurt as well.
