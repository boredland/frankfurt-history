# Elise Hoffmann

*Hansaallee 146*

<p>- deported from the retirement home and murdered</p>

![Elise Hofmann, ca. 1941](../../images/f356ee6edffff25885cd09696c1efa0c_original.jpg)
*ca.1941 — Author: unbekannt | License: Karin Levi | Description: Elise Hofmann, ca. 1941*

In 1930, Elise Hofmann was one of the first residents to move into the newly built retirement home of the Henry and Emma Budge Foundation at Hansaallee 146. In 1939, the home was "Aryanized" and renamed "Heim am Dornbusch". The last Jewish residents living there were expelled in March of that year.

Elise Hofmann had already moved to Telemannstraße 12 in 1938. She then lived in a retirement home at Zeil 92 and finally in the Israelite Hospital. She was deported from there to Theresienstadt in 1942 and ultimately to Treblinka. The date of her death is unknown.

Her daughter Gertrude, born in 1896, managed to escape to the USA. After the war, the former retirement home was used as a dental clinic. Today, the old buildings belong to the Grünhof senior citizens' residence.

A "Stolperstein" commemorates Elise Hofmann.

*Author: Hartmud Schmidt*
