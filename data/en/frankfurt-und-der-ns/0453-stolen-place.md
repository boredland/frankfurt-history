# Stolen place

*Am Leonhardsbrunn 12-14*

![Picture postcard with villa Merton](../../images/C80373_jpg_8ca22ea6bfb0de78074bd139caa7c18a_original.jpg)
*around 1932 — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80373 | Description: Picture postcard with villa Merton*

The lot and villa at Leonhardsbrunn 12–14 belonged to the Jewish businessman and politician Richard Merton. After studying law, Merton worked for the Berg- und Metallbank from 1902 onwards. Later he was active in the Metallgesellschaft, an industrial conglomerate, where he was a member of the supervisory board and the board of directors. During World War I he served as an officer at the front and as an adjutant in the military administration. He spoke out in favour of the state taking over the food industry and working with the unions. In 1919 he was a member of the peace delegation in Versailles. After that he withdrew from politics for a time. In the 1920s he was active primarily as a member of the board of directors of the Metallgesellschaft. From 1928 to 1933 he represented the German People’s Party on the Frankfurt city council. He was a member of the Reichstag—the national parliament—between 1932 and 1933. From 1933 onwards he was persecuted by the Nazis on account of his Jewish descent. He had to vacate his post in the Metallgesellschaft in 1936 and was dispossessed in 1938. In this context he also lost the land and villa at Leonhardsbrunn 12–14. Merton was held in the Buchenwald concentration camp for three weeks; in 1939 he managed to flee to England. After the war the Britons wanted to appoint him as Secretary of Business, but he once again became active in industry. He was a member of the supervisory board of the Metallgesellschaft and served as president of the International Chamber of Trade. He also had ties to the University of Frankfurt, where he endowed a chair for social policy in 1956. Merton died in 1960 at the age of seventy-nine.
