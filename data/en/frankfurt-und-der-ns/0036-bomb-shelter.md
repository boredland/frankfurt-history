# Bomb shelter

*Europa-Allee 50*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Europa-Allee](../../images/6763b0112cc44b4a2a955faf401c6246_original.jpg)
*1998 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Europa-Allee*

This was the site of the main goods yard. The bomb shelter tower once located here had seven floors. Built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme, it was designed to accommodate 275 persons and intended as a protective facility for employees of the Reich Railway. Construction began in July 1941. It was the designer Leo Winkel who had the idea for this type of bomb shelter, for which reason such structures are also known as “Winkel towers”. The Deutsche Bahn ceased operation of the goods yard in 1998. The tower was torn down. Construction work on the “Europaviertel” got underway in 2005.

Construction company: unknown
 Architect: Leo Winkel
