# Festhalle

*Ludwig-Erhard-Anlage 1*

![Photo of the Festhalle](../../images/8709fb97696f7e044e87653ed1eefb96_original.jpg)
*ca. 1936 — Author: Friedrich Lauffer | License: HMF Ph11572 | Description: Photo of the Festhalle*

During the November pogroms of 1938, some 3,000 Jewish men between the ages of eighteen and sixty were detained in the festival hall in Hohenzollernanlage (now Ludwig-Erhard-Anlage). They had been arrested and taken there by the SA, SS, police, and Gestapo between 10 and 14 November 1938. There they were tormented and humiliated by their guards. The Frankfurt opera star Hans Erl, for example, was forced to sing the aria “Within these sacred portals revenge is unknown” from Mozart’s Magic Flute. Then he was released. Various eyewitness reports in the holdings of the Wiener Library in London testify to the fact that the men detained in the festival hall were robbed, humiliated, and beaten. One of them relate: “When your name was called, you had to come forward and say ‘I’m the SOW JEW such and such, born on etc.’ Woe to him who forgot to say the words ‘sow Jew’. He was chased around the entire huge hall, receiving blows all the while.”
 
 Busses left the festival hall over and over again, taking groups of Jewish men to the South Station for transportation to the Buchenwald and Dachau concentration camps. Approximately 10,000 Jewish men from all over the German Reich were deported to Buchenwald during the November pogroms. 2,621 of them were from Frankfurt. 534 Jewish men of Frankfurt were transported to the Dachau concentration camp.
