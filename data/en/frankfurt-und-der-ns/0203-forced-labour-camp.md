# forced labour camp

*Fischerfeldstraße 13*

![Forced labor camp, Fischerfeldstraße 13](../../images/C27897_jpg_1700195052f811de780f0d25be6fb4a8_original.jpg)
*1932 — Author: Leonhard Kleemann | License: HMF, C27897 | Description: Forced labor camp, Fischerfeldstraße 13*

The situation of young people in training in the vocational workshop changed when the war began. The opportunities to emigrate dwindled. At the same time, Jewish families came under ever greater pressure from numerous laws and measures. Between 1940 and 1943, Jewish welfare organizations in Frankfurt such as the vocational workshop were kept under surveillance by Ernst Holland—a municipal employee who carried out this task on behalf of the Gestapo—and ultimately dissolved. The young people in the vocational workshop had to perform forced labour for Frankfurt companies. This did not protect them from deportation. After the third deportation in the summer of 1942, there were no further trainees and the school was closed.
 
 This was also the location of a camp where 21 Frankfurt companies lodged their forced labourers. It was designated exclusively for Eastern Europeans. The E. F. Wiedmann metalware factory and the regional chamber of industry and trade are thought to have overseen the camp. Some 250 persons were housed here.
