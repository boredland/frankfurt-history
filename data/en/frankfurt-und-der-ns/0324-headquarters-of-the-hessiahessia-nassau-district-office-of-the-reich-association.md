# Headquarters of the Hessia/Hessia-Nassau district office of the Reich Association of Jews in Germany

*Hermesweg 5-7*

![Floor plan of the former Israeli religious school and synagogue on Hermesweg, which was used as a ghetto house and as the headquarters of the Hesse/Hesse-Nassau district office of the Reich Association of Jews in Germany](../../images/28fc45c70689e0b1180147843d68a335_original.jpg)

In 1943, the rooms of the former Jewish religious school and synagogue at Hermesweg 5-7 housed the Hesse regional office of the Reich Association of Jews in Germany (“Reichsvereinigung der Juden in Deutschland”). The Association, which was under the Gestapo, was created by the mandatory merger of all Jewish communities and religious organisations existing since 1939. Since all Jews were required to be Association members, it was almost impossible to hide from the Nazi regime’s administrative apparatus. Initially, only Jews with non-Jewish spouses were exempt from joining the association. The Gestapo used the card files in the Association’s regional offices to prepare expropriation measures and deportations. On 10 June 1943, the Association was dissolved, its assets seized, and its members deported. From June 1943, Jews were also deported who had lost their non-Jewish spouses due to divorce or death. Prior to their deportation, they were moved into the “ghetto house” in Hermesweg. From March 1943, foreign Jews were also deported. Since Hermesweg could not accommodate the growing numbers of people, the Gestapo established another “ghetto house” at Ostendstrasse 18. Siegfried Halberstadt, whose spouse was non-Jewish and died in 1935, recalled: “The Gestapo arrested (...) and interrogated me on 26 March 1943. On 31.7.1943 I was (…) released, which meant I could no longer go home and was taken to the Jewish houses in Hermesweg and (…) Ostendstrasse. On 8.1.1944 we were driven in trucks to Ostbahnhof station and transported from there to the Theresienstadt concentration camp.” In the night of the 4th to 5th October 1943, the building in Hermesweg was destroyed in a bombing raid. Although many documents were also destroyed, records have survived of 45 cases of Jews deported between 1942 and 1944 from the ghetto houses in Hermesweg and Ostendstrasse..
