# Sterilization of prostitutes and people leading nonconformist sexual lives

*University psychiatric clinic*

![The university psychiatric clinic. Photo: Otto Emmel, ca. 1931, HMF Ph35169.](../../images/4849bb78894f1044224b8d7d01dd389d_original.jpg)
*ca. 1931 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: HMF Ph35169 | Description: The university psychiatric clinic. Photo: Otto Emmel, ca. 1931, HMF Ph35169.*

In many cases, the dermatological clinic, the so-called “care office” (Pflegeamt), or the youth welfare office committed prostitutes and people leading nonconformist sexual lives to the psychiatric clinic. Sophie Gotthardt and Otto Kohlmann were two such cases. Both were queer and, from late 1939, a couple. Sophie Gotthardt earned her livelihood primarily with sex work. In 1934, the dermatological clinic had her taken to the psychiatric clinic after examining her for venereal disease. At the behest of the youth welfare office, Otto Kohlmann was committed to the psychiatric clinic in 1935, when she was still a minor, for her alleged “perverse disposition.” Her parents had christened her Johanna; she went by the name Otto. She also wore men’s clothing whenever possible. Later she and Sophie Gotthardt referred to themselves as lesbian.

During the Weimar Republic, prostitutes and people leading nonconformist sexual lives had been committed to psychiatric clinics for the purpose of institutionalizing them. Under Nazi rule, the psychiatric clinic’s medical personnel were moreover instructed to arrange for such people’s sterilization on the basis of a genetic assessment in keeping with the “Law for the Prevention of Offspring with Hereditary Diseases.” To this end, the psychiatric clinic frequently committed them to the Hadamar regional “mental sanatorium.” The sanatorium nearly always applied for the sterilization. That was also true in the case of Otto Kohlmann. Sometimes, as in the case of Sophie Gotthardt in 1934, it was the medical personnel of the psychiatric clinic that submitted the respective application to the Frankfurt “hereditary health court.”
