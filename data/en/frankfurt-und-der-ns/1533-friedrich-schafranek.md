# Friedrich Schafranek

*Lessingstraße 4*

Podcast about a student at Wöhler School

![](../../images/a76eea36021aa0eb07b6591e6fbc80d6_original.png)
*License: Wöhlerschule*

Friedrich Schafranek (16.5.1924 in Vienna - 14.3.2013 in Bobingen) was a student at the Wöhler School in Frankfurt from 1931 to 1935. He was an enthusiastic footballer for Eintracht Frankfurt, was talented with languages and had a strong personality.

Because he was Jewish, he was subjected to terrible persecution and lost his entire family. He survived the Lodz ghetto and the Ausschwitz and Dachau concentration camps, where he had to perform forced labor in a subcamp. After the war, he emigrated to Australia for several years.

The Wöhlerschule's Trace Research Group looked into his fate. Based on an interview with him published by the House of Bavarian History, they recounted his life in a podcast.

This resulted in four episodes, which can be heard here.
