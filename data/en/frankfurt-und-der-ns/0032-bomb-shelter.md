# Bomb shelter

*Gründenseestraße 6*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Gründenseestraße](../../images/fd88e0ad7a77cc6a2bbb4fc304a78dbf_original.jpg)
*n.d. — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Gründenseestraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. Because it was roughly the same size as the nearby town hall and church, it was impossible to identify as a bomb shelter from the air. It provided space for 690 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt could accommodate a total of only 35,000 residents. The others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering them. Construction began in April 1941. In 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, the bomb shelter was refurbished to provide protection for one to two days in the event of an attack. It has been a listed monument since 2012.

Construction company: Hoch- und Tiefbau Wilhelm Franz, Hanau am Main
 Architect: Adam Heinrich Aßmann, Frankfurt
