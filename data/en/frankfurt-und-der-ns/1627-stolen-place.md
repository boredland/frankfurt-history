# Stolen Place

*Corneliusstraße 17*

![Corneliusstraße 17](../../images/5396b0ba6d4ea71750dd18a60b31fe98_original.jpg)
*1880 — Author: A. Mayer | License: ISG | Description: Corneliusstraße 17*

The property at Corneliusstrasse 17, with a plot size of 660 square meters, originally belonged to the Heinemann heir community, whose members consisted of anti-Semitic persecutees. Some of them were in an unknown location at the time of the sale. Due to persecution, they were forced to sell their property to the wife of a factory director from Wiesbaden and a daughter of one of the heiresses, who was not fully Jewish according to Nazi ideology, at a price of 60,000 Reichsmark, each in equal shares. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 61,700 Reichsmark and the market value to 56,400 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 7,987 Reichsmark. The parties shared the costs of the contract, including the land transfer tax. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of a rent increase.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
