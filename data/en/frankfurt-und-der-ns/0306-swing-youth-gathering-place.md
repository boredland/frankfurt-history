# swing youth gathering place

*Schneidhainer Straße 17*

![Schneidhainer Straße 17](../../images/d615954355e0da2f82ff23c0718c31c5_original.jpg)
*1931 — Author: Hannah Reeck | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-12240 | Description: Schneidhainer Straße 17*

The “Club der Kameruner” gathered in the restaurant now called the Menzza, formerly the Hellerhof. The clique was made up of some 60 to 160 young people—members of the swing scene aged 15 to 18 from Gallus, a working-class district. They called themselves the “Kameruner Kippe”. “Kippe” meant community; “Kamerun” was a colloquial but common name for Gallus. Swing fans set themselves apart from the Hitler Youth and the League of German Girls with their clothing and hairstyles: The boys often had shoulder-length hair and wore wide, bellbottom trousers, patterned sports jackets, and hats pushed down over the brow. The girls wore makeup and clothes that accentuated their figures. Boys and girls moreover spent their free time together, thus resisting the strict gender segregation of the National Socialist youth organizations.
