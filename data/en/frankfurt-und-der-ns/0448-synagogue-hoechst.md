# Synagogue Höchst

*Schleifergasse 5*

![Schleifergasse 5](../../images/Ph00482_jp_d3ed7e96d01c36d0c1a1e3aa28be7f99_original.jpg)
*1939 — Author: Leonhard Kleemann | License: HMF, Ph00482 | Description: Schleifergasse 5*

In addition to the synagogue on the market, the Jewish religious community had a mikvah (ritual bathhouse), a religious school, and a cemetery, which was located, however, in Bad Soden. From 1897 onwards, the community also had the option of burying their dead at the newly founded Jewish cemetery in Griesheim. The members of the community ranged from moderately orthodox to liberal. In 1932, 200 Jews lived in Höchst, most of them merchants or artisans of simple economic circumstances. The synagogue seated 150. It was dedicated on 14 December 1905 in the framework of a festive worship service. In the course of the excesses committed during the pogroms of 9/10 November 1938 it was burnt to the ground and its interior furnishings and religious objects destroyed. Mayor Friedrich Krebs of Frankfurt called for the demolition of the remains. In the years 1940–1942 the city built an air-raid shelter on the site. In the early 1960s, the Jewish Restitution Successor Organization (JRSO) demanded compensation in the amount of 236,900 Deutschmarks. The authorities rejected the JRSO demands on grounds that they were too high, and lowered the estimate by more than half. The JRSO was ultimately awarded compensation in the amount of 143,050 Deutschmarks.
