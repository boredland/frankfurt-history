# Clementine Children's Hospital

*Bornheimer Landwehr 62*

![Picture postcard of the Clementine Children's Hospital](../../images/C80395_jpg_6ef7e7c72899f209502322e5d38cfdf6_original.jpg)
*ca. 1900 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church C80395 | Description: Picture postcard of the Clementine Children's Hospital*

Starting in 1875, this was the site of one of the first and most modern children’s hospitals in Germany. After initially treating only girls, from 1928 onwards it also admitted boys. It was endowed by Louise von Rothschild and is named after her daughter Clementine von Rothschild, who died at a young age. Children of all faiths were admitted and treated here. In 1933 and the following years, the Jewish doctors fled the country to escape the Nazis. The Rothschild Foundation was dissolved in 1938 and the hospital came to be called the Deutsches Rotes Kreuz-Kinderkrankenhaus (German Red Cross Children’s Hospital). The building was destroyed in an air raid on the night of 4 October 1943. The patients and nurses survived the attack in an air-raid shelter in the cellar. Two reliefs depicting the donor and her daughter also survived. Later the clinic was moved to Schmitten in the Taunus Mountains, and in 1954 to a convalescent home for indigent Jewish children in Oberursel. The sale of the land at Bornheimer Landwehr 110 in the early 1950s generated enough proceeds to re-establish the clinic at a new site. It has been located at Theobald-Christ-Strasse 16 since 1954.
