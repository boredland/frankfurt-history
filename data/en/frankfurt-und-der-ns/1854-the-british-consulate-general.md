# The British Consulate General

*Guiolettstraße 62*

![Die Gedenktafel in der Guiollettstraße 62 erinnert an das Wirken und die Hilfe des Britisches Generalkonsulat: Smallbones und Dowden retteten mehrere Tausende Juden und Jüdinnen vor der nazionalsozialistischen Verfolgung.](../../images/1f940010fbe71768ef825b8374661b91_original.jpg)
*Author: Aliah Habl Corona | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Die Gedenktafel in der Guiollettstraße 62 erinnert an das Wirken und die Hilfe des Britisches Generalkonsulat: Smallbones und Dowden retteten mehrere Tausende Juden und Jüdinnen vor der nazionalsozialistischen Verfolgung.*

The British Consulate General in Frankfurt saved the lives of many Jewish people. Consul General Robert T. Smallbones and Vice Consul Arthur Dowden issued visas that allowed people to emigrate to Great Britain. These visas allowed people to stay in Great Britain for up to two years before emigrating elsewhere. Smallbones negotiated with the British Home Secretary and also with the Gestapo to free men from the concentration camps, making it possible for some of them to flee with their families.
The consulate aided Jews following the pogroms in November 1938, and 10,000 Jewish children were able to escape to England thanks to this help. In 2013, a bronze plaque was unveiled to honour and commemorate Smallbones and Dowden.
