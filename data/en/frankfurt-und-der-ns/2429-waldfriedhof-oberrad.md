*Burgenlandweg 10*

This cemetery is the last resting place of 756 Dutch victims of concentration camp made to work as forced laborers and who died in southern Germany. In total, 30,000 Dutch forced laborers, men and women, died in Germany between 1940 and 1945. The cemetery also has a section for German war victims. This is where Dimitry Szyló is buried. He was a forced laborer at the H. & K. Vetter furniture store. When he had a child with Emma Höser, a German chambermaid, he was arrested by the Gestapo and tortured. In April 1943, he died from his injuries. It is not known whether other forced laborers from other countries are also buried in the cemetery.
