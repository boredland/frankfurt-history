# Golub Lebedenko Square

*Lahnstreet 32*

![Commemorative plaque on Golub-Lebedenko Square in German.](../../images/0dcbcae5595b3f7b951239e9b13f3106_original.jpg)
*2025 — Author: Thomas Altmeyer | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Commemorative plaque on Golub-Lebedenko Square in German.*

In Memory of Two Forced Laborers Murdered at this Site

Since 1998, the official name of the square on the corner of Lahn- and Kriegkstrasse has been Golub-Lebedenko-Platz. On March 14, 1945, just days before the war’s end, two Ukrainian inmates fled the “Katzbach” concentration camp in the Adlerwerke. They were the 19-year-old Adam Golub and the 21-year-old Georgij Lebedenko. The SS guard units immediately took up the search, and half the neighborhood joined them. The two young men were shot to death by SS on Lahnstrasse before the eyes of the local residents.
