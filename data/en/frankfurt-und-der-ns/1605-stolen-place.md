# Stolen Place

*Arndtstraße 25*

![Arndtstraße 25](../../images/95b561e214b61da5a446fd2ce89d267b_original.jpg)
*ca. 1910 — License: ISG | Description: Arndtstraße 25*

The three-storey apartment building at Arndtstraße 25 with five apartments originally belonged to Erna Mina Goldmeier, née Lippmann. Due to persecution, the Jewish owner had to sell the property to the wife of a Frankfurt veterinarian for 40,000 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated August 16, 1939. The buyer revoked the tenancy right originally granted for the apartment used by the seller one day after the contract was concluded because the NSDAP Gau economic advisor demanded this. The local NSDAP group at the university judged the buyer's husband to be "politically impeccable (conditionally!). Very reluctant to buy badges for national celebrations." The authorities refrained from charging a compensatory levy to the German Reich.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
