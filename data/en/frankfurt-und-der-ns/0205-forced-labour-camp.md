# forced labour camp

*Am Römerhof*

![Forced labor camp, Am Römerhof](../../images/322c9938e8139c5da29a10ade95e90fb_original.jpg)
*1929 — Author: Landesbildstelle Hessen | License: HMF, Ph06335 | Description: Forced labor camp, Am Römerhof*

The Max Gerner company manufactured aircraft at Frankfurt’s first airport in the direct vicinity of the Rebstock grounds (now Am Römerhof). Forced labourers were presumably lodged on the company premises from 1942 onwards. The camp consisted of six barracks—one for the guard personnel and five for the forced labourers. In September 1942, 260 French and 53 Russian prisoners of war as well as 1 Czech and 35 Bulgarian forced labourers were registered here. By 1943 the number had risen to 493 Russian prisoners of war and 164 forced labourers from Eastern Europe and 14 from Italy. The Gerner company also operated two other forced labour camps in Frankfurt, at Boelckestrasse 45 (now Schmittstrasse) and Gutleutstrasse 394.
