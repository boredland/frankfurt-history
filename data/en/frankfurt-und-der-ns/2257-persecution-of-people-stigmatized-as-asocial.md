# Persecution of people stigmatized as "asocial"

*“Department of Hereditary and Racial Health”, City Health Authority*

![Braubachstraße 18-22](../../images/2aa84899f45940a0c6be7cb0b27b27a2_original.jpg)
*ca. 1936 — Author: Max Göllner | License: HMF, NS1366 | Description: Braubachstraße 18-22*

The group of persons listed in the so-called “hereditary register” also encompassed many who had been stigmatized as “asocial.” The Nazis considered alleged “asociality” hereditary. To keep persons labelled “asocial” from reproducing, they were subjected to forced sterilization. The “hereditary health court” drew on the “hereditary register” to arrive at the decision to sterilize a person. For each of the persons registered, the “keeper of the register” Hans Heimpel and his staff recorded the name, place of birth, and place of residence as well as information about any tubercular illnesses, addictive disorders, mental illnesses, and venereal diseases the person had. They received this information from clinics, the health authorities, and the welfare office. As a basis for a “genetic assessment,” the department staff also drew up a registry card for Sophie Gotthardt, née Müller, a queer who was persecuted for being a prostitute.

*Author: Frauke Steinhäuser*
