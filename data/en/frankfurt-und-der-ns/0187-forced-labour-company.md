# forced labour company

*Hahnstraße 18-22*

![Picture postcard with aerial view of the dry-cleaning works of the company Färberei Gebrüder Röver](../../images/d62a0d1c024837349ae161a685823967_original.jpg)
*after 1900 — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80412 | Description: Picture postcard with aerial view of the dry-cleaning works of the company Färberei Gebrüder Röver*

During World War II, the drycleaning plant and dye works Gebrüder Röver operated two forced labour camps in Frankfurt. In 1940 the company made use of 20 prisoners of war from Belgium and 10 from France, who were presumably lodged on the factory grounds here in Kesselbergstrasse (now Hahnstrasse). The second camp was located in a barrack on the Union sports field in Güntherstrasse. In 1942 and 1943, some 80 forced labourers, primarily from Russia, were housed there.
