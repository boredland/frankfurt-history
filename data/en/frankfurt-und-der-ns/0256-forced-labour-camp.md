# Forced labour camp

*Musikantenweg 80*

![Photo of a building belonging to Calor Emag Elektrizitäts-AG, which was used as a forced labour camp](../../images/e22e059b94de13afd03003f8d9f14158_original.jpg)
*2012 — Author: Martin Starl | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2013-564 | Description: Photo of a building belonging to Calor Emag Elektrizitäts-AG, which was used as a forced labour camp*

The Calor Emag Elektrizitäts-AG, a company from Ratingen specializing in high- and low-voltage systems, exploited forced labourers in its Frankfurt plant. Some of them were lodged in a camp at Musikantenweg 80; there was another camp at Bornheimer Landwehr 41. Both men and women worked at the factory. They were lodged separately. In 2003, the Institut für Stadtgeschichte (Institute of Municipal History) found evidence that 20 men from Russia were registered in Musikantenweg in 1943. All that is known about the other camp is that Russian women had their lodgings there.
