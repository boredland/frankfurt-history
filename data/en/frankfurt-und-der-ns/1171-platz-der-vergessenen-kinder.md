# Platz der Vergessenen Kinder

*Hans-Thoma-Straße 22-24*

![Children's home of the Association of Female Welfare e.V](../../images/isg_gt_kin_113dc4be4c1a4fe776c895e329a4476f_original.jpg)
*License: © Manfred Rosenthal, Middletown (USA) | Description: Children's home of the Association of Female Welfare e.V*

The children's home of the Verein der Weiblichen Fürsorge e.V., which was co-founded by the Frankfurt social reformer Bertha Pappenheim, went into operation in 1911 at the latest. Initially, a house in Schulstraße rented from the city treasury served as a hostel. In 1918, the house at Hans-Thoma-Straße 24 was acquired thanks to various donations, in particular from the merchant's widow Bertha Schwarzschild. From March 1919, around 40 Jewish children up to the age of six, who had been orphaned or could not be cared for by their parents, found a place to stay here. From around 1935, the home also took in children whose parents had become impoverished due to Nazi persecution or were preparing to flee Germany. There were also children from closed homes and children from surrounding rural communities who were to be protected from the anti-Jewish atmosphere there. Individual children from the children's home were deported with their families as early as October 1941. The majority of the children and their caregivers were deported to Theresienstadt in September 1942, most of them later murdered in Auschwitz.

 

In April 1943, the NSDAP acquired the building and used it as an office. In the post-war years, the CDU-Hessen had its office here before the building was restituted to the Jewish Restitution Successor Organization in October 1952.The building was later used by the Protestant Relief Organization.In 2002, a new owner had the houses 22-24 in Hans-Thoma-Straße demolished and erected a uniform building on the double plot.

*Author: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt*
