# Lilo Hides during the War

*Out and about in Frankfurt with Lilo*

![](../../images/4bed915cb2676c63b5aeb3d0a372a2b2_original.jpg)

More and more of Frankfurt’s old town was destroyed by bombs. That’s why Lilo’s family moved to a new housing development in the Westend district.
The housing development was originally intended for people who worked for the IG Farben company, but many of them had fled because of the war. That’s how Lilo’s family was able to find an apartment there. They couldn’t leave the city, because they were classified as a Jewish family.
Lilo lived in constant fear of being discovered, but tried to stay brave.
It was in this period that her little sister Gerdi was born, which brought her a lot of joy.
Shortly before the end of the war, the horrible news came: Helmut and Lilo’s mother were sent to the Terezín camp. Everyone was extremely worried about them.
Now Lilo had to take responsibility and care for Gerdi.
When her father was drafted for war duty, she went to live with neighbors and Gerdi was put in a children’s home.
Later, the neighbors also wanted to flee, but Lilo refused.
She hid in the cellar until the war was over. In Frankfurt, the war ended on March 29, 1945.
Lilo’s father came back, and they went and picked up Gerdi from the children’s home. Soon afterwards, her mother and Helmut also came back to Frankfurt.
The family was reunited and everyone had survived the war.

How did people find out about Lilo’s story? If you’d like to know, have a look at “A Street for Lilo.”

*Author: Susanne Thimm*
