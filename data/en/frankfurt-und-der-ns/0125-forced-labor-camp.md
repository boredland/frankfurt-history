# Forced labor camp

*Schwanthalerstraße 50*

![Picture postcard with exterior and interior view of the Schwanthalerhof Inn](../../images/f6f9b4390340f181677c60ba5b8048f1_original.jpg)
*n.D. — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80416 | Description: Picture postcard with exterior and interior view of the Schwanthalerhof Inn*

During World War II, the Schwanthaler Hof restaurant and dance hall was the location of a forced labour camp. The inmates had to work primarily for the German Reich Railway, but also for a number of companies in Sachsenhausen. A register of the persons who lived in this building during the Nazi era has come down to us in the Institut für Stadtgeschichte (Institute of Municipal History). It lists the names of approximately 640 forced labourers. Between 16 June 1944 and 20 March 1945, the E. F. Wiedmann metalware factory also used this camp to lodge 463 forced labourers from Russia and France. In 1944, some of the Russian forced labourers were taken to the Hadamar killing facility and presumably murdered. According to post-war police records, the Wiedmann company began using forced labourers as far back as 1942. The Schoeller & Co. electrical engineering factory based in Mörfelder Landstrasse 115–119 probably also housed forced labourers here.
