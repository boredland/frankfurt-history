# forced labor company

*Solmsstraße 2-26*

![Forced labor company, Solmsstraße 2-26](../../images/1b3e44d0512bf9099fe8967245314627_original.jpg)
*before 1945 — Author: Max Göllner | License: HMF, Z.2005Göllner.000002 | Description: Forced labor company, Solmsstraße 2-26*

Frankfurter Maschinenbau (formerly Pokorny & Wittekind), a mechanical engineering company, was based at Solmsstrasse 2–26. It exploited forced labourers and operated three camps of its own to lodge them. As many as 200 forced labourers had their accommodations in Hotel Savoy at Wiesenhüttenstrasse 42. In 1943, 74 women and 9 men from Russia, 77 women and 24 men from Ukraine, 2 Belarusian women, 16 Polish women, and 2 further forced labourers were registered at another camp at Ginnheimer Stadtweg 108. Virtually nothing is known about the company’s third camp apart from the fact that it was located in Bismarckallee (no number, now Theodor-Heuss-Allee).
