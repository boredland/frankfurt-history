# forced labour camp

*Bornheimer Landwehr 48*

![Forced labor camp](../../images/ac885220c332892cfb60280695c3c989_original.jpg)
*2019 — Author: Martin Starl | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2019-721 | Description: Forced labor camp*

This was the location of the Philipp Schlecht bakery. Schlecht also made use of forced labourers. Research on the subject has not yet brought information to light on how many people worked here or where they came from. During World War II, the German economy could only be sustained with the aid of forced labour. Widespread use was made of forced labourers—in business establishments from the small-scale shop to the large corporation, as well as on farms and in private households. The Nazis forced altogether some 26 million persons to perform labour in the German Reich and the occupied territories. In Frankfurt, the average number of forced labourers at any given time during the war years was approximately 25,000. They comprised civilians who had been recruited voluntarily or forcibly as well as prisoners of war. Owing to the fact that many of them died, the demand for new labourers was high. The majority of them were deported to the German Reich from the occupied territories in Eastern Europe. Some 50,000 persons who performed forced labour in Frankfurt during the war years are known by name. As the residential registration records of the years 1933–1945 have not come down to us in their entirety, the actual number of labourers forced to work here is probably much higher.
