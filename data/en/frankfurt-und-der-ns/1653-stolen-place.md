# Stolen Place

*Friedberger Anlage 16*

![Friedberger Anlage 16](../../images/1c0bdf783b2e81bb0029f64e975fee44_original.jpg)
*2012 — Author: Martin Starl | License: ISG | Description: Friedberger Anlage 16*

The apartment building at Friedberger Anlage 16 originally belonged to the Jewish private owner Alfred Max Bottenwieser and his brother, the merchant Ernst Bottenwieser, who had inherited the house from their parents. Max Alfed Bottenwieser lived at Friedberger Anlage 16 in 1939, while his brother lived in exile in London. The property covered an area of 621 square meters. The house contained a 7-room apartment, a 4-room apartment, a 3.5-room apartment and a 2-room apartment. In May 1939, there were three Jewish tenants living in the house who, according to the property manager, "will have to move out shortly". Presumably Alfred Max Bottenwieser also wanted to flee into exile. Due to the persecution, they had to sell their house to a non-Jewish Friedberg student councilor, his wife and another buyer from Friedberg for 30,500 Reichsmark in a contract dated March 2, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value was 31,500 Reichsmark and the market value was 33,000 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 5,280 Reichsmark. The contract costs, including the estate agent's commission, were shared by the parties. The building authorities approved the purchase contract on June 9, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of rent increases.
