# House of the Frohnhausen and Cohn families

*Eckenheimer Landstraße 345*

![](../../images/448e13371ccd8dceec349cc83e19aefa_original.jpg)
*2023*

Frieda and Albert Frohnhausen and their daughter Chana, born in the same year, have lived here on the first floor since the house was built in 1938. Albert Frohnhausen was temporarily imprisoned in Buchenwald after the November pogrom of 1938. The family later had to move to Eckenheimer Landstraße 238. From there, they were deported to Raasiku in Estonia in 1942 and presumably murdered there. Rosalie Cohn lived on the 2nd floor with her husband Siegmund. Siegmund Cohn died in November 1939 and was buried in the Jewish cemetery in Rat-Beil-Straße. Rosalie Cohn was initially deported to Theresienstadt in 1942. In 1944, she was deported to Ausschwitz and probably murdered there.
