# Stolen Place

*Bockenheimer Anlage 40*

![Bockenheimer Anlage 40](../../images/b75d72aa75b0981b3b696eef43313e86_original.jpg)
*Author: Leonhard Kleemann | License: HMF | Description: Bockenheimer Anlage 40*

The property at Bockenheimer Anlage 40 originally belonged to the writer Isaak Weiller, who lived at Hammanstraße 21. Due to persecution, he had to sell his apartment building with a plot size of 1,310 square meters, a officially tax-assessed value of 74,900 Reichsmark and a market value of 77,000 Reichsmark to Metallgesellschaft for 71,000 Reichsmark in a purchase agreement dated 11 January 1939. The apartment building had two floors and a total of four 3-room apartments, two 2-room apartments and one 1-room apartment. The house had central heating. The rental income amounted to 780 Reichsmark per month. By July 1939, the Metallgesellschaft had also acquired the properties at Bockenheimer Anlage 41, Leerbachstraße 9 and 19 from Jewish owners because it wanted to expand its administrative building. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy in favor of the Reich. The buyer and seller each bore half of the costs of the sale.
