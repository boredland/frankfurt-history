# Family home of the Beit von Speyer family

*Kennedyallee 70*

One of 170 buildings that went to the City of Frankfurt through dispossession.

![Villa Beit from Speyer. Built in 1901 (contemporary photo from 1902)](../../images/4c05c1a778880be74368d704a20026f0_original.jpg)
*1902 — Author: unbekannt | Description: Villa Beit from Speyer. Built in 1901 (contemporary photo from 1902)*

Part of today's luxury hotel Villa Kennedy was once the home of the Jewish Beit von Speyer family. Lucie Beit, née Speyer, died in 1918, Eduard Beit von Speyer in 1933. Their three children emigrated. The city of Frankfurt planned to convert the 8 000 square metre property into a Kaiser Wilhelm Institute (forerunner of the Max Planck Institute). The family heirs were obliged to sell their property below value and the Institute of Biophysics took it over in 1938. During the Second World War, radiation research vital to the war effort was carried out there. In 1948, the city of Frankfurt compensated the heirs with DM150,000. The real value of the property, however, was revealed in the year 2000: the city sold the site for DM180 million. This was not an isolated case: the villa was one of around 170 properties appropriated by the city in this way.

*Author: Dieter Wesp*
