# Café Ahl / Café Goetheplatz

*Roßmarkt*

![Black-and-white drawing with ink and foil pencil of a dancing couple inside a bar](../../images/6cc229e585670999cbfe2083b0b1ee34_original.jpg)
*2022 — Author: Katharina Müller | License: VG Bild-Kunst, Bonn 2022 | Description: Black-and-white drawing with ink and foil pencil of a dancing couple inside a bar*

Café Goetheplatz was a popular gathering place of Frankfurt’s jazz and swing scene from 1939 onwards. Particularly the “O.K.-Gang” and the “Harlem-Club” met here to listen to the prohibited swing records. They were able to escape quickly through the back entrance when a Hitler Youth patrol arrived on the scene.
 The “Harlem-Club” was made up of about 20 young men and women who knew each other from their neighbourhoods, clubs, or schools, or through friends. The name was inspired by the well-known jazz piece “Harlem”, whose melody became a kind of secret password among swing fans. They would whistle it to show that they belonged. One member, Franz Kremer, later recalled that, starting in the fall of 1940 or the spring of 1941, the club would meet at Café Goetheplatz around 5.00 or 6.00 pm. The owner, Wilhelm Ahl, permitted them to play their records. A lively and cheerful mood prevailed. From there the young people would make the rounds to various other bars in the old town. Café Goetheplatz was shut down on 6 March 1942 by order of the police. Its owner Wilhelm Ahl had already been in custody since December 1941.
 
 To learn more about what happened to the “Harlem-Club” after that, visit the former Frankfurt am Main Gestapo headquarters at Lindenstrasse or read the biography of Franz Kremer (in German).
