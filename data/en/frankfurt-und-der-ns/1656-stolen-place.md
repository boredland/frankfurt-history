# Stolen Place

*Friedrichstraße 63*

![Friedrichstraße 63](../../images/4c6eb00c260e76d5c00dd72856006312_original.jpg)
*ca. 1970 — Author: unbekannt | License: ISG | Description: Friedrichstraße 63*

The villa at Friedrichstraße 63 / corner of Siesmayerstraße originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Eduard Riesser and his wife Elisabeth, née Mayer. The property covered an area of 1,484 square meters. The house had been converted from a detached house into a tenement. It contained two 4-room apartments, one 3-room apartment and two 2-room apartments. A large garden surrounded the building. Due to persecution, the executor of the couple's final will had to sell their property to a non-Jewish private individual from Frankfurt and his wife for 67,000 Reichsmark under a contract dated March 3, 1941. The officially tax-assessed value was 61,600 Reichsmark and the market value was 66,100 Reichsmark. The contract costs, including land transfer tax and estate agent's commission, were shared between the parties. Although the responsible local NSDAP group judged the buyer to be a "grumbler" and criticized his unwillingness to donate to the NSV and WHW, the building authority approved the purchase contract on 23 June 1941. The district economic advisor had previously determined "that it is hardly possible to speak of a special de-Jewification gain that should be denied to politically unobjectionable persons". The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
