# Early (“wild”) concentration camp

*Windthorstraße 60-62*

![Höchst Barracks, west entrance.](../../images/94ac1b4c7d41d6881a6081cc2b69f89b_original.jpg)
*1923 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Sig. S7A, 1998-22802 | Description: Höchst Barracks, west entrance.*

In May and June 1933, the SA (Storm Troopers), a paramilitary wing of the Nazi Party, set up a concentration camp in the Höchster Kaserne barracks. The SA’s political opponents from Unterliederbach, Nied, Zeilsheim, Sindlingen, Schwanheim, Sossenheim, Griesheim and Höchst were arrested and brought here, where they were humiliated, threatened and abused. In these actions, the NSDAP local branch leaders also played a significant role, since they had ordered the arrests. Josef Schimmel, Höchst local branch leader, and Bernhard Wellenkamp, Nied local branch leader, were renowned for their brutality. These early concentration camps aimed at destroying political opposition to the Nazis.
