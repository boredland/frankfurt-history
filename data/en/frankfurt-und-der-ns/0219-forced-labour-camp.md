# forced labour camp

*Norbert-Wollheim-Platz 1*

![Forced labor camp, Norbert-Wollheim-Platz 1](../../images/6c3cd7858d4fbb52ae73dd9370ff1257_original.jpg)
*1937 — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, C31431 | Description: Forced labor camp, Norbert-Wollheim-Platz 1*

The dyestuffs manufacturer I.G. Farben-Industrie had its administrative headquarters at Grüneburgplatz 20 (now Norbert-Wollheim-Platz) and operated a forced labour camp here. In the autumn of 1942, the camp is thought to have lodged 8 men from Russia. During the war the company operated altogether more than 20 forced labour camps in Frankfurt alone. It was already making use of forced labourers in its three Frankfurt chemical plants in Höchst, Griesheim, and Mainkur as early as March 1940. By the end of 1945, I.G. Farben had exploited altogether some 10,000 forced and civilian labourers from more than 20 countries in Frankfurt alone. These persons included prisoners of war as well as civilians who were recruited voluntarily or forcibly, but also many who had been deported to Frankfurt from Eastern Europe. Civilian workers were forced labourers who were neither prisoners of war nor concentration camp inmates.
