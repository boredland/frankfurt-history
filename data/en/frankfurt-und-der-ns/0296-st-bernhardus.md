# St. Bernhardus

*Koselstraße 11-13*

![Photo of St. Bernhard Church](../../images/6725708e581821b2f4d138f90d8816c0_original.jpg)
*before 1945 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF C26535 | Description: Photo of St. Bernhard Church*

Father Alois Eckert (1890–1969) was the priest of the Catholic Church of St Bernard from 1926 to 1950. In April 1933 he published an article in the Catholic Rhein-Mainische-Volkszeitung condemning the National Socialists’ hostility towards Jews. In 1934, he and other clerics founded the Rhein-Mein-Klerus-Front, a loose alliance which called for the Catholic Church to make a public declaration against the persecution of Jews. Eckert also took a clear stand when he refused to hoist the swastika flag on 9 November 1935. This was the day commemorating the Nazis who had died during Hitler’s Beer Hall Putsch in 1923. For his “flag offence”, Alois Eckert had to go to prison until 1937. He moreover supported young Catholics who continued meeting despite the ban on Catholic youth organizations. Among them were a group of Catholic “Sturmscharen” (stormtroopers) around Bernhard Becker, who studied ecclesiastical crafts at the Städel School and met a cruel death in the Gestapo detention centre in Hammelsgasse in 1937. Approximately 1,000 persons came to his funeral, which was presided over by Father Alois Eckert. Their attendance was a sign of protest against the brutal persecution of citizens by the Nazi regime. After the war, Alois Eckert dedicated himself to the cathedral reconstruction cause.
