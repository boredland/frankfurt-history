# forced labour camp

*Palleskestraße 32*

![Forced labor camp, Palleskestraße 32](../../images/bbcb9fedc56e853b77de79fbc39f494b_original.jpg)
*ca. 1914 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-23146 | Description: Forced labor camp, Palleskestraße 32*

This was the location of a forced labour camp of the Breuerwerke machine factory, which was based at Kurmainzer Strasse 2–4. 
 When the war began, all manufacturers adapted their production activities to the needs of the Wehrmacht (the German army under National Socialism). Forced labourers were put to work to maintain production that was vital to the war effort. They had to replace the workers who had been called up for military service.
 In September 1942, this camp lodged 154 forced labourers from Russia. According to the Institut für Stadtgeschichte (Institute of Municipal History), by 1943 the camp population was made up of 352 forced labourers from Russia, 45 from Poland, 118 from France, 37 from Belgium, and 2 from Italy. Many of them were women. In 1945 some 375 persons lived in this camp. Owing to the racist worldview of the Nazis, forced labourers from Eastern Europe were paid and treated worse than other workers. The nineteen-year-old Julia Swiniuch from Russia was deported to Hadamar and murdered there, as can be inferred from her death certificate.
