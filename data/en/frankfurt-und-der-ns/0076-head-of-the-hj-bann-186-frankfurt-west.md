# head of the HJ-Bann 186 (Frankfurt-West)

*Guiollettstraße 21*

![Photo of the headquarters of the leadership of the HJ-Bann 186 (Frankfurt-West)](../../images/d0278c1de76097b8d1631f40b10788a7_original.jpg)
*1863 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-13074 | Description: Photo of the headquarters of the leadership of the HJ-Bann 186 (Frankfurt-West)*

The Frankfurt chapter of the Hitler Youth organization was founded in 1927. The Hitler Youth was subordinate to the Reich Youth Leadership and, like other Nazi organizations, hierarchically structured. It oversaw so-called “Gebiete”, or areas, which were each responsible for some 150,000 male members of the Hitler Youth between the ages 10 and 18 years. Frankfurt was located in Gebiet 13, to which all male Hitler Youth in the Hesse-Nassau region belonged. The Gebiete were subdivided into “Banne”, each of which numbered approximately 3,000 boys. Bann 186, which had its headquarters here around 1933, was responsible for the Frankfurt West district.
