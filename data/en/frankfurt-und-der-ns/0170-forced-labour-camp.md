# forced labour camp

*Alt Schwanheim 8*

![Picture postcard showing the buildings at Alt-Schwanheim 8 (left) and Alt-Schwanheim 6 (right)](../../images/11f05813b969cd9b2b9435bcd01345b4_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: Unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80406 | Description: Picture postcard showing the buildings at Alt-Schwanheim 8 (left) and Alt-Schwanheim 6 (right)*

The Jakob Heisack restaurant housed 18 forced labourers from France in its main dining room. In 1947, the 21st police district reported that 20 persons from Belgium were also lodged here in the period from 1940 to March 1945. They were liberated by the U.S. Army on 29 March 1945. On this document, someone entered the number “25” by hand—that is, 25 persons from Belgium—and added “unknown” with regard to their occupation. This document is an example of the efforts made in the post-war period to document forced labour in Frankfurt as precisely as possible. The document is among the holdings of altogether 329 kept in the Arolsen Archives on this subject.
