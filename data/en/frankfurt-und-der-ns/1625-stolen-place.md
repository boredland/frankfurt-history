# Stolen Place

*Brückenstraße 26*

![Brückenstraße 26](../../images/31b6daea34695e13ba29364980462228_original.jpg)
*before 1941 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: HMF | Description: Brückenstraße 26*

The property at Brückenstraße 26 / corner of Dreikönigstraße originally belonged to the company J. C. Foltz-Eberle oHG, which had Jewish owners. It was a property with guest rights and a floor area of 176 square meters. Due to persecution, the property had to be sold to the Frankfurt Henninger brewery for 34,500 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated January 12, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value was 34,500 Reichsmark and the market value was 34,000 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 5,319 Reichsmark. The property had already been handed over on December 1, 1938. All costs arising from the contract, including the estate agent's commission, were shared by the parties. Adolf Miersch ordered the contract to be approved immediately because the German Labor Front (DAF) had intervened so that the salary claims of the Folz-Eberle employees could be satisfied as quickly as possible from the sale. The Nazi authorities did not stipulate a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of a rent increase.
