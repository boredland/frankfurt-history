# Forced labour camp

*Saarbrücker Straße 4*

![Picture postcard with exterior view of the Schwanheim gymnastics club](../../images/11118bc2b3e06577663bc84e73151e25_original.jpg)
*1939 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF / Sammlung Church C80405 | Description: Picture postcard with exterior view of the Schwanheim gymnastics club*

The I.G. Farben-Industrie, a manufacturer of dyestuffs, made use of forced labourers in its three Frankfurt chemical plants, Höchst, Griesheim, and Mainkur, from March 1940 onwards. The company operated more than twenty forced labour camps in Frankfurt. One of them was located here in the gymnasium of the Schwanheim gymnastics club at Saarbrücker Strasse 6. I.G. Farben also had several adjoining buildings erected to accommodate French prisoners of war. Between 1943 and 1944, the camp lodged 34 forced labourers who worked at the Griesheim plant.
 By the end of 1945, I.G. Farben had exploited altogether some 10,000 forced and civilian labourers from more than 20 countries in Frankfurt alone. This was the only way of maintaining production that was vital to the war effort at a time when more and more employees were called up for military service. Among the forced labourers were prisoners of war as well as civilian workers who had been recruited voluntarily or forcibly in the occupied countries of Western Europe, but also many who had been deported to Frankfurt from Eastern Europe. Civilian workers were forced labourers who were neither prisoners of war nor concentration camp inmates.
