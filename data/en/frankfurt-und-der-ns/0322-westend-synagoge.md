# Westend Synagoge

*Freiherr-vom-Stein-Straße 28-32*

![Westend Synagogue, Freiherr-vom-Stein-Straße 28-32](../../images/fd5c07293bd77b08600cefeef0136ce2_original.jpg)
*ca. 1911 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2009-145 | Description: Westend Synagogue, Freiherr-vom-Stein-Straße 28-32*

Established for the liberal members of the Jewish community, the Westend Synagogue was consecrated in 1910. The central domed structure was built in the Egyptian-Assyrian style and seated about 1,000. The building also housed administration rooms, flats, a small prayer room for weekday services, and a prayer room used as a lecture room by yeshiva students. This synagogue differed from others in Frankfurt in that women sat not only in the galleries, but also on the ground floor. There, however, the worshippers were separated by gender: the men at the right, the women at the left. The synagogue had a marble Torah shrine, ten Torah scrolls, numerous Torah finials, Torah shields, and ritual objects such as Hanukkah menorahs as well as an organ. 
 
 On 10 November 1938, members of the SA (Storm Troopers) and the Hitler Youth set fire to the synagogue. The interior and the roof were severely damaged, valuable books and music completely destroyed. Shortly thereafter the Jewish community was forced to sell the building to the city, which used it to store opera stage sets and the furniture of bombing victims. On 20 March 1944, a bomb caused irreparable damage to the interior. Unlike the other large synagogues in Frankfurt, however, the Westend Synagogue was never completely destroyed, either by pogroms or by the war. After the war, the building was returned to the Jewish community, which launched its reconstruction in 1948. The reconsecration of the synagogue took place on 7 May 1950. In a further project carried out between 1988 and 1994 it was restored to its original state. Today the large main sanctuary serves as an Orthodox synagogue.
