# Stolen Place

*Eschersheimer Landstraße 68*

![Eschersheimer Landstraße 68](../../images/5113d10ffa8bd6c80634a8a608e0117a_original.jpg)
*2002 — Author: Martin Starl | License: ISG | Description: Eschersheimer Landstraße 68*

The property at Eschersheimer Landstraße 68 originally belonged to the Viennese Jew Jachiel Türkl. The property comprised a plot of 332 square meters. Due to persecution, he had to sell his house to a non-Jewish doctor from Karlsruhe and his wife for 30,000 Reichsmark in a contract dated September 2, 1938. The annual rental income amounted to 5,028 Reichsmark. The officially tax-assessed value also amounted to 30,000 Reichsmark. The market value was 32,200 Reichsmark. The contract costs, including the estate agent's commission and land transfer tax, were borne by the buyer. The building authority approved the contract on 25 August 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of rent increases.
