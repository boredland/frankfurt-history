# Anne Frank's House

*Marbachweg 307*

![Anne Frank House at Marbachweg 307](../../images/dffc67d5b7e4a886bdf755dca7ee6a5f_original.jpg)
*Description: Anne Frank House at Marbachweg 307*

Anne Frank was born on June 12, 1929, and her family lived at Marbachweg 307 and later at Ganghoferstraße 24. Her father Otto Frank's ancestors can be traced back to the 16th century in Frankfurt's Judengasse. Anne and her sister Margot grew up in a Christian neighborhood. The family had long since left behind the restrictions that had hindered Jews before their legal equality.
Shortly after the National Socialist Party took over the city government in Frankfurt am Main in 1933, the Franks moved to the Netherlands. The rapidly growing hostility towards Jews and the economic situation forced them to do so.
When the Netherlands was occupied by the German Reich in 1940, the anti-Jewish persecution there caught up with them. To escape the persecution, the family hid in the back of their business premises in Amsterdam.
This is where Anne Frank's diary was written. It is one of the most important documents from the time of the Holocaust. Anne Frank's thoughts are also an appeal for humanity, fed by the spirit of the liberal bourgeoisie.
On August 4, 1944, the hiding place was betrayed and those in hiding were deported to the extermination camps. Edith died in Auschwitz, Anne and Margot died in Bergen-Belsen. Otto Frank was the only survivor of the family.

*Author: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt*
