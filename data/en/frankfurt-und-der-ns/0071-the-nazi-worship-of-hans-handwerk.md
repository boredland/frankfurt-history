# the nazi worship of Hans Handwerk

*Lange Straße 4-6*

![Photo of an antechamber at the Heilig Geist Hospital](../../images/65b1e7e92bce31aa391cca46bd145f00_original.jpg)
*1934 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. H.14.10 Nr. 82 | Description: Photo of an antechamber at the Heilig Geist Hospital*

On 4 July 1932, Hans Handwerk was shot in the vicinity of his home. He was a member of the SA (Storm Troopers), a paramilitary association in the Nazi party. After taking part in a Nazi parade, he had been present at a street brawl with anti-fascists that also involved handguns. But there was never any definite evidence to show which gun fired the bullet that killed him. In the “Perlenfabrik” (“glass bead factory ”) concentration camp in July 1933, the SA extracted a confession under torture from 19-year-old Communist Josef Reitinger. Later, Reitinger retracted his confession. At his trial, the court rejected eyewitness reports in his favour as not credible. Josef Reitinger was sentenced to death and executed on 10 November 1934 in Preungesheim prison. In honour of Hans Handwerk, the Nazi city administration renamed Lange Straße after him, and set up a memorial plaque in the entrance hall of the Hospital zum Heiligen Geist. Even years after his death, Hans Handwerk was depicted as one of National Socialism’s early champions. This style of veneration is still prevalent in right-wing circles today. Josef Reitinger’s trial exemplifies how the law was interpreted at that time to facilitate judicial murder. Reitinger’s sentence has still not been revoked today.
