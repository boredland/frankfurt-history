# Jewish welfare house

*Königswarterstraße 16*

![Floor plan of the House of Jewish Welfare](../../images/62b5ce5b07b976a65906a5c75f80a728_original.jpg)
*ca. 1875 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-14049 | Description: Floor plan of the House of Jewish Welfare*

In the National Socialist era, a large number of charitable institutions such as the Jewish Welfare Agency and the Jewish Winter Relief Agency were located at Quinckestrasse 24–26 (now Königswarter Strasse 16). The property belonged to the Jewish Community of Frankfurt. Originally this was also the site of the Jewish Community Hospital, which had been built around 1873 and dedicated in 1875. A generous donation from the banker Isaak Königswarter had made the construction possible. In 1879, the street’s name had been changed from Grüner Weg to Königswarterstrasse in honour of the donor. The Nazi municipal administration renamed it Quinckestrasse after the “Aryan” physician Prof. Dr. Heinrich Irenäus Quincke. 
 
 On 9 November 1938, members of the Storm Troopers (SA) and the National Socialist German Workers’ Party plundered the building and closed it. They arrested the directors and the management and took them to the Festhalle, or festival hall, which served as an assembly point for deportations during the November pogroms.
 
 The Jewish Restitution Successor Organization (JRSO) was the official trustee of heirless Jewish property in the American occupied zone from 1948 onwards. It later claimed damages for the destruction of the interior. In 1961, however, the compensation authorities rejected this claim. The building was destroyed by aerial attacks during the war and later torn down. In 1945, the street name was changed back to Königswarterstrasse. Today the Red Cross Clinic is located at the site.
