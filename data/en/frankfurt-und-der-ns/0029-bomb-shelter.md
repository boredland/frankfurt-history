# Bomb shelter

*Fabriciusstraße 61-63*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Fabriciusstraße](../../images/5a9ba8799c7b50408c579ad24e8dec7d_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Fabriciusstraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme” and offered protection to 434 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. In 1940, 46 persons were forced to work on the construction site. The building was completed on 1 July 1942. The heavy air-raid doors were concealed behind wooden doors still in existence today. The shelter currently houses the “Künstlerbörse”, which offers rehearsal rooms for musicians.

Construction company: Robert Kögel
 Architect: Karl Olsson
