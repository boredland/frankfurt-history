# Bomb shelter

*Praunheimer Landstraße 78*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Praunheimer Landstraße](../../images/38f03fe1184e1f267d5c9202755f44d0_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Praunheimer Landstraße*

This bomb shelter was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and served to protect residents of Hausen. It provided space for 536 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered protection for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction began in April 1941. The building imitated those nearby in form and size as a means of camouflaging it. In 1965, during the Cold War, the shelter was renovated to provide protection in the event of an attack. It was torn down in 1991.

Construction company: Gebrüder Weil, Limburg
 Architects: Karl und Stefan Blattner, Frankfurt
