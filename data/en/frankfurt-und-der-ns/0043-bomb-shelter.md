# Bomb shelter

*Kaiser-Sigmund-Straße 72*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Kaiser-Sigmund-Straße](../../images/8874b2d0b4dc79d08ef705eddd77680e_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Kaiser-Sigmund-Straße*

This bomb shelter was part of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme” and offered protection to 1,480 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. This shelter was well camouflaged, as the residential buildings in the area were of a similar size. Construction began in August 1941. This was the largest and most expensive air-raid protection shelter in the city. Today it belongs to the Institute for Federal Real Estate (Bima). Until March 2022, it offered rehearsal rooms for musicians.

Construction company: Gebrüder Weil, Limburg
 Architects: Franz Hufnagel, government architect, Frankfurt; Fritz Maier, engineer and stress analyst, Frankfurt
