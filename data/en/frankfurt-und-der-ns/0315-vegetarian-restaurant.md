# vegetarian restaurant

*Steinweg 10*

![Ufa Theatre in the Swan with film poster for ‘Der Gasmann’](../../images/ns0558_jpg_8deda27bef7c3ddbf8b0b7d207e00d6d_original.jpg)
*1941 — Author: unbekannt | License: DFF – Deutsches Filminstitut &amp; Filmmuseum, Frankfurt | Description: Ufa Theatre in the Swan with film poster for ‘Der Gasmann’*

The vegetarian restaurant (VeGa) run by Anna Beyer and Ludwig Gehm—both members of the resistance—was on the first floor of the building at Steinweg 10. With their income they financed the Internationaler Sozialistischer Kampfbund (ISK; International Socialist Militant League), which was active in the underground. In the cellar of the restaurant, they initially printed antifascist leaflets in editions of up to 2,000. One of the leaflets, entitled “The Nazis Are Our Misfortune”, had a distorted picture of Adolf Hitler’s face on it. When it became too risky for Beyer and Gehm to print the leaflets, Rhine boatmen and railwaymen smuggled leaflets printed elsewhere to Frankfurt. This was made possible by the network of the International Transport Workers’ Federation. 
 
 The Frankfurt ISK group counted some 10 to 15 members. They organized spectacular acts of resistance and sabotaged mass Nazi events such as the May 1st, 1934 celebration in the Ostpark. Before the inauguration of the first section of the Darmstadt–Frankfurt motorway near the stadium on 20 May 1935, they destroyed the loudspeakers, sawed into the stand meant to accommodate the speakers for the radio broadcast, and painted slogans such as “Hitler = War” and “Down with Hitler” on the bridges and roadway. As there was not enough time to remove the slogans by the time of the ceremonial act, the Nazis covered them with swastikas. The aim of the ISK operation was to encourage people to join in the struggle against the Nazis. Ludwig Gehm was ultimately found out when he tried to help a conscientious objector flee but the escape plan failed. During his interrogation, the conscientious objector told the Gestapo about the members of the resistance group. Ludwig Gehm was arrested in the VeGa, sentenced to two years in prison, and in 1939 deported to the Buchenwald concentration camp. Anna Beyer fled into exile in 1937 and continued her resistance activities there.
