# Home of the resis­tance figh­ter Lore Wolf

*Gerlachstraße 24*

![Lore Wolf with her daughter Hannelore ca. 1926 in front of the house in Gerlachstreet.](../../images/698c02299c1672526336f2dd32936d4e_original.jpg)
*approx. 1926 — License: Privat (Anja Bandas) | Description: Lore Wolf with her daughter Hannelore ca. 1926 in front of the house in Gerlachstreet.*

The resistance fighter Lore Wolf lived here from 1924 to 1929 and from 1933 to 1934.

In 1925 her daughter Hannelore was born. In 1929 the family emigrated to the USA in search of work. At the height of the Great Depression, Hans Wolf remained unemployed. In 1932 Lore and Hans took jobs with the Ford Company in the USSR and the family moved once more.

After Hitler took power in 1933 they no longer heard from their parents in Frankfurt. Worried, family Wolf traveled to Frankfurt in April. At the train station, the Gestapo confiscated their documents: they had to stay. Lore Wolf joined the KPD and was active in the „Red Help“. She hid illegal materials with Polizeihauptwachtmeister Emil Monno, who also lived in this building.

In August 1934 Lore Wolf had to flee to the neutral Saar area, her family and Monno were arrested on Sept. 1st, 1934. Hans Wolf was sentenced to one and a half years in prison, Monno was acquitted due to lack of evidence. On Jan 1st, 1937 Hans Wolf escaped to Switzerland with Hannelore.

*Author: Susanne Thurnay (Studienkreis Deutscher Widerstand 1933-1945 e.V.)*
