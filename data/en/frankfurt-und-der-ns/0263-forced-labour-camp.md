# Forced labour camp

*Oeserstraße 16*

![Picture postcard with the Saalbau zur Wanderlust in Nied](../../images/6c5914f65866453f72987d9ce727a6a8_original.jpg)
*1935 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF Sammlung Church C80389 | Description: Picture postcard with the Saalbau zur Wanderlust in Nied*

The forced labour camp serving to accommodate the German Reich Railway’s Express Track-Laying Crew 6 (Griesheim) was located in the dance hall of the Stapf restaurant at Oeserstrasse 16. Thanks to the fact that the health insurance fund of the German Reich Railway documented the company’s workforce in detail, the names of the forced labourers are known. These documents are accessible in digital form on the Arolsen Archives website. Among other things, they provide evidence that the 19-year-old Irma Angioli of Italy was put to work laying tracks starting on 15 December 1944. Track-laying is heavy physical labour. Angioli had already worked in a coal mine for several months. Nothing is known about her later fate.
