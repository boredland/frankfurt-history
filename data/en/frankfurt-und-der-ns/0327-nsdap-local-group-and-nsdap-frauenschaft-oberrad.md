# NSDAP local group and NSDAP Frauenschaft Oberrad

*Offenbacher Landstraße 281*

![Photo of the offices of the NSDAP local group and NSDAP Women's League Oberrad](../../images/dccf594676626b1298bf413978d008de_original.jpg)
*1934 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Z Nr. 2006-1 | Description: Photo of the offices of the NSDAP local group and NSDAP Women's League Oberrad*

This was the location of the local Oberrad branch of the National Socialist German Workers’ Party (NSDAP) and the associated National Socialist Women’s League. After bombing raids on the town, this local branch saw to the welfare of the district’s residents. The bombing raids in the night of 4-5 October 1943 left 3,175 people in Oberrad homeless. The branch’s party functionaries documented the damage street by street. They also issued a so-called “bomb damage pass” (“Fliegergeschädigtenausweise”). With this ID confirming damage suffered in air raids, the holder was eligible for new household goods and clothes. After the raids, the branch members also issued permission for 1,268 of those bombed out to leave Frankfurt. Without such an official document, people were forbidden to leave their place of residence. In the war years, many Frankfurt residents left the city seeking safety in the surrounding countryside.
