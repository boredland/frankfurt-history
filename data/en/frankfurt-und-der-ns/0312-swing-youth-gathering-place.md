# swing youth gathering place

*Am Hauptbahnhof 16*

![Picture postcard with the Schumann cellar](../../images/2e4f13ccf387620acdb9248e9b09d568_original.jpg)
*1940 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80371 | Description: Picture postcard with the Schumann cellar*

The Schumann Theatre at Hindenburg Square (now Am Hauptbahnhof Square) was a favourite gathering place for young people who liked to listen and dance to swing. Until 1943, well-known orchestras from abroad performed here. Many of them were from politically neutral countries—that is, countries not participating in World War II. Others came from countries occupied by the German Reich. The establishment not only had a variety theatre but also, on the lower level, a bar and on the first floor the Schumann Café, which was very popular with the “Club der Kameruner”—young swing fans from the Gallus district.
