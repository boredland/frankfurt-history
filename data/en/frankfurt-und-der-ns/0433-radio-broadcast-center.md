# Radio Broadcast Center

*Eschersheimer Landstraße*

![Picture postcard "Frankfurt a. M. - Rundfunkhaus"](../../images/3cfc36585c20dcd5f6cccbc2cccb4d8e_original.jpg)
*after 1933 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF / Sammlung Church | Description: Picture postcard "Frankfurt a. M. - Rundfunkhaus"*

After the Nazi Party came to power in 1933, institutions throughout society were soon forcibly aligned (“gleichgeschaltet”) with its goals. Frankfurt’s radio broadcaster was similarly aligned and utilised for propaganda. From 1 April 1933, the broadcaster’s Jewish staff were all banned from the building, dismissed together with those regarded as holding objectionable views or “politically unreliable”. Ernst Schoen, the broadcaster’s artistic director, was imprisoned in March and April 1933. On his release, he went into exile in London. Wilhelm Schüller, the broadcaster’s business director, was also forcibly dismissed, and was then taken to court in a show trial. After the broadcaster was forcibly aligned with Nazi goals, speeches by Nazi leaders became a standard part of the programme.
