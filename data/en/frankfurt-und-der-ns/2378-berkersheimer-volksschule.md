# Berkersheimer Volksschule

*Berkersheimer Untergasse 18*

![Exterior view of Berkersheim public school. ISG FFM S7A/1998-19782.](../../images/975c35e7a887b8166d6fb4b901e42acf_original.jpg)
*ca. 1935 — Author: Theodor Derlam | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt | Description: Exterior view of Berkersheim public school. ISG FFM S7A/1998-19782.*

From 1929 to 1935, there was a camp for Sinti and Roma on Friedberger Landstrasse. Its purpose was to concentrate members of this group on the outskirts of the city and make their lives more difficult. That aim also applied to school attendance by children.

The board of education identified 24 school-age children who were to attend the Berkersheim Volksschule (eight-year grammar school), located a 45-minute walk from the camp. However, the Berkersheim district immediately refused to admit the children. Sinti children were denied access to the school based on pretexts such as overcrowded classrooms and alleged health risks.

The authorities initially planned to set up a school barrack in the camp but did not have the funds to do so. The children received no schooling at all for a year and a half. It was not until the school year 1930/31 that, at the urging of their parents, who repeatedly fought for the cause, they were finally permitted to attend a school in the city center that they could reach only by bus. Despite the many obstacles, the children went to school regularly until shortly before the camp was closed in 1935.

*Author: Verband Deutscher Sinti und Roma, Landesverband Hessen*
