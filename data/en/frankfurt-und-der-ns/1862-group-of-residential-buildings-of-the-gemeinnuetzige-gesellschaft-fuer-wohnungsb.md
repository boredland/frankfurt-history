# Group of residential buildings of the Gemeinnützige Gesellschaft für Wohnungsbau, 1924-29,

*built by Fritz Epstein*

![](../../images/c3bfca2c30a0e09ab83e16b8cbae4b86_original.jpg)
*2024 — License: BDA - Bund deutscher Archiktektinnen und Architekten Hessen e.V.*

Group of residential buildings at Ravensteinstrasse 1-5 and 7-11. In 1924, Fritz Epstein and his (office) colleague Hans Schönbein founded the “Gemeinnützige Gesellschaft für Wohnungsbau”, whose aim was to build modern housing estates in Frankfurt. The group of houses in Ravensteinstrasse was built between 1924-29. 

In January 2024, the granddaughters of architect and BDA member Fritz Epstein approached the BDA in Frankfurt. They provided the impetus for a search for traces of Epstein's work and the history of the BDA. The aim of this search for traces was to make Fritz Epstein and his work visible - as a representative of many other people persecuted under National Socialism in the Third Reich.

Epstein's story shows how people can be driven out of their sphere of life and work due to political changes. In our architectural offices, colleagues with different religions, cultural backgrounds and diverse origins shape the built world together. We value this diversity as a valuable asset that must not be called into question by polarization and extremism.

The architect Fritz Epstein planned, built or converted around 150 buildings in Frankfurt and thus had a strong influence on the cityscape of the time. His work led from buildings characterized by the Wilhelminian style to the modernism of the 1920s and 1930s - and then came to an abrupt end. Due mainly to war damage, only a few of his buildings remain today. Some of them are documented here with their structural changes.

*Author: Moritz Kölling und Kristian Hüsen (BDA Hessen e.V.) und Susanne Thimm*
