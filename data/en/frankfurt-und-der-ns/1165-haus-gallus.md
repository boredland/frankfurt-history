# Haus Gallus

*Frankenallee 111*

![On the 30th anniversary of the trial in 1993, the plaque designed by Michael Sander was placed on the outside of the Saalbau Gallus](../../images/isg_gt_aus_f99c762b361abc1e5dd7e1a6235950fc_original.jpg)
*Author: © Institut für Stadtgeschichte | Description: On the 30th anniversary of the trial in 1993, the plaque designed by Michael Sander was placed on the outside of the Saalbau Gallus*

The first Frankfurt Auschwitz trial took place in Haus Gallus from April 1964 to August 1965. It had begun in December 1963 in Frankfurt's Römer.
Over one million people were murdered in the Auschwitz concentration and extermination camp between 1940 and 1945. The judicial authorities of the two German states did not systematically prosecute the perpetrators of Auschwitz. It was not until the end of the 1950s that comprehensive investigations began on the initiative of Attorney General Fritz Bauer. The defendants in Frankfurt am Main were former members of the SS and a former prisoner.
360 witnesses testified. Of these, 211 were survivors of Auschwitz.
Six defendants were sentenced to life imprisonment as murderers, one (under the age of 21 at the time of the crime) to ten years as a juvenile. Ten defendants received prison sentences of between three and a quarter and fourteen years as "accomplices". Three defendants were acquitted for lack of evidence.
The public was confronted with the crimes of the perpetrators and the suffering of the victims of Auschwitz. Hundreds of spectators, including many school classes, followed the trial. This marked the beginning of an intensive phase of public reappraisal of the past in the Federal Republic of Germany.
On the 30th anniversary of the trial in 1993, the plaque designed by Michael Sander was placed on the outside of the Saalbau Gallus and the memorial installation he designed inside the building was opened.
