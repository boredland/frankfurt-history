# Bomb shelter

*Im Wörth 2-6*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Im Wörth](../../images/ef903ac59722d7fb7b3f923114b4a5a9_original.jpg)
*1940 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Im Wörth*

This bomb shelter was the first of altogether twelve constructed from December 1940 onwards as part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. It provided space for 791 persons on three floors. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from using the shelters. The Philipp Holzmann AG company of Frankfurt carried out the construction work. Holzmann profited from forced labour. In 1940, 90 persons were made to perform labour on the construction site; 8 of them were prisoners of war. The shell construction was finished in July 1942. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. The building was refurbished in 1987, towards the end of the Cold War. Today it houses a club meeting space and private flats.

Construction company: Philipp Holzmann AG
 Architect: Franz C. Throll
