# Bomb shelter

*Konrad-Broßwitz-Straße 45*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Konrad-Broßwitz-Straße](../../images/d487d91963798f6deb7b3cb31013d66d_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Konrad-Broßwitz-Straße*

This was the site of a bomb shelter built in Werderstrasse (now Konrad-Brosswitz-Strasse 45) within the framework of the “Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme”. On its three floors it offered primarily bunk beds. It was intended for some 756 members of the Hesse-Nassau “Gauleitung”—that is, the leadership elite of the National Socialist German Workers’ Party for Frankfurt and the region to the city’s southwest. Construction work, which was carried out by Heinrich Flach & Co. of Frankfurt, lasted from December 1940 to July 1942. The company made use of forced labourers. After the war, the shelter was converted and used primarily as residential space. It was torn down in 2003.
 Construction company: Heinrich Flach & Co., Frankfurt
 Architect: Karl Christoph Gräf, Frankfurt
