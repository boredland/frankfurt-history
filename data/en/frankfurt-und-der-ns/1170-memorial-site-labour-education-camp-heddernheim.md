# Memorial Site Labour Education Camp Heddernheim

*Oberschelder Weg 10-12*

![Memorial pyramid during the commemorative event in 2024.](../../images/b427db789c25bc6ff6dd95dc92bd8fbd_original.jpg)
*Author: Lisa Schrimpf | License: gemeinfrei | Description: Memorial pyramid during the commemorative event in 2024.*

The memorial is located at Oberschelder Weg 10–12, which is accessed by way of Ludwig-Reinheimer-Strasse. Ludwig Reinheimer was a Heddernheim labor re-education camp (AEL) inmate who was murdered in the Flossenbürg concentration camp in 1945.

The memorial commemorates the AEL Heddernheim and its inmates’ suffering. Several initiatives joined forces to have an initial wooden plaque installed and thus create a place of remembrance in 1983. In 1987, under pressure from survivors and the general public, a municipal memorial was established. The memorial is overseen by Local Advisory Council 8 (Heddernheim, Niederursel, Nordweststadt). The remains of walls, presumably once belonging to the detention cells, were secured and the bars replaced.

When the memorial was redesigned in 2017/2018, a pyramid of bricks and corten steel was erected. The inscription reads: “1942–1945 Heddernheim Labor Education Camp. Human dignity is inviolable.” An LED running text display in the extant section of the detention cell provides information about the camp, its inmates, and the perpetrators. Since 2023, an annual memorial service has made active commemoration on site possible.

*Author: Studienkreis Deutscher Widerstand 1933-1945 e.V.*
