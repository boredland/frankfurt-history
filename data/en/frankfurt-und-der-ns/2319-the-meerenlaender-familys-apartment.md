# The Meerenländer family's apartment

*Rechneigrabenstraße 9*

![](../../images/aee2fcb98f23da009c4ac1c0a17de7ec_original.png)

The Meerenländer family apartment

Erna and Moschek Meerenländer owned an apartment in the building on Rechneigrabenstraße. After Moschek Meerenländer's death, his wife Erna was forced to sell their shared apartment in November 1941 for 38,000 Reichsmarks and move to Hölderlinstraße 3. She was not free to dispose of the money as she wished. Due to a “security order” issued by the foreign exchange office on February 27, 1940, she was only allowed to withdraw 300 to 350 Reichsmarks.
