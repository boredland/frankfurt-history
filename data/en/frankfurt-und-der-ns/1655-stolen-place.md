# Stolen Place

*Friedberger Landstraße 27*

![Friedberger Landstraße 27](../../images/b99e58754b1c186686969ee1c2676f80_original.jpg)
*ca. 1930 — Author: Hans Möhring | License: ISG | Description: Friedberger Landstraße 27*

The four-storey residential and commercial building at Friedberger Landstraße 27 originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Otto Neumaier, who lived at Gärtnerweg 62. The property covered an area of 450 square meters. There were two stores on the first floor, each with a 2-room apartment and six 4-room apartments above. The top floor was also converted into four small apartments. The annual rental income amounted to 9,600 Reichsmark. Since February 23, 1939, the house had been under forced administration. Due to persecution, he had to sell his house to a non-Jewish businessman from Bübingen near Saarbrücken and his wife for 59,000 Reichsmark under a contract dated May 27, 1939. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 64,700 Reichsmark and the market value to 66,600 Reichsmark. The contract costs, including land transfer tax and estate agent's commission, were shared by the parties. Otto Neumaier emigrated to the USA at the beginning of 1939. He therefore asked for accelerated approval of the purchase contract. The building authorities approved the sale on July 24, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich. However, the contracting parties revoked the purchase agreement itself on September 13, 1939 because, contrary to expectations, the value appreciation tax was to be 30 percent instead of 15 percent.
