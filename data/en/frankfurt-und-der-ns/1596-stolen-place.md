# Stolen Place

*Allerheiligenstraße 28*

![Allerheiligenstraße 28](../../images/a359890f462399a668445d4265ef6042_original.jpg)
*ca. 1945 — License: HMF | Description: Allerheiligenstraße 28*

The four-storey residential and commercial building at Allerheiligenstraße 28 with twelve apartments, two stores and storage rooms in the rear building and a plot size of 298 square meters originally belonged to Siegmund Rothschild. Due to persecution, the Jewish owner had to sell his property to a non-Jewish engraver and a waiter and his wife for 27,150 Reichsmarks in a purchase contract dated February 13, 1939. The seller was allowed to continue using his apartment for rent until he left for exile. A new purchase contract on October 14, 1940 named only the couple as the owners. No compensatory levy to the German Reich was imposed. Later, a prison labor camp was housed here, where around 100 people were forced to live in 1943 and 1944.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
