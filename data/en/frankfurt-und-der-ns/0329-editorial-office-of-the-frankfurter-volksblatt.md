# Editorial Office of the Frankfurter Volksblatt

*Neue Mainzer Straße 8*

Editorial Office of the Frankfurter Volksblatt

Until 1933, this was the location of the Frankfurter Volksblatt’s editorial offices. The Frankfurter Volksblatt was the leading Nazi party newspaper in the “Gau” (administrative district) of Hesse-Nassau, roughly corresponding to today’s Rhine-Main region and Southern Hesse. The paper stirred up antisemitic sentiment and agitated against the Jewish population. On 1 April 1933, for instance, it published a call to boycott Jewish-owned shops and businesses, lawyers’ offices, doctors’ practices, and companies. In 1935, the editorial offices moved into Grosse Gallusstrasse. This was also home to the National Socialist Publishing Company, a press for books disseminating Nazi ideas and ideology. In 1941, it also published the book “That’s how we fought! Descriptions from the Nazi Party’s ‘time of struggle’ in the Gau Hesse-Nassau”, a publication idealising the early years of the National Socialists in Frankfurt.
