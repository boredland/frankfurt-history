# Stolen Place

*Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 12*

![Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 12](../../images/284a7a959e6cdd9db0bc6c9151dc5556_original.jpg)
*1981 — Author: Falk Kynast | License: ISG | Description: Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 12*

The property at Bürgerstraße 12 (today: Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße) with a plot size of 559 square meters originally belonged to the widow of the merchant Lepold Wolfgang, Toni Wolfgang, née Feist, who also lived there. The property consisted of a front and rear house built in 1889. In total, there were three 6-room apartments and one 5 1 / 2-room apartment as well as several small apartments and central heating in the front house. Due to persecution, Toni Wolfgang had to sell her rented house to a non-Jewish tenant couple from her house for the price of 60,375 Reichsmark in a contract dated October 31, 1941. The officially tax-assessed value was 57,500 Reichsmark and the market value was 66,200 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 10,282 Reichsmark. The buyers assumed all costs arising from the contract, including the estate agent's fee. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of a rent increase.
