# Stolen Place

*Beethovenstraße 16*

![Beethovenstraße 16](../../images/1f56c6e6a847cfc15bf880b3e99b3674_original.jpg)
*License: ISG | Description: Beethovenstraße 16*

The property at Beethovenstraße 16 originally belonged to the merchants Nathan, known as Walter, and Adolf Schmidt, who were already in exile in Britain at the time. Due to persecution, they had to sell the apartment building with a plot area of 770 square meters to a non-Jewish merchant for 56,000 Reichsmark in a purchase contract dated 5 December 1938. The buyer was already living at Beethovenstraße 16 at the time the contract was concluded. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 62,700 Reichsmark. The building authority reserved the right to levy a compensatory levy to the German Reich in the event of future rent increases. Nathan Schmidt's family was expatriated in 1939. This meant that the assets of the anti-Semitic persecutees still in Germany were forfeited to the Reich.
