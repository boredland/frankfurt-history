# forced labour camp

*Ginnheimer Hohl 2*

![Former forced labor camp, Ginnheimer Hohl 2](../../images/786cf2b5e87b8a8e600fd057bc8cd18f_original.jpg)
*2019 — Author: Claus Graubner | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7C Nr. 2020-41 | Description: Former forced labor camp, Ginnheimer Hohl 2*

Forced labourers were also lodged here in the Zum Adler restaurant. They were put to work building air-raid protection facilities and bomb shelters for the municipal air-raid protection department. They also had to clean up after bombing attacks, a task that was often life-endangering. In 2003, the Institut für Stadtgeschichte (Institute of Municipal History) was able to establish that 20 forced labourers from Russia and 40 from Ukraine were lodged here in 1943. Other sources indicate that French forced labourers lived here as well. Shortly after the war, the new chief of police toured the building and determined that the building was “unclean and infested” and in “very poor condition”. This underscores the inhumane circumstances under which the forced labourers were compelled to live.
