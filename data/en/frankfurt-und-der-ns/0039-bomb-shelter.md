# Bomb shelter

*Hermann-Eggert-Straße*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Hermann-Eggert-Straße](../../images/41dd25a437dd20738f89ec91152fa22d_original.jpg)
*2011 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Hermann-Eggert-Straße*

This bomb shelter is on the former grounds of the main goods yard. It was built within the framework of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. With space for 40 persons on three floors, it offered air-raid protection to employees of the Reich Railway. It was designed to look like other train maintenance halls nearby so that it would not be recognizable as a bomb shelter. Construction began in September 1940. As railway facilities were frequently the target of air-raid attacks, this shelter was to be an especially solid structure. More concrete was used in its construction than for any other bomb shelter in Frankfurt. The building has been a listed monument since 2012 and is used by the Deutsche Bahn for storage.
