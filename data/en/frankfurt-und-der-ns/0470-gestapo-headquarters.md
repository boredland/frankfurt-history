# Gestapo Headquarters

*Lindenstraße 27*

![Memorial plaque at the former Gestapo headquarters](../../images/isg_gt_ges_85960433acd34566d0b7275f6dc9d500_original.jpg)
*License: © Institut für Stadtgeschichte | Description: Memorial plaque at the former Gestapo headquarters*

The brass plaque mounted on a sandstone base commemorates the general history of the building and was unveiled in January 1987.
Just a few weeks after the seizure of power in 1933, the Secret State Police in Prussia had become a political police force independent of the constitutional limits on its powers. By 1936 at the latest, after Heinrich Himmler had seized control of the entire police apparatus in the Reich, the expansion of the repressive apparatus made it necessary to separate it from the general police administration both formally and physically. The Gestapo initially moved from the old police headquarters in Friedrich-Ebert-Anlage to the 5th floor of the Siemens building in Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße and, after a further increase in staff, to the property in Lindenstraße, which had been acquired under pressure from the previous owner. The former girls' boarding house from the Wilhelminian era became the most feared building in the city. Hundreds of alleged and actual opponents of the regime, Jews and foreigners were interrogated, tortured and threatened with death here. In March 1945, while American troops occupied Frankfurt, the Gestapo officers burned all the documents except for a file of names or took them out of the building. After the war, the building served for a second time as the seat of the mayor and several offices.
