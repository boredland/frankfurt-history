# forced labour camp

*Burglehen 7*

![Forced labor camp, Burglehen 7](../../images/35d0aefd6b061071af521137fc11e3c7_original.jpg)
*1938 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2010-16 | Description: Forced labor camp, Burglehen 7*

Voigt & Haeffner, a company specializing in heavy current equipment, operated a forced labour camp in the dance hall of the Zum Mainbörnchen restaurant. In 1942, 3 Belgian and 25 Dutch forced labourers had their lodgings here. Records show that 9 Italian forced labourers were registered at the camp in 1943. Plans to expand it were presumably never carried to realization. During the war the company exploited several hundred forced labourers and operated further camps on the factory grounds in Hanauer Landstrasse and on neighbouring lots as well as at Kronprinzenstrasse 56 (now Münchener Strasse) and Röderbergweg 122.
