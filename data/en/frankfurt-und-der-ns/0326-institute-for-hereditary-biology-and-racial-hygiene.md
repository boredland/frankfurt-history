# Institute for Hereditary Biology and Racial Hygiene

*Gartenstraße 140*

![Outside view of the Institut für Erbbiologie und Rassenhygiene (Institute for Hereditary Biology and Racial Hygiene)](../../images/130eb3e62814dad808af4a7c64d61e96_original.jpg)
*1936 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 2002-131 | Description: Outside view of the Institut für Erbbiologie und Rassenhygiene (Institute for Hereditary Biology and Racial Hygiene)*

In 1935, the “Institute for Hereditary Biology and Racial Hygiene” was established at Goethe University under the direction of Otmar Freiherr von Verschuer. Among the institute’s staff was Josef Mengele, who later became the camp doctor at Auschwitz and wrote his dissertation there. The institute was located in the “House of Public Health” at Gartenstraße 140.
It was one of the central institutions for the dissemination of the pseudoscientific Nazi racial ideology in research, teaching, and practice. Through its “Consulting Office for Hereditary and Racial Care II,” which annually examined around 1,000 Frankfurt residents under the supervision of public health officials—who were required to appear there for “marriage counseling, examinations for marriage certificates, issuance of certificates of fitness for marriage, and evaluations regarding sterilization,” but it also performed very practical functions.
The head of the department and his staff, through their racist research, not only laid the ideological groundwork for the mass murder but were —like Josef Mengele—directly in the crimes.

After von Verschuer’s departure, he was succeeded as director by Heinrich Wilhelm Kranz from Gießen, who had distinguished himself particularly through his contributions to the “solution of the so-called anti-social problem.” During an air raid on November 5, 1944, the “House of Public Health” sustained severe bomb damage, forcing the institute to partially relocate. Kranz, who had been appointed rector of the university as recently as March 1945, took his own life on May 5, 1945.
After the liberation, the institution was merely given a different name: University Institute for Genetics, instead of being dissolved in light of its involvement in crimes.
