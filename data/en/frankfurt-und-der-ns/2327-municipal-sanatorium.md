# Municipal Sanatorium

*today Theodor-W.-Adorno-Platz 6*

![Aerial view of the municipal sanatorium from the Kleist family photo album “Memories of the Psychiatric and Neurological Clinic”. HMF Hoff1066_011.](../../images/1d8c0716df289d6d14446939e6867ab6_original.jpg)
*1900-1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: Aerial view of the municipal sanatorium from the Kleist family photo album “Memories of the Psychiatric and Neurological Clinic”. HMF Hoff1066_011.*

In 1864, the psychiatric clinic, at that time extremely modern, was opened as an “Institute for the Insane and Epileptic.” Closed in 1928 as it no longer met technical standards, the building was demolished. Afterwards, the psychiatric clinic was moved to Niederrad.

On order of Frankfurt’s district court, the trans* person W. Heinrich, then aged 16, was admitted to the clinic in March 1925. Although initially supposed to be held for only six weeks, W. Heinrich was kept there for nearly five months. In late July 1925, W. Heinrich fled, most likely, with their mother’s help.

*Author: Clara Hartmann*
