# Stolen Place Fischerfeldstraße 8

*Fischerfeldstraße 8*

![Fischerfeldstraße 8](../../images/197ed88a6a6c6d86e259a10b04a72384_original.jpg)
*1944 — Author: BLDAM | License: HMF, Bildarchiv, Neg.Nr. 42 b 31/6442.1 | Description: Fischerfeldstraße 8*

The property at Fischerfeldstraße 8 originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Jakob Messing, who lived at Zeil 13 at the end of 1938. The property covered an area of 329 square meters. The property consisted of a three-storey front building and a two-storey rear building. The houses contained one 4-room, two 3-room and five 2-room apartments as well as a wine cellar in the basement, which had previously served a wine shop. Due to persecution, he had to sell his house to a non-Jewish Frankfurt merchant, who was already a tenant in the house, for the price of 33,480 Reichsmark in a contract dated December 5, 1938. The price also included the sale of the wine cellar equipment. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 27,900 Reichsmark and the market value to 31,800 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 5,544 Reichsmark. The contract costs including land transfer tax were borne by the buyer. The building authority approved the contract on August 21, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich.
