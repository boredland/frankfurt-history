# Bomb shelter

*Lassallestraße 2a*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Lassallestraße](../../images/4aba624db8d678727aab409b2766f331_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Lassallestraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and offered protection to 877 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt provided space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war were prohibited from entering. Construction work lasted from March 1941 to July 1942. The shelter was designed as a long, narrow structure to look like the surrounding rowhouses so that it would be difficult to recognize from the air. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office lodged refugees here. Towards the end of the Cold War, the building was refurbished to provide shelter for a few hours in the event of an attack. Since 2016 it has belonged to the City of Frankfurt and in the future will offer rehearsal rooms for musicians and space for clubs.

Construction company: Eduard Züblin AG
 Architect: Karl Olsson
