# Stolen Place

*Eysseneckstraße 3*

![Eysseneckstraße 3](../../images/56a4ceda9e7427f55f06cbb70dc36c88_original.jpg)
*1983 — Author: Jutta Hofmann | License: ISG | Description: Eysseneckstraße 3*

The property at Eysseneckstraße 3 originally belonged to the Jewish commercial teacher Berthold Schiff and his wife Jenny, née Löwenberg. The couple lived at Friedrichstraße 42 and the property covered 552 square meters. Due to persecution, they were forced to sell their house to a non-Jewish factory owner from Staudernheim and his wife for 34,400 Reichsmark in a contract dated December 15, 1938. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 34,400 Reichsmark and the market value 32,300 Reichsmark. The parties shared the costs of the contract, including land transfer tax and estate agent's commission. The transfer was agreed for January 1, 1939, as the seller had undertaken to the Gestapo to emigrate by February 1, 1939. The British Consulate General required him to enter the country with his entire family by February 28, 1939 at the latest. Berthold Schiff therefore asked for accelerated approval of the purchase contract. This was because they would only receive the necessary clearance certificate once the Reich Flight Tax and Jewish property levy, which the sellers had to pay from the proceeds of the house, had been paid. In fact, the district economic advisor approved the sale on January 5, 1939, followed by the approval of the Wiesbaden district president on January 10, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich. The purchasers had also acquired the property at Liebigstrasse 51 in 1937 - also from the property of anti-Semitic persecutees.
