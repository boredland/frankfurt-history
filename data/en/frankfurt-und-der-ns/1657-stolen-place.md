# Stolen Place

*Fuchshohl 67*

![Fuchshohl 67](../../images/a07ce71fab4311a0c370903ff801feeb_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Franz Roeckle | License: ISG | Description: Fuchshohl 67*

The detached house at Fuchshohl 67 originally belonged to the historian and medievalist Professor Dr. Ernst Kantorwicz, who also lived there. Due to persecution, he had to sell his house to a non-Jewish Frankfurt resident and his wife under a contract dated January 9, 1939 for 19,000 Reichsmark, which corresponded to the officially tax-assessed value. The market value was 21,000 Reichsmark. The parties shared the costs of the contract, including land transfer tax. The property was to be handed over on February 15, 1939. The building authorities approved the purchase contract on 15 May 1939. The Nazi authorities did not impose a compensatory levy to the German Reich. Professor Dr. Ernst Kantorwicz emigrated to the USA in 1939.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
