# Stolperstein for Nikolaus Kopp

*Mainzer Landstraße 137*

Nikolaus Kopp (1901–1941)

Nikolaus Kopp was born in the Höchst district of Frankfurt in 1901. He worked in agriculture and industry and became unemployed in 1931. He was tormented by existential fears. He lived more or less openly as a homosexual and was sentenced to seven months in prison in 1938. A medical examiner classified him as “feebleminded.” On that basis he was committed to the Eichberg “sanatorium” after his release from prison. He died—that is, was probably murdered—in the Hadamar “sanatorium.” Mainzer Landstrasse 137 was his last place of residence in Frankfurt. 

The Stolperstein für Niklaus Kopp was initiated by Christian Setzepfandt, the gab magazine, and the Initiative for a Memorial to the Persecution of Homosexuals. It was funded by Christian Setzepfandt and laid in 2009.

Stolpersteine

A Stolperstein—literally “stumbling stone”—is a 10 x 10 x 10-cm concrete block with a brass plate mounted on the upper side. The brass plate is inscribed with the name of the person and brief information about their biography and persecution during the Nazi period. Learn more about the Initiative Stolpersteine Frankfurt: https://stolpersteine-frankfurt.de/en
