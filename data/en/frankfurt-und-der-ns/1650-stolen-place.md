# Stolen Place

*François-Mitterrand-Platz 27*

![François-Mitterrand-Platz 27](../../images/4cb2ff776d3523ffe78214dbe52fc633_original.jpg)
*1933 — Author: Hannah Reeck | License: ISG | Description: François-Mitterrand-Platz 27*

The property at Blittersdorfplatz 29 had originally belonged to the consul Gustav Mayer-Alberti, his wife Alice Mayer-Alberti, née Langenbach, and the merchant Artur Joseph. They ran an export business on the second floor, which had to be liquidated at the beginning of 1939 due to persecution. Until March 1938, the first floor had been rented as classrooms by the school association Studienanstalt Anna Schmidt e.V.. Due to the persecution, the owners had to sell their multi-family residential and commercial building from 1859 with a plot area of 6 acres and 82 square meters to a non-Jewish assessor at the Frankfurt city administration for 50,000 Reichsmarks. The buyer was a member of the NSDAP. The officially tax-assessed value amounted to 53,800 Reichsmark. The purchase price had to be paid into a blocked bank account controlled by the foreign exchange office, to which the sellers had no direct access. The building authority did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich. Later, a camp for civilian workers of the company Bauausführungen W. Schmidt-Diehler & Co. was housed here.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
