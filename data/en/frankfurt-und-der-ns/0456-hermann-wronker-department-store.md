# Hermann Wron­ker department store

*Zeil 97*

![Photo of a Hermann Wronker AG department store located on Zeil](../../images/49587b41df234c13fd7eb18cf076105a_original.jpg)
*ca. 1940 — Author: Stuhl | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-10243 | Description: Photo of a Hermann Wronker AG department store located on Zeil*

Zeil 97 was the site of a department store belonging to the Hermann Wronker company, founded in 1921. At its height, the department store chain employed a staff of some 2,000. Hermann Wronker developed his store after the American model. It was the largest of its kind in Frankfurt and offered its customers a widely diversified product range. The unparalleled assortment was further enhanced by an art salon with exhibitions and a prints and decorative arts department. Hermann Wronker and his wife Ida were philanthropists and art patrons and actively involved in numerous social projects. As a consequence of the boycott on 1 April 1933, the number of Wronker employees decreased to 500. Members of the SA (Storm Troopers) prohibited Wronker from entering his store. The Hansa company took over the enterprise in 1934 in the context of “Aryanization”. 
 Hermann Wronker’s children Max and Alice fled to Egypt and later to the U.S. Hermann and Ida Wronker visited their son Max in Egypt, then returned to Frankfurt. In 1939 they fled to Paris. By that point in time, it was no longer possible to emigrate to Egypt from the occupied zone in France. The couple ultimately fled to the Departement Vienne in Western France, where they were arrested. By way of Poitiers and the internment camp in Drancy, they were deported to the Auschwitz extermination camp on 23 September 1942 and presumably murdered there after their arrival.
