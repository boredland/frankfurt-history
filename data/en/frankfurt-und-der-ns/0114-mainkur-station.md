# Mainkur Station

*An der Mainkur 9-11*

![Picture postcard with the station building of Mainkur station](../../images/2d39c1036939f5822dc62c4cc51937b2_original.jpg)
*after 1913 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church C80390 | Description: Picture postcard with the station building of Mainkur station*

The Mainkur Station in Fechenheim played a key role in the deportation of Jews from Bergen and Fechenheim. During the 1938 November pogroms, the Gestapo arrested Jewish men and deported them to the Buchenwald concentration camp. Between 10 and 16 November 1938, altogether 30,756 Jews from within the German Reich were deported to the Dachau, Buchenwald, and Sachsenhausen concentration camps. Their families received no news of them for weeks or even months.
 
 On 30 May 1942, for example, after being forced to make their way to the Mainkur Station on foot, eleven Jews from Bergen were deported to the Majdanek concentration camp with persons from the Hanau region. On 5 September 1942, 17 Jews from Bergen were deported to the Terezín Ghetto near Prague along with citizens of Hanau.
