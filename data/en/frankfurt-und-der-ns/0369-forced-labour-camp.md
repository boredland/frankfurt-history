# Forced labour camp

*Emser Straße*

![Placeholder image](../../images/Hier%20fehlt_dfdc6d631f271e842082099187aa5623_original.jpg)
*License: HMF*

At this site, the VDM aviation works operated a forced labour camp that existed from 1942 onwards. In February 1942, at least 251 Soviet prisoners of war from the Stalag IX A Ziegenhain POW camp were lodged here. According to a report by the company doctor, they were in poor health and showed clear signs of malnutrition. More than 3,500 forced labourers from all over Europe were registered in this camp in 1942 alone. In 1943 the number rose to 4,132.
 
 The working conditions were inhumane. The forced labourers had to perform extremely heavy physical labour for up to 72 hours a week. They were moreover subjected to despotic treatment. One forced labourer, for example, was chained to a machine with his arms raised for an entire night. He had been accused of sabotaging the machine. He was subsequently transferred to the special Gestapo camp in Heddernheim and tortured.
 
 There were also instances in which German workers acted in solidarity with the forced labourers. Franz Schmitz, a Communist and trained master craftsman, tried to provide them with food. The German workers also performed spontaneous acts of charity: on the way to work, they threw food and cigarettes out of tram windows for the forced labourers. The SS (Schutzstaffel: a major paramilitary organization of the Nazi party) immediately began patrolling the trams and nailed the windows shut to prevent such actions.
