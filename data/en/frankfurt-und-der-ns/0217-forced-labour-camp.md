# forced labour camp

*Große Gallusstraße 12*

![Forced labor camp, Große Gallusstraße 12](../../images/cfb2f7da785535c2f542f51c5b5257a9_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: Repro Schirg | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-8414 | Description: Forced labor camp, Große Gallusstraße 12*

In 1942/43, the German Reich Post Office operated a forced labour camp at Grosse Gallusstrasse 12. Three further forced labour camps were located at Gutleutstrasse 36, Wurzelstrasse, corner of Gutleutstrasse, and Friedberger Warte / Giessener Strasse. In September 1942, this camp lodged 21 Czech and 129 Italian male forced labourers, in 1943 1 woman from Slovakia, 40 persons from the so-called Protectorate of Bohemia and Moravia (now Czech Republic), 31 Dutch men, and 98 Italian men. According to the Catalogue of Camps and Prisons (CCP), a total of some 480 persons were lodged here between 1940 and 1945. Numbers like this go back to (among other sources) police district records that documented how many persons lived in what camp.
 
 In 1946, the 4th police district reported to the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration (UNRRA) that there were 35 forced labourers from the Netherlands, 120 from Czechia, 45 from Belgium, 220 from Italy, and 60 from France housed in the camp at Grosse Gallusstrasse 35. The UNRRA was in charge of registering persons from the member states of the U.N. (founded in October 1945), looking after them, and helping them return to their home countries. UNRRA information is accordingly classified by country. For example, the 4th police district had records of 60 French men lodged at the camp at Grosse Gallusstrasse 12 from 1940 onwards. They were liberated by the U.S. Army on 29 March 1945 and taken back to France.
