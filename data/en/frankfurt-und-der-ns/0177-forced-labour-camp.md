# forced labour camp

*Berger Straße 147*

![Picture postcard with the vehicle fleet of the Heinrich Herget company](../../images/c7086f7756e58f3a6e3832c1a79f5e88_original.jpg)
*ca. 1900 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80364 | Description: Picture postcard with the vehicle fleet of the Heinrich Herget company*

The Heinrich Herget furniture transport company based at Berger Strasse 147 profited from forced labour. The labourers had their lodgings in a joint camp at Allerheiligenstrasse 26 that was used by 31 different companies. The Ceka Christalle & Kriegshammer company had established the camp in March 1943, originally for 50 forced labourers from Western Europe. Later that year, there were already 75 persons registered here. Among the companies that lodged forced workers in this camp were three that manufactured uniforms for the Wehrmacht. The camp was destroyed by bombing in October 1943. From then on, the forced labourers were lodged in a joint camp at Reuterweg 91. The previous owner of the land on which this camp was located had been forced to sell it because he was Jewish.
