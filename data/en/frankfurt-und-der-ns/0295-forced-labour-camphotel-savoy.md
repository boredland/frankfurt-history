# Forced labour camp/"Hotel Savoy"

*Wiesenhüttenstraße 42*

![Picture postcard showing the ‘Hotel Savoy’ at Wiesenhüttenstraße 42](../../images/0d5e00baaf5fa5bf12cd9aa148de4647_original.jpg)
*1909 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF / Sammlung Church C80365 | Description: Picture postcard showing the ‘Hotel Savoy’ at Wiesenhüttenstraße 42*

The Hotel Savoy at Wiesenhüttenstrasse 42 served as a camp for forced labourers of Frankfurter Maschinenbau (formerly Pokorny & Wittekind), a mechanical engineering company. In September 1942, it accommodated 89 women and 18 men from France, 7 men and 1 woman from Belgium, and 85 men and 1 woman from Italy. In 1943 2 Ukrainian women, 66 women and 40 men from France, 34 Belgian men, 6 Dutch men, 48 Italian men, and 1 woman and 1 man from Germany were lodged here. The camp was one of three operated by the company in Frankfurt during World War II.
