# Stolen Place

*Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 10*

![Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 10](../../images/9e2e0db26d0492e6d173684c74039920_original.jpg)
*1981 — Author: Falk Kynast | License: ISG | Description: Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 10*

The property at Bürgerstraße 10 (today: Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße) originally belonged to the Jewish merchant August Mohrenwitz. Due to persecution, he had to sell his apartment building to two non-Jewish buyers from Saabrücken for the price of 50,000 Reichsmark in a contract dated December 31, 1938. The officially tax-assessed value was 60,600 Reichsmark and the market value 53,900 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 8,964 Reichsmark. The parties shared the costs of the contract and the estate agent's fee. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of a rent increase.

Research: Jutta Zwilling
