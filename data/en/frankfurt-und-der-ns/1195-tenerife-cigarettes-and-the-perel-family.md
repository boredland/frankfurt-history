# Tenerife cigarettes and the Perel family

*Alte Gasse 27-29*

![](../../images/ea937ef38185f0dd60e73b613e2d145d_original.jpg)

The stateless Jewish family Perel from Vilna/Lithuania lived in Frankfurt since 1912. Father Rubin founded the Rubin Perel tobacco and cigarette factory in 1915, in which his wife Pessa and their two sons also worked. David was a chemist, Sussmann was responsible for sales. The family's second line of business was the owners' association R. Perel & Söhne. It owned 4 houses in Wiesbaden, Bad Homburg and Frankfurt. In 1928, 3 years before the death of their father, the brothers founded the successor company Teneriffa-Zigaretten Frankfurt at Alte Gasse 27-29. Sussmann was the owner, David continued to be responsible for the difficult work of mixing tobacco. With 1933 began the boycotts and anti-Jewish repression against Jewish businesses. In order to save the company and thus its existence, the houses had to be encumbered more and more and finally forcibly sold below value. David emigrated to the USA, his family followed him later. If the company had survived in 1936 thanks to the new advertising methods, the end came a year later: the large cigarette companies wanted to oust the smaller ones, and they received compensation if they gave up the business. As a Jewish company, however, Teneriffa-Zigaretten Frankfurt was excluded from this. Instead, Sussmann Perel received the order to scrap all machines, otherwise the NSDAP would do it. He was finally arrested in the November pogrom of 1938, and was in the Buchenwald concentration camp until the end of December. On January 1, 1939, Sussmann Perel deregistered the company. He fled to Switzerland in April 1939. Here he worked as a volunteer for the Israelite Refugee Aid. He remained in Switzerland after 1945, married in 1948, and their son was born in 1951. Pessa Perel was expelled from the apartment at Scheffelstraße 1. After having to live several times in different deportation houses, she was deported to the Theresienstadt ghetto on 9/1/1942, where she perished on 9/9/1942. The brothers' compensation proceedings took so long that they did not live to see the final decisions; Sussmann died Jan. 31, 1959, David on Oct. 14, 1967.

*Author: Helga Roos, Sportkreis Frankfurt e.V.*
