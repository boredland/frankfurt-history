# Klapperfeld police prison

*Klapperfeldstraße 5*

![Photo of the Klapperfeld police prison](../../images/567a737a5685d4e4d8aaa0619ebc123f_original.jpg)
*after 1945 — Author: Paul Wolff | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7Wo Nr. 7260 | Description: Photo of the Klapperfeld police prison*

The police prison at Klapperfeldstraße 5 was used not only by the police but also by the Gestapo. The detainees were often brutally interrogated and severely mistreated. Several people died. The prison had darkened "wire cages" in which prisoners had to spend several hours before an interrogation, their removal to a labor or education camp or a concentration camp. The third floor was under the sole control of the Gestapo, which maintained a "Judenabteilung" here, i.e. a section for Jews. People who were persecuted as Jews in the sense of National Socialism were imprisoned there. Many of them were wives of non-Jewish men. They were deported to the concentration and extermination camp Auschwitz and murdered there. The cells were small, a sack filled with wood wool served as a mattress. It lay on a bed that had to be folded up during the day and often was full of vermin. In the cells there was only one bucket used as a toilet, which the prisoners had to empty in the morning. In view of the terrible conditions and widespread torture, several people decided to end their own lives in the Klapperfeld police prison. One of them was Catholic student Bernhard Becker, who died here in 1937 under unexplained circumstances.
