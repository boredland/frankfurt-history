# “Department of Hereditary and Racial Health”, City Health Authority

*Braubachstraße 18-22*

![The City Health Authority was located in this building in Braubachstraße 18-22.](../../images/dab492d407c9a4d5bfb533365f67ccf4_original.jpg)
*ca. 1936 — Author: Max Göllner | License: HMF, NS1366 | Description: The City Health Authority was located in this building in Braubachstraße 18-22.*

In the Nazi period, the municipal health authorities played a key role in implementing the regime’s racial health policy. This policy was based on a concept of diverse human “races” with some of greater value than others. The Nazis even attributed the actions of certain individuals to alleged racial predispositions, entirely ignoring the specific conditions of people’s lives and cultural backgrounds. The National Socialists referred to themselves as “Aryan” and considered others innately inferior. The regime’s entire rhetoric and policies were aimed at disseminating and constantly confirming the fallacy of this delusional racial ideology, supported by numerous laws and provisions which disadvantaged, excluded and degraded people.

Frankfurt had no less than two municipal counselling centres deciding who would be allowed to marry and have children. One of these was the “Department of Hereditary and Racial Health” in Braubachstrasse 18-22. Founded in late 1933 or early 1934 within the city’s health authority, the department was headed by municipal physician Kurt Gerum. He set up a “hereditary card index” where he and his staff could collect and record information on the “genealogy” of all Frankfurt’s residents.
By March 1935, the index already held details of 30,000 people, a number reaching no less than 380,000 by the end of the war, i.e., two thirds of the city’s then population. The ultimate aim was to compile a comprehensive and complete reference work to be used, for example, to prevent couples marrying.
The municipal health authority staff worked closely together with the public health and forensic physicians as well as heads of homes and institutions to forcibly sterilise Jews, Sinti and Roma, the sick, non-conformist youths, or those categorised as “asocial”. In this way, they also prepared the ground for robbing innumerable people, deporting them to forced labour or concentration camps, or torturing and murdering them in so-called psychiatric hospitals and sanatoriums. In the denazification process after the war, Kurt Gerum was merely classified as a “follower”.
