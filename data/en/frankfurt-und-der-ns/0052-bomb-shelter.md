# Bomb shelter

*Palleskestraße 8a*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Palleskestraße](../../images/52c1269e7a059935cf2487c57653fed6_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Palleskestraße*

This bomb shelter was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme and served to protect residents of Höchst. It could accommodate 630 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered space for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought protection in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction began in April 1941. To prevent the shelter from being recognized from the air, it was designed to conform to the surrounding buildings and decorated with sandstone elements. The heavy air-raid doors were concealed behind wooden doors. After the war, the welfare office lodged refugees here. In 1987, towards the end of the Cold War, the bomb shelter was refurbished.
