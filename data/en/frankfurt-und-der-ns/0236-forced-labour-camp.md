# forced labour camp

*Kaiserstraße 39*

![Forced labor camp, Kaiserstraße 39](../../images/42548a8c791f51756facd57b505ed517_original.jpg)
*1909 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-11821 | Description: Forced labor camp, Kaiserstraße 39*

G. Hoffmann, a construction materials company based at Kaiserstrasse 39, profited from forced labour. The labourers lived in a camp at Allerheiligenstrasse 26 that was used jointly by 31 different companies. The Ceka Christalle & Kriegshammer company had established it in March 1943, initially for 50 forced labourers from Western Europe. Not long afterwards, however, the camp population already numbered 75 persons who performed forced labour for these companies, three of which manufactured uniforms for the Wehrmacht (the German Army under National Socialism). The camp was destroyed by bombing in October 1943. From then on, the forced labourers were lodged in a joint camp at Reuterweg 91. The previous owner of the land on which this camp was located had been forced to sell it because he was Jewish.
