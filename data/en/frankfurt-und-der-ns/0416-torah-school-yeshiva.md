# Torah school “Yeshiva”

*Friedberger Anlage 4*

![Friedberger Anlage 4](../../images/isg_ffm_s7_aad24b2e240d88f35899c19f9194e455_original.jpg)
*ca. 1935 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7A Nr. 1998-10817 | Description: Friedberger Anlage 4*

This was the Community Centre of the secessionist orthodox Israelitische Religionsgesellschaft (IRG), which acquired and enlarged the building in 1928. In addition to the administration offices, there was also a yeshiva, an educational institution for Torah study and training rabbis. This school was founded in 1892 by Salomon Breuer, Chief Rabbi of the Israelitische Religionsgesellschaft from 1890 to 1926. A radical advocate of the secessionist neo-orthodox community, he supported its separation from the main Jewish community. The yeshiva also stored valuable ritual objects from the adjacent synagogue, probably including 45 of its 80 Torah scrolls. During the 1938 November Pogroms, members of the SA (Storm Troopers) ransacked and destroyed the interior rooms, the furnishings and the ritual objects. Shortly afterwards, the secessionist orthodox community and the main community were forced to merge into the “Jewish Cultural Association – Jewish Community” and sell their properties to the city. In February 1940, the Community Centre was forcibly sold to the non-Jewish company Byk-Guldenwerke Chemische Fabrik AG. By this time, the interior rooms were completely demolished: “All the doors and windows had been torn out, the heating pipes removed, (…) and most of the floors ripped out.”
 In the 1960s, the Jewish Restitution Successor Organization (JRSO) submitted a claim for compensation. The JRSO, designated the official custodian of heirless Jewish property, was active in the American zone from 1948. It claimed DM 745,450 for the damage to the building, the interior and the destroyed ritual objects. The compensation authority rejected the estimate as too high, arguing that some damage was caused by the war and not by the pogroms. It finally set the compensation at DM 280,855.
