# forced labour camp

*Friedrich-Ebert-Anlage 60-64*

![Forced labor camp, Friedrich-Ebert-Anlage 60-64](../../images/b7216e1bf4322629411b1d65b34a213e_original.jpg)
*before 1920 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S17 Nr. 00579-8 | Description: Forced labor camp, Friedrich-Ebert-Anlage 60-64*

The German Reich Post Office headquartered at Hohenzollernanlage 60–64 (now Friedrich-Ebert-Anlage) made use of several hundred forced labourers from all over Europe. They were lodged in different locations. According to the Catalogue of Camps and Prisons (CCP), 480 persons were housed at Grosse Gallusstrasse 12 alone between 1940 and 1945. There were also camps at Gutleutstrasse 36, the corner of Wurzelstrasse and Gutleutstrasse, and Friedberger Warte / Giessener Strasse.
