# Stolen Place

*Eschersheimer Landstraße 132*

![Eschersheimer Landstraße 132](../../images/af9782640251b1e0918c177ff206d24e_original.jpg)
*2002 — Author: Martin Starl | License: ISG | Description: Eschersheimer Landstraße 132*

The property at Eschersheimer Landstraße 132 with a plot size of 604 square meters originally belonged to the Jewish merchant Adolf Hamburger and his wife Rahe, née Marx. At the time of the sale, the couple lived at Oeder Weg 113. The house they purchased around 1920 consisted of eight four-and-a-half-room apartments with a kitchen, tiled bathroom and balcony. It had central heating and a hot water supply as well as a fully equipped laundry room. There was a bleaching area and a garden in excellent condition on the property. Due to the persecution, the owners had to sell their apartment building to a non-Jewish businessman from Bad Soden and his wife for 84,000 Reichsmark in a contract dated November 26, 1938. The officially tax-assessed value was 69,900 Reichsmark and the market value 79,500 Reichsmark. The annual rental income amounted to 11,730 Reichsmark. However, the district economic advisor of the NSDAP refused to agree to this purchase price and demanded a reduction to 75,000 Reichsmark. They agreed on this new purchase price in a supplementary contract dated December 27, 1938. The parties shared the contract costs, including land transfer tax and broker's commission. The building authority approved this new contract on May 23, 1939. The Nazi authorities did not set a compensatory levy to the German Reich, but reserved the right to do so in the event of a rent increase. The landlord was not present when the contract was signed. This suggests that he had been deported to a concentration camp after the November pogrom and was still there. The landlady therefore had to sign the contract alone.
