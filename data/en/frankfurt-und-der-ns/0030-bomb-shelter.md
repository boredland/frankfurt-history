# Bomb shelter

*Freiligrathstraße 11*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Freiligrathstraße](../../images/0525ac03d413767a44794839805d78d4_original.jpg)
*1943 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt Best. S7B Nr. 1998-4895 | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Freiligrathstraße*

Part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme, this bomb shelter was one of Frankfurt’s larger facilities for air-raid protection. It offered space for 1,025 persons on four floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt could accommodate a total of only 35,000 residents. Others sought refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from entering. Construction began in September 1941. The shelter had a shape similar to that of the buildings in the area, including several schools. Despite this camouflage, it was struck by bombs that caused severe damage to the roof and stair tower. The swastikas at the entrances were removed after the war. The shelter was used as a storage depot by the Historisches Museum and the Museum für Kunsthandwerk (now the Museum Angewandte Kunst) and later as a rehearsal space for musicians. It was torn down in 2020.

Construction company: unknown
 Architect: Adam Heinrich Aßmann
