# Bomb shelter

*Küferstraße 7*

![Photo of a bomb shelter located in Küferstraße](../../images/cf8532fb1595e61f4a8446765391f5c0_original.jpg)
*1984 — Author: unbekannt | License: Denkmalamt Stadt Frankfurt | Description: Photo of a bomb shelter located in Küferstraße*

This bomb shelter served the residents of the Sindlingen district. It provided space for 624 persons on three floors. The bomb shelters of Frankfurt offered air-raid protection for a total of only 35,000 residents. Others took refuge in the cellars of their houses or in public air-raid cellars, which were not bombproof. When word of this got out, more and more people crowded into the bomb shelters. Jews, forced labourers, and prisoners of war, however, were prohibited from using them. Construction work lasted from April 1941 to July 1942. The shelter was built in the style of the surrounding buildings and decorated with red sandstone elements. It was part of the so-called Fuehrer’s Emergency Programme. After the war, the Frankfurt welfare office lodged residents of the Sindlingen district here who had lost their homes. The Hoechst AG company used the building from 1947. Later it served civil defence purposes.

Construction company: unknown
 Architect: Karl Christoph Gräf
