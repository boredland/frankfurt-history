# Bar Kochba Frankfurt

*Sondershausenstraße*

![](../../images/5e60ec40eb933d4ad779733212b98a3a_original.jpg)
*License: Eintracht Frankfurt Museum*

The Bar Kochba Frankfurt club had to search for new sports grounds several times because of protests from Nazi organizations for their handball and soccer teams. In 1935, however, there was a government directive to be more "cautious" in its anti-Jewish sports policy toward the outside world. Because of the upcoming 1936 Olympics, there was fear of protests and boycotts from other countries. Thus, in December, Bar Kochba was assigned the rear space of the Sondershausenstraße sports facility in the Gallus district. At the same time, the front part was occupied by the Postsportverein. This was National Socialist in orientation. NS and HJ organizations trained in the shooting range. Private rooms were rented for the other sports. Unique in Germany were large sports festivals in the Hippodrom. The last event of the club was a soccer tournament for schoolchildren on November 6, 1938. After the pogrom on November 9, all Jewish clubs were deprived of their places. By the end of 1938 all Jewish clubs were banned. From 1942 there was a large forced labor camp on the sports field.

*Author: Helga Roos, Sportkreis Frankfurt e.V.*
