# Swing meeting place "Rokoko-Diele" in the Hotel Kyffhäuser

*Kaiserstraße 62*

![Picture postcard showing the Rococo hall at the Hotel Kyffhäuser](../../images/c36b7ab6be2abf657ea95de842c594a1_original.jpg)
*1942 — Author: unbekannt | License: HMF, Sammlung Church, C80368 | Description: Picture postcard showing the Rococo hall at the Hotel Kyffhäuser*

The Rokoko-Diele was the most important gathering place for young people who listened, danced to, or played Swing. It was located in a back room in Hotel Kyffhäuser. There were a piano and a set of drums in the room that anyone could play. Emil Mangelsdorff, who studied at the Hoch’sches Konservatorium in 1942/43, performed in the Rokoko-Diele with his band. The so-called Kameruner Kippe (Cameroonians’ Club) also met there. A few of its members were friends of Mangelsdorff’s. “Kippen” were loose-knit groups of young people from the swing scene who knew each other from their neighbourhoods, clubs, or schools, or through friends. These cliques called themselves “Kippe Nord-Ost” (“Bornheimer”), “Elbe-Kippe”, “Halbmond-Club”, and “Club der Kameruner” after the colloquial name for the Gallus district. There were also groups such as the “Harlem-Club”, the “O.K.-Gang”, and the “Tarantella-Club” with more middle-class members. The Gestapo persecuted the young swing fans and carried out raids in the venues. In an interview with the FAZ in 2003, the band pianist Hans-Otto Jung recalled: “You could already see from a distance when the Gestapo man, Mr Baldauf, was on his way. Then we quickly changed songs; instead of the Tiger Rag we played Tigerjagd im Taunus for example.” Those who were caught faced interrogation. The Gestapo also carried out house searches and confiscated gramophone records with English titles. They moreover demanded that boys with long hair get it cut short. The Rokoko-Diele was destroyed by bombing in 1943. The musicians from the circle around this venue had a lasting influence on the post-war jazz scene in Frankfurt.
