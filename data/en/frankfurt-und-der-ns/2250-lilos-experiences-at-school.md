# Lilo’s Experiences at School

*Out and about in Frankfurt with Lilo*

![Lilo on her first day of school](../../images/ec728bde4cbe7aabe5453cd559b54d03_original.jpg)
*1939 — Author: unbekannt | License: Privat, Lilo Günzler: Endlich Reden, Henrich Editionen Frankfurt | Description: Lilo on her first day of school*

Lilo’s first day of school was right after Easter 1939. In those days, the school year began after the Easter—not the summer—vacation.
Lilo could hardly wait to become a schoolgirl and learn to read, write, and do arithmetic. Like all children, she got a Schultüte (a big cone filled with gifts and sweets), and she was also very happy about the pretty dress she wore on her first day at school.
However, her happiness didn’t last long. Lilo wasn’t sent to the same school as the other children in her district, but to a different one. She went to the Liebfrauen School, which was located further from where she lived. There, no one knew that her mother and brother were Jewish, or that Lilo herself was classified as a “first-degree Mischling [crossbreed].” Her parents sent her to a different school as a way of protecting her.
But Lilo had horrible experiences there anyway.
For example, the time came when she could no longer do her homework because her father’s radio had been confiscated. Here you can listen to a passage from an interview with Lilo.
