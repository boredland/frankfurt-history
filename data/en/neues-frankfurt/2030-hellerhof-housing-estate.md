# Hellerhof housing estate

*Frankenallee 220*

![Aerial view of the housing estate on Frankenallee with row and front buildings.](../../images/52b535bd01d6e33408188d984e1fe4e5_original.jpg)
*1928 — Author: Ilse Bing | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of the housing estate on Frankenallee with row and front buildings.*

The housing estate was the realisation of the “flat for the breadline”, a topic discussed at the CIAM congress in 1929. Mart Stam, a renowned Dutch architect, designed three- to four-storey row buildings arranged in parallel, with wide green areas in between, in three construction phases. The north-south orientation resulted in living rooms and balconies receiving the afternoon sun, while bedrooms were lit by the morning sun. Stam closed off the rows on Frankenallee with two-storey front buildings, in order not to restrict the lighting. Their balconies alternate continuously with high ribbon windows, creating a dynamic facade. Striking stairs on the front sides lead to the access balconies on the rear of the front buildings. In 1976, they were torn down at Frankenallee 202–218 and replaced by new buildings.

Construction management: Aktiengesellschaft Hellerhof

Construction period: 1929–1933

Architect: Mart Stam

Further reading: DW Dreysse: May-Siedlungen. Architekturführer durch zehn Siedlungen des Neuen Frankfurt 1926-1930. 4th ed., Köln 2019, p. 36-39.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
