# Primary school Niederursel (today: Heinrich Kromer School)

*Niederurseler Landstraße 60, 60439 Frankfurt am Main*

![Perspective drawing of the school building](../../images/7d8c73c5d08977d1f2ee1e715f50beb4_original.jpg)
*1927 — Author: Franz Schuster | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1928, Heft 11/12, S. 231 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Perspective drawing of the school building*

After Niederursel was incorporated into the city of Frankfurt in 1910, the number of pupils at the Niederursel primary school rose steadily in the following years. In 1928, the school moved from the town centre to a modern new building on what was then the outskirts of the town. The design of the building was influenced by the progressive education movement and, despite having two storeys, it was conceived as a pavilion school. The four wings of the building framed the schoolyard, which was thus shielded from the street. The entrance to the school grounds was located at Hohemarkstraße, with the school lunchroom to the west, the school caretaker’s apartment to the east, and the assembly hall on the upper floor. The two wings of the building with the classrooms lay to the east and west of the schoolyard. Starting from the schoolyard, the classrooms were connected by a pergola, while on the other side, they opened onto the school gardens at the edge of the site. The school library and the gymnasium were situated in the northern wing of the building. During the Second World War, the building was severely damaged by a bomb attack and was not rebuilt until 1960, when it was significantly altered, including an additional storey. Later years saw further extensions.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architect: Franz Schuster
