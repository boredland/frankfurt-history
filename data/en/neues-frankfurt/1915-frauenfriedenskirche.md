# Frauenfriedenskirche

*Zeppelinallee 101*

![Aerial view of the Frauenfriedenskirche](../../images/c009a3d8bc06f6386cbce726affd626c_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Aerial view of the Frauenfriedenskirche*

The most striking feature of the Frauenfriedenskirche in Frankfurt-Bockenheim, which was consecrated in 1929, is the 20-metre-high portal with three round arched niches. The mosaic statue of the Queen of Peace, made by sculptor Emil Sutor, is in the centre niche. The women’s rights activist and chairwoman of the Catholic German Women’s Association, Hedwig Dransfeld, initiated the church as a memorial for peace and reconciliation. The mosaics by Friedrich Stichs, which are arranged on either side of the Queen of Peace and depict war and peace, emphasise the focus on remembrance and hope. During the 2018–2020 renovation, the original colourful interior design of the church was restored. In addition, thanks to modern technology, the architect’s original idea for the illumination of the church was fully realised for the first time.

Construction management: Katholischer Deutscher Frauenbund (Catholic German Women’s Association)

Construction period: 1927–1929

Architect: Hans Herkommer

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 171-176.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
