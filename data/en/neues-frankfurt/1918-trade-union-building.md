# Trade union building

*Wilhelm-Leuschner-Straße 69–77*

![Street view of the trade union building.](../../images/56e4a86ca43339d92c1dc88e86ad9d49_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Street view of the trade union building.*

On 12 July 1931, the new trade union building designed by Berlin-based architects Max Taut and Franz Hoffmann was inaugurated. It was the fourth “high-rise” in Frankfurt – alongside the Mousonfabrik, the wholesale market hall (Großmarkthalle), and the I.G. Farben building. Originally planned as a people’s house including a hall and hotel building on the Main river, only the administration building was realised. Despite opposition from the neighbouring villa residents, the building permit was granted. The building was constructed as an iron-frame structure with an axial dimension of four windows. The framework remained visible, whereas the scraped concrete was filled and clad with Danube shell limestone slabs. In 1933, the trade unions were broken up and the building was renamed “House of Labour”.

Construction management: ADGB (Allgemeiner Deutscher Gewerkschaftsbund / German Trade Union Association)

Construction period: 1930–1931

Architects: Max Taut, Franz Hoffmann
