# Brentanopark pavilion

*Rödelheimer Parkweg 11*

![View of the rear of the pavilion with viewing terrace.](../../images/dcae6cfcd2a20edb2fed1a21603ca21f_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: View of the rear of the pavilion with viewing terrace.*

Built in 1931 and based on plans by architect Eugen Kaufmann, the pavilion is located in the southern part of Brentanopark, which itself was redesigned by horticultural director Max Bromme starting in 1928. The pavilion was designed as a combination of shelter and educational building to give Frankfurt’s city youth a deeper understanding of horticulture. Fan-shaped working gardens for pupils lay in front of the semi-circular pavilion. The building is characterised by its clear design: white facades, black-brown window frames, and an edge of the flat roof painted in red. On the ground floor, the structure is supported by red columns on copper plinths.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1931

Architect: Eugen Kaufmann

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 206.
