# Broadcasting centre of Südwestdeutscher Rundfunk

*Eschersheimer Landstraße 29-33*

![View of the cubic building with its light-coloured.](../../images/7acfddd1312f9a26ce4054420fcf09c7_original.jpg)
*1932 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: View of the cubic building with its light-coloured.*

The Südwestdeutscher Rundfunk AG (now Hessischer Rundfunk) broadcasting centre, which opened in 1930, is one of the most important yet little-known buildings of Neues Frankfurt. Architect Willi Cahn created a functional structure with innovative acoustic solutions. The grand broadcasting hall, today part of the Academy of Music and Performing Arts, set new technical standards. The trapezoidal shape of the hall minimised reverberation times while state-of-the-art technology ensured optimum sound conditions. The building survived the Second World War largely undamaged. Significant details such as the elegant walnut panelling and the coloured ornamental window, which gives the hall an atmospheric lighting, have been preserved to this day.

Construction management: Südwestdeutsche Rundfunk AG

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architect: Willi Cahn

Further reading: Klaus Strzyz und Roswitha Väth: Zu Unrecht vergessen: der Architekt Willi Cahn. In: maybrief 52. Frankfurt am Main 2019, p. 4-6.
