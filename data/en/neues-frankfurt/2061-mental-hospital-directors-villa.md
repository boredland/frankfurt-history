# Mental hospital director’s villa

*Heinrich-Hoffmann-Straße 2A*

![Mental hospital director’s villa](../../images/1f45186f75e0da5635e60a055ea3a121_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — License: Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: Mental hospital director’s villa*

Although many housing estates were constructed in the era of Neues Frankfurt, only few villas were built. Thus, the residence for the director of the mental hospital, located next to the house, is somewhat special. The two to three-storey, flat-roofed building was and still is plastered white. It appears to be composed of cubic elements, with only a semi-circular residential wing protruding slightly from the ground floor and including panoramic windows. The roof terrace above is ideal for sunbathing. Despite the early modifications to the floor plan and the division into flats, the villa was listed as a historical monument in 1986. Its ground floor has been vacant since 2005 (as of 2024). For many years, the Martin Elsaesser Foundation has been committed to finding a proper use for the building and renovating it in line with its listed status.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1928–1930

Architect: Martin Elsaesser

Further reading: www.martin-elsaesser.de/projekte/direktorenvilla-der-klinik-fuer-gemuets-und-nervenkranke-frankfurt-am-main/
