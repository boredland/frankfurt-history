# Wholesale market hall

*Sonnemannstraße 20*

![View of the wholesale market hall](../../images/eb100005c4b6708cb88664eb2803aecc_original.jpg)
*around 1929 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: View of the wholesale market hall*

With its monumental dimensions – 220 metres long, 23 metres high, and 50 metres deep – the Großmarkthalle seems like a cathedral of commerce, earning it the nickname “vegetable church”. The largest column-free structure in Europe at the time was created using an innovative roof construction consisting of 15 half-cylinder shells. These delicate concrete shells were a technical novelty. The market stalls were located in the hall, while the traders’ warehouses, as well as technical and sanitary rooms, were in the basement below. The two eight-storey brick front buildings housed the offices and the cold store.

 

During the period of National Socialism, the hall was misused as a deportation centre for over 10,000 Frankfurt Jews; today, a memorial commemorates these crimes. Its use as a market hall ended in 2004. Since 2015, the remodelled hall has been serving as the entrance to the European Central Bank. It was supplemented by a glass double tower with a height of 185 metres, designed by the architectural firm Coop Himmelb(l)au. To make this construction possible, the listed building status, which had been in place since 1972, was partially lifted.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main
Construction period: 1926–1928
Architect: Martin Elsaesser

Further reading: Christina Gräwe, Jörg Schilling: Grossmarkthalle Frankfurt am Main 1928, martin-elsaesser-bauhefte 07, Hamburg 2015.
