# St Boniface’s church

*Holbeinstraße 70*

![View of St Boniface’s church.](../../images/3f0a489efee4261f0204b2bcb9f8974c_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: View of St Boniface’s church.*

St Boniface’s church, consecrated in 1927, was built according to designs by Martin Weber in the style of brick expressionism. It is considered to be the first modernist Catholic church building in Frankfurt. The construction was the result of an architectural competition, combining two of Weber’s submitted designs, both of which were awarded. The church fulfils two functions: it serves as a parish church for daily services and as a memorial church for solemn commemoration. Weber succeeded in expressing both functions in the interior and exterior by deliberate, complementary contrasts. The hexagonal choir tower is particularly striking. The use of reinforced concrete, as well as the facade being clad in clinker brick, initially led to fierce controversy, since these modern materials were considered “unworthy” for a place of worship.

Construction management: Sachsenhausen Catholic parish, Limburg diocese

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architect: Martin Weber

Further reading: Adrian Seib: Der Kirchenbaumeister Martin Weber (1890–1941). Leben und Werk eines Architekten für die liturgische Erneuerung. Mainz 1999, p. 104-122.
