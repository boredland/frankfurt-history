# The “Volksstimme” (“The People’s Voice”) – Publishing company building and administration building of the Union-Druckerei printing firm

*Bockenheimer Landstraße 136-138*

![Street view of the building, 1929](../../images/203308bb360e0441d742b1814fda6e45_original.jpg)
*1929 — Author: unbekannt / unknown | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1929, Heft 12, S. 234 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Street view of the building, 1929*

The “Volksstimme” was an influential social democratic daily newspaper published in Frankfurt am Main from 1889 to 1933. In the 1920s, the Volksstimme publishing house had over 200 employees. To accommodate this growth, a new building was constructed on Bockenheimer Landstraße in 1929, which was also to house the administration of the Union-Druckerei printing firm. The neighbouring printing building was accessible via underground passages. It was constructed in a steel frame structure and was characterised by its cubic design. Iron supports clad in wired glass supported the building. Large window ribbons and glass partitions allowed plenty of daylight to reach the employees’ workplaces. The facade was designed with vertically laid tiles. The canopy over the entrance area, closed on one side and extending towards the street, was grey with striking orange columns. The latest issues of the Volksstimme newspaper were displayed in showcases in the entrance area. The Volksstimme was banned by the National Socialists in March 1933, the building was occupied by the SA in May 1933 and destroyed during the Second World War.

Construction management: Union-Druckerei und Verlagsanstalt G.m.b.H., Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929

Architect: Johann Wilhelm Lehr
