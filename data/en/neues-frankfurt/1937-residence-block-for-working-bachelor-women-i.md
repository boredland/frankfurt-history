# Residence block for working bachelor women I

*Adickesallee 21–23*

![View of the residence block for working bachelor women from Adickesallee](../../images/23c741a5ebbffe766663c25be4a2064e_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1930, Heft 4/5, S. 97 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: View of the residence block for working bachelor women from Adickesallee*

Women became more emancipated during the Weimar Republic and increasingly lived alone and found employment. Still, there was a lack of suitable forms of housing – many women could not afford a flat in the new housing estates. Women’s organisations thus called for bachelor women’s homes. However, the organisation of landladies feared the loss of their subletting business and opposed the construction of single-room flats. The project realised on Adickesallee was accordingly subject to the condition that no flats with fewer than three rooms were to be built. Therefore, Bernhard Hermkes developed floor plans that allowed several tenants to move into a flat together. Three blocks of flats were built here, aligned in a horseshoe shape, with a total of 43 flats, in which eventually around 120 working women lived. 

Construction management: Siedlungsgenossenschaft berufstätiger Frauen (Working women’s housing cooperative)

Construction period: 1927–1930

Architect: Bernhard Hermkes

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 227f.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
