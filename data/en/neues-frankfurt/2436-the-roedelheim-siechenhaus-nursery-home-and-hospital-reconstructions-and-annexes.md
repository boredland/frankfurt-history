# The Rödelheim Siechenhaus nursery home and hospital – Reconstructions and annexes (destroyed)

*Alexanderstraße 96*

![Street view of Siechenhaus Rödelheim](../../images/68641f3b145c8e29c624ed4796b46889_original.jpg)
*after 1930 — Author: Albert Roth | License: Deutsches Architekturmuseum, Frankfurt am Main | Description: Street view of Siechenhaus Rödelheim*

With the incorporation of Rödelheim in 1910, the local hospital, which had been established by a Jewish foundation, was placed under the administration of the city of Frankfurt. In 1922, it was converted into a special nursery home and hospital, which was reconstructed and expanded by Max Cetto from 1928 onwards. A modern facade united the central old building and two new wings into a single structure. Wide window ribbons provided for well-lit interiors. In addition to the bedrooms with a total of 101 beds, day rooms, lounges, bathrooms, a kitchen and laundry room, as well as a new prayer room were created. Radio headphones at all beds provided entertainment even for residents with limited mobility. A large garden at the rear of the building offered views and access to green spaces. In 1983, the building was torn down. In its place, the Frankfurt Association for Care of the Elderly built the Social and Rehabilitation Centre West.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main, Frankfurter Verband für Altersfürsorge e.V. (Frankfurt Association for Care of the Elderly)

Construction period: 1928–1930

Architects: Max Cetto (general planning by Karl Weber, municipal building officer)

Edgar Bönisch, Joseph und Hannchen May’sche Stiftung für Kranke und Hilfsbedürftige – Baugeschichte und Personen, 2013 (https://www.juedische-pflegegeschichte.de/joseph-und-hannchen-maysche-stiftung-fuer-kranke-und-hilfsbeduerftige-baugeschichte-und-personen / reviewed 11/9/2025).
