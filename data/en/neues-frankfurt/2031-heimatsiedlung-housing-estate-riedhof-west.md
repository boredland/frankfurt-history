# Heimatsiedlung housing estate (Riedhof-West)

*Unter den Kastanien 1*

![Street view in the Heimatsiedlung](../../images/994648ae5ab676a4ff81fb93a91b6dcb_original.jpg)
*Unbekannt — Author: Paul Wolff | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: Street view in the Heimatsiedlung*

The Heimatsiedlung presents itself as a structurally closed unit. Its floor plan is characterised by nine residential rows running parallel to Mörfelder Landstraße. Multi-storey blocks on the outer edges shield the lower rows of houses from the railway embankment and Stresemannallee. Like in a city wall, all house entrances are arranged in the interior part. The complex is entered through uniformly designed, gate-like driveways. Wide green areas extend between the rows. The residential streets are each named after the respective type of tree planted there. Their different trunks and treetops create variety in the repetitive spatial structure. The continuous, glazed veranda strips facing south constitute another characteristic of the housing estate.

Construction management: „Heimat“, Gemeinnützige Bau- und Siedlungs-AG, Berlin

Construction period: 1927–1930

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Fritz Berke (planning); Franz Roeckle (architectural adaptation)

Horticultural design: Leberecht Migge

Further reading: DW Dreysse: May-Siedlungen. Architekturführer durch zehn Siedlungen des Neuen Frankfurt 1926-1930. 4th ed., Köln 2019, S. 44-47.
