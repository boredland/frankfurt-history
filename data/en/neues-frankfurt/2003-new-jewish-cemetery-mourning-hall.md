# New Jewish cemetery, mourning hall

*Eckenheimer Landstraße 238*

![View of the mourning hall](../../images/8b958367728af0e7293c45f807aeb030_original.jpg)
*Unknown — Author: Hermann Collischonn | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: View of the mourning hall*

The strictly cubic architecture of the new Jewish cemetery, designed by Fritz Nathan, combines elements of Neues Bauen with antique motifs and is reminiscent of ancient Egyptian temples in its monumentality. A three-part portal with a Hebrew inscription leads into a square inner courtyard lined with arcades. To the north of this rises the imposing mourning hall, flanked by a mortuary, while the administration building is located south. The cemetery follows a clear symmetry and has a simple design in keeping with Jewish tradition. On both sides of the main axis, there are 800 identically designed graves of Frankfurt Jews who committed suicide as a result of deportation orders during the Nazi era. To this day, the cemetery serves the Jewish community as a burial site and memorial.

Construction management: Jewish congregation

Construction period: 1928–1929

Architect: Fritz Nathan

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 203.
