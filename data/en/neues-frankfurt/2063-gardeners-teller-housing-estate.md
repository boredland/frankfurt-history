# Gardeners’ “Teller” housing estate

*Im Teller*

![Gardeners in the “Teller” housing estate](../../images/cf8fb025b8f7329127df866d791256ee_original.jpg)
*1929 — Author: Kiehm | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Gardeners in the “Teller” housing estate*

The Oberrad district was and still is characterised by market gardens, where the herbs for Frankfurt’s famous green sauce are also grown. In 1927, the “Teller” gardeners’ housing cooperative, which was founded specifically for this purpose, built a small housing estate as a model development here, based on plans by Franz Roeckle. To the right and left of the central street “Im Teller”, ten semi-detached houses are lined up as two-storey brick buildings with a service wing and a flat roof. The garden plots, originally quite large, with greenhouses are located to the rear. Today, the houses have been privatised, plots have been divided or merged, and only a few market gardens are still in operation. Despite numerous structural changes, the estate gives a good impression of the original planning.

Construction management: Gardeners’ Housing Cooperative “Teller”

Construction period: 1927

Architect: Franz Roeckle

Further reading: Franz Roeckle. Bauten 1902–1933. Ed. by Marianne Hilti-Roeckle, Hanna Roeckle, Peter Zimmermann. Ostfildern 2016, p. 71-74.
