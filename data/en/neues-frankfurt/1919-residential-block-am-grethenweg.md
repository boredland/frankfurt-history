# Residential block Am Grethenweg

*Kranichsteiner Straße 22-26*

![View of residential block Am Grethenweg.](../../images/0c6fb78ce7bac25465b3434ae659428c_original.jpg)
*around 1928 — Author: Hermann Collischonn | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1928, Heft 7/8, S. 152 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: View of residential block Am Grethenweg.*

The small residential complex between Grethenweg and Kranichsteiner Straße was one of the first Neues Frankfurt projects in 1926/27. The sharp-angled plot, which sloped in two directions, challenged the creativity of architect Ernst Balser. The result was 20 flats in three staggered building sections. A four-storey, slightly curved transverse building with a hipped roof is connected to the already existing buildings. Two lower structures with flat roofs follow parallel to Grethenweg, with the front building ending in a striking “round corner”. Architect Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky once lived here in a studio with a terrace on the top floor. The facades have a varied design with dark red clinker bricks and plastered surfaces. The listed building was renovated and restored in 2017–2020.

Construction management: AG für kleine Wohnungen

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architect: Ernst Balser

Quelle: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 241-243.
