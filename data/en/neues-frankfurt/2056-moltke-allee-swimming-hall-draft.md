# Moltke-Allee swimming hall – draft

*Theodor-Heuss-Allee 1*

![Draft of a swimming hall at the trade fair grounds](../../images/2c6c3b715dbdb24c9dae02ab92a9f401_original.jpg)
*1928-1929 — Author: Martin Elsaesser | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung / Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: Draft of a swimming hall at the trade fair grounds*

Martin Elsaesser designed a facility that combined an indoor swimming pool and an outdoor area, also incorporating the exhibition garden on the trade fair grounds. In addition to public use, competitions with up to 1,000 spectators would also have been possible there. The planning was both ingenious and pragmatic. The large indoor swimming pool could be opened up to the sun terraces and the park in the south using electrically operated sliding windows. Tub baths and showers could be used by visitors who did not have their own bathroom in their flat. In his design, Elsaesser drew on his experience from the construction of the Fechenheim swimming hall. Unfortunately, the facility, which would have been close to the city centre and the trade fair, was not realised due to the grave economic situation.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Draft: 1928–1929

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
