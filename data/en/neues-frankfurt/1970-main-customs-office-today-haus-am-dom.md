# Main customs office (today: Haus am Dom)

*Domstraße 3*

![](../../images/4a6dcb33b6600e082c547551ce2ec39c_original.jpg)

In a prominent location, next to the imperial cathedral, the main customs office was the most centrally located building of the Neues Frankfurt and later one of the last relics of the pre-war old city centre, which was destroyed in 1944. Werner Hebebrand erected the four-storey office building on a slightly curved ground plan in 1927, after the “Kleiner Rebstock” courtyard had to make way for the breakthrough in Braubachstraße. The glazed corner staircase on the northern side, which is considered a reference to the Fagus works by Walter Gropius and Adolf Meyer, is characteristic of the building. The society of friends of the old city centre around Fried Lübbecke was successful in advocating a pitched roof instead of the flat roof that was originally planned. After being damaged in the war, the building was given a flat provisional roof. In the early 2000s, the diocese of Limburg converted the building into the “Haus am Dom”. After some controversy – reminiscent of the debates in the 1920s – about the design of the roof, the decision was again made in favour of a pitched roof.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1927

Architect: Werner Hebebrand

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 115f.
