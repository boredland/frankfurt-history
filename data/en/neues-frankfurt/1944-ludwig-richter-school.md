# Ludwig Richter school

*Hinter den Ulmen 10*

![View of the Ludwig Richter school](../../images/9441cdd4c0f352c8d997617948aee942_original.jpg)
*around 1928 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Martin Elsaesser: Bauten und Entwürfe aus den Jahren 1924–1932, Berlin 1933 | Description: View of the Ludwig Richter school*

Since the beginning of the 20th century, the number of schoolchildren in Eschersheim had constantly increased. The construction of the Ludwig Richter school, which Anne Frank’s sister Margot also attended, was long overdue. Martin Elsaesser designed a three-winged facility with various building complexes around a schoolyard. However, only the three-storey centre wing of the design was inaugurated in 1928. The gymnasium, an assembly hall with a singing hall in a semi-circular annexe, and the kindergarten pavilion fell victim to cost-cutting constraints. The planned completion did not take place in 1930, but in 1954 – again in a reduced version. Today’s primary school is located in the immediate neighbourhood of the “Am Lindenbaum” housing estate and features typical Elsaesser elements, such as the corner of the building at the main entrance being “dissolved” in glass.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1928/1954

Architect: Martin Elsaesser, Walter Schütte
