# Charles Hallgarten school (since 2008) / Friedrich Ebert school (1930–1933, 1945–2008), Dietrich Eckhart school (1933–1945)

*Am Bornheimer Hang 10*

![School at Bornheimer Hang, seen from the garden](../../images/d5e0e6c450a65994ed1b19c53ab9d7af_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Paul Wolff | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: School at Bornheimer Hang, seen from the garden*

As part of Ernst May’s large-scale school building programme, the Röderberg reform school, founded in the early 1920s, was given its own building. The concept of the open-space school, initially renamed Friedrich-Ebert-Schule, focussed on a close connection between children and nature. A multi-storey administrative wing with a sports hall, library, ballroom, and two flats was built on the green Bornheimer Hang. Three single-storey buildings branch off from a corridor at right angles. This is where the classrooms are located, each with direct access to its own garden, where teaching could also take place with the help of flexible furniture. Between the building axes, there was a large area for sports, a paddling pool, and a school garden. Today, the ensemble is a listed building.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Ernst May, Albert Loecher

Further reading: Jutta Frieß: Der Frankfurter Reformschulversuch 1921–1937. Frankfurt am Main 2007.

*Author: Ulrike May*
