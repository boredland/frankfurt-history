# Church of the Holy Spirit

*Schäfflestraße 19*

![Church of the Holy Spirit in the Riederwald housing estate](../../images/0b826cf232f2239e8c9a62b87196e5f5_original.jpg)
*1931–1943 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: Church of the Holy Spirit in the Riederwald housing estate*

Until the consecration of the church of the Holy Spirit in 1931, the Catholics of the Riederwald housing estate had to hold their services in temporary accommodation. The church builder Martin Weber created a compact steel frame structure based on a grid system of four by four metres. The tower with a bell frame dominates the exterior, and round windows structure the facade while large concrete reliefs adorn the entrances. Upon entering, a surprisingly bright, wide sacral space opens up, with a flat ceiling supported by concrete columns. In keeping with the church reform of the time, the altar stands freely and is accentuated by the open choir tower and coloured light from the south. The controversial simplicity of the building was also due to cost-cutting constraints. The church was partially destroyed during the Second World War and some changes were later made in the interior.

Construction management: Gesamtverband der Altfrankfurter Pfarreien (Association of Old Frankfurt Parishes)

Construction period: 1930–1931

Architects: Martin Weber, Franz Carl Throll

Sculptor: Arnold Hensler

Further reading: Adrian Seib, Der Kirchenbaumeister Martin Weber (1890–1941). Leben und Werk eines Architekten für die liturgische Erneuerung, Mainz 1999.
