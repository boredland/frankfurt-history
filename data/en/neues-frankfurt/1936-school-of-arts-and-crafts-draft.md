# School of Arts and Crafts - Draft

*Theodor-Stern-Kai 1*

![Isometric drawing of the planned building](../../images/5503cca74d379dac914f96d055f0dc99_original.jpg)
*1926/1927 — Author: Martin Elsaesser | License: Martin Elsaesser: Bauten und Entwürfe aus den Jahren 1924–1932, Berlin 1933 | Description: Isometric drawing of the planned building*

The representative building for the new School of Arts and Crafts was to be located at the head of today’s Friedensbrücke. The City of Frankfurt initially intended to hold an architectural competition for the design, but cancelled it in favour of building director Martin Elsaesser. His design, reminiscent of the Bauhaus in Dessau, was consistently adapted to the curriculum developed for the new school by Fritz Wichert. However, Elsaesser also set his own priorities, planning a striking glass curtain wall and interlocking cubes that formed a compact, complex ensemble with an imposing tower. Eventually, the realisation failed, as the construction plans were rejected due to the economic crisis. The current building on the site is at least somewhat reminiscent of Elsaesser’s plans: it includes a tower at the bridge head and an elongated structure along Theodor-Stern-Kai.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1925–1927

Architect: Martin Elsaesser

Further reading: 

Thomas Elsässer, Christina Gräwe, Jörg Schilling, Peter Cachola Schmal: Martin Elsaesser und das Neue Frankfurt. Tübingen/Berlin 2009, p. 158-160.
