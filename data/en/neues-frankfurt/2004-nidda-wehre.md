Rödelheimer Wehr, Hausener Wehr, Praunheimer Wehr, Eschersheimer Wehr

Even before the First World War, recurrent floods of the Nidda had motivated Max Bromme, the city’s long-serving horticultural director, to plan a reconstruction of the course of the river. In the context of this work, bridges, paddling and swimming pools, extensive green spaces, as well as four weirs were built in Rödelheim, Hausen, Praunheim, and Eschersheim. The weirs were designed by architect Adolf Meyer, who directed the municipal building consultancy department. With the straightening of the river and the construction of the weirs, the Nidda developed into what is now an ecologically questionable canal. Thus, the “Natural Nidda” plan was adopted in 1998, followed by measures of renaturation. In the long term, this will also include the dismantling and conversion of the weirs from the 1920s.

Construction management: Municipal department for public works

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architect: Adolf Meyer (in cooperation with August Brauneck)
