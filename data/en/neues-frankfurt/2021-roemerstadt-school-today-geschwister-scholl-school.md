# Römerstadt school (today: Geschwister Scholl school)

*Hadrianstraße 18*

![View of the school with schoolyard](../../images/6748ea4eb2f212cb41b314ff69d012e8_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: View of the school with schoolyard*

The Römerstadt school was the first Neues Frankfurt school building to implement an architectural adaptation for principles of reform pedagogics. Martin Elsaesser designed the building together with Wilhelm Schütte, inspired by the light-coloured plastered buildings in the housing estate. Elements such as the brick window braces emphasised Elsaesser’s personal style. Another of Elsaesser’s trademarks, the stair tower with its numberless clock, became the “exclamation mark” of the housing estate. The main wing with its long ribbon windows and flush design ensured optimal lighting conditions. The terraced outdoor space with gardens, gymnastics lawn, and water basin opened up to the Nidda valley and connected the school with its natural surroundings. In addition, flexible interior spaces with movable furniture incorporated progressive educational ideas of the time. A roof terrace was also used for teaching. Part of the schoolyard had to be given up for the construction of Rosa-Luxemburg-Straße in the early 1970s, whereas an extension was added to the south of the building in 1994.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Wilhelm Schütte

Further reading: Christina Gräwe: Die Römerstadt-Schule. In: Elsaesser/Gräwe/Schilling/Schmal (eds.): Martin Elsaesser und das Neue Frankfurt. Tübingen, Berlin 2009, p. 121-125.
