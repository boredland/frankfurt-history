# Extension of the new main cemetery

*Eingang Ecke Marbachweg/Gießener Straße*

![Model of the extension of the new main cemetery](../../images/aca4cd72011c5b9ab803a6f143efa398_original.jpg)
*1928 — Author: Unbekannr | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1928, Heft 6, S. 101 | Description: Model of the extension of the new main cemetery*

In 1928, exactly 100 years after the opening of the new cemetery, it was decided to expand it. The last area still available was in the north-east corner, bordered by Marbachweg and Gießener Straße (sections XVI-XX). A fan-shaped section connects the new part of the cemetery with the existing one. The slightly sloping terrain was divided into five terraces, which branch off from the southern main path at an oblique angle. The access paths were accentuated with double rows of trees. Only a few plant species were to be used in order to calm the overall atmosphere of the site. This was also the aim of the newly issued cemetery regulations. They prescribed the shape of the gravestones, as well as the use of local materials. Even today, visitors can still find such stones, crafted in simple geometric shapes.

Construction period: 1928

Plan: Ernst May, Max Bromme

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 200-203.
