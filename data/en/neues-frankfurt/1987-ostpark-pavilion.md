# Ostpark pavilion

*Ratsweg 10*

![View of the enclosed bathing area of the Ostpark pond with numerous bathers, pavilion in the rear.](../../images/c7522d3d703f22fbe7fd27e9614c1c4a_original.jpg)
*1937 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: View of the enclosed bathing area of the Ostpark pond with numerous bathers, pavilion in the rear.*

In 1928, architect Max Cetto designed a small pavilion for the north-western bank of the Ostpark pond. Its roof, consisting of a semi-circular slab, is supported by three slender columns. A bench forms the edge of the pavilion, which blends harmoniously into the landscape of the 32-hectare park. Originally, the pavilion was used by children bathing in an enclosed area of the pond. The windows, which followed the curved shape and were divided into vertical rectangles, created a sheltered space with a view of the water. During the Second World War, the pavilion was severely damaged and the glazing was lost. It was reconstructed in 2019 as part of the Ostpark redevelopment, although the original glass wall was not restored.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1928

Architect: Max Cetto

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
