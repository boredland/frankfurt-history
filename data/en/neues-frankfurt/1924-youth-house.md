# Youth house

*Hansaallee 150*

![West facade of the youth house with entrance.](../../images/a708b307413d861316faf6bb6c5cb89c_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: West facade of the youth house with entrance.*

After the First World War, Frankfurt youth organisations called for the creation of meeting and accommodation facilities. To finance a new building, the youth house association organised a lottery. In 1930, independent architect Franz Thyriot designed a modern building on an angular floor plan. The slightly curved main facade with the entrance is dominated by a tower-like, five-storey section of the building with a roof terrace. To the right of this was the dormitory wing, with communal and event rooms in a lower section to the left. Once built in a relatively open area, the former “Haus der Jugend” is now squeezed in between new residential buildings. It houses the “Anne Frank educational centre” and the town hall for senior citizens, among other institutions.

Construction management: Verein Haus der Jugend (youth house association)

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architect: Franz Thyriot

Further reading: Dorothea Deschermeier/Peter Cachola Schmal/Wolfgang Voigt (eds.): Neuer Mensch, Neue Wohnung. Die Bauten des Neuen Frankfurt 1925–1933. Berlin 2019, p. 152-153.

Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 223-225.

*Author: Ulrike May*
