# Trade fair buildings

*Ludwig Erhard Anlage 1*

![Aerial view of the trade fair centre with the festival hall, built between 1907 and 1909, and the “House of Fashion” (“Haus der Moden”), designed by Peter Behrens in 1924.](../../images/faf0eb6fe5bea233c7db23d59666a281_original.jpg)
*1-9-1925 — Author: Südwestdeutsche Luftverkehrs-AG | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of the trade fair centre with the festival hall, built between 1907 and 1909, and the “House of Fashion” (“Haus der Moden”), designed by Peter Behrens in 1924.*

Even before the Neues Frankfurt took on a leading role in modernist architecture, the trade fair centre was a driving force behind the city’s creative take-off. Two outstanding buildings shaped this phase:

The “Werkbundhaus” (1921), designed by Fritz Voggenberger, set new accents with its striking colour scheme – black facing bricks below, red plaster above. With a street front of 65 metres of length, as well as with its spacious exhibition rooms, it became the centre of design discussions. Formative exhibitions were held here, including “Die Wohnung für das Existenzminimum” (“Flats for People on the Breadline”, 1929).

The “House of Fashion” (1924), designed by Peter Behrens, surprised with its expressive facade design: geometric incrustations and irregular pilaster strips covered the building and continued in the window bars – an unexpectedly sculptural, presumably multi-coloured design.

In addition, the “Haus der Technik” was built in 1927 by converting and remodelling an existing hall. The concrete slabs for the first prefabricated houses were produced here.

Although they are not preserved, these buildings reflect the avant-garde aspirations of the Frankfurt trade fair centre – as pioneers of a new architecture.

Further reading: Klaus Klemp: In: Moderne am Main 1919–1933. Frankfurt am Main 2019, p. 21-47.
