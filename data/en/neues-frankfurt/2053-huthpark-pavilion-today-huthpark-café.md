# Huthpark pavilion (today: Huthpark café)

*Propst-Goebels-Weg 17*

![Huthpark pavilion](../../images/b4baffe6c999e8e6e08036cfebd3457f_original.jpg)
*Unbekannt — Author: Kurt Weiner | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main, S7FR 10164 | Description: Huthpark pavilion*

The reinforced concrete frame structure of the pavilion with plastered round pillars and brickwork is characterised by semi-circular and semi-cylindrical shapes. Originally, it housed public toilets, changing rooms, and showers for schools and sports clubs in the neighbourhood. After the completion of the Seckbach-Süd district sports centre in the 1970s, the pavilion lost its function and its demolition was discussed. Listed as a historical monument in 1986, it was extensively renovated between 2010 and 2012. The formerly open hall was given a semi-circular glass front and now serves as a café.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Eugen Kaufmann, Eugen Blanck, Gottlob Schaupp (renovation 2010–2012: Dreysse-Architekten)

Further reading: Alexander Brockhoff: Unterstandshalle im Huthpark. In: Neuer Mensch, neue Wohnung. Die Bauten des Neuen Frankfurt 1925–1933. Berlin 2019, p. 186-187.
