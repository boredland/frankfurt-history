# Retirement home of the Henry und Emma Budge-Stiftung

*Hansaallee 146a, 60320 Frankfurt am Main*

![Title page of the magazine “Das Neue Frankfurt” (1930, issue 7) with the isometric drawing of the building.](../../images/fd3d66e38273e0b94a18d528c6d1b905_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Title page of the magazine “Das Neue Frankfurt” (1930, issue 7) with the isometric drawing of the building.*

A dignified life for elderly people of Jewish and Christian faith: that was the wish of the Jewish married couple Henry and Emma Budge. Commissioned by their foundation, a light-flooded, two-storey building with an H-shaped floor plan was built, which ensured short distances between the care wing and the flats. All senior citizens had their own flats with kitchenettes and access to a balcony or terrace. However, the retirement home was only able to fulfil its purpose for a short time: the National Socialists, who came to power in 1933, took offence at the concept and the Jewish origins of the foundation. Residents were to be separated and the Jewish proportion reduced to a third. The last Jewish resident moved out in 1939; many former residents were deported. Today, the facility – partially destroyed during the Second World War – is again a nursing home for the elderly under different management.

Construction management: Henry und Emma Budge-Stiftung

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Ferdinand Kramer, Werner Max Moser, Mart Stam
