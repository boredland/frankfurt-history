# Riederwald housing estate

*Am Erlenbruch 102*

![](../../images/e0fe63a671df9fb4d9e8ab5373feb898_original.jpg)

The Riederwald housing estate combines several architectural styles, thus telling a piece of architectural history. Its first sections were built in the so-called homeland defence style (Heimatschutzstil) as early as 1909 as a workers’ housing estate, followed by a 1926 extension in the context of Neues Frankfurt. A small, remarkable residential unit was built to the west of Schäfflestraße. Embedded in the older part of the housing estate, it incorporates playful varieties of the sloping roofs of the surrounding buildings. To the east, three residential streets were developed with single-family houses on both sides. Transverse apartment blocks shield them from the street Am Erlenbruch. The projecting semi-circular balconies with their brick-red parapets, which have unfortunately become faded, are noteworthy. Narrow passages still allow us direct access to the housing estate today.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm (planning); E. and O. Fucker, F. Thyriot, building inspector Moritz (architectural adaptation)

Further reading: DW Dreysse: May-Siedlungen. Architekturführer durch zehn Siedlungen des Neuen Frankfurt 1926-1930. 4th ed., Köln 2019, p. 32-35.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
