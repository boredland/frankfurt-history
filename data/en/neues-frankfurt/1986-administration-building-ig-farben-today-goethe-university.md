# Administration building I.G. Farben (today: Goethe University)

*Norbert-Wollheim-Platz 1*

![Aerial view of the I.G. Farbenindustrie administration building](../../images/b20d427a52c5350461b5d5118033b9f4_original.jpg)
*around 1933 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Aerial view of the I.G. Farbenindustrie administration building*

The I.G. Farben building was completed in 1931 as the administrative headquarters of I.G. Farbenindustrie AG, the largest industrial corporation in the world at the time, founded in 1925. The building was based on a design by Hans Poelzig, inspired by the General Motors building in Detroit. The steel structure, 254 metres long and clad with thin travertine slabs, was erected in just four months. The narrow, slightly curved main building comprises six rhythmically arranged transverse wings, which provide optimal lighting for the offices. Access is facilitated by decentralised stairwells with paternoster elevators. To the rear, there is a park with a water basin, as well as a canteen. The monumental architecture was largely rejected by the protagonists of the Neues Frankfurt. The magazine “Das Neue Frankfurt” expressed its reservations by printing a night shot that only emphasised the illuminated windows.

Construction management: I.G. Farbenindustrie AG

Construction period: 1931

Architect: Hans Poelzig

Further reading: Wolfgang Voigt: Verwaltungsgebäude der I.G. Farben. In: Neuer Mensch, Neue Wohnung. Die Bauten des Neuen Frankfurt 1925–1933. Ed. by Wolfgang Voigt, Dorothea Deschermeier, and Peter Cachola Schmal. Berlin 2019, p. 202f.
