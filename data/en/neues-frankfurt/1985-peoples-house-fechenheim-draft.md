# People’s house Fechenheim - draft

*Bodenseestraße 26, 60386 Frankfurt am Main*

![Draft of the people’s house Fechenheim (left). Swimming hall, which was built in 1929, to the right.](../../images/42ae85182d9e16c98455ee85eb1119e7_original.jpg)
*1929 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Zentralblatt der Bauverwaltung, 1929, Jg. 49, H. 33, S. 529 | Description: Draft of the people’s house Fechenheim (left). Swimming hall, which was built in 1929, to the right.*

Housing estates were not only supposed to serve as places to live. The inhabitants also wished for people’s houses as places of community, education, and culture. Many of these houses were planned in the Neues Frankfurt, but were never built for economic reasons – this presumably includes the people’s house Fechenheim designed by Martin Elsaesser in 1928–1929. Along with a gymnasium, which was also never built, it would have expanded the area around the Gartenbad swimming hall, which is still used today, into a larger sports and cultural centre. The design of the people’s house is similar to that of the swimming hall. Ribbon windows surround its central facade, the centre of which is emphasised by a tall mast. As with the swimming hall, a large glass facade to the south was intended to illuminate the interior.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main 

Draft: 1928–1929

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
