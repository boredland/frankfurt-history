# The Gumpertz’sches Siechenhaus nursery home and hospital – annexe (destroyed)

*Röderbergweg 82*

![Entrance hall of Gumpertz’sches Siechenhaus](../../images/7c3e8afa42a0448418da12cd60715e9b_original.jpg)
*around 1932 — Author: Jeanne Mandello | License: Deutsches Architekturmuseum, Frankfurt am MainCopyright: James Bauer, New York / Isabel Mandello de Bauer, Barcelona | Description: Entrance hall of Gumpertz’sches Siechenhaus*

The Gumpertz’sches Siechenhaus nursery home and hospital once stood on the site where the August Stunz Centre is located today. It was established by a Jewish foundation and dedicated to the care of sick and elderly Jews and non-Jews. It consisted of a large villa on Röderbergweg and an older rear building. In 1929, the city of Frankfurt leased the front building in order to expand its municipal healthcare services. At the same time, it undertook to construct extensions to the rear building. Based on plans by Max Cetto, a bright entrance area, a lounge, and a washhouse were built there. After 1933, the Jewish residents were increasingly subjected to repression. In 1941, the rear building was forcibly vacated. The remaining buildings were gradually torn down after 1945. Today, a memorial plaque commemorates the Siechenhaus and its founder Betty Gumpertz.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1932

Architect: Max Cetto
