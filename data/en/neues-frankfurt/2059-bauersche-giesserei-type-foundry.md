# “Bauersche Gießerei” type foundry

*Hamburger Allee 45*

![View of the “Bauersche Gießerei” company building.](../../images/36a27207a886947dfeb09c09627d6347_original.jpg)
*1926 — Author: unbekannt / unknown | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt am Main | Description: View of the “Bauersche Gießerei” company building.*

The “Bauersche Gießerei” foundry played a key role in the typographic design of the Neues Frankfurt. Founded in Frankfurt am Main in 1837, it became one of the leading type foundries of its time from the 1920s onwards under the artistic direction of Heinrich Jost. Its most significant contribution was the “Futura” typeface, designed by Paul Renner in 1927. With its clear, geometric form, it became the epitome of functional typography and characterised the Neues Frankfurt – in publications, on signboards, and in urban design. In addition to the “Futura”, the foundry developed other typefaces that supported modern aesthetics. Thus, it was not only a manufacturer, but also a formative player in the creative realisation of the Neues Frankfurt.

Further reading: Petra Eisele, Annette Ludwig, Isabel Naegele (eds.): Futura. Die Schrift, Mainz 2016.
