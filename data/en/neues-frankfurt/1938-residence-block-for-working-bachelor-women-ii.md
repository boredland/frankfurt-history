# Residence block for working bachelor women II

*Platenstraße 31–69*

![Isometric drawing of the living room](../../images/3696d54b9cdc593b9bd8474b110a4ca8_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1930, Heft 4/5, S. 100 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Isometric drawing of the living room*

After the completion of the first Frankfurt bachelor women’s home on Adickesallee, a second one was built on Platenstraße, but this time with independent small flats. The magazine “Das Neue Frankfurt” justified this by stating that communal flats should be avoided in order to provide working women with real external independence. 60 residential units were constructed on three upper floors with access balconies, 10 flats per floor on either side of the central staircase. There were two types of flats with different sizes (22.5 and 31 square metres respectively), and all flats were fully furnished, including a kitchen equipped with electrical appliances. On the ground floor, there were bathrooms, a central laundry, and a caretaker’s flat.

Construction management: Frauenwohnungsverein (Women’s housing association)

Construction period: 1930–1931 

Architect: Bernhard Hermkes

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 228f.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
