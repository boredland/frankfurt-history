# Eschersheim transformer station

*Thielenstraße 21*

![Eschersheim transformer station](../../images/0159d503f20bd60279207f421c27b0a3_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Max Cetto-Archiv, Deutsches Architekturmuseum, Frankfurt am Main | Description: Eschersheim transformer station*

After the war and inflation, the demand for electricity from households, trade, and industry as well as from public transport in the form of trams increased significantly from 1925 onwards. This made it necessary to expand the urban infrastructure, adapt the electricity distribution network to a supra-regional system, and increase the voltage. Thus, transformer stations were built as hubs for different voltages. Max Cetto’s building in Eschersheim displays an undecorated elegance in historical photographs, but today it is barely recognisable. In particular, the colourful paintings with idyllic nature scenes in the base area and graffiti transfigure the functional architecture. There are also extensions, recent glass blocks, and a few new window frames.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1930

Architect: Max Cetto
