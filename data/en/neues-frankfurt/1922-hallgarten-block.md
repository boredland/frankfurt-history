# Hallgarten block

*Hallgartenstraße 40*

![View of the Hallgarten block on the corner of Rotlintstraße/Hallgartenstraße](../../images/37700de3d7c079c6cb3bdc950c6489ac_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Emil Klar: Vier Jahrzehnte Wohnungsbau. Denkschrift zum vierzigjährigen Bestehen der Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen in Frankfurt am Main. 1890-1930. Frankfurt 1930, S. 37. | Description: View of the Hallgarten block on the corner of Rotlintstraße/Hallgartenstraße*

With its cladding of clinker bricks, the Hallgarten block stands out in the Nordend district, which is characterised by the Wilhelminian style. The complex around several inner courtyards comprised small flats, a bathhouse with a central laundry, and public baths, as well as premises for various social offices. The kindergarten, centrally located on Gellertstraße, occupied three storeys. Its furniture was designed by Ferdinand Kramer with students from the School of Arts and Crafts. In the courtyard at the rear, children could play in a paddling pool during the summer. This part of the building was destroyed during the Second World War and replaced by a new building with flats. The partially destroyed bathhouse on Hallgartenstraße is now also used for residential purposes.

Construction management: AG für kleine Wohnungen

Construction period: 1924–1926

Architects: Karl Blattner and Franz Roeckle

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 240-241.

*Author: Ulrike May*
