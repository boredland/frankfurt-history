# Municipal dermatological clinic for children – extension

*Sandhof-Schleife*

![Terrace of dermatological clinic for children](../../images/13d37c197240f5a02edb32258b041026_original.jpg)
*1929 — Author: Grete Leistikow | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung / Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: Terrace of dermatological clinic for children*

Today’s university hospital was established after 1914 as a result of the expansion of the Sachsenhausen municipal hospital on the same site. Built in 1908 and extended by Martin Elsaesser and Walter Körte in 1929, the dermatological clinic for children was one of the early specialised wards. In its exterior appearance, the two-storey extension building stands out with its rounded corners. Large windows open onto the adjoining garden area. From the sickrooms on the upper floor, the beds with the young patients could be wheeled onto a terrace, which was protected from the wind by glass panes. As in the observation ward, also designed by Elsaesser, the children enjoyed the sun and light, which was intended to aid their recuperation. The building still exists in a modified form. Today, the children of the “Uni-Strolche” daycare centre frolic around here (as of 2025).

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Walter Körte
