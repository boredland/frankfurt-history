# Raimundstraße housing estate

*Fallerslebenstraße 25*

![](../../images/549f32ef88ccb37548ac9f02b0c8dde0_original.jpg)

Originally, a large neighbourhood with apartment blocks and bright, green courtyards was planned between the Dichterviertel and the Platensiedlung: the Raimundstraße housing estate. However, the global economic crisis prevented its realisation. Only one building unit and one building group were constructed. Franz Roeckle’s “Mavest building unit” with 47 flats aptly demonstrates the intended interplay between the buildings and nature. Continuously glazed verandas and open loggias structure the eastern sides of the four-storey buildings horizontally. Four hook-shaped structures with another 80 flats were built south of Raimundstraße, based on plans by Carl-Hermann Rudloff. The flats were furnished differently and in some cases quite comfortably. Due to remodelling, the houses have lost their period character today. The new residential area was connected to the city centre via a tram line that ran from Ginnheim along Raimundstraße to Dornbusch.

Building unit Mavest:

Construction management: Materialien- und Auftragsvermittlungsstelle GmbH

Construction period: 1926–1929

Architect: Franz Roeckle

Building unit for I.G. Farben employees:

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen (ABG)

Construction period: 1930

Architects: Ernst May and Carl-Hermann Rudloff

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 243f.
