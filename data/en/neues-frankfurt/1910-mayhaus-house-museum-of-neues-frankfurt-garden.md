# mayhaus - House museum of Neues Frankfurt + garden

*Im Burgfeld 136*

![Street view of the mayhaus, Römerstadt housing estate.](../../images/fcbc613ef68d75443086eb1d0cc48779_original.jpg)
*2013 — Author: Reinhard Wegmann | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Street view of the mayhaus, Römerstadt housing estate.*

Since 2010, the ernst-may-haus in the Römerstadt housing estate has offered the unique opportunity to experience a terraced house of the Neues Frankfurt in the condition in which it was built around 1928. The original colour scheme of the housing estate was used for the restoration. Including a basement, a kitchen, and a bathroom, the two-storey, low-rise building offers a living space of 88 square metres. The rooms are furnished with functional period furniture. The built-in Frankfurt kitchen, which is the only one that can still be viewed in its functional context, is one major attraction. Behind the house, there is a terrace and a garden, which both have been reconstructed according to original plans as well. They were used for growing fruit and vegetables, as well as for recreation. The ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. is the initiator and operator of the house museum.

Construction management: Mietheim AG

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architectural adaptation: Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff, Albert Winter, et al.

Interior design/furniture: Ferdinand Kramer, Franz Schuster, Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky

Colour concept: Hans Leistikow

Horticultural design: Leberecht Migge

Further reading: 
Christina Treutlein, Philipp Sturm (eds.), MAYHAUS. Das Musterhaus des Neuen Frankfurt, Frankfurt am Main 2021.
