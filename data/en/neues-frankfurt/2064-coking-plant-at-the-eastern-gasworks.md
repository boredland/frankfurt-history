# Coking plant at the eastern gasworks

*Schielestraße 29-30*

![Coal silo of the coking plant at the eastern gasworks](../../images/142cf5e94f35d003082611c9954c9c67_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Max Göllner | Description: Coal silo of the coking plant at the eastern gasworks*

As the population of Frankfurt grew in the 1920s, productivity in the energy sector had to be increased. Thus, it was decided to expand the gasworks at the eastern harbour (buildings by Peter Behrens, 1911–1912) to include a coal silo, a grinding plant, coal containers, conveyor belts, and trolleys. The architect Adolf Meyer, one of Behrens’s former students, was entrusted with this task. Meyer used different materials for each of the buildings to symbolise the respective task of the individual building in the production process. While the container structures were made of reinforced concrete, the machine rooms were made of clinker, and the conveyor belts and trolleys were made of iron framework. The coking plant was destroyed in 1945. Today, some of Peter Behrens’s buildings have been preserved.

Construction management: Frankfurter Gasgesellschaft

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architect: Adolf Meyer, Anatol Selmer
