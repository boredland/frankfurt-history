# Ebelfeld school (since 1955) / formerly named: Hindenburg school, Praunheim school

*Praunheimer Hohl 4*

![Lessons in the open-air classroom.](../../images/7be5cf2a7177b6b759a88da8e31c5e7a_original.jpg)
*around 1927 — Author: Institut für Stadtgeschichte, Frankfurt am Main | License: unbekannt / unknown | Description: Lessons in the open-air classroom.*

The new housing estates also urgently needed schools. In Praunheim, however, the building was realised late and only in a reduced form due to cost savings. It followed the progressive concept of an open-space school in close contact with nature. Three single pavilions and one double pavilion, each with three classes, were arranged parallel to each other. Between them were gardens to which the classrooms could be opened. This meant that lessons could also take place outdoors using movable furniture. Each pavilion had one additional storey on its north side. The planned administration wing was never built. It would have created a connection across the street to a gymnasium and a gymnastics terrace, which were added after 1945. The primary school, which was renovated in 2016–2019, is a listed building today.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1931

Architects: Eugen Kaufmann, Willi Pullmann

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 46-47.
