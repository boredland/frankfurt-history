# Hoechst AG technical administration building

*Brüningstraße*

![From the book by Walter Müller-Wolckow: Bauten der Arbeit und des Verkehrs](../../images/fd3207ac09656be3429a39237d0416fc_original.jpg)
*1924 — License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: From the book by Walter Müller-Wolckow: Bauten der Arbeit und des Verkehrs*

The Hoechst AG administration building, built between 1920 and 1924 according to plans by Peter Behrens, is one of the most important buildings of expressionist architecture. Two wide ribbons of differently coloured clinker bricks – red brick and purple iron clinker – run around the structure, emphasising the horizontal extension. With its length of 168 metres, the street front is divided into two office wings, a transverse wing with a tower, and a connecting bridge to the main office built in 1893. The tower with its belfry is a striking feature while the arched window also characterises the building. The entrance and reception hall is particularly impressive: eight pillars on a triangular floor plan rise up like stalactites. Their colour scheme – including deep blue and red – and three light domes give the room a solemn, almost sacral effect.

Construction management: Hoechst AG

Construction period: 1920–1924

Architect: Peter Behrens

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 119-123.
