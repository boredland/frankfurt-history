# Römerstadt housing estate

*Hadrianstraße 18*

![](../../images/0513f016df1fe5a21a5633ae6966cb0b_original.jpg)

The name of the Römerstadt housing estate refers to historical events, its design is adapted to the landscape: the remains of the ancient Roman town of Nida lie beneath the housing estate while the course of the streets follows the contour lines of the terrain rising gently from the Nidda river. As a result, the eastern streets are curved, whereas the streets in the western part of the housing estate are linear. A fortress-like concrete wall with semi-circular bastions marks the boundary between the housing estate and the Nidda valley. Below the eastern bastion wall, there are self-sufficiency allotment gardens, which could be cultivated by the residents of the multi-dwelling units. Gardens were also laid out between all the rows of single-family homes. Hadrianstraße is at the centre of the housing estate. There is a school (architects: Martin Elsaesser, Wilhelm Schütte) located there, as well as two striking apartment blocks: the block of flats designed by architect Franz Schuster to the south and the front building with its curved facade, porthole windows, and shops on the ground floor, planned by Carl-Hermann Rudloff to the north. In the 1970s, the famous housing estate was divided by the Rosa-Luxemburg-Straße.

Construction management: Mietheim AG

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Wolfgang Bangert (planning); Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff, Karl Blattner, Gottlob Schaupp and Franz Schuster (architectural adaptation)

Horticultural design: Leberecht Migge

Further reading: Julius Reinsberg: Siedlung Römerstadt. In: Neuer Mensch, Neue Wohnung. Die Bauten des Neuen Frankfurt 1925–1933. Ed. by Wolfgang Voigt, Dorothea Deschermeier, and Peter Cachola Schmal. Berlin 2019, p. 120f.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
