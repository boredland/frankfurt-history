# Am Lindenbaum housing estate

*Unter den Ulmen 16*

![View of a row of houses](../../images/924bc033d55b1e6117f398aaf6d9ae1c_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1930, Heft 4/5, S. 89 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: View of a row of houses*

Surprisingly, this housing estate in Eschersheim, designed by Walter Gropius, is relatively unknown. Located behind a 300-year-old lime tree and the Ludwig Richter school, four residential blocks are staggered between the streets Am Lindenbaum and Hinter den Ulmen. The buildings arranged transversely between them create a U-shaped block, whose green courtyards are open to the south. Another block adjoins the structure to the northeast. The facades above the reddish brick base were originally white, the window frames painted a delicate yellow. A total of 198 flats with rather small floor plans each have a loggia or balcony. Unlike the Frankfurt kitchen, the kitchens here offer space for a dining area. The housing estate, which now belongs to the housing company Vonovia, has been a listed ensemble since 2017.

Construction management: Gemeinnützige Aktiengesellschaft für Angestellten-Heimstätten (GAGFAH)

Construction period: 1930

Architect: Walter Gropius

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 293.

*Author: Ulrike May*
