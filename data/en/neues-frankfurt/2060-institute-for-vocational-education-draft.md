# Institute for vocational education - draft

*Tilly-Edinger-Platz/Georg-Voigt-Straße*

Martin Elsaesser planned an educational institution for industrial teachers that would have been located on the site of today’s “Senckenberg Biodiversity and Climate Research Centre”. With its nearly triangular floor plan, the unrealised design, of which two versions exist, takes into account the sharp-angled construction site. A large lecture hall was located on the first floor in a “round tip”, facing what was then Königsplatz. The following cross sections were staggered in their height towards the east up to a five-storey closing-off structure, where the main entrance was also located. In addition to the vocational classrooms, such as a teaching kitchen and a carpentry workshop, there were smaller lecture halls, as well as a terrace, an inner courtyard, and a garden.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Draft: 1931

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
