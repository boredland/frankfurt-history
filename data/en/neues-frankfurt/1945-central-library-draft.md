# Central library - draft

*Senckenberganlage / Mertonstraße*

![Drawing of the street view.](../../images/c5ad65df553661b955a1f601d0e37f42_original.jpg)
*1929 — Author: Martin Elsaesser | License: Martin Elsaesser: Bauten und Entwürfe aus den Jahren 1924–1932, Berlin 1933, S. 329. | Description: Drawing of the street view.*

At the site of the Juridicum building of Goethe University Frankfurt in Bockenheim, a central library was to be built, uniting various Frankfurt libraries. For economic reasons, however, it was never realised. Martin Elsaesser’s design envisaged a large block of buildings along what was then Viktoria-Allee, dominated by a 25-storey book depository. An adjoining annexe building bridged Mertonstraße and ended in a cantilevered, presumably glazed semicircle. The building offered reading rooms, depositories, and workshops, as well as administration, lecture, and exhibition rooms. An imposing, round university auditorium was also integrated. The modern library building would have been located directly next to the Institute for Social Research, which was built in 1923/24.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Draft: 1929

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
