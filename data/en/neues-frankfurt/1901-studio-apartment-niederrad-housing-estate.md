# Studio apartment, Niederrad housing estate

*Haardtwaldstraße 2*

![Frontal view of the block of houses on Haardtwaldplatz. Tower building at Haardtwaldstraße 2 on the left.](../../images/532ca58514de3c226203440d2152e4c4_original.jpg)
*1927 — Author: Nicht genannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Frontal view of the block of houses on Haardtwaldplatz. Tower building at Haardtwaldstraße 2 on the left.*

The tower building at Haardtwaldstraße 2 is a prominent example of Neues Frankfurt architecture. In contrast to many housing estates built at the same time, architect Carl-Hermann Rudloff experimented with the volume of the buildings here in Niederrad. Originally, a second, similar tower was planned on the corner of Trifelsstraße and Kalmitstraße to mark the corners of Haardtwaldplatz. However, since the school that was planned there was not realised, the tower remained a solitary structure. Its bay window, which conceals a two-storey studio, is particularly striking. In the days of Neues Frankfurt, this was the study of Jewish professor of pathology Philipp Schwartz, who also helped thousands of scholars and scientists to flee Germany after his escape in 1933 with his “Notgemeinschaft Deutscher Wissenschaftler im Ausland” (“Emergency Association of German Scientists Abroad”).

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architects: Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff

Further reading: Brigitte Dippold-Theile: Siedlung Bruchfeldstraße / Zick-Zackhausen, 2005, www.ernst-may-gesellschaft.de/fileadmin/Redakteure/Seiten_Anlagen/DNF/Wohnsiedlungen/Bruchfeldstr/Zick_Zackhausen_A4.pdf, (accessed on 12.10.2024).
