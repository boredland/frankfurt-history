# Transformer station North

*Eschersheimer Landstraße 147*

![Transformer station at Eschersheimer Landstraße](../../images/e654d06ddc32e7790f525d9eae4aad35_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Max Cetto-Archiv, Deutsches Architekturmuseum, Frankfurt am Main | Description: Transformer station at Eschersheimer Landstraße*

In the second half of the 1920s, the demand for electricity in Germany rose drastically. Reasons for this were the progressive electrification of households, industry, and commerce, as well as the increase in tram traffic. However, Frankfurt’s electricity grid was unable to cope with this growth. Transformer stations were needed to connect the higher voltage that was required now to the existing distribution network. For this purpose, Max Cetto designed a timeless building with a striking facade. Three dominant window ribbons stretch across the entire length and one corner of the structure. They become lower towards the top, making the building seem taller. The appearance of this elegant industrial building, which has been unjustly overlooked, has hardly changed over the course of almost 100 years.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1930

Architect: Max Cetto
