# Goldstein housing estate

*Schüttenhelmweg 7*

![Aerial view of the housing estate with the eponymous Goldstein estate at the centre of the picture.](../../images/9ff08fbcb9ce4548890123e142f194cb_original.jpg)
*1937 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of the housing estate with the eponymous Goldstein estate at the centre of the picture.*

The Goldstein project around an old estate was initially planned for employees of the Höchst and Griesheim industries. Designed in 1929 as the largest housing estate project for 35,000 people, its realisation was initially delayed by the global economic crisis. It was not until 1932, after another architectural competition, that the project was realised in a very simplified form as a provisional housing estate for unemployed families. Although the modified development plan still reveals influences of Neues Frankfurt, the semi-detached houses with pent or pitched roofs and the agrarian way of life on the outskirts of the city seem like a step backwards. Praised by the National Socialists, who came to power in 1933, the project gives a first impression of the development of housing estates in the period that followed.

Greater project Goldstein housing estate (not realised)

Construction management: Gartenstadt A.G.

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Walter Schwagenscheidt, Erich Mauthner

 

Goldstein housing estate for the unemployed

Construction management: Gemeinnützige Wohnungsbau AG, Hellerhof AG, Nassauische Heimstätte
