# Lohrpark

![The paddling pool in the children’s recreation garden.](../../images/086df03e40fd9893a9cbf3f7cbc53c22_original.jpg)
*1927 — Author: Unbekannt | Description: The paddling pool in the children’s recreation garden.*

The Lohrpark on the Lohrberg was mainly designed by Max Bromme between 1924 and 1930. After Carl Heicke, head of the city’s horticultural department, had begun initial works in 1919, Bromme completed the site to create a versatile public park. To the south of the permanent allotment gardens, he created an extensive playing area, crossed by an alley that ends in a tree-lined viewing roundel. The place offers impressive views of the Taunus, Wetterau, Spessart, and Vogelsberg heights. To the south-east, Bromme created a children’s recreation garden with a paddling pool, a merry-go-round, and a “sand bath”. Alongside meadows and allotment gardens, these elements characterise the 18-hectare park, which has been a listed monument since 1986.

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 210f.
