# Nonnenpfad housing estate

*Nonnenpfad 16-51*

![Street view of the Nonnenpfad housing estate.](../../images/8510a202a4e41b2160f181a0a6383ba1_original.jpg)
*around 1935 — Author: Hans Beckstein | License: 25 Jahre Gartenstadt-Gesellschaft Frankfurt am Main A.G. 1910-1935, herausgegeben vom Vorstand der Gesellschaft, Februar 1935, S. 54 | Description: Street view of the Nonnenpfad housing estate.*

The Nonnenpfad housing estate in Oberrad comprises two rows of houses along the street of the same name, as well as multi-storey houses on what is now Melanchthonplatz. The 78 apartments that have been built represent only a part of the original plan, which was to be continued to the east and west, as well as on Kirchplatz. Two types of single-family terraced houses with gardens at the rear are located along the steep street. The slightly wider type of houses also has granny flats and roof terraces on the upper floor with a view of the private gardens. Starting in 1995, the low-rise buildings made of brickwork were offered for sale to their tenants by ABG Frankfurt Holding GmbH. This privatisation has had a significant influence on the current appearance of the housing estate.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architect: Friedrich Sander
