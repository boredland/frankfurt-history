# People’s house Praunheim - draft

*Damaschkeanger 156*

![Model of the people’s house Praunheim](../../images/66143d7327aebfbee75937d3863aa1c1_original.jpg)
*1928 — Author: Paul Wolff | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1928, Heft 7/8, S. 132 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Model of the people’s house Praunheim*

A people’s house was planned for a central location in the Praunheim housing estate. The unrealised multifunctional building was intended to strengthen the neighbourhood community as a meeting and event space. The floor plan is shaped like a circle segment. A roofed colonnade leads visitors into the curved entrance area. They then pass through a lobby into a large hall. At its apex, there is a stage in front of the rows of seats arranged in a fan shape. Additional office spaces would have complemented the building. On the outside, the structure is characterised by the staggered heights of the building sections. Today, instead of the people’s house, the church of Christ the King is located on the bend of Damaschkeanger.

Draft: 1928 

Architect: Max Cetto

Further reading:

Das Neue Frankfurt, vol. 2, 1928, issue 7/8, p. 132 and vol. 4, 1930, issue 2/3, p. 45.
