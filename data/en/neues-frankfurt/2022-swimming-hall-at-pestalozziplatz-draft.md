# Swimming hall at Pestalozziplatz - draft

*Pestalozziplatz 1*

![Draft sketch for swimming hall at Pestalozziplatz, exterior view](../../images/d1ed986d45748e83f22631a80bdc78fa_original.jpg)
*1926 — Author: Carl-Hermann Rudloff | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Draft sketch for swimming hall at Pestalozziplatz, exterior view*

A circular swimming hall – that was architect Carl-Hermann Rudloff's marvellous idea for Pestalozziplatz. But as the city could not provide sufficient funding, it was never built. Almost everything in the building was supposed to be round. From the entrance area, a spiral corridor leads upwards along the outer wall past the changing rooms. After changing their clothes, visitors are led back down to the shower rooms on a corridor past the lockers. Only after showering do people reach the round swimming pool in the centre of the building. The swimming hall would have been very colourful with red and blue walls, green lockers and changing rooms, as well as a yellow-golden ceiling. The heating system in the basement of the swimming hall would also have supplied the neighbouring residential buildings with heat.

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen

Draft: 1926

Architect: Carl-Hermann Rudloff
