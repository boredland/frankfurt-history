# Church of the Holy Cross

*Kettelerallee 45*

![View of the church of the Holy Cross.](../../images/8e7c98af3031847325eec4a44faef17b_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: View of the church of the Holy Cross.*

The church of the Holy Cross, designed by Martin Weber as the “crown” of the Bornheim housing estate, is a cubic building with a steel frame construction. A large flight of steps leads to the entrance of the church, whose interior is one level above the street. Its tower, 25 metres high, is particularly striking. It has four protruding beams, which support the belfry inside and symbolise the four evangelists. Winged creatures bear the heads of a human, a lion, a bull, and an eagle. The highlight: the shadows of the sculptures, which are designed to have an oblique effect, move on the white surface with the position of the sun, but are identical in their shadow outlines despite the different heads. The church has been a listed building since 1986.

Construction management: Catholic St Joseph’s parish, supported by Bonifatius-Verein

Construction period: 1928–1929

Architect: Martin Weber

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 176-180.
