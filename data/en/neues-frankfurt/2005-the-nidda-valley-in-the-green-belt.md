# The Nidda Valley in the green belt

*Niddapark*

![The Nidda valley with a view of the Römerstadt housing estate.](../../images/3446f74d62a3d468091be3f76a9e7af2_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Privat | Description: The Nidda valley with a view of the Römerstadt housing estate.*

Like a ring, the green belt runs around Frankfurt’s city centre. Protected by the “Green Belt Constitution” (1991), numerous green spaces are located next to each other from the city forest in the south, as well as various parks, to the Nidda park in the north. The idea of the green belt can be traced back to the 1920s. It was a crucial element of Neues Frankfurt. In accordance with the satellite town system, new residential districts were constructed in the periphery. Thus, the housing estates Westhausen, Praunheim, Römerstadt, and Höhenblick were created around the Nidda valley – this was the so-called Nidda valley project. The entire area is used for a variety of purposes. In addition to the housing estates, there are park-like arrangements, sports grounds and playgrounds, permanent allotments, and areas reserved to intensive agriculture.

The Nidda valley is also characterised by an intensive infrastructural use. Since 1850, the Main-Weser railway line cuts through the valley. After the construction of the Nordweststadt housing estate in the 1960s, the Rosa-Luxemburg-Straße crosses the Nidda valley since 1980.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: from 1925
