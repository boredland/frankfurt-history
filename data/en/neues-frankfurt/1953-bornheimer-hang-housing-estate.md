# Bornheimer Hang housing estate

*Wittelsbacherallee 157*

![](../../images/45ca380e05926bbffa2e87d3bfae9b36_original.jpg)

The housing estate was built on the urban expansion area of the 19th century, which led to two special features: At Pestalozziplatz, where it adjoins the existing buildings from the early 1920s, the building regulations required the multi-dwelling units to have pitched roofs – in contrast to the flat roofs typical of Neues Frankfurt. The urban character of the housing estate is equally untypical. At the intersection of Saalburgallee and Wittelsbacherallee, it consistently takes up the block development of the Wilhelminian era and welcomes us with a wide, tree-lined main road. Its slight curve leads to Martin Weber’s church of the Holy Cross (Heilig-Kreuz-Kirche) – the “crown” of the housing estate that can be seen from afar. The housing estate itself ends close to the upper edge of the Bornheim slope, thus adapting perfectly to the natural boundary of the site. Only in the inner area of the block development, 48 single-family terraced houses were built in Karl-Albert-Straße.

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen (ABG)

Construction period: 1925–1929

Planning: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm; architects: Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff, Adolf Aßmann (architectural adaptation); Martin Weber (church);

Design of green spaces: Max Bromme, Ulrich Wolf (architectural adaptation)

Further reading: DW Dreysse: May-Siedlungen. Architekturführer durch zehn Siedlungen des Neuen Frankfurt 1926–1930. 4th ed., Köln 2019, p. 28-31.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
