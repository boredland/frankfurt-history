# St Joseph’s church

*Eichwaldstraße 37*

![View of St Joseph’s church.](../../images/bf304536fcfad79f9f0bca279828097a_original.jpg)
*around 1932 — Author: Unbekannt | License: privat | Description: View of St Joseph’s church.*

St Joseph’s church in Bornheim was extended in 1931–1932 by architects Hans and Christoph Rummel. In order to enlarge the space, they removed the south-east wall of the existing church and added a new nave, stretching it as far as Berger Straße at a right angle. The striking tower with a height of 32 metres is aligned with the street and adorned with a statue of Saint Joseph with the infant Jesus. This extension not only created more space and public visibility, but also made it possible to create a quiet inner courtyard, which can be entered through three arcades. The harmonious transition between the old church and the new building, which is enhanced by Gothicised details such as pointed arches, is particularly congenial. During the renovation in 2013, the church was painted a vibrant shade of red.

Construction management: St Joseph’s parish, Frankfurt am Main

Construction period (extension): 1931–1932

Architects (extension): Hans and Christoph Rummel

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 184ff.
