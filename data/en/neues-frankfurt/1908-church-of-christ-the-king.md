# Church of Christ the King

*Damaschkeanger 156*

![Church of Christ the King in the Praunheim housing estate](../../images/a2e84bfe3f1d39541fa70ce6f09a0345_original.jpg)
*Unknown — Author: Unbekannr | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Church of Christ the King in the Praunheim housing estate*

A people’s house was originally planned for a central location in the Praunheim housing estate, but this was not realised due to economic reasons. Instead, the growing Catholic congregation erected a provisional church there. In a very short time, a box-shaped, plastered and half-timbered building without a bell tower was erected, blending in with the architecture of the housing estate. The lettering “Christo Regi” and a central cross adorned the facade. The slightly elevated church was entered through a simple wooden vestibule. Inside, light streams in through an almost completely glazed wall. To the opposite side, there is a gallery, lit by a narrow horizontal window ribbon. In the post-war period, the church was remodelled and redesigned several times. A mosaic by Hans Leistikow and a bell frame were added to the exterior.

Construction management: Limburg diocese

Construction period: 1930 

Architects: Martin Weber (1931), Alois Giefer and Hermann Mäckler (1956), Hans Busch (1975)

Further reading: www.marien-frankfurt.de/kirchen-und-orte/christ-koenig

Adrian Seib, Der Kirchenbaumeister Martin Weber (1890–1941). Leben und Werk eines Architekten für die liturgische Erneuerung, Mainz 1999.
