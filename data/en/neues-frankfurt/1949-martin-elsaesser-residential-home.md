# Martin Elsaesser residential home

*Im Höhenblick 37*

![View of the rear of the residential building with terrace, balcony, and roof terrace.](../../images/790f624ee872abea3fdbeda565a9fa85_original.jpg)
*1926 — Author: Hermann Collischonn | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: View of the rear of the residential building with terrace, balcony, and roof terrace.*

Martin Elsaesser’s residential building constitutes the architectural completion of the Höhenblick housing estate in the direction of the Nidda valley. Elsaesser saw his house as a programmatic contribution, but set himself apart from the housing estate in terms of architectural language, despite the flat roof. With its expressionist details and characterised by mullioned windows, the brick building looks like a fortress. Corners reinforced with buttresses and a second, squat upper floor for guest rooms and domestic staff reinforce the closed impression. Nevertheless, Elsaesser demonstrated his commitment to modernity by integrating a garage, a spacious roof terrace, and a high radio aerial. Today, a new structure blocks the view of the listed building, which was restored in 2002 and currently serves as the residence of the Swiss consul general.

Construction management: Martin Elsaesser

Construction period: 1925

Architect: Martin Elsaesser 

Further reading: Christina Gräwe: Haus Elsaesser 1926. In: Martin Elsaesser und das Neue Frankfurt. Ed. by Elsaesser/Gräwe/Schilling/Schmal. Tübingen, Berlin 2009, p. 92-97.
