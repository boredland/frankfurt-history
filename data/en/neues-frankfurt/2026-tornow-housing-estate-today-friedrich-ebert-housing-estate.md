# Tornow housing estate (today: Friedrich Ebert housing estate)

*Mainzer Landstraße 415*

![Aerial view of the housing estate.](../../images/3c8799038530a73396280c847958b602_original.jpg)
*around 1939 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of the housing estate.*

The Friedrich Ebert housing estate in the Gallus district was intended to create living space for poor working-class families with many children, but initially only micro-flats were built in multi-storey terraced buildings. Their north-south orientation and long ribbon windows ensured that the small rooms were well lit. The furniture and equipment, on the other hand, were sparse: a Frankfurt kitchen in a reduced form, no central facilities, and no balconies. Green spaces between the blocks provided public open spaces, which were shielded from street noise by the single-family detached houses on Mainzer Landstraße. Construction on the housing estate continued even after 1933. After 90 percent of it was damaged in the Second World War, it was rebuilt in a simplified form. Since 2020, passive houses have replaced the multi-storey buildings of the first construction phase.

Construction management: Gartenstadt AG

Construction period: 1930–1932 (first and second phase)

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Walter Schwagenscheidt (planning); Ernst May, Walter Schwagenscheidt, Walter Kratz, Albert Winter (architectural adaptation)

Further reading: 

Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 292.
