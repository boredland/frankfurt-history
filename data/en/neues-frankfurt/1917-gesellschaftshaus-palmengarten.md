# Gesellschaftshaus Palmengarten

*Palmengartenstraße 11*

![View of the Gesellschaftshaus](../../images/f99f71771f7e0f18b1c5bb58f03cb879_original.jpg)
*Unknown — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: View of the Gesellschaftshaus*

The “Gesellschaftshaus Palmengarten” is one of the few projects in which Ernst May and Martin Elsaesser worked together. Originally built in 1871 in the Wilhelminian style and featuring a magnificent ballroom, the building underwent a radical transformation in 1929. From then on, the old met the Neues Frankfurt here: cubic structures with flat roofs were built in front of the historicist building, including a pergola with a height of ten metres, made of concrete pillars, which adorned the now white facade and created space for climbing plants. The semi-elliptical “wedding room” on the west side is just as striking. Further construction phases were not realised due to financial constraints. The resulting hybrid building was restored from 2009 to 2013.

Construction management: Palmengarten AG

Construction period (remodelling/extension): 1929

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Werner Hebebrand, Ernst May

Further reading: Jörg Schilling: Gesellschaftshaus Palmengarten, martin-elsaesser-bauhefte 01, Hamburg 2012.
