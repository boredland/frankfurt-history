# Residential buildings of the mental hospital

*Heinrich-Hoffmann-Straße 2*

![Southern side, facing the garden, of the residential buildings for hospital employees.](../../images/06bfa70e1962bbde6020ab0ac42f392f_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Hermann Collischonn | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung / Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: Southern side, facing the garden, of the residential buildings for hospital employees.*

When the mental hospital was built on the grounds of the university hospital, residential buildings were also planned for its staff. On Heinrich-Hoffmann-Straße, parallel to the main front of the mental hospital, two rows of residential buildings were erected, originally with two or three storeys. Between them, the street leads directly to the main entrance of the hospital. The lower buildings flanked this passageway almost like a gate. Today, the residential buildings have been heavily modified and extended. The semi-circular balconies facing the small green area behind the rows of buildings are still part of Martin Elsaesser’s design.

Construction management: Gemeinnützige Siedlungsgesellschaft mbH Frankfurt 

Construction period: 1928–1930

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Walter Körte

Further reading: www.martin-elsaesser.de/projekte/wohngebaeude-der-klinik-fuer-gemuets-und-nervenkranke-frankfurt-am-main/
