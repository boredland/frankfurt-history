# Sommerhoff park

*Gutleutstraße 315*

![Day nursery in Sommerhoff park. Construction site of the test authority centre 6 (Prüfamt 6) in Gutleutstraße in the background.](../../images/b6f6dc039025e4ea970012b8b9f32b78_original.jpg)
*around 1928 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Day nursery in Sommerhoff park. Construction site of the test authority centre 6 (Prüfamt 6) in Gutleutstraße in the background.*

Sommerhoff park is a hidden gem in an industrialised environment. In the 19th century, there was a country estate here, with a manor house in an English garden. In 1928, the city took over the estate and had it remodelled by horticultural director Max Bromme. School gardens, a paddling pool, playgrounds, as well as a nursery designed by Adolf Meyer including changing rooms, washrooms, a kitchen, and a lounge for the manager, were built on the site. Children from working-class families were to be looked after here all day in the summer. The manor house was destroyed during the Second World War and the park was later modified. A retirement home was built here in 1952. The pavilion on Gutleutstraße was converted and used as a daycare centre until 2020. It is now to be listed as a historical monument and put to a new use (as of March 2025).

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architects: Max Bromme, Otto Valentin (horticultural design), Adolf Meyer in cooperation with August Brauneck, Hans Craemer (pavilion) 

Further reading: 

Annemarie Jaeggi, Adolf Meyer – Der zweite Mann, Berlin 1994, p. 359-360.
