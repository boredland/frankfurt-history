# Inauguration of the new “Old Bridge”

*Alte Brücke*

![Boat parade for the inauguration of the new “Old Bridge”](../../images/1b24837497ba5dbc7ec49728a8e89be1_original.jpg)
*15-8-1926 — Author: Unbekannr | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Boat parade for the inauguration of the new “Old Bridge”*

For centuries, the “Old Bridge” was the only connection between the two banks of the Main river in Frankfurt. Due to the changing requirements of road traffic and shipping, plans for a new bridge were made as early as the end of the 19th century. However, it was not until 1926 that the new “Old Bridge” was inaugurated. High-ranking guests were invited to the celebrations spanning three days in August. Magnificent street parades, as well as a boat parade, attracted thousands of spectators to the river. The artist Oskar Schlemmer was engaged for a “bridge revue” in the festival hall, incorporating figures from his now famous “Triadic Ballet” into the performance. In autumn, the “Bund tätige Altstadtfreunde” (society of friends of the old city centre) published an illustrated commemorative publication, the cover of which was designed by Hans Leistikow. The appearance of the bridge has changed considerably over the last 100 years.

Inauguration: 1926

Architects: Franz Heberer, Franz von Hoven, Karl Leonhard
