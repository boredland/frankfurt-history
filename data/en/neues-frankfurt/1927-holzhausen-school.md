# Holzhausen school

*Bremer Straße 25*

![Aerial view of Holzhausen school. At a closer look, a circle of pupils can be seen in the inner courtyard.](../../images/2beecb2c5c1fea156614ff0d58c6ebea_original.jpg)
*around 1927 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of Holzhausen school. At a closer look, a circle of pupils can be seen in the inner courtyard.*

The functional four-storey building on Eschersheimer Landstraße has a flush design in order to arrange the classrooms towards the western side, which is away from the noise of the street. A single-storey singing hall is adjoined to the north side at a right angle. Two annexe buildings follow on the south side, also at a 90-degree angle. The symmetrical corners of the main wing were opened up with large windows, giving the building a bright appearance. The southern staircase is another special feature: two flights of stairs, winding into each other, separate the ascent and descent so that the streams of pupils do not cross each other. Outdoor lessons were held on the partially accessible flat roof. The school, which has been a listed building since 2010, is currently being renovated and extended.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1927–1929

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Walter Körte

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 43-44.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
