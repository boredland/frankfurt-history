# Hessestraße housing estate

*Georg-Wolff-Straße 1-41*

![View of Georg-Wolff-Straße with shops at the opening, around 1931.](../../images/84483644a61f52f7009fb7db5fe87bb3_original.jpg)
*around 1931 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Privatsammlung | Description: View of Georg-Wolff-Straße with shops at the opening, around 1931.*

The small housing estate consists of 38 four-room flats and two shops. The shops form a gateway to a straight street, with flat-roofed single-family houses lining the sides. The street widens towards the north and is visually closed off by four houses positioned at a right angle. Only a narrow street leads back to Hessestraße. There is a garden at the rear of each house. The houses were built for members of a war veterans’ association (Reichsbund der Kriegsbeschädigten, Kriegsteilnehmer und -hinterbliebenen) and were allocated according to homestead law.

Construction management: Nassauische Heimstätte

Construction period: 1929–1931

Architect: Johann Petry

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 290.

*Author: Christina Treutlein*
