# The Brentanobad outdoor swimming pool and recreational site in Brentano Park

*Rödelheimer Parkweg 13, 60489 Frankfurt am Main*

![Aerial view of Brentanobad](../../images/4482d5b61cf5b355a174458d28b45eca_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: unbekannt / unknown | License: Privat | Description: Aerial view of Brentanobad*

Once in private ownership, the Stumpf-Brentano Park became the property of the city of Frankfurt in 1926. It was located on the Nidda river and underwent some horticultural changes when the river was regulated. In this context, a spacious outdoor swimming pool was created in the north-eastern part of the site. As spending time outdoors was considered highly important during that period, other existing river baths in Hausen, Praunheim, and Eschersheim were also redesigned at the same time. With its extensive sunbathing lawns, changing rooms, and a restaurant with a terrace, the Brentanobad was particularly popular. A canal branching off from the Nidda supplied the water for the swimming pool with its size of 2.5 hectares. In the 1960s, however, the water quality had deteriorated to such an extent that the basin had to be separated from the river.

Construction management: Municipal Horticultural Office

Construction period: 1928–1930

Architects: Adolf Meyer (cooperation by August Brauneck)
