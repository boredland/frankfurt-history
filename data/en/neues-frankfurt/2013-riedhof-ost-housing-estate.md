# Riedhof-Ost housing estate

*Mörfelder Landstraße, Stresemannallee, Riedhofweg*

![Model of the Riedhof-Ost housing estate](../../images/635159c9209178d475581414ca43da06_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1930, Heft 4/5, S. 116 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Model of the Riedhof-Ost housing estate*

The Riedhof project was planned as a large housing estate in Frankfurt-Sachsenhausen, centred around a former grange (Riedhof). However, as part of Neues Frankfurt, only the Riedhof-West housing estate (Heimatsiedlung, planned: 850 flats) to the west of Stresemannallee and the smaller Riedhof-Ost housing estate (planned: 253 flats) to the north of Mörfelder Landstraße were actually built. Turned at a 90-degree angle, there are long rows of houses with quiet courtyards behind the two or three-storey row of houses (with an additional storey added) that are lined along the busy street. The flats with two to three and a half rooms were to be equipped with a Frankfurt kitchen and central heating.

Construction management: SÜWAG Südwestdeutsche A.G. für Kleinwohnungsbauten

Construction period: 1930–1931

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Fritz Berke, Nassauische Heimstätte (architectural adaptation) 

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 284.

*Author: Florian Wehrle*
