# Höhenblick housing estate

*Höhenblick 1*

![](../../images/9aca9b8870fe0cc95cdbaab5e277086f_original.jpg)

The Höhenblick housing estate, with a total of just around 100 buildings, mostly single-family houses, is the smallest unit within the Nidda valley project. Compared to the much larger Römerstadt, Praunheim, and Westhausen housing estates, it also occupies a special position due to its upscale amenities, its apartment sizes (up to 125 square meters), and its exclusive location. At the eastern end of Kurhessenstraße, it welcomes us with two tower-like corner buildings. At its western end, it opens up to the Nidda valley and offers a view of the Taunus heights. The centre of the housing estate is formed by a broad intersection lined with row houses, all of which have gardens and roof terraces. 

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen (ABG)

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architects: Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff

Further reading: Dorothea Deschermeier: Siedlung Höhenblick. In: Neuer Mensch, Neue Wohnung. Die Bauten des Neuen Frankfurt 1925–1933. Ed. by Wolfgang Voigt, Dorothea Deschermeier, and Peter Cachola Schmal. Berlin 2019, p. 104-107.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
