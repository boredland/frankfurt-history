# Construction unit Denzerstraße/Sauerstraße

*Sauerstraße 7-15, Denzerstraße 4-22*

![Residential building in Sauerstraße](../../images/dc23fecb59c2727a58febf02c676dcfa_original.jpg)
*2023 — Author: Christina Treutlein | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Residential building in Sauerstraße*

Nied was incorporated on 1 April 1928 and has been a district of Frankfurt ever since. The incorporation agreement stipulated the construction of modern flats, bringing the urban atmosphere of the Neues Frankfurt to the former village. The first 26 flats were built in Sauerstraße. At the centre of the row of two-storey houses, where the street curves slightly, there is a building with four three-room flats. Adjacent to the right and left of this house, there is a total of eleven terraced houses, each with two flatlets (37.6 square metres). The row of three-storey houses in Denzerstraße comprises 42 flats, each with two or three rooms. All of the houses had a slate-covered pitched roof with dormers. The houses are not listed buildings.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main and Nassauische Heimstätte

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Herbert Boehm (planning), Eugen Kaufmann (architecture)

Further reading:

Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 288.
