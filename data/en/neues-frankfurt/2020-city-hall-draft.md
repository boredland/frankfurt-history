# City hall – draft

*Römer Römerberg 23*

![Sketch for a high-rise city hall](../../images/fb3ca5c0afd1065c1c89805fc88f0a67_original.jpg)
*Unknown — Author: Martin Elsaesser | License: Martin Elsaesser: Bauten und Entwürfe aus den Jahren 1924–1932, Berlin 1933 | Description: Sketch for a high-rise city hall*

The Neues Frankfurt almost would have become part of today’s city skyline. Martin Elsaesser’s “Sketch for a high-rise city hall”, published in 1933, shows a visionary design for a 26-storey building on a cross-shaped ground plan. The four wings of the building merge into eight-storey projections. At the centre of the designed structure, there is a glass staircase, whose protruding corners underline the futuristic aspirations of the construction. The draft, without being assigned a defined location, was intended to unite several municipal offices. However, Elsaesser’s vision was not only functional, but it can also be interpreted as a statement of urban planning: Frankfurt’s skyline, characterised by medieval structures, was to be given a modern “crown”.

Bauherrschaft: City of Frankfurt am Main

Draft: 1930

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
