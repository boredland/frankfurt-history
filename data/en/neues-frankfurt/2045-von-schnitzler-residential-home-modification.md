# Von Schnitzler residential home modification

*Westendstraße 41*

![The dining room, redesigned by Elsaesser, with a view of the garden](../../images/e485e93a717a21c39048d86e937b7a1c_original.jpg)
*around 1927 — Author: Waldemar Titzenthaler | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung | Description: The dining room, redesigned by Elsaesser, with a view of the garden*

The entrepreneur Georg von Schnitzler was involved in the merger of several chemical companies to form the I.G. Farben trust in 1925. The previous year, he and his wife Lilly, an art collector and patron of Max Beckmann, had acquired a patrician house from the 1870s. Their commission for a remodelling went to Martin Elsaesser, who designed the interior as a “synthesis of old and new”, with functional austerity and as a presentation area for collected art and fine furniture. The courtyard garden was also redesigned. In the following years, the villa became an important Frankfurt meeting place for celebrities and cultural exchange. The house was destroyed during the Second World War. Having been a member of the board of I.G. Farben from 1926 to 1945, Georg von Schnitzler was later convicted of war crimes.

Construction management: Georg and Lilly von Schnitzler

Construction period (modifications): 1926–1927

Architect: Martin Elsaesser

 

Further reading: Andreas Hanser: Brechung der Moderne. Der Salon der Lilly von Schnitzler in Frankfurt a.M. In: „Bereitschaft zum Risiko“. Lilly von Schnitzler 1889–1981. Sammlerin und Mäzenin. Schloßmuseum Murnau 2011, p. 26-32.
