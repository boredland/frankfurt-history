# Bruchfeldstraße housing estate / Zickzackhausen

*Breubergstraße 30*

![](../../images/c5909aa9947716685fd5b2f80514467a_original.jpg)

The existing partial development of the area made it impossible to create a uniform design for the housing estate. However, this also gave it a special charm. Its western area around Haardtwaldplatz and Donnersbergstraße is characterised by multi-storey blocks and single-family terraced houses. The eastern part, south of Bruchfeldstraße, is dominated by a closed residential block, with a courtyard comprising playgrounds and a paddling pool (no longer existent today). Its saw-shaped rows of houses led to the nickname “Zickzackhausen”. The reason for the expressive appearance of the facade was better lighting, which resulted from the slight rotation of the buildings. The communal central building of the residential block housed a laundry, radio station, kindergarten, nursery, as well as branches of the welfare and health department.

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen (ABG)

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architects: Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff

Further reading: DW Dreysse: May-Siedlungen. Architekturführer durch zehn Siedlungen des Neuen Frankfurt 1926–1930. 4th ed., Köln 2019, p. 40-43.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
