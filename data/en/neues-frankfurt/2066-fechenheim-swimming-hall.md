# Fechenheim swimming hall

*Konstanzer Straße 16*

![Entrance portal of the swimming hall with brick plinth, mullioned windows, and brick framing as part of the facade.](../../images/d2d14f8cefcb768ce45479b65b4a3149_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Max Göllner | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Entrance portal of the swimming hall with brick plinth, mullioned windows, and brick framing as part of the facade.*

The swimming hall in the newly incorporated district of Fechenheim was opened in 1929. The cubic building, designed by Martin Elsaesser, overcame traditional concepts for swimming halls: instead of placing the pool inside, the swimming hall opens up to the south with a glass wall, enabling a direct transition to the sunbathing area, the “Luftbad”. The high-contrast design with white facades, a brick plinth, majolica tiles in the entrance area, and bricks on the window frames combines Elsaesser’s typical stylistic features. Mullioned windows and bricks are also among his trademark elements. Moreover, Elsaesser considered socio-hygienic aspects, such as the separation of the changing rooms from the pool. The swimming hall was considered one of the most advanced of its kind in Europe and set new standards for the construction of swimming facilities.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1928–1929

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
