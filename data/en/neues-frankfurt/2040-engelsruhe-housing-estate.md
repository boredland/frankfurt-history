# Engelsruhe housing estate

*Engelsruhe 51*

![Plan of the Engelsruhe housing estate. The houses built in 1929 are colored black.](../../images/8cc2b7c944c1ea3bb63d8591a101319f_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1930, Heft 4/5, S. 132 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Plan of the Engelsruhe housing estate. The houses built in 1929 are colored black.*

The Engelsruhe housing estate in Unterliederbach was built in several phases by different developers, starting in 1892. It offered housing primarily for workers in the chemical paint industry. After the incorporation of Höchst in 1928, the City of Frankfurt built 60 apartments in single-family houses in the fifth construction phase, some with a granny flat. The floor plan types were adopted from the Praunheim housing estate, but the rows of houses were built in a continuous north-south orientation for better lighting. Only a fraction of the overall plan, on the streets Engelsruhe, Alemannen-, Markomannen- and Cheruskerweg, was realised. Due to the expansion of the A66 motorway and the “Social City” project, some of the rows of houses were demolished or privatised in the 2000s. As of 2024, a few remaining rows of houses are in poor condition.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm (planning), Eugen Kaufmann

Further reading: 
Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 287-288.
