# Ernst May’s residential home

*Am Schwalbenschwanz*

![Villa May, garden view with large window.](../../images/9c1a640187bd9e3f285732038b5f9ae0_original.jpg)
*1925 — Author: Unbekannt | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: Villa May, garden view with large window.*

Ernst May’s residential home is the first and programmatic building of the Neues Frankfurt. Due to its modern architectural style with a flat roof and unadorned white walls, it became a destination for many onlookers. Today, the two-storey, cubic building has been partially modified. Its special feature was the multi-storey living room with a large window reaching around the corner and overlooking the garden. Ilse May’s loom, acquired at the Bauhaus, stood on the gallery in the living room. An extension above the garage provided bedrooms for the domestic staff. The sun terrace on top was to be realised on hundreds of buildings in the new housing estates shortly afterwards. The garden designed by Leberecht Migge was equally exemplary.

Construction management: Ernst May

Construction period: 1925

Architects: Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff
