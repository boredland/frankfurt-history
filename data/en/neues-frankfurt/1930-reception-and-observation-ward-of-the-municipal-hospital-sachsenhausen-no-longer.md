# Reception and observation ward of the municipal hospital Sachsenhausen – no longer extant

*Eschenbachstraße 14*

![Children’s observation ward](../../images/25a482b7939c0bfa0054f37f282bc322_original.jpg)
*around 1929 — Author: Grete Leistikow | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1931, Heft 4/5, S. 82 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Children’s observation ward*

The children’s ward was located on the southern edge of the hospital grounds and was built at the same time as the disinfection centre. Children with infectious diseases could be isolated in the ward and observed at the same time. The single-storey low-rise building was perfectly designed for these tasks. Glass bunks for the young patients were arranged around a central nurses’ station. Thus, one could keep an eye on the patients without disturbing them. Floor-to-ceiling sliding windows could be opened completely towards a surrounding terrace. During good weather, the hospital beds were simply wheeled outside. The building was damaged during the Second World War, later repurposed, and ultimately torn down.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Walter Körte
