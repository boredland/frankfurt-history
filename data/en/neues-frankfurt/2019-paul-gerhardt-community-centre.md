# Paul Gerhardt community centre

*Gerauer Straße 52*

![Shell of the Paul Gerhardt church](../../images/243f03623da717ac469614f0b838bc97_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Bauwelt, 1930, Heft 41, Seite 16 | Description: Shell of the Paul Gerhardt church*

In 1900, Niederrad became an incorporated district of Frankfurt. It was not until thirty years later that the district was provided with its own Protestant church, named after the Lutheran theologian and hymn writer Paul Gerhardt (1607–1676). The cubic building had a rectangular floor plan with a vestibule and two parish halls on the ground floor. On the hall floor above, there was an altar to the east and a stage to the west. The attached tower wing housed a youth room. After being destroyed in the Second World War, the community centre was rebuilt in 1950 and reconsecrated in 1952. Today, the stage is also used for theatre performances.

Construction management: Paul Gerhardt parish

Construction period: 1929-1930

Architect: Gottlob Schaupp
