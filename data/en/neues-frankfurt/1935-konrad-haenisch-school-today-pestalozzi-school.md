# Konrad Haenisch school (today: Pestalozzi school)

*Vatterstraße 1-3*

![Front of the school building. The large, numberless clock can be seen at the top left.](../../images/eddaad29d48c67d241e69c2767bfe157_original.jpg)
*around 1927 — Author: Buch vom Bauen (1927) | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Front of the school building. The large, numberless clock can be seen at the top left.*

Opened in 1927, the school was the first school building in Frankfurt constructed after 1918. It was named after politician Konrad Haenisch, a member of the Social Democratic Party. The building was to become the centre of the Riederwald housing estate and of its extension that was never realised. In addition to classrooms, workshops, terraces for outdoor lessons, and a gymnasium, the design also included an assembly hall for school and community events, dining rooms, a public library, and various facilities for the care of small children. A school garden with a paddling pool was also part of the complex. At the time, nature-based teaching was considered ground-breaking. One of Elsaesser’s trademarks was the large, numberless clock on the facade. Renamed Pestalozzi school in 1933, the school is still in use today.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1926–1927

Architect: Martin Elsaesser

Further reading: Christina Gräwe: Martin Elsaessers Frankfurter Schulen. In: Martin Elsaesser und das Neue Frankfurt. Ed. by Thomas Elsaesser, Christina Gräwe, Jörg Schilling, Peter Cachola Schmal. Tübingen, Berlin 2009, p. 112-116.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
