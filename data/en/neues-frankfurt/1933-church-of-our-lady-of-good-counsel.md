# Church of Our Lady of Good Counsel

*Bruchfeldstraße 51*

![Church of Our Lady of Good Counsel at Bruchfeldstraße in Niederrad](../../images/c317b1942d4b2f4911d30f9e3665ffd5_original.jpg)
*around 1933 — Author: Otto Emmel | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: Church of Our Lady of Good Counsel at Bruchfeldstraße in Niederrad*

Even before the First World War, the neo-Gothic church in Niederrad was no longer big enough for the Catholics in the neighbourhood. It was therefore decided to build a new basilica in the immediate vicinity. The simple, steel-framed church appears to be constructed of individual cubic elements. Its entrance is located at Bruchfeldstraße next to a striking bell tower with a height of 40 metres, pointing to the church like a landmark. Adjoining the tower is the crypt chapel of the von Weinberg family. Like the entire nave, the chapel is structured and lit by narrow, high arched windows. The church suffered severe damage during the Second World War and was subsequently remodelled several times, particularly its interior. Accordingly, the eye-catching red exterior colour dates back to the post-war period.

 

Construction period: 1932–1933

Architects: Hans and Christoph Rummel
