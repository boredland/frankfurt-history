# Mammolshainer Straße housing estate

*Anspacher Straße*

![Mammolshainer Straße housing estate, still unplastered houses built with prefabricated slabs on the left.](../../images/b21bb5d817ad72d6c8d693e4f740182c_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1930, Heft 4/5, S. 130 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Mammolshainer Straße housing estate, still unplastered houses built with prefabricated slabs on the left.*

The Mammolshainer Straße housing estate was built in the Gallus district in 1927, comprising 296 flats for homeless families. 200 of these flats were built with prefabricated concrete slabs. This cost-effective and rapid construction method was used to provide housing to as many homeless people as possible in the shortest possible time. With two rooms, the flats were designed to have a size of 32 square metres, at a cost of 20 Reichsmark. However, in the event of a change in the tenants’ economic situation, it was possible to combine two flats. The housing estate had a communal bath and wash house and generous open spaces, which were intended to compensate for the cramped living conditions. It was entirely torn down in 1982.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1927

Architects: Ernst May, Wilhelm Derlam, Anton Brenner
