# Museum garden of the ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.

*Kleingartenanlage Heddernheim II, Parzelle 16*

![Historical view of the allotment garden site in the east of the Römerstadt housing estate with standardised gardens and sheds.](../../images/3fe117f0ae6c45c289a93cd51eb5e54a_original.jpg)
*around 1930 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Historical view of the allotment garden site in the east of the Römerstadt housing estate with standardised gardens and sheds.*

In the east of the Römerstadt housing estate, along the bastion wall, there is an allotment garden site that was constructed at the same time as the residential buildings at the end of the 1920s. The plots could be leased by people who lived in the multi-dwelling units and did not have their own garden next to their house. In their allotment gardens, they could grow fruit and vegetables and take a break from the strenuous work in the factories and offices. The gardens were standardised and therefore all looked the same. The shed (design: Margarete Schütte-Lihotzky) was the centre of each garden.

The museum garden of the ernst-may-gesellschaft is cultivated by a team of volunteer gardeners according to historical guidelines and can be visited.

*Author: Christina Treutlein*
