# Municipal power house, test authority centre 6

*Gutleutstraße 280*

![Power house with test authority centre 6 on Gutleutstraße, with large garages of the Frankfurt automobile company, designed by Ferdinand Kramer, adjoining on the right.](../../images/6466d52b0b92e4b4906bc3435ee7bb30_original.jpg)
*1933 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Power house with test authority centre 6 on Gutleutstraße, with large garages of the Frankfurt automobile company, designed by Ferdinand Kramer, adjoining on the right.*

Test authority centre 6 was built not far from the Kraftwerk West power house as part of a larger complex. The steel frame building, which is hardly noticed today, is considered one of the most advanced industrial buildings of its time. It housed warehouses, cable stores, laboratories, and workshops. Meters and measuring devices, which were increasingly needed, were calibrated in the test centre. The use of the so-called Zeiss-Dywidag shell construction method, which was also used in the construction of the wholesale market hall, was completely new. The roofing of the assembly yard with a dome 26 metres in diameter and a thickness of just four centimetres of concrete was considered sensational. Other parts of the building were covered with bold concrete barrel vaults. For the administration building facing the street, Adolf Meyer set striking design accents with varied window grids.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1928–1929

Architect: Adolf Meyer
