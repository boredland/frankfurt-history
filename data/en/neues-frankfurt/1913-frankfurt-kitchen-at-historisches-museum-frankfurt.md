# Frankfurt kitchen at Historisches Museum Frankfurt

*Saalhof 1*

![The Frankfurt kitchen at Historisches Museum Frankfurt](../../images/d2a11c40919fe7d25b7029e31a44b244_original.jpg)
*2017 — Author: Petra Welzel | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: The Frankfurt kitchen at Historisches Museum Frankfurt*

In its “100 x Frankfurt” section, the Historisches Museum Frankfurt presents a Frankfurt kitchen from the Römerstadt housing estate. In this early fitted kitchen, all necessary items were accommodated in a very small space. In the exhibition, glass panes replace the kitchen walls. This allows visitors to study the wooden construction of the built-in furniture elements from the rear. They were originally installed by carpenters on site instead of being mass-produced in factories. The pea-green colour corresponds to the first coat of paint and is one of around 30 known colour variations of the Frankfurt kitchen. The Prometheus electric cooker on display was part of the concept of the fully electrified Römerstadt housing estate and was one of the very first large household electrical ppliances at the time.

*Author: Ulrike May*
