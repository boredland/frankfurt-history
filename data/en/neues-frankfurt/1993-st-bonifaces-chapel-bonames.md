# St Boniface’s chapel, Bonames

*Oberer Kalbacher Weg 7*

![St Boniface’s chapel in Frankfurt-Bonames.](../../images/9c13d0d7d7e01f60e7b4a5fa70453afa_original.jpg)
*1933-1944 — Author: Friedrich Robert Otto Emmel | License: Historisches Museum Frankfurt | Description: St Boniface’s chapel in Frankfurt-Bonames.*

The Roman Catholic St Boniface’s chapel in Bonames is a frugal building with an octagonal floor plan and a pointed tent roof. It was designed by church architect Martin Weber in 1932/33 during economically difficult times as a simple timber frame construction, which was lined with pumice stones. Thus, the chapel was built in just ten days. The central room has a diameter of 14 metres. The altar niche is located on its north wall, a small entrance hall is on the opposite side. The vestry was located in an extension to the west. When the neighbouring parish church was built in 1966, the chapel lost its function and soon only served as a parish hall. Since 2013, the listed building has remained unused due to its poor structural condition and has been awaiting a thorough renovation.
