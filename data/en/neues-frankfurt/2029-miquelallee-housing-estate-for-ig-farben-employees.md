# Miquelallee housing estate (for I.G. Farben employees)

*Carl-von-Weinberg-Straße 1*

![View from Eschersheimer Landstraße into Miquelallee with apartment blocks.](../../images/1d6dda0908c78c48116232cb718137af_original.jpg)
*after 1930 — Author: Max Göllner | License: ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V. | Description: View from Eschersheimer Landstraße into Miquelallee with apartment blocks.*

The housing estate was built for employees of I.G. Farben, who were to work in the nearby Poelzig building. Bordered by Miquelallee and Eschersheimer Landstrasse, the housing estate is based on the rectangular outline of streets. During the planning, there were intense discussions about rational development methods, with double rows of multi-storey buildings along a north-south axis being favoured. Within the projects of Neues Frankfurt, these were implemented most consistently here. Several double-row buildings are arranged at a right angle to Miquelallee, consisting of 4- to 5-storey apartment blocks. In the rear area, they are complemented by residential streets with 2-storey single-family homes. In the post-war period, the housing estate was named after the Jewish patron and founder Carl von Weinberg.

Construction management: Aktienbaugesellschaft für kleine Wohnungen (ABG)

Construction period: 1930

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Wolfgang Bangert (planning); Ernst May, Carl-Hermann Rudloff (architectural adaptation)

Further reading: Christoph Mohr/Michael Müller: Funktionalität und Moderne. Das Neue Frankfurt und seine Bauten 1925–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 236.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
