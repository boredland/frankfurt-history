# St Paul’s church reconstruction

*Paulsplatz 11*

![Drawing of the eastern view.](../../images/8eb4d0017d68f0838d513815dea8a0bb_original.jpg)
*1946 — Author: Martin Elsaesser | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung / Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: Drawing of the eastern view.*

After the Second World War, the war-damaged St Paul’s church (Paulskirche) became the subject of an architectural competition organised in 1946. The plan was to construct an additional administration building while keeping the shape of the oval central building recognisable. The latter’s interior was to be redesigned. Martin Elsaesser’s estate contains an undated sketch project that envisaged an adjoining new building complex to the north of Paulskirche: a semi-circular, single-storey foyer flanked by multi-storey administration buildings attached to the side. Elsaesser intended to close off the interior of the church with a domed or tent roof construction and to leave the upper edge of the wall open in the appearance of a ruin. The signed sketches indicate that Elsaesser did not participate in the official competition.
