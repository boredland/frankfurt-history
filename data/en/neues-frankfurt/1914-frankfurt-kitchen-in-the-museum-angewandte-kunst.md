# Frankfurt kitchen in the Museum Angewandte Kunst

*Schaumainkai 17*

![The Frankfurt kitchen in the Museum Angewandte Kunst](../../images/9dd11ca261b18b6a4c915be744b11968_original.jpg)
*2017 — Author: Anja Jahn | License: Museum Angewandte Kunst | Description: The Frankfurt kitchen in the Museum Angewandte Kunst*

Frankfurt kitchens from housing estates can be found in museum collections around the world. The Museum Angewandte Kunst Frankfurt presents an example from 1929 in its permanent exhibition “Elementarteile. Aus den Sammlungen”. This kitchen was removed from an apartment block in the Bornheimer Hang housing estate as late as 2015. The kitchen, which has hardly changed in almost 100 years, is displayed in an open arrangement. Visitors can look into the installation from the former entrance to the kitchen and from the window wall. The floor tiles made of Solnhofen shell limestone, the wall tiles, the door frames, and the furniture elements are original. Items that have not been preserved, such as the kitchen window and the ceiling lamp, have not been reconstructed. The kitchen was originally grey; now, the latest coat of white paint can be seen.

Further reading: Klaus Klemp, Matthias Wagner K (eds.): Das Neue Frankfurt und die Frankfurter Küche. Frankfurt am Main 2020.
