# Rütschlehen housing estate

*Am Rütschlehen 4*

![Jakobsbrunnenstraße in the Rütschlehen housing estate](../../images/7ce761f6ea00aa4c1e485050676978e8_original.jpg)
*around 1940 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Jakobsbrunnenstraße in the Rütschlehen housing estate*

The Rütschlehen housing estate is located to the south of the former Cassella chemical plant in Fechenheim, for whose workers the 20 apartment blocks were intended. An angular perimeter development was built on Jakobsbrunnenstraße and Am Rütschlehen in two construction phases. A third construction phase on what is now Leo-Gans-Straße was planned but not realised under Ernst May. The 103 flats with two or three rooms were all equipped with a Frankfurt kitchen. The block of flats surrounded a communal garden area, and the ground floor flats even had adjoining gardens. The fact that the apartment blocks had gabled rather than flat roofs may have been a concession to the existing buildings in the neighbourhood.

Construction management: Nassauische Heimstätte

Construction period: 1929–1930

Architects: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm (planning); Hugo Jahnel

Further reading: 

Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 289-290.
