# Westhausen housing estate

*Ludwig-Landmann-Straße 208*

![Aerial view of the Westhausen housing estate.](../../images/245cbcf411e8bfad01b87d3644553dd8_original.jpg)
*around 1932 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Privat | Description: Aerial view of the Westhausen housing estate.*

Westhausen was built during the global economic crisis, being one of the last housing estates of Neues Frankfurt to be realised. It offers accommodation for people on the breadline and consists of 450 terraced houses in a north-south orientation and nine four-storey access balcony blocks arranged transversely. The strict seriality of the entire housing estate is consistently maintained, as neither the houses are offset nor are the streets curved. Although the housing estate is part of the Nidda valley development, it lacks any kind of urbanistic reference to the river. With their open stairwells and access balconies, the houses designed by Ferdinand Kramer and Eugen Blanck embody a cost-saving approach. Kramer’s central laundry with its high chimney is the only striking element in the otherwise uniform housing estate, which already foreshadowed the style of the 1950s.

Construction management: Nassauische Heimstätte, Gartenstadt AG, City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929–1931

Planning: Ernst May, Herbert Boehm, Wolfgang Bangert

Architects: Ernst May, Emil Kaufmann, Ferdinand Kramer, Eugen Blanck, Otto Fucker, Franz Schuster

Horticultural design: Max Bromme

Further reading: Heike Risse: Frühe Moderne in Frankfurt am Main 1920–1933. Frankfurt am Main 1984, p. 277-281.

*Author: Kenneth Hujer*
