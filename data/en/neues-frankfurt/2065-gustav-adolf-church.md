# Gustav Adolf church

*Alt-Niederursel 30*

![View of the Gustav Adolf church](../../images/7e7017a1124e1ccfdc9af8f87dbdc8a2_original.jpg)
*unknown — Author: unbekannt / unknown | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung / Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: View of the Gustav Adolf church*

Until the beginning of the 20th century, Protestants in Niederursel used an old, dilapidated church for their services. After its demolition in 1927, a new building was quickly erected according to plans by the experienced church builder Martin Elsaesser. The modern central building with its copper-covered tent roof and narrow bell tower blends in surprisingly well with the small-scale village structure. Inside, the preaching room is illuminated by high window ribbons. The baptistry, vestry, and a staircase to the gallery are connected to its octagonal floor plan. Due to the slope of the property, it is possible to enter the parish hall in the basement from the outside. In 2016/2017, a careful restoration returned the interior to its original colour scheme of red, grey, white, blue, yellow, and green.

Construction management: Lutheran synod of the City of Frankfurt

Construction period: 1927–1928

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Gerhard Planck

Further reading:

Jörg Schilling (ed.), Gustav-Adolf-Kirche Niederursel in Frankfurt am Main 1927–1928, 2nd ed., Hamburg 2017.

*Author: Ulrike May*
