# Municipal and university mental hospital (today: Clinic for psychiatry, psychosomatic medicine, and psychotherapy)

*Heinrich-Hoffmann-Straße 10*

![Northern facade with entrance to the mental hospital](../../images/81045ae4ebf958bbdba8e82074ece5bd_original.jpg)
*1930s — Author: Unbekannt | License: Martin-Elsaesser-Stiftung / Architekturmuseum TU München | Description: Northern facade with entrance to the mental hospital*

The new mental hospital was built in Niederrad, as the I.G. Farben administration building was erected on the site of the former “Sanatorium for the Insane and Epileptic”. The modern clinic concept was developed by architect Martin Elsaesser in cooperation with Karl Kleist, a psychiatrist. A well-lit, symmetrical facility was created in a very short time. The central building housed the administration, operating, and staff rooms, as well as the polyclinic department. Annexes to the side of the entrance housed the kitchen and an auditorium that is still in good condition today. Two wing buildings with wards are adjoined to the south. Roof terraces, garden courtyards, and green areas were available for patients. The clinic, which has been a listed building since 1986, was partially destroyed in 1944, rebuilt in 1956, and has been awaiting renovation for years.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1928–1930

Architect: Martin Elsaesser; Max Bromme (horticultural design)

Quelle: Jörg Schilling, Klinik für Gemüts- und Nervenkranke, Frankfurt a. M. 1930 (martin elsaesser bauheft 10), Hamburg 2018.
