# Wilhelmsbrücke swimming hall – draft

*Friedensbrücke, Ecke Untermainkai*

![Draft of Wilhelmsbrücke swimming hall, south view from the Main river.](../../images/f6cefa4620fd9eb08de4b1b432df4fe0_original.jpg)
*1930 — Author: Martin Elsaesser | License: Martin Elsaesser: Bauten und Entwürfe aus den Jahren 1924–1932, Berlin 1933 | Description: Draft of Wilhelmsbrücke swimming hall, south view from the Main river.*

Martin Elsaesser designed several swimming halls, of which only the building in Fechenheim was realised. A swimming hall was also planned on the northern bank of the Main river, directly next to today’s Friedensbrücke. There were changing rooms separated by gender on the lower floor, while access to tub baths, massage rooms, and the light-flooded two-storey indoor swimming pool was on the upper storey. A large glass wall to the south opened onto a sun terrace with a view of the Main river and Sachsenhausen. Access to the building was from the bridge on the second floor. This would have provided a connection to the “Nizza” green area, as well as to the river baths in the Main towards the old city centre. Diagonally across the river, Martin Elsaesser planned the School of Arts and Crafts, which was also never realised.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Draft: 1928–1929

Architect: Martin Elsaesser
