# Waldstadion

*Mörfelder Landstraße 362*

![Aerial view of the stadium grounds with a view of the main stand and its antique facade.](../../images/9892f84332b12f9f99dd711eceddd1ef_original.jpg)
*around 1928 — Author: Unbekannt | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of the stadium grounds with a view of the main stand and its antique facade.*

The Waldstadion in Frankfurt was opened on 21 May 1925 after four years of construction and was considered one of the most modern stadiums of its time. It offered a capacity for 35,000 visitors. Reusing the site of a former military shooting range in the city forest, a sports park, a festival meadow, as well as a cycling and swimming stadium were also built. The sports field was designed by Max Bromme, head of the urban horticultural department, while the grandstand structure was designed by Gustav Schaumann, Ernst May’s predecessor as head of the urban planning department. The main stand on the north side was provided with an antique Greek facade, while the remaining stands were based on earth fillings. The grandstand structure was torn down in the 1950s. Today, the Deutsche Bank Park (formerly Commerzbank Arena), built in 2002–2005, stands in its place.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1921–1925

Architects: Max Bromme, Gustav Schaumann
