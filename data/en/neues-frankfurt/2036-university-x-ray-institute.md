# University X-ray institute

*Eschenbachstraße 14*

![Exterior view of the university X-ray institute](../../images/defc8d553c4f7fff1d84dce7d0d7bb4c_original.jpg)
*1931 — Author: Hermann Collischonn | License: Das Neue Frankfurt, 1931, Heft 4/5, S. 86 (Sammlung der ernst-may-gesellschaft e.V.) | Description: Exterior view of the university X-ray institute*

Since the discovery of X-rays in 1895, Frankfurt had been one of the centres for radiological research in Germany. The expansion of the discipline required a new and modern radiological institute with a separate ward. At the time, there were no models for a building of this type yet. Elsaesser and Körte added a highly functional building to the side wing of the surgical clinic built in 1900. The main challenge lay in the technical equipment and infrastructure. The building, which no longer exists today, was constructed in reinforced concrete and iron. One of the four storeys consisted of plastered brickwork, whereas the remaining storeys had glass block facades for better lighting.

Construction management: City of Frankfurt am Main

Construction period: 1929 

Architects: Martin Elsaesser, Walter Körte

Further reading: www.martin-elsaesser.de/projekte/roentgeninstitut-der-universitaet-staedtisches-krankenhaus-sachsenhausen-frankfurt-am-main/
