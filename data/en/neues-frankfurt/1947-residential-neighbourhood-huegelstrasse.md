# Residential neighbourhood Hügelstraße

*Fontanestraße 8*

![Aerial view of the residential neighbourhood Hügelstraße.](../../images/19ece140e20c1149ba86d0bd2e8292c7_original.jpg)
*June 1929 — Author: Hugo Junkers Luftbild | License: Institut für Stadtgeschichte Frankfurt am Main | Description: Aerial view of the residential neighbourhood Hügelstraße.*

Displaying a variety of architectural styles, the group of residential buildings on Hügelstraße was developed for cooperative forms of housing, with three building companies involved. In 1926, KOMBA built a closed block with six two-room flats for homeless bank employees on Jakob-Schiff-Straße, planned by Franz Roeckle, and 59 two-storey single family houses along Fontanestraße in 1929. The sawtooth arrangement of the houses at Fontanestraße 15-35 is particularly striking, reminiscent of the so-called “Zickzackhausen” block in Niederrad, and it was expanded in the 1950s in the style of the time. Also in the late 1920s, GAGFAH built several rows of 51 single family houses, as well as multiple apartment blocks with a total of 130 flats based on designs by Ludwig Bernoully. To the east of these buildings, the “Südwestdeutsche A.-G. für Kleinwohnungsbau” realised the SÜWAG block, designed by Gustav Schaupp, with 73 flats and spacious green areas.
